Cantonese Dictionary

A modern edition of 《英華分韻撮要》 A Tonic Dictionary of the Chinese Language in the Canton Dialect (1856) by Samuel Wells Williams.

Source

The version of Tonic Dictionary used as the master reference is ark:/13960/s20jtk4f14j at archive.org, sourced from a Google Books scan of 83600-B held at ÖNB Hauptabt. Heldenplatz.

While it would have been more preferable to use ark:/13960/t0ps1ph66 at archive.org from the Library of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (being both a higher-quality colour scan and not watermarked by Google), that version has severe paging issues:

  1. Pages xxxiv and xxxv are missing (they should appear between n38 and n39).
  2. Pages are duplicated:
    • Pages 14 through 40 appear twice: once at n53 through n79, and again at n85 through n111.
    • Pages 9* through 13* appear twice: once at n80 through n84, and again at 9 through 13.
    It is unclear whether this is an error on the part of the book printer, the scanner, or both.

Modernisation

The modernisation is a ongoing solo effort by Conway, consisting of:

  1. Normalisation of characters and readings to account for modern usage;
  2. Transcribing its entries (black serif text);
  3. Making corrections to typos [in square brackets];
  4. Collating the entries by Jyutping (noting that there are phonological differences between the Ts'ing Dynasty Cantonese recorded by Williams and modern Hong Kong Cantonese);
  5. Under each Jyutping pronunciation, sorting the entries by Unicode code point;
  6. Inserting Jyutping (crimson sans-serif text) alongside the Williams romanisations;
  7. Inserting characters (crimson sans-serif text) if not already supplied alongside the Williams romanisations, where practicable;
  8. Inserting commentary (crimson sans-serif text) and cross-reference links (blue);
  9. Adding entries for some characters that were not recorded by Williams.

No effort has been made to alter or censor the Williams text for the sensitivities of 21st-century readers.

Cantonese Dictionary

A modern edition of 《英華分韻撮要》 A Tonic Dictionary of the Chinese Language in the Canton Dialect (1856) by Samuel Wells Williams.

Modernisation

The version of Tonic Dictionary used as the base reference is ark:/13960/t0ps1ph66 at archive.org, which is a scan of the copy held at the Library of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

Where there be missing pages or damage to the base reference version, a secondary version is used a subsidiary reference, namely ark:/13960/s20jtk4f14j at archive.org, from a scan by Google.

The modernisation is a solo effort by Conway, consisting of:

  1. Transcribing its entries (black serif text);
  2. Make corrections to typos [in square brackets];
  3. Collating the entries by Jyutping (noting that there are phonological differences between the Ts'ing Dynasty Cantonese recorded by Williams and modern Hong Kong Cantonese);
  4. Under each Jyutping pronunciation, sorting the entries by Unicode code point;
  5. Inserting Jyutping (crimson sans-serif text) alongside the Williams romanisation of entries;
  6. Inserting characters and Jyutping (crimson sans-serif text) alongside the romanisation-only examples, where practicable;
  7. Inserting remarks (crimson sans-serif text) and cross-reference links (blue);
  8. Adding entries for some characters that were not recorded by Williams.

No effort has been made to alter or censor the Williams text for the sensitivities of 21st century readers.

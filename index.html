Other useful references

  • 《江湖尺牘分韻撮要合集》 (1838), by 虞學圃 and 溫岐石.

    An 1838 re-engraving (道光十八年重鐫) containing Fan Wan 《分韻撮要》 in the lower half of its pages, as scanned by Trove (in collaboration with the National Library of Australia).

  • A Chinese Dictionary in the Cantonese Dialect (1877), by Ernst Johann Eitel.

    An expansion of Williams' Tonic Dictionary. The goals of this expansion included:

    • Utilising the newly available works by James Legge (on the Chinese Classics) to better document the literary language, which Eitel felt received insufficient attention compared to the Cantonese vernacular in Williams' original work.
    • Including some characters from Fan Wan 《分韻撮要》 and Kangxi 《康熙字典》 which Eitel felt indispensable.
    • Including readings and English translations of definitions from Kangxi.
    • Better distinguishing the register (Classical vs Colloquial vs Mixed) of the example phrases.
    • Supplying the Chinese characters that were not included in the example phrases.

    See Eitel's Introduction for details.

  • 韻典網 (ytenx.org), by BYVoid.

    A database of the olden rime dictionaries (《廣韻》 etc.).

  • Chinese Text Project Dictionary, by Donald Sturgeon.

    Easy reference to radical+strokes, Unihan readings, 《廣韻》, and 《康熙字典》.

  • 萌典 (moedict.tw), by Audrey Tang.

    Taiwan g0v (gov-zero) dictionary.

Modernisation

The modernisation here of Williams' original is an ongoing solo effort by Conway, consisting of:

  1. Normalisation of characters and readings to account for modern usage;
  2. Transcribing its entries (black serif text);
  3. Making corrections to typos [in square brackets];
  4. Associating entries with those in the original Fan Wan 《分韻撮要》;
  5. Collating the entries by Jyutping (noting that there are phonological differences between the Tsʻing Dynasty Cantonese recorded by Williams and modern Hong Kong Cantonese);
  6. Under each Jyutping pronunciation, sorting the entries by radical+strokes, then Unicode code point;
  7. Inserting Jyutping (crimson sans-serif text) alongside the Williams romanisations;
  8. Inserting characters (crimson sans-serif text) if not already supplied alongside the Williams romanisations, where practicable (with assistance from Eitel's 1877 Dictionary);
  9. Inserting commentary (crimson sans-serif text) and cross-reference links (blue);
  10. Adding entries for some characters that were not recorded by Williams.

No effort has been made to alter or censor the 1856 Williams text for the sensitivities of 21st-century readers.

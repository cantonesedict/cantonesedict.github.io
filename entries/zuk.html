囑 Chuk꜆ (zuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 21
Unicode code point
U+56D1
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 63 囑) 囑括又吩咐也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 35* 囑꜆)
    • To order, to bid, to enjoin;
    • chuk꜆ fú[꜄] (zuk1 fu3 囑咐) to desire, to charge;
    • chuk꜆ ꜀shü (zuk1 syu1 囑書), a will;
    • chuk꜆ tʻok꜆ (zuk1 tok3 囑託) to commission;
    • ꜃mái chuk꜆ (maai5 zuk1 買囑) to bribe, to get evidence in one's favor.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 囑咐: Changed fú꜅ (fu6) to common reading fú꜄ (fu3).

捉 Chuk꜆ (zuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 7
Unicode code point
U+6349
Reading variations
  • zuk3 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 94 [捉] Chéuk꜆ (zoek3)) 捕也[搦]也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 35* 捉꜆)
    • To seize, to grasp, to gripe and hold;
    • to catch;
    • chuk꜆ ꜁ná (zuk1 naa4 捉拿), to pursue, for the purpose of seizing;
    • chuk꜆ wok꜇ (zuk1 wok6 捉獲) to arrest a criminal;
    • chuk꜆ yung꜅ ꜁shan (zuk1 jung6 san4 捉用神), to divine;
    • chuk꜆ ꜃lò ꜂shü (zuk1 lou5 syu2 捉老鼠), to catch rats;
    • chuk꜆ ꜀tò (zuk1 dou1 捉刀), 'to grasp the knife,' to countenance or help one, to inform the government.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword ⿰木足 to 捉, and corrected 榒 to 搦.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【捉】 (zuk1):
    1. To play a board game.
      • 捉棋 (zuk1 kei4-2): "Play chess."
      • 捉圍棋 (zuk1 wai4 kei4-2): "Play surrounding-chess" (i.e. go).
      • 捉一鋪 (zuk1 jat1 pou1): "Play one game [of chess]."
      • 唔識捉 (m4 sik1 zuk1): "[I] don't know [how to] play."
  • 【捉伊人】 (zuk1 ji1 jan4-1):
    1. Hide and seek.
  • 【捉字蝨】 (zuk1 zi6 sat1):
    1. To nit-pick choice of words (or writing more generally).

足 Tsuk꜆ (zuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾜ + 0
Unicode code point
U+8DB3
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 65 足) 脚也備足足完足
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 598 足꜆)
    • The leg;
    • the foot;
    • enough, sufficient;
    • full, satiated, no deficiency;
    • entirely, in full, wholly;
    • to satisfy, to please;
    • to satisfy the wants of;
    • pure, as silver;
    • actions, conduct;
    • the 137th radical of characters relating to going;
    • shap꜇ tsuk꜆ (sap6 zuk1 十足) complete in all respects;
    • ꜂shau tsuk꜆ (sau2 zuk1 手足) hands and feet, brothers;
    • tsuk꜆ ꜃ling (zuk1 ling5 足領), enough! no more, I beg of you!
    • tsz'꜅ tsuk꜆ (zi6 zuk1 自足) self-sufficient, conceited;
    • tsuk꜆ tsuk꜆ ꜂wan ꜃ní pún꜄ yat꜇ (zuk1 zuk1 wan2 nei5 bun3 jat6 足足搵你一日) I've annoyed you half a day;
    • shat꜆ tsuk꜆ (sat1 zuk1 失足) to slip and fall; to make a blunder, a faux-pas;
    • tsuk꜆ há꜅ (zuk1 haa6 足下) your presence, said to a friend;
    • ꜁'m ꜀chí tsuk꜆ (m4 zi1 zuk1 唔知足) discontented, never satisfied;
    • pat꜆ tsuk꜆ tò꜅ (bat1 zuk1 dou6 不足道) beyond description, can not be spoken of;
    • tsuk꜆ shò꜄ ꜁'m tsuk꜆ (zuk1 sou3 m4 zuk1 足數唔足) is the number just right?
    • ꜀fung tsuk꜆ (fung1 zuk1 豐足) prosperous, to the heart's content;
    • ꜁yan ꜀sam ꜃mò ím꜄ tsuk꜆ (jan4 sam1 mou5 jim3 zuk1 人心冇饜足) the heart is never satisfied;
    • ꜁ün tong꜄ tsuk꜆ ꜂tsz' (jyun4 dong3 zuk1 zi2 圓當足子) whole dollars alone are reckoned to be full coin.

    Read tsü꜅ (zeoi6); (see 足 zeoi6).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 不足道: More not worth speaking of, than beyond description.
  • Williams' 足數唔足: This is dated syntax. In modern Cantonese, it would be 足唔足數, with only the verb hugging the negative.

僉 ꜀Tsʻím (cim1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 11
Unicode code point
U+50C9
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 47 僉) 咸也皆也共也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 47 ⿳人⿻丅口从) 咸也皆也共也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 571 ꜀僉)
    • All, the whole;
    • an unanimous voice, the general opinion;
    • a flail;
    • ꜀tsʻím [át]꜆ (cim1 aat3 僉押) all signed it.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 僉押: Changed Middle Chinese áp꜆ (aap3) to common reading át꜆ (aat3).

㔐 ꜀Tsʻím (cim1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 16
Unicode code point
U+3510
Alternative forms
  • 𠠃 (⿰⿳从一韭刂)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 47 ⿰⿳艹非一刂) [切]也割也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 571 ꜀𠠃 (⿰⿳从一韭刂); here normalised to 㔐)
    • To cut;
    • to stick in the throat;
    • ꜀tsʻím ꜃kʻü ꜁hau ꜁lung (cim1 keoi5 hau4 lung4 㔐佢喉嚨), pierce his weasand;
    • ꜀tsʻím ꜀chü (cim1 zyu1 㔐豬), to stick a pig;
    • ꜀tsʻím shü꜅ (cim1 syu6 㔐樹) to ingraft by slips.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 竊 to 切.
  • I have normalised 𠠃 (⿰⿳从一韭刂) to 㔐 for the better font support.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【㔐】 (cim1):
    1. To cut or sever.
    2. (Chinese chess) To capture a piece on an upper corner of the palace using an advisor.
      • 士㔐 (si6 cim1): "Advisor captures [on the upper corner]."

Olden attestation for 㔐 ꜀tsʻím (cim1) meaning "cut" or "sever":

  • 《廣韻》七廉切：𠠃：𠠃切割也
  • 《康熙字典》𠠃：《五音集韻》七廉切音籤切割也
  • 《康熙字典》㔐：《玉篇》千廉切音僉割也〇按《五音集韻》作𠠃

[籤] ꜀Tsʻím (cim1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽵ + 17
Unicode code point
U+7C64
Alternative forms
  • 簽
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 47 籖) 神籤騐也又竹籌
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 571 ꜀簽; here normalised to 籤)
    • Slips of bamboo written on for lots (…);
    • (…);
    • ꜀tsʻím ꜃ü (cim1 jyu5 籤語), to draw lots for a response.
  • (Page 571 ꜀[籤])
    • Similar to the above (簽);
    • a slip, a lot;
    • a warrant, a permit, a ticket having the officer's name on it;
    • to probe, to bore through;
    • sharp-pointed;
    • chai꜄ ꜀tsʻím (zai3 cim1 掣籤) or ꜀chá ꜀tsʻím (zaa1 cim1 揸籤), to lot out officers to their stations;
    • ꜁ngò ꜀tsʻím (ngou4 cim1 摮籤), to draw lots before idols;
    • ꜀tsʻím ꜁tʻung (cim1 tung4 籤筒), a cup to hold the slips;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀tsʻím (ceot1 cim1 出籤), to issue a warrant or permit;
    • ꜁kʻau ꜀tsʻím (kau4 cim1 求籤), to divine.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams misprints 籤 as 韱.
  • Williams' 摮籤: More shaking out than drawing.

諂 ꜂Chʻím (cim2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾔ + 8
Unicode code point
U+8AC2
Alternative forms
  • 讇
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 49 [諂]) 諛媚也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 49 [讇]) 同[上]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16* ꜂諂꜂[讇])
    • To flatter, to lie to;
    • to cajole;
    • to give a god or man worship or praise not due to him;
    • to fawn on, to court;
    • gratifying to one's feelings, adulation, sycophancy;
    • ꜂chʻím mí꜅ ꜀chí ꜁yan (cim2 mei6 zi1 jan4 諂媚之人), a flatterer;
    • ꜂chʻím ü꜅ (cim2 jyu6 諂譽) to praise extravagantly;
    • ꜂chʻím siú꜄ (cim2 siu3 諂笑) to cajole and laugh with;
    • ꜁pʻan ꜁í ꜁mò ꜂chʻím (pan4 ji4 mou4 cim2 貧而無諂), poor without being sycophantic.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword 謟 to 諂, headword ⿵門謟 to 讇, and headword self-reference to 上.
  • Williams misprints 讇 as 𨶒 (⿵門舀).

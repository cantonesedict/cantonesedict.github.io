擠 ꜀Chai ꜀Tsai (zai1)

Radical + residual strokes

Reading variations

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 28 擠) 排也推也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 9 ꜀擠 ꜀Chai (zai1))

    A colloquial word.

    • To place, to put down, to put by;
    • ꜀chai ꜁mái (zai1 maai4 擠埋), put it aside;
    • ꜀chai ꜀pín chʻü꜄ (zai1 bin1 cyu3 擠邊處) where shall I put it?
    • ꜀chai ꜀pín chʻü꜄ ꜀tò ꜂hò (zai1 bin1 cyu3 dou1 hou2 擠邊處都好), put in it down anywhere.
  • (Page 548 ꜀擠 ꜀Tsai (zai1))
    • To push (Kangxi: 排也一曰推也), to push over, to upset (Kangxi: 擠墜也);
    • to press upon, to crowd;
    • ꜀tsai ꜂yung (zai1 jung2 擠擁), to crowd and press upon.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【擠】 (zai1)
    1. To put, or put down.
      • 擠喺度 (zai1 hai2 dou6): "Put [it] over here."
  • 【擠迫】 (zai1 bik1)
    1. Crowded.
      • 人多擠迫 (jan4 do1 zai1 bik1): "Lots of people [and] crowded."

  • (Page 548 ꜂仔)

    A colloquial word.

    • A child, a son;
    • a diminutive;
    • a little thing, the smaller of two;
    • ꜂tsai ꜃nü (zai2 neoi5-2 仔女), sons and daughters, children;
    • ꜀kwái ꜂tsai (gwaai1 zai2 乖仔), a good boy;
    • ꜂tsai tái꜅ ꜂tsai shai꜄ kái꜄ (zai2 daai6 zai2 sai3 gaai3 仔大仔世界) when a son is old he must take care of himself;
    • sai꜄ ꜀man ꜂tsai (sai3 man1 zai2 細蚊仔), children, lads;
    • ꜀chü ꜂tsai (zyu1 zai2 豬仔), a pig;
    • ꜂kau ꜂tsai (gau2 zai2 狗仔), a puppy;
    • ꜀kung ꜂tsai (gung1 zai2 公仔), images, pictures, playthings;
    • sz'꜅ ꜂tsai (si6 zai2 事仔), a servant boy;
    • tsʻau꜄ ꜂tsai ꜃má (cau3 zai2 maa5-2 湊仔媽), a nurse.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 豬仔: One sold into slave labour, as in 賣豬仔.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【仔】 (zai2)
    1. A kid.
      • 生仔 (saang1 zai2): "Have kids."
    2. A son.

      • 兩仔乸 (loeng5 zai2 naa2): "[The] two [of them], son [and] mother."
      • 兩仔爺 (loeng5 zai2 je4): "[The] two [of them], son [and] father."
      • 大個仔 (daai6 go3 zai2): "[A] big boy [now]."
    3. A guy, lad, etc.
      • 佢條仔 (keoi5 tiu4 zai2): "Her guy" (i.e. boyfriend).
      • 後生仔 (hau6 saang1 zai2): "Young lad."
    4. A diminutive suffix.
      • 狗仔 (gau2 zai2): "Puppy."
      • 矮仔 (ai2 zai2): "Shorty."
      • 耳仔 (ji5 zai2): "Ears."
      • 華仔 (waa4 zai2): "Wahsy."

    • A limit, a border, a region perimeter (Kangxi: 方也, 邊也畔也), the line of junction of two things walls (Kangxi: 壁會也);
    • the time or place where another begins (Kangxi: 際接也);
    • to join (Kangxi: 合也, 會也), to begin;
    • the time of occurring, now, since;
    • ꜂sz' ꜀shang ꜀chí tsai꜄ (si2 sang1 zi1 zai3 死生之際) between life and death;
    • tsai꜄ tsíp꜆ (zai3 zip3 際接) to receive, to blend, to meet;
    • ꜂hò tsai꜄ ü꜅ (hou2 zai3 jyu6 好際遇) a good opportunity, a favorable juncture;
    • ꜂tsʻz' tsai꜄ (ci2 zai3 此際) this time;
    • ꜀fung ꜁wan tsai꜄ úi꜅ (fung1 wan4 zai3 wui6 風雲際會) the winds and clouds have met; met. to receive a favor.

嚌 Tsai꜅ (zai6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 14
Unicode code point
U+568C
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 549 嚌꜅)

    Read ꜀tsai (zai1).

    • To taste (Kangxi: 嘗也), to sip, to wet the lips;
    • (…).

    A colloquial word.

    • A superlative, denoting that the extreme has been reached;
    • tám꜅ ꜃tʻám tak꜆ tsai꜅ (taam5 dak1 zai6 淡得嚌) excessively weak, as tea;
    • ꜃mò mat꜆ tsai꜅ (mou5 mat1 zai6 冇乜嚌) not much there, nothing to speak of;
    • tái꜅ tak꜆ tsai꜅ (daai6 dak1 zai6 大得嚌) far too large;
    • hai꜅ tak꜆ tsai꜅ (hai6 dak1 zai6 係得嚌) certainly.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "Read ꜀tsai": This is probably an incorrect parsing of Kangxi 音劑, which is intended in the departing tone.
  • Williams' 淡得嚌: Corrected tám꜅ (daam6 去聲) to ꜃tʻám (taam5 上聲).
  • Williams' 係得嚌: Dated.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【得嚌】 (dak1 zai6)
    1. A final particle of excessive degree.
      • 多得嚌 (do1 dak1 zai6): "Too much."
      • 熱得嚌 (jit6 dak1 zai6): "[It's] too hot."
  • 【咁嚌】 (gam3 zai6)
    1. A final particle of almostness.
      • 熱到死咁嚌 (jit6 dou3 sei2 gam3 zai6): "[It's] so hot [we've] died, almost."
      • 食完咁嚌 (sik6 jyun4 gam3 zai6): "[We've] finished eating, almost."
  • 【乜嚌】 (mat1 zai6)
    1. A final particle of almost-nothingness.
      • 冇乜嚌 (mou5 mat1 zai6): "Almost nothing."
      • 冇食乜嚌 (mou5 sik6 mat1 zai6): "Barely ate any [of it]."

  • 【滯】 (zai6)
    1. Stagnated in the stomach, usually from eating too much.

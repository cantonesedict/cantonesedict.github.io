之 ꜀Chá (zaa1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼃ + 3
Unicode code point
U+4E4B
Reading variations
  • za1 (shorter syllable)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
(Not present)
Conway's commentary
  • Modern Cantonese dictionaries indicate a reading of zi1 maa3 for 之嘛. I disagree with this reading for three reasons:
    1. I have only heard za1 maa3.
    2. I believe that 之嘛 (za1 maa3) is a contraction of 啫 (ze1) + 吖嘛 (a1 maa3), in the same way that 𠺢嘛 (ga1 maa3) is shortened from 嘅 (ge3) + 吖嘛 (a1 maa3).
    3. I believe that any insistence on the reading of zi1 maa3 is a case of incorrectly letting the spelling dictate the pronunciation. It would be more helpful if za1 maa3 were written 𠾵嘛 instead, but unfortunately the spelling 之嘛 is already well established.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【之嘛】 (za1 maa3):
    1. A final particle of limitation, especially used defensively.
      • 講笑之嘛 (gong2 siu3 za1 maa3): "Just kidding!"
      • 等兩日之嘛 (dang2 loeng5 jat6 za1 maa3): "[It's] just [a] two-day wait."

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【揸】 (zaa1):
    1. To hold, to grasp, to control.
      • 揸拿 (zaa1 naa4): "[A] grasp [of the situation]."
      • 揸筷子 (zaa1 faai3 zi2): "Control chopsticks."
    2. To drive a vehicle.
      • 揸棍波 (zaa1 gwan3 bo1): "Drive stick."
      • 揸車 (zaa1 ce1): "Drive [a] car."

咱 ꜀Tsá (zaa1)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 6
Unicode code point
U+54B1
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 547 ꜀咱)

    A colloquial word in the north;

    • I, myself;
    • ꜀tsá ꜀ká (zaa1 gaa1 咱家), I, me;
    • ꜀tsá ꜁mún (zaa1 mun4 咱們), we.

𠾵 (⿰口揸) ꜀Chá (zaa1)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 12
Unicode code point
U+20FB5
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 ꜀𠾵)

    A colloquial particle implying a short time;

    • ꜂tang ꜃haá ꜀chá! (dang2 haa5 zaa1 等吓𠾵) wait a moment;
    • ꜂yam ꜁chʻá ꜀chá (jam2 caa4 zaa1 飲茶𠾵), only just to take a cup of tea.

揸 ꜀Chá (zaa1)

Radical + residual strokes
手 + 9
Unicode code point
U+63F8
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 ꜀揸)

    A unauthorized character.

    • To grasp, to grab, to clutch;
    • to hold or take firmly;
    • to work, as a bellows;
    • to use;
    • a handful;
    • ꜀chá ꜀fung ꜀séung (zaa1 fung1 soeng1 揸風箱), to work a bellows;
    • ꜀chá yat꜆ tái꜅ ꜀chá (zaa1 jat1 daai6 zaa1 揸一大揸), grab a big handful;
    • ꜀chá ꜂kan (zaa1 gan2 揸緊), hold it fast;
    • ꜀chá ꜀tʻán (zaa1 taan1 揸攤), to keep a gambling-table;
    • ꜀chá tak꜆ ꜂wan (zaa1 dak1 wan2 揸得穩), I hold it safely;
    • ꜀chá ꜂shui ꜀chü yuk꜇ (zaa1 seoi2 zyu1 juk6 揸水豬肉) water-sogged pork;
    • ꜀chá ꜂fo ꜁tʻung (zaa1 fo2 tung4 揸伙？), to be a scullion;
    • ꜃yau ꜀chá ꜂shau (jau5 zaa1 sau2 有揸手) I have security;
    • ꜃mò ꜀chá ꜁ná (mou5 zaa1 naa4 冇揸拿), nothing to hold on by; a Budhist priest is so nicknamed;
    • ꜀chá ꜁kʻün ꜁tʻau (zaa1 kyun4 tau4 揸拳頭), to double up the fist;
    • ꜀chá chü꜅ kai꜄ ꜂tsai (zaa1 zyu6 gai3 zai2 揸住髻仔), to grab him by the tail;
    • ꜀chá lán꜅ (zaa1 laan6 揸爛) to crush in the hand.
Conway's entry
  • 【揸】 (zaa1):
    1. To hold, to grasp, to control.
      • 揸拿 (zaa1 naa4): "[A] grasp [of the situation]."
      • 揸筷子 (zaa1 faai3 zi2): "Control chopsticks."
    2. To drive a vehicle.
      • 揸棍波 (zaa1 gwan3 bo1): "Drive [a] manual."
      • 揸車 (zaa1 ce1): "Drive [a] car."

渣 ꜀Chá (zaa1)

Radical + residual strokes
水 + 9
Unicode code point
U+6E23
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 ꜀渣)
    • Name of a river;
    • dregs, grounds, residuum;
    • sediment, settlings, refuse after expressing juice;
    • ꜂tsz' ꜂kang ꜀chá (zi2 gang2 zaa1 紫梗渣), shell lac;
    • ꜃mò ꜀chá (mou5 zaa1 冇渣), there's no refuse left.

    Often used for the two next (樝, 皻).

Conway's entry
  • 【渣】 (zaa1):
    1. The residual after moisture is removed; the unswallowable remains after chewing untender meat or stringy vegetables.
      • 渣都冇 (zaa1 dou1 mou5): "Even [the] scraps are gone."
      • 𦧲渣 (loe1 zaa1): "Spit out [the] scraps."

鮓 ꜂Chá (zaa2)

Radical + residual strokes
魚 + 5
Unicode code point
U+9B93
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 ꜂鮓)
    • A rank condiment of fish or flesh and salted hashed fine, with red rice and oil;
    • ꜂chá ꜁ü (zaa2 jyu4 鮓魚) a sort of Medusa or sea-blubber;
    • [꜃]lá ꜂chá (laa5 zaa2 揦鮓), rank, dirty, foul.
Conway's entry
  • 【鮓】 (zaa2):
    1. Low quality or crappy.
      • 咁鮓 (gam3 zaa2): "[How is it] so crappy?"
      • 鮓嘢 (zaa2 je5): "[A] crappy thing."

乍 Chá꜄ (zaa3)

Radical + residual strokes
丿 + 4
Unicode code point
U+4E4D
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 8 乍꜄)
    • At first, suddenly, hastily;
    • for a moment;
    • inadvertently, unexpectedly;
    • chá꜄ ꜃yau chá꜄ ꜁mò (zaa3 jau5 zaa3 mou4 乍有乍無), all at once there is some and then we have none;
    • chá꜄ ꜁ín (zaa3 jin4 乍然), abruptly, at once;
    • chá꜄ kin꜄ (zaa3 gin3 乍見) happened to see.

吒 Chá꜄ (zaa3)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 3
Unicode code point
U+5412
Alternative forms
  • 咤 (see 咤 caa3)
Reading variations
  • caa3 (uncommon)
  • zaa1 (alternative)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 9 吒꜄咤꜄ chʻá꜄ (caa3); here normalised to chá꜄ (zaa3))
    • To foam and sputter in anger;
    • to talk angrily;
    • to grumble;
    • chʻik꜆ [chá]꜄ (cik1 zaa3 叱吒) to rail at.
Conway's commentary
  • The uncommon reading caa3 is that given in 《分韻撮要》.
  • The alternative reading zaa1 is not unattested; Kangxi quotes 《玉篇》知加切《集韻》陟加切.

咋 Chá꜄ (zaa3)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 5
Unicode code point
U+548B
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 8 咋꜄)
    • A loud sound, a rude noise.

    [A colloquial word.]

    • A colloquial particle implying doubt, perhaps;
    • also a final sound, insplying it is so; I understand it:
    • ꜂kòm yéung꜅ chá꜄ (gam2 joeng6-2 zaa3 噉樣咋) so will do, eh!
Conway's entry
  • 【咋】 (zaa3):
    1. A final particle of limitation.
      • 三個人咋 (saam1 go3 jan4 zaa3): "[Only] three people"

詐 Chá꜄ (zaa3)

Radical + residual strokes
言 + 5
Unicode code point
U+8A50
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 8 詐꜄)
    • To deceive, to cheat, to impose upon;
    • erroneous, false;
    • to feign, to make believe;
    • artful, cunning;
    • fraudulent;
    • chá꜄ ꜂kwai chá꜄ ꜃má (zaa3 gwai2 zaa3 maa5 詐鬼詐馬), pretending to act like a fool;
    • ꜁ngo chá꜄ (ngo4 zaa3 訛詐) to extort money falsely;
    • ꜀kán chá꜄ (gaan1 zaa3 奸詐) to delude;
    • chá꜄ [꜀]ká ꜀í (zaa3 gaa2-1 ji3-1 詐假意), false;
    • chá꜄ ngai꜅ (zaa3 ngai6 詐偽) to humbug, suppositious;
    • chá꜄ fan꜄ (zaa3 fan3 詐瞓) to sham sleep;
    • chá꜄ ching꜄ kí꜄ (zaa3 zing3 gei3 詐正記) to feign to be good.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 詐假意: Changed ꜂ká (gaa2) to vernacular reading ꜀ká (gaa1).
Conway's entry
  • 【詐諦】 (zaa3 dai3):
    1. To pretend.

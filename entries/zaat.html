扎 Chát꜆ (zaat3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 1
Unicode code point
U+624E
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 72 扎) 拔也作也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 24 扎꜆)
    • To pluck up (Kangxi: 拔也);
    • (…).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 紮. See 紮 zaat3 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To pierce, to jab, to push through.
  2. To spread, to extend.
  3. Used in 掙扎 (to struggle, to break free).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【扎馬】 (zaat3 maa5):
    1. To do the horse stance.
  • 【扎醒】 (zaat3 seng2):
    1. To wake with a start.
  • 【扎扎跳】 (zaat3 zaat3 tiu3):
    1. To jump up and down rapidly.

    • ꜂kán chát꜆ (gaan2 zaat3 柬札) or ꜀shü chát꜆ (syu1 zaat3 書札) a letter.

Reading variations
  • zaat6-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 560 曱꜇ Tsʻát꜇ (caat6); here corrected to Tsát꜇ (zaat6) and normalised to 甴)

    A colloquial word.

    • [Kát꜇] tsát꜇ (gaat6 zaat6 曱甴) a cockroach.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 曱甴: Changed ꜀Ká (gaa1) to Kát꜇ (gaat6). The second character is correctly tsát꜇ (zaat6) despite the entry being erroneously placed under tsʻát꜇ (caat6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【曱甴】 (gaat6 zaat6, gaat6 zaat6-2):
    1. A cockroach.

It is disputed whether gaat6 zaat6 is written 曱甴 or 甴曱 (neither character appears in the Middle Chinese rime books). The form 曱甴 is commonly used, but the form 甴曱 is considered standard by HK Education Bureau's Lexical Items and by 《商務新字典》 (ISBN: 9620701402).

A blog post by 陳凱文 has investigated this dispute. To paraphrase, his concluding points include that:

  1. Based on the readings recorded in 《四聲篇海》, 《篇海類編》, and 《字彙補》, neither 曱甴 nor 甴曱 have an expected reading of exactly gaat6 zaat6, but 甴曱 is the reading with the greater discrepancy.
  2. It is possible that the form 甴曱 arose from a mistake by Eitel in parsing 反切 readings when looking up older [Chinese] sources, with modern dictionary editors following Eitel's form.
  3. The HK Education Bureau should not insist on the form 甴曱.

I concur with points 1 and 3 above. I disagree with point 2 above, because Eitel's work is based off of Williams', which had already used the form 甴曱 in 1856 (notwithstanding Williams' reading is ꜀ká tsát꜇ (gaa1 zaat6)).

Moreover, I am obliged to point out some inaccuracies in 陳凱文's post:

  1. Whilst 陳凱文's text refers to the 1877 edition of Eitel's [A] Chinese Dictionary in the Cantonese Dialect (wherein 甴 is found under Kát꜇ (gaat6) on Page 228), 陳凱文's screenshot is actually of the 1910 edition of Eitel's dictionary revised and enlarged by Immanuel Gottlieb Genähr (wherein 甴 is found under Kát꜇ (gaat6) on Page 330).
  2. In my copy of 《商務新字典》, whose colophon has 9th print, August 1996 as the latest entry in the list of editions, the native reading for 甴 (on Page 438, under Radical ⽥) is "〔駕壓切低入〕", not just "【駕壓切】" as written by 陳凱文. It is possible that this discrepancy is down to edition (but I note that the year 1992 referenced by 陳凱文 does not appear in the list of editions in the colophon of my edition). In any case, I do not believe that modern dictionary editors have "虛構出一個【駕壓切】" as claimed by 陳凱文. Instead, "〔駕壓切低入〕" in 《商務新字典》 is just a modern (i.e. not Middle Chinese) and native (i.e. not romanisation) but clumsy way of representing the reading gaat6.

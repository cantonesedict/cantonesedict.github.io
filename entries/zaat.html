Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗжӣұз”ҙгҖ‘ (gaat6 zaat6, gaat6 zaat6-2)
    1. A cockroach.

It is disputed whether gaat6 zaat6 is written жӣұз”ҙ or з”ҙжӣұ (neither character appears in the Middle Chinese rime books). The form жӣұз”ҙ is commonly used, but the form з”ҙжӣұ is considered standard by HK Education Bureau's Lexical Items and by гҖҠе•ҶеӢҷж–°еӯ—е…ёгҖӢ (ISBN: 9620701402).

A blog post by йҷіеҮұж–Ү has investigated this dispute. To paraphrase, his concluding points include that:

  1. Based on the readings recorded in гҖҠеӣӣиҒІзҜҮжө·гҖӢ, гҖҠзҜҮжө·йЎһз·ЁгҖӢ, and гҖҠеӯ—еҪҷиЈңгҖӢ, neither жӣұз”ҙ nor з”ҙжӣұ have an expected reading of exactly gaat6 zaat6, but з”ҙжӣұ is the reading with the greater discrepancy.
  2. It is possible that the form з”ҙжӣұ arose from a mistake by Eitel in parsing еҸҚеҲҮ readings when looking up older [Chinese] sources, with modern dictionary editors following Eitel's form.
  3. The HK Education Bureau should not insist on the form з”ҙжӣұ.

I concur with pointsВ 1 andВ 3 above. I disagree with pointВ 2 above, because Eitel's work is based off of Williams', which had already used the form з”ҙжӣұ in 1856 (notwithstanding Williams' reading is кңҖkГЎ tsГЎtкңҮ (gaa1 zaat6)).

Moreover, I am obliged to point out some inaccuracies in йҷіеҮұж–Ү's post:

  1. Whilst йҷіеҮұж–Ү's text refers to the 1877 edition of Eitel's [A] Chinese Dictionary in the Cantonese Dialect (wherein з”ҙ is found under KГЎtкңҮ (gaat6) on PageВ 228), йҷіеҮұж–Ү's screenshot is actually of the 1910 edition of Eitel's dictionary revised and enlarged by Immanuel Gottlieb GenГӨhr (wherein з”ҙ is found under KГЎtкңҮ (gaat6) on PageВ 330).
  2. In my copy of гҖҠе•ҶеӢҷж–°еӯ—е…ёгҖӢ, whose colophon has 9thВ print, AugustВ 1996 as the latest entry in the list of editions, the native reading for з”ҙ (on PageВ 438, under RadicalВ вҪҘ) is "гҖ”й§•еЈ“еҲҮдҪҺе…ҘгҖ•", not just "гҖҗй§•еЈ“еҲҮгҖ‘" as written by йҷіеҮұж–Ү. It is possible that this discrepancy is down to edition (but I note that the year 1992 referenced by йҷіеҮұж–Ү does not appear in the list of editions in the colophon of my edition). In any case, I do not believe that modern dictionary editors have "иҷӣж§ӢеҮәдёҖеҖӢгҖҗй§•еЈ“еҲҮгҖ‘" as claimed by йҷіеҮұж–Ү. Instead, "гҖ”й§•еЈ“еҲҮдҪҺе…ҘгҖ•" in гҖҠе•ҶеӢҷж–°еӯ—е…ёгҖӢ is just a modern (i.e. not Middle Chinese) and native (i.e. not romanisation) but clumsy way of representing the reading gaat6.

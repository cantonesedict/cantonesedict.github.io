抄 ꜀Chʻáu (caau1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6284
Alternative forms
  • 剿
  • 𠞰
  • 勦
  • 鈔
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 41 抄) 抄寫
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 29 ꜀抄)
    • To take, to seize (Kangxi: 略取也);
    • to search;
    • to engross, to transcribe (Kangxi: 謄寫也);
    • to lade out, to spoon out (Kangxi: 叉取也);
    • to confiscate, to escheat, to sequestrate, to resume;
    • ꜂shau ꜀chʻáu (sau2 caau1 手抄), a MS. copy;
    • ꜀chʻáu ꜀tsʻing ꜀ká (caau1 cing1 gaa1 抄清家), all his property is taken;
    • chiú꜄ ꜂pún ꜀chʻáu ꜁tʻang (ziu3 bun2 caau1 tang4 照本抄謄), copied exactly;
    • ꜀chʻáu ꜂sé (caau1 se2 抄寫), to copy;
    • ꜀chʻáu pák꜇ (caau1 baak6 抄白) it is copied clearly;
    • ꜀chʻáu sái꜄ (caau1 saai3 抄嗮) it is all copied.
  • (Page 30 鈔꜄ Chʻáu꜄ (caau3); ꜀Chʻáu (caau1) implied)
    • Interchanged with 抄 to copy (Kangxi: 今俗別作抄, 謄寫也);
    • (…).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【抄】 (tsrhaew, tsrhaewH)
    • (verb-like) [to] seize
    • (verb-like) [to] copy

鈔 ꜀Chʻáu (caau1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 4
Unicode code point
U+9214
Reading variations
  • caau3 (per 《廣韻》初敎切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 42 鈔 Chʻáu꜄ (caau3)) 鈔錢又畧取也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 鈔꜄ Chʻáu꜄ (caau3); here normalised to ꜀Chʻáu (caau1))
    • (…);
    • a document, a government paper, a receipt (Kangxi: 官收物而給印信文憑也);
    • paper money (Kangxi: 楮貨名);
    • ꜁shün chʻáu꜄ ꜀chʻáu (syun4 caau1 船鈔) tonnage dues, charges on ships;
    • fong꜄ chʻáu꜄ ꜀chʻáu (fong3 caau1 放鈔) to burn paper money to the gods;
    • ꜁tsʻín chʻáu꜄ ꜀chʻáu (cin4 caau1 錢鈔) bills and money;
    • chʻáu꜄ ꜀chʻáu ꜀kwán (caau1 gwaan1 鈔關), a custom house;
    • shui꜄ chʻáu꜄ ꜀chʻáu (seoi3 caau1 稅鈔) duties on goods, transit dues.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 抄. See 抄 caau1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Both the level reading 楚交切 ꜀chʻáu (caau1) and the departing reading 初敎切 chʻáu꜄ (caau3) are attested in 《廣韻》, explained 略也 "seize".

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜂吵)
    • To clamor, to wrangle;
    • ꜂chʻáu náu꜅ (caau2 naau6 吵鬧) to scold;
    • ꜁tsʻò ꜂chʻáu (cou4 caau2 嘈吵), to raise a row;

炒 ꜂Chʻáu (caau2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 4
Unicode code point
U+7092
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 42 炒) 乾熬食物曰炒
  • (Vol. 2 Image 42 煼) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜂炒)
    • To roast in a boiler;
    • to fry in fat or batter;
    • ꜀kon ꜂chʻáu (gon1 caau2 乾炒), to fry brown; to roast to dryness;
    • ꜂chʻáu ꜁chʻá (caau2 caa4 炒茶), to fire tea;
    • ꜂chʻáu ꜀ká ꜀fí ká꜄ ꜀fé (caau2 gaa3 fe1 炒咖啡), to roast coffee;
    • ꜂chʻáu mái꜅ (caau2 maai6 炒賣) to cook and sell;
    • ꜂chʻáu ꜀fung lut꜇ (caau2 fung1 leot6-2 炒風栗) to roast chestnuts;
    • ꜀tsín ꜂chʻáu (zin1 caau2 煎炒), to fry;
    • ꜂siú ꜂chʻáu yuk꜇ (siu2 caau2 juk6 小炒肉) fried hash and vegetables;
    • ꜂chʻáu shuk꜇ (caau2 suk6 炒熟) to fry thoroughly.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 炒咖啡: Changed now dated ꜀ká ꜀fí (gaa1 fei1) to modern ká꜄ ꜀fé (gaa3 fe1). This change is perhaps inappropriate as it is anachronistic for 1856; see the page-level commentary at fe.
  • Williams' 炒賣: Now to buy speculatively and sell.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【炒】 (caau2)
    1. To stir-fry.
    2. Short for 炒魷魚.
      • 炒咗佢 (caau2 zo2 keoi5): "Fired him."
    3. To speculate, or to flip.
      • 炒賣 (caau2 maai6): "Speculate [and] sell."
      • 炒樓 (caau2 lau4-2): "Flip property."
    4. To crash.
      • 炒單車 (caau2 daan1 ce1): "Stacked [my] bike."
  • 【炒魷魚】 (caau2 jau4 jyu4-2)
    1. To sack, to fire.
  • 【炒魷】 (caau2 jau4-2)
    1. Short for 炒魷魚.

巢 ꜁Chʻáu (caau4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼮ + 8
Unicode code point
U+5DE2
Reading variations
  • caau4-2 (rising second in vernacular 巢巢地)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 41 巢) 鳥在木上曰巢
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 29 ꜁巢)
    • A nest on a tree (Kangxi: 鳥在木上曰巢 etc.);
    • a den, a retreat;
    • a lurking place;
    • to nestle, to make a nest;
    • ꜁chʻáu üt꜇ (caau4 jyut6 巢穴) to skulk in, as a brigand;
    • ꜁chʻáu ꜀wo (caau4 wo1 巢窩), or tséuk꜆ ꜁chʻáu (zoek3 caau4 雀巢), a nest;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜁chʻáu (caak6 caau4 賊巢), a den or resort of robbers;
    • pák꜆ ꜃niú ꜀kwai ꜁chʻáu (baak3 niu5 gwai1 caau4 百鳥歸巢), the birds have gone to roost.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 漅. See 漅 caau4 for the canonical entry.)

  • (Page 30 ꜁窠; here corrected to 巢)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • Crumpled, wrinkled, corrugated, rough;
    • shriveled, as dried fruit;
    • ꜂hò ꜂tsʻz' ꜃tsʻz' á꜄ ꜁pʻo ꜁pʻí kòm꜄ ꜁chʻáu (hou2 ci5 aa3 po4 pei4 gam3 caau4 好似阿婆皮咁巢), wrinkled as a granny's face;
    • ꜁chʻáu ꜀mang ꜀mang (caau4 mang1 mang1 巢𠵼𠵼), creased, rumpled.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 好似阿婆皮咁巢: Corrected ꜂tsʻz' (ci2) to ꜃tsʻz' (ci5).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A large free-reed instrument (Kangxi: 樂器, 大笙謂之巢).
  2. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【巢】 (dzraew)
    • (noun-like) nest
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【巢】 (caau4)
    1. Wrinkled.
      • 巢𠵼𠵼 (caau4 mang1 mang1): "Wrinkly."
      • 巢皮 (caau4 pei4): "Wrinkled skin."

漅 ꜁Chʻáu (caau4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 11
Unicode code point
U+6F05
Alternative forms
  • 巢
Reading variations
  • ziu2 (per 《廣韻》子小切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 41 漅) 湖名在合肥亦作巢
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜁漅)
    • Name of a large lake in the south of the province of Ngánhwui (安徽) (Kangxi: 湖名).

    Also read ꜀tsiú ꜂tsiú (ziu2). The last (巢) is usually used for this (Kangxi: 亦作巢).

Conway's commentary
  • The reading ꜁chʻáu (caau4) is not unattested; 《集韻》 has the reading 鋤交切, explained 湖名.
  • Williams' "Also read […]": Corrected ꜀tsiú (ziu1) to ꜂tsiú (ziu2).

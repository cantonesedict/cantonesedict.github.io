昏 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 4
Unicode code point
U+660F
Alternative forms
  • 昬
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 昏) 日暮也暗也昧也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀昏)
    • The sun setting (Kangxi: 日冥也);
    • twilight;
    • dusk (Kangxi: 昏代也, 代明也, 日入後二刻半爲昏昏來則明往故云代明);
    • obscure, confused, dull (Kangxi: 昏亂也);
    • in disorder;
    • to oblige to do;
    • ꜁wong ꜀fan (wong4 fan1 黃昏), dusk;
    • ꜀fan òm꜄ (fan1 am3 昏暗) dark, dull;
    • ꜀fan lün꜅ (fan1 lyun6 昏亂) confused;
    • ꜀fan múi꜅ (fan1 mui6 昏昧) unintelligible;
    • ꜀fan yé꜅ (fan1 je6 昏夜) night;
    • ꜀fan ꜀fá (fan1 faa1 昏花), motes in the eye.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 婚. See 婚 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "twilight": Specifically, dusk.
  • Williams' 昏夜: Better rendered "gloomy night".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To endeavour, to make an effort (Kangxi: 昏彊也).
  2. Not yet named (Kangxi: 未名曰昏).
  3. To lose consciousness.

熏 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 10
Unicode code point
U+718F
Alternative forms
  • 燻 (for smoke-related senses)
  • 薰
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 熏) 烟也又[熱]氣蒸也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀熏)
    • Vapor, exhalations, steam (Kangxi: 火氣盛貌);
    • smoke rising (Kangxi: 火煙上出也);
    • to smoke, to fumigate, to heat (Kangxi: 熱也), to parch (Kangxi: 熏灼也);
    • to offend;
    • to becloud;
    • (…);
    • ꜀fan ꜀fung (fan1 fung1 熏風), warm wind (Kangxi: 東南曰熏風);
    • (…);
    • ꜀fan ꜂fo ꜂tʻúi (fan1 fo2 teoi2 熏火腿), to smoke hams;
    • ꜀fan ꜀fan (fan1 fan1 熏熏), uneasy, fidgetty;
    • ꜀fan ꜀kon (fan1 gon1 熏乾), to dry at the fire;
    • ꜀fan chek[꜆] (fan1 zek3 熏炙) to cauterize.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 曛. See 曛 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 醺. See 醺 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised 𤍠 to 熱.
  • Williams' "uneasy, fidgetty": This is a rendering of Kangxi 熏熏坐不安之貌, which is one of the explanations for 《詩大雅》公尸來止熏熏, but not a very good one, in my opinion. The other explanations (熏熏和悅也 and 熏說文作醺醉也) are better.
  • Williams' 熏炙: Corrected chek꜇ (zek6) to chek꜆ (zek3).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Amiable and cheerful (Kangxi: 熏熏和悅也).
  2. Warm.

閽 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾨ + 8
Unicode code point
U+95BD
Alternative forms
  • 𨵽
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 閽) 閽人守門之役
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀閽)
    • To shut the door at evening;
    • a porter (Kangxi: 閽者守門之賤者也);
    • ꜀fan ꜁yan (fan1 jan4 閽人), a doorkeeper, an eunuch who keeps the hareem;
    • kʻau꜄ ꜀fan (kau3 fan1 叩閽), to visit the palace (Kangxi: 凡吏民冤抑詣闕自愬者曰叩閽).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "To shut the door at evening": This is a misreading of Kangxi 常以昏閉門隸也, which refers to the lowly servant (隸) who shuts the door at evening.
  • Williams' "a porter": Specifically, a lowly porter.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A palace gate.

饙 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾷ + 12
Unicode code point
U+9959
Han unification vigilance
May appear with 艹 having 3 strokes (+12 assumed here) or 4 strokes (+13), depending on the font.
Alternative forms
  • 餴
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 餴) 蒸熟米以水沃之乃再蒸也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀餴; here normalised to 饙)
    • Rice steamed thoroughly;
    • to steam rice (Kangxi 饙: […]然則蒸米爲之饙饙必餾而熟之[…]).
Conway's commentary
  • Kangxi's treatment is confusing.
    • For the form 餴, it gives a single sense, defined 半蒸飯也與饙同 (half-steamed-rice, same as 饙), along with the commentary 餴蒸米一熟而以水沃之乃再蒸也 (to steam rice till cooked, then add water, then steam again). This is consistent with Williams' "Rice steamed thoroughly".
    • For the form 饙, it splits things up into:
      1. The "half-steamed-rice" sense (半蒸飯); and
      2. What appears to be the "thoroughly cooked" sense, based on a heap of commentary on 《爾雅釋言》饙餾稔也.

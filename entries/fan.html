分 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5206
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 分) 分别分離
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀分)
    • To separate, to divide (Kangxi: 別也);
    • to halve (Kangxi: 半也);
    • to distribute (Kangxi: 賦也施也), to divide amongst;
    • to give, to partake with (Kangxi: 與也);
    • a candareen, a tenth in decimal notation.

    (…)

    • ꜁Pʻing ꜀fan (ping4 fan1 平分), to divide evenly;
    • ꜀fan ꜀hoi (fan1 hoi1 分開), to separate;
    • ꜀fan pít[꜇] (fan1 bit6 分別) to divide, to leave;
    • ꜀fan pún[꜅] (fan1 bun6 分伴) to dissolve partnership;
    • ꜀fan ꜁chʻing shò꜄ (fan1 cing4 sou3 分程數) to pay in proportion;
    • ꜀fan ꜀ká (fan1 gaa1 分家), to divide the estate;
    • ꜀fan [pʻái]꜄ (fan1 paai3 分派) to give amongst;
    • yat꜆ ꜀fan ꜁hong fo꜄, yat꜆ ꜀fan ꜁tsʻín (jat1 fan1 hong4 fo3 jat1 fan1 cin4 一分行貨一分錢), each kind of goods has its own price;
    • ꜀fan ꜀kòm ꜁tʻung mí꜅ (fan1 gam1 tung4 mei6 分柑同味) same taste as the carpels of an orange;
    • ꜀fan ꜁ün (fan1 jyun4 分？), a deputy district magistrate.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 紛. See 紛 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a candareen": That is, a tenth of a mace (錢), or a hundredth of a tael (両).
  • Williams' 分別: Corrected pít꜆ (bit3) to pít꜇ (bit6). Also "separately".
  • Williams' 分伴: Corrected pún꜄ (bun3) to pún꜅ (bun6).
  • Williams' 分派: Corrected pái꜄ (baai3) to pʻái꜄ (paai3).
  • Williams' 分柑同味: So spelt because of his reference to "orange". The more common form is 分甘同味, with "sweetness" replacing "orange".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To distinguish (Kangxi: 判也).
  2. A minute.
  3. A point, a mark (in scoring).
  4. A tenth of a Chinese inch (寸).

氛 ꜀Fan (fan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽓ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6C1B
Alternative forms
  • 雰
Reading variations
  • fan4 (alternative per 《廣韻》符分切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 氛) 沃氛
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 46 ꜀氛)
    • Fume, vapor (Kangxi: 氣也);
    • shadowy signs, a will-o'-the-wisp, airy omens (Kangxi: 氛氳祥氣也, 氛祲妖氣也);
    • ꜀fan ꜀wan (fan1 wan1 氛氳), aerial influences;
    • ꜂iú ꜀fan (jiu2 fan1 妖氛), noxious influences;
    • ꜂hoi ꜀fan (hoi2 fan1 海氛), pirates;
    • kwok꜆ ꜀fan (gwok3 fan1 國氛), demagogues, disturbers of the peace.
Conway's commentary
  • 《廣韻》 has 符分切 ꜁fan (fan4) for 氛氲祥氣 "auspicious ambiance", and 撫文切 ꜀fan (fan1) for 氛侵妖氣 "malign ambiance". Modern usage prefers the latter reading, and it is debatable whether the distinction was necessary in the first place.
  • Williams' 國氛: Better rendered "disturbings of the peace".

Radical + residual strokes

  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 熏) 烟也又[熱]氣蒸也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀熏)
    • Vapor, exhalations, steam (Kangxi: 火氣盛貌);
    • smoke rising (Kangxi: 火煙上出也);
    • to smoke, to fumigate, to heat (Kangxi: 熱也), to parch (Kangxi: 熏灼也);
    • to offend;
    • to becloud;
    • (…);
    • ꜀fan ꜀fung (fan1 fung1 熏風), warm wind (Kangxi: 東南曰熏風);
    • (…);
    • ꜀fan ꜂fo ꜂tʻúi (fan1 fo2 teoi2 熏火腿), to smoke hams;
    • ꜀fan ꜀fan (fan1 fan1 熏熏), uneasy, fidgetty;
    • ꜀fan ꜀kon (fan1 gon1 熏乾), to dry at the fire;
    • ꜀fan chek[꜆] (fan1 zek3 熏炙) to cauterize (Kangxi 焄: 焄猶熏炙之也).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 曛. See 曛 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 醺. See 醺 fan1 for the canonical entry.)

  • (Page 45 ꜀蔒꜀焄)
    • Vapor or steam (Kangxi 焄: 火上出也, 亦作熏);
    • (…).
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised 𤍠 to 熱.
  • Williams' "uneasy, fidgetty": This is a rendering of Kangxi 熏熏坐不安之貌, which is one of the explanations for 《詩大雅》公尸來止熏熏, but not a very good one, in my opinion. The other explanations (熏熏和悅也 and 熏說文作醺醉也) are better.
  • Williams' 熏炙: Corrected chek꜇ (zek6) to chek꜆ (zek3).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Amiable and cheerful (Kangxi: 熏熏和悅也).
  2. Warm.

Alternative forms
  • 餴
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 80 餴) 蒸熟米以水沃之乃再蒸也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀餴; here normalised to 饙)
    • Rice steamed thoroughly;
    • to steam rice (Kangxi 饙: […]然則蒸米爲之饙饙必餾而熟之[…]).
Conway's commentary
  • Kangxi's treatment is confusing.
    • For the form 餴, it gives a single sense, defined 半蒸飯也與饙同 (half-steamed-rice, same as 饙), along with the commentary 餴蒸米一熟而以水沃之乃再蒸也 (to steam rice till cooked, then add water, then steam again). This is consistent with Williams' "Rice steamed thoroughly".
    • For the form 饙, it splits things up into:
      1. The "half-steamed-rice" sense (半蒸飯); and
      2. What appears to be the "thoroughly cooked" sense, based on a heap of commentary on 《爾雅釋言》饙餾稔也.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 46 ꜀汾 ꜀Fan (fan1); here normalised to ꜁Fan (fan4))

    A river in Shánsí (山西), a branch of the Yellow R (Kangxi: 水名 etc.).

    • [꜁]Fan ꜁yéung ꜁wong (fan4 joeng4 wong4 汾陽王), a personage in the Táng (唐) dynasty, whose name is a synonym for happiness;
    • as [꜁]Fan ꜁yéung ꜂tím ꜃hòm (fan4 joeng4 dim2 ham5 汾陽點頷), Fanyéung's king nodding his chin (because he did not know all his descendants).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "A river in Shánsí": Note that Kangxi 水出太原晉陽山西南入河 is to be punctuated as "水出太原晉陽山、西南入河". That we know it is in Shánsí is due to "太原晉陽山", not "山、西".

Unicode code point

Alternative forms

Reading variations

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 713 ꜂份)

    (…)

    The proper sound in colloquial is fan꜅ (fan6), though sometimes read ꜂fan (fan6-2).

    It is the same word as 分꜅ (fan6) when used as a noun.

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A share or part amongst a whole.
    • 股份 (gu2 fan6-2): "Shares."
  2. Classifier for documents.
  3. Classifier for gifts.
  4. Classifier for jobs.
  5. Suffixed after 年, 月, 省, etc. to indicate an interval (in time) or division (in space).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【份】 (fan6-2):
    1. A part, a partaking.
      • 有份出席 (jau5 fan6-2 ceot1 zik6): "Have [a] part [in] attending."
      • 冇佢分 (mou5 keoi5 fan6-2): "There's no part [for] him" (i.e. he is excluded).

分 Fan꜅ (fan6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5206
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 45 ꜀分 read Fan꜄ (fan3); here corrected to Fan꜅ (fan6))

    (…)

    • Read fan[꜅] (fan6) a portion (Kangxi: 均也分劑也), a duty, a part (Kangxi: 分位也);
    • ꜀sám fan[꜅] ꜀chí yat꜆ (saam1 fan6 zi1 jat1 三分之一) one third.

    (…)

    (Alternative form of 份. See 份 fan6.)

Conway's commentary
  • In Cantonese, the fraction-building sense of 分 is a noun, not a verb. It makes sense for 三分之一 to be parsed as "one of three shares (or portions)". As such, it is to be read in the departing tone, fan꜅ (fan6). It is not to be read in the level tone, as in Mandarin.
  • By extension, the "fraction" sense of 分 is also to be read in the departing tone in Cantonese. This distinguishes 分數 fan6 sou3 "fraction" from 分數 fan1 sou3 "score/points".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A fraction (of mathematics).
    • 分數 (fan6 sou3): "Fraction."
    • 約分 (joek3 fan6): "Reducing fractions."

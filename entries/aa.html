Á [J: aa; C: aa]

Words in a [J: a; C: a] or á [J: aa; C: aa], are often heard beginning with ng [J: ng; C: ng], as ngá [J: ngaa; C: ngaa], ngai [J: ngai; C: ngai], ngat [J: ngat; C: ngat].

꜀Á [陰平; J: aa1; C: aa1]

丫 [U+4E2B] [J: aa1; C: aa1]

Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
  • A fork, a crotch;
  • the parting of two branches, fingers, or tines;
  • ꜀á ꜁tʻau [丫頭; J: aa1 tau4; C: aa1 t'au4] or ꜀á ꜁wán [丫鬟; J: aa1 waan4; C: aa1 waan4], a slave girl;
  • ꜀á kok꜆ kai꜄ [丫角髻; J: aa1 gok3 gai3; C: aa1 kok8 kai3] a little boy;
  • ꜀á ꜀chʻá [丫杈; J: aa1 caa1; C: aa1 ch'aa1], a fork, a rest for clothes' sticks;
  • shü꜅ ꜀á [樹丫; J: syu6 aa1; C: shü6 aa1] fork of a tree;
  • ꜀sám ꜀á lò꜅ [三丫路; J: saam1 aa1 lou6; C: saam1 aa1 lou6] meeting of three ways, a trivium.
Conway's commentary
  • 丫頭 and 丫鬟 for slave girl (or maidservant): slavery was a thing in 1856.

吖 [U+5416] [J: aa1; C: aa1]

Alternative forms
[呀; 啊]
Reading variations
[J: a1; C: a1: shorter syllable]
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
  • ꜂hò ꜀á [好吖; J: hou2 aa1; C: hou2 aa1] well, very well;
  • ꜀á wai꜄ [?; J: aa1 wai3; C: aa1 wai3] poor, necessitous;
  • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á [佢噉話吖; J: keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1; C: k'_ü kam2 waa6 aa1], he said so;
  • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á [乜嘢吖; J: mat1 je5 aa1; C: mat7 ye5 aa1], what?
  • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á [噉做吖; J: gam2 zou6 aa1; C: kam2 tsou6 aa1], no, this is the way to do it.
Conway's commentary
  • Jyutping suggests 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.

呀 [U+5440] [J: aa1; C: aa1]

Alternative forms
[吖; 啊]
Reading variations
[J: a1; C: a1: shorter syllable]
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
A colloquial word.
  • An interjection used in answers, denoting surprise or alarm;
  • alas! dreadful.
Read ꜁ngá: [see ngaa#呀].
Conway's commentary
  • Williams does not provide a separate entry for 呀 under ꜁ngá [J: ngaa4; C: ngaa4].

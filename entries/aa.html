Á [aa]

Words in a [a] or á [aa], are often heard beginning with ng [ng], as ngá [ngaa], ngai [ngai], ngat [ngat].

꜀Á [陰平; aa1]

丫 [U+4E2B] ꜀á [aa1]

Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
  • A fork, a crotch;
  • the parting of two branches, fingers, or tines;
  • ꜀á ꜁tʻau [丫頭; aa1 tau4] or ꜀á ꜁wán [丫鬟; aa1 waan4], a slave girl [it's 1856];
  • ꜀á kok꜆ kai꜄ [丫角髻; aa1 gok3 gai3] a little boy [it's 1856];
  • ꜀á ꜀chʻá [丫杈; aa1 caa1], a fork, a rest for clothes' sticks;
  • shü꜅ ꜀á [樹丫; syu6 aa1] fork of a tree;
  • ꜀sám ꜀á lò꜅ [三丫路; saam1 aa1 lou6] meeting of three ways, a trivium.

吖 [U+5416] ꜀á [aa1]

Alternative forms
[呀; 啊]
Reading variations
[a1: shorter syllable]
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
  • ꜂hò ꜀á [好吖; hou2 aa1] well, very well;
  • ꜀á wai꜄ [?; aa1 wai3] poor, necessitous;
  • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á [佢噉話吖; keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1], he said so;
  • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á [乜嘢吖; mat1 je5 aa1], what?
  • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á [噉做吖; gam2 zou6 aa1], no, this is the way to do it.
Conway's remarks
  • Jyutping suggests 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.

呀 [U+5440] ꜀á [aa1]

Alternative forms
[吖; 啊]
Reading variations
[a1: shorter syllable]
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
A colloquial word.
  • An interjection used in answers, denoting surprise or alarm;
  • alas! dreadful.

鴉 [U+9D09] ꜀á [aa1]

Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
  • A raven with a white streak on its breast;
  • ꜃lò ꜀á [老鴉; lou5 aa1], a raven or crow;
  • ꜁tʻò ꜀á [塗鴉; tou4 aa1], written roughly [now graffiti];
  • ꜀á pʻín꜄ ꜁nai [鴉片坭; aa1 pin3 nai4], opium [dated; now just 鴉片].

꜂Á [陰上; aa2]

啞 [U+555E] ꜂á [aa2]

Alternative forms
瘂
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
  • The confused noise of children studying;
  • dumb;
  • to keep silent, for which the next [瘂] is used;
  • ꜂á mai꜅ [啞謎; aa2 mai6] an enigma;
  • ꜁tsʻing ꜁nán ꜂á ꜂yan [情難啞忍; cing4 naan4 aa2 yan2], hard to bear it patiently.
Also read ak꜆ [ak1]: [see ak#啞1].
Conway's remarks
  • Williams does not provide a separate entry under ak꜆ [ak1].

瘂 [U+7602] ꜂á [aa2]

Alternative forms
啞
Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
  • Dumb, unable to speak;
  • dull, faded;
  • a cracked sound, as a bell;
  • wheezing;
  • nape of the neck;
  • ꜂á ꜂tsai [瘂仔; aa2 zai2], a dumb boy;
  • ꜂á ꜂hau ꜁mò ꜁ín [瘂口無言; aa2 hau2 mou4 jin4], will not speak, sulky;
  • ꜂á tuk꜇ [瘂毒; aa2 duk6] dumb people are revengeful;
  • ꜀sheng tái꜄ ꜂á [聲帶瘂; seng1 daai3 aa2], the sound is indistinct.

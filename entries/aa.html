Words in a (a) or á (aa), are often heard beginning with ng (ng), as ngá (ngaa), ngai (ngai), ngat (ngat).

丫 ꜀Á (aa1)

Radical + residual strokes
丨 + 2
Unicode code point
U+4E2B
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 ꜀丫)
    • A fork, a crotch;
    • the parting of two branches, fingers, or tines;
    • ꜀á ꜁tʻau (aa1 tau4 丫頭) or ꜀á ꜁wán (aa1 waan4 丫鬟), a slave girl;
    • ꜀á kok꜆ kai꜄ (aa1 gok3 gai3 丫角髻) a little boy;
    • ꜀á ꜀chʻá (aa1 caa1 丫杈), a fork, a rest for clothes' sticks;
    • shü꜅ ꜀á (syu6 aa1 樹丫) fork of a tree;
    • ꜀sám ꜀á lò꜅ (saam1 aa1 lou6 三丫路) meeting of three ways, a trivium.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 丫頭, 丫鬟: Yes, people kept slaves in the 19th century.
  • Williams' 丫頭, 丫鬟, 丫角髻: From the double-bun hairstyle.
  • Williams' 三丫路: Now rarely heard; 三叉路 is more common.

吖 ꜀Á (aa1)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 3
Unicode code point
U+5416
Alternative forms
  • 呀
  • 啊
Reading variations
  • a1 (shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 ꜀吖)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interrogative particle;
    • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
    • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
    • ꜂hò ꜀á (hou2 aa1 好吖) well, very well;
    • ꜀á wai꜄ (aa1 wai3 吖？) poor, necessitous;
    • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á (keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1 佢噉話吖), he said so;
    • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á (mat1 je5 aa1 乜嘢吖), what?
    • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á (gam2 zou6 aa1 噉做吖), no, this is the way to do it.
  • (Page 1 ꜀呀; here normalised to 吖)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interjection used in answers, denoting surprise or alarm;
    • alas! dreadful.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' aa1 wai3: not sure what he means by "poor, necessitous".
  • Jyutping.org suggests using 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.
Conway's vernacular entry
  • 【吖】 (aa1, a1):
    1. An interrogative-ish final particle suggesting the preceding.
      • 今晚吖 (gam1 maan5-1 a1): "[How about] tonight."
      • 你行先吖 (nei5 haang4 sin1 a1): "[Why don't] you go first."
    2. An imperative-ish final particle (when the preceding is spoken with force).
      • 飲吖 (jam2 a1): "Drink [it]."
    3. An emphatic final particle.
      • 好吖 (hou2 a1): "OK" (as an active, enthusiastic endorsement).
  • 【吖嘛】 (a1 maa3):
    1. An indicative-ish final particle pointing out the preceding. Similar to ending on a falling intonation in English.
      • 天氣凍吖嘛 (tin1 hei3 dung3 a1 maa3): "[Because] the weather's cold."
    2. An interrogative-ish final particle seeking affirmation of the preceding.
      • 今晚吖嘛 (gam1 maan5-1 a1 maa3): "[That's] tonight, [right]?"

啞 ꜂Á (aa2)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 8
Unicode code point
U+555E
Alternative forms
  • 瘂
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 ꜂啞)
    • The confused noise of children studying;
    • dumb;
    • to keep silent, for which the next (瘂) is used;
    • ꜂á [꜁]mai (aa2 mai4 啞謎) an enigma;
    • ꜁tsʻing ꜁nán ꜂á ꜂yan (cing4 naan4 aa2 jan2 情難啞忍), hard to bear it patiently.

    Also read ak꜆ (ak1); (see 啞 ak1).

  • (Page 1 ꜂瘂; here normalised to 啞)
    • Dumb, unable to speak;
    • dull, faded;
    • a cracked sound, as a bell;
    • wheezing;
    • nape of the neck;
    • ꜂á ꜂tsai (aa2 zai2 啞仔), a dumb boy;
    • ꜂á ꜂hau ꜁mò ꜁ín (aa2 hau2 mou4 jin4 啞口無言), will not speak, sulky;
    • ꜂á tuk꜇ (aa2 duk6 啞毒) dumb people are revengeful;
    • ꜀sheng tái꜄ ꜂á (seng1 daai3 aa2 聲帶啞), the sound is indistinct.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 啞謎: Changed Middle Chinese mai꜅ (mai6) to common reading ꜁mai (mai4).

亞 Á꜄ (aa3)

Radical + residual strokes
二 + 6
Unicode code point
U+4E9E
Alternative forms
  • 阿 (not considered an alternative in modern usage)
Reading variations
  • a3 (shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 2 亞꜄阿꜄)
    • Hunchbacked;
    • to esteem lightly, to regard as inferior;
    • second, next to, junior, inferior;
    • (see 阿 aa3 for name prefix senses)
    • pat꜆ á꜄ ꜀ü ꜁yan (bat1 aa3 jyu1 jan4 不亞於人), not inferior to others.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams does not provide separate entries for 亞 and 阿, but notes that 阿 is "often used" for the name prefix sense. I have split things up accordingly.

啊 Á꜄ (aa3)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 8
Unicode code point
U+554A
Alternative forms
  • 呀
Reading variations
  • a3 (shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 呀꜄; here normalised to 啊)
    • A final particle, adding intensity to the meaning;
    • ꜁'m ꜂hò á꜄ (m4 hou2 aa3 唔好啊) not at all good.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 唔好啊: Also an emphatic form of the imperative "don't".
  • Jyutping.org suggests using 啊 as the canonical character for aa3.
Conway's vernacular entry
  • 【啊】 (aa3, a3):
    1. A final particle.
      • 到未啊 (dou3 mei6 a3): "[Are we] there yet?"
      • 未到啊 (mei6 dou3 a3): "Not there yet."
      • 有排啊 (jau5 paai4 a3): "[It'll] be ages."
  • 【啊】 (aa3):
    1. A particle after each item in a list of examples. Similar to the non-falling intonation for list items in English.
      • 例如蘋果啊、橙啊、香蕉啊 (lai6 jyu4 ping4 gwo2 aa3, caang4-2 aa3, hoeng1 ziu1 aa3): "Such as apples, oranges, bananas, …."

Radical + residual strokes
女 + 8
Unicode code point
U+5A6D
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 2 婭꜄)
    • Brothers-in-law;
    • ꜀yan á꜄ (jan1 aa3 姻婭) relatives by marriage, especially brothers or parents;

阿 Á꜄ (aa3)

Radical + residual strokes
阜 + 5
Unicode code point
U+963F
Alternative forms
  • 亞 (not considered an alternative in modern usage)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 2 亞꜄阿꜄)
    • (see 亞 for the "second/inferior" senses)
    • a syllable prefixed to proper names, for which the second (阿) is often used;
    • it also impersonates epithets;
    • á꜄ múi꜅ (aa3 mui6-2 阿妹) a younger sister, a lass;
    • á꜄ mat꜆ (aa3 mat1 阿乜) Mr. So and So;
    • á꜄ pí꜅ (aa3 bei6 阿？) a silly fellow;
    • á꜄ ꜀chʻo (aa3 co1 阿初), a raw hand;
    • á꜄ ꜀pá (aa3 baa1 阿爸), papa;
    • á꜄ ꜃tʻai (aa3 tai5 阿娣), boy, you lad;
    • (…).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams does not provide separate entries for 亞 and 阿. I have split things up accordingly.
  • Williams' aa3 bei6: Not sure which bei6 means "a silly fellow".

Á꜅ (aa6 陽去)

呀 Á꜅ (aa6)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 4
Unicode code point
U+5440
Reading variations
  • a6 (shorter syllable for elided "ten" sense)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 呀꜄)

    A colloquial word.

    (…)

    Also spoken á꜅ (aa6);

    • ten, used after a higher number;
    • an answer, a word of reply like Aye!
    • á꜅ ꜂hò (aa6 hou2 呀好), yes, well;
    • sz'꜄ á꜅ ꜁tsʻín (si3 aa6 cin4 / sei3 aa6 cin4 四呀錢), forty cash.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 四呀錢: The "ten" sense would never actually appear in writing as 呀; it would still be spelt 十, with the pronunciation being a matter of preference.

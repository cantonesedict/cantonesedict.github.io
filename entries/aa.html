Á (aa)

Words in a (a) or á (aa), are often heard beginning with ng (ng), as ngá (ngaa), ngai (ngai), ngat (ngat).

꜀Á (陰平; aa1)

丫 (U+4E2B) ꜀á (aa1)

Williams entry
(Page 1)
  • A fork, a crotch;
  • the parting of two branches, fingers, or tines;
  • ꜀á ꜁tʻau (丫頭; aa1 tau4) or ꜀á ꜁wán (丫鬟; aa1 waan4), a slave girl (it's 1856);
  • ꜀á kok꜆ kai꜄ (丫角髻; aa1 gok3 gai3) a little boy (it's 1856);
  • ꜀á ꜀chʻá (丫杈; aa1 caa1), a fork, a rest for clothes' sticks;
  • shü꜅ ꜀á (樹丫; syu6 aa1) fork of a tree;
  • ꜀sám ꜀á lò꜅ (三丫路; saam1 aa1 lou6) meeting of three ways, a trivium.

吖 (U+5416) ꜀á (aa1)

Alternative forms
(呀; 啊)
Reading variations
(a1: shorter syllable)
Williams entry
(Page 1)
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
  • ꜂hò ꜀á (好吖; hou2 aa1) well, very well;
  • ꜀á wai꜄ (?; aa1 wai3) poor, necessitous;
  • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á (佢噉話吖; keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1), he said so;
  • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á (乜嘢吖; mat1 je5 aa1), what?
  • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á (噉做吖; gam2 zou6 aa1), no, this is the way to do it.
Conway's remarks
  • Jyutping suggests using 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.

鴉 (U+9D09) ꜀á (aa1)

Williams entry
(Page 1)
  • A raven with a white streak on its breast;
  • ꜃lò ꜀á (老鴉; lou5 aa1), a raven or crow;

瘂 (U+7602) ꜂á (aa2)

Alternative forms
啞
Williams entry
(Page 1)
  • Dumb, unable to speak;
  • dull, faded;
  • a cracked sound, as a bell;
  • wheezing;
  • nape of the neck;
  • ꜂á ꜂tsai (瘂仔; aa2 zai2), a dumb boy;
  • ꜂á ꜂hau ꜁mò ꜁ín (瘂口無言; aa2 hau2 mou4 jin4), will not speak, sulky;

  • ꜀sheng tái꜄ ꜂á (聲帶瘂; seng1 daai3 aa2), the sound is indistinct.

Á꜅ (陽去; aa6)

呀 (U+5440) á꜅ (aa6)

Reading variations
(a6: shorter syllable for elided "ten" sense)
Williams entry
(Page 1)
A colloquial word. (...)
  • ten, used after a higher number;
  • an answer, a word of reply like Aye!
  • á꜅ ꜂hò (呀好; aa6 hou2), yes, well;
  • sz'꜄ á꜅ ꜁tsʻín (四呀錢; sei3 aa6 cin4), forty cash.
Conway's remarks
  • Williams has this entry within that for á꜄ (aa3); see aa#呀3.
  • The "ten" sense would never actually appear in writing as 呀; it would still be spelt 十, the pronunciation being a matter of preference.

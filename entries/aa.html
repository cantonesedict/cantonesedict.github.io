Á [J: aa; C: aa]

Words in a [J: a; C: a] or á [J: aa; C: aa], are often heard beginning with ng [J: ng; C: ng], as ngá [J: ngaa; C: ngaa], ngai [J: ngai; C: ngai], ngat [J: ngat; C: ngat].

Williams entry
[(Page 1)]
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
  • ꜂hò ꜀á [好吖; J: hou2 aa1; C: hou2 aa1] well, very well;
  • ꜀á wai꜄ [?; J: aa1 wai3; C: aa1 wai3] poor, necessitous;
  • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á [佢噉話吖; J: keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1; C: k'_ü kam2 waa6 aa1], he said so;
  • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á [乜嘢吖; J: mat1 je5 aa1; C: mat7 ye5 aa1], what?
  • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á [噉做吖; J: gam2 zou6 aa1; C: kam2 tsou6 aa1], no, this is the way to do it.
Conway's commentary
  • Jyutping suggests 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.

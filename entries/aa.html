Á [Conway: aa; Jyutping: aa]

Words in a [Conway: a; Jyutping: a] or á [Conway: aa; Jyutping: aa], are often heard beginning with ng [Conway: ng; Jyutping: ng], as ngá [Conway: ngaa; Jyutping: ngaa], ngai [Conway: ngai; Jyutping: ngai], ngat [Conway: ngat; Jyutping: ngat].

Williams entry
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle ;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable ;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning ;
  • (2)h(o) (1)á well, very well ;
  • (1)á wai(3) poor, necessitous ;
  • (5)k(')(u") (2)k(o)m wá(6) (1)á, he said so ;
  • mat(7) (5)y(e/) (1)á, what ?
  • (2)k(o)m ts(o)(6) á, no, this is the way to do it.

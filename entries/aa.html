Á. (aa)

Words in a (a) or á (aa), are often heard beginning with ng (ng), as ngá (ngaa), ngai (ngai), ngat (ngat).

꜀Á. (aa1 陰平)

丫 (U+4E2B) ꜀á (aa1)

Williams (1856) entry
(Page 1)
  • A fork, a crotch;
  • the parting of two branches, fingers, or tines;
  • ꜀á ꜁tʻau (aa1 tau4 丫頭) or ꜀á ꜁wán (aa1 waan4 丫鬟), a slave girl;
  • ꜀á kok꜆ kai꜄ (aa1 gok3 gai3 丫角髻) a little boy;
  • ꜀á ꜀chʻá (aa1 caa1 丫杈), a fork, a rest for clothes' sticks;
  • shü꜅ ꜀á (syu6 aa1 樹丫) fork of a tree;
  • ꜀sám ꜀á lò꜅ (saam1 aa1 lou6 三丫路) meeting of three ways, a trivium.
Conway's remarks
  • 丫頭, 丫鬟: Yes, people kept slaves in the 19th century.
  • 丫頭, 丫鬟, and 丫角髻: From the double-bun hairstyle.
  • 三丫路: Rarely heard; 三叉路 is now more common.

吖 (U+5416) ꜀á (aa1)

Alternative forms
(呀; 啊)
Reading variations
(a1: shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 1)
A colloquial word.
  • An interrogative particle;
  • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
  • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
  • ꜂hò ꜀á (hou2 aa1 好吖) well, very well;
  • ꜀á wai꜄ (aa1 wai3 吖喂) poor, necessitous;
  • ꜃kʻü ꜂kòm wá꜅ ꜀á (keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1 佢噉話吖), he said so;
  • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀á (mat1 je5 aa1 乜嘢吖), what?
  • ꜂kòm tsò꜅ á (gam2 zou6 aa1 噉做吖), no, this is the way to do it.
Conway's remarks
  • 好吖: "OK" (as an active, enthusiastic endorsement).
  • 吖喂: A polite "oi!" to get someone's attention at the end of a sentence. Not sure if these were the characters intended by Williams' "poor, necessitous".
  • [Jyutping suggests using 吖 as the canonical character for aa1.][jpp]

鴉 (U+9D09) ꜀á (aa1)

Williams (1856) entry
(Page 1)
  • A raven with a white streak on its breast;
  • ꜃lò ꜀á (lou5 aa1 老鴉), a raven or crow;
  • ꜁tʻò ꜀á (tou4 aa1 塗鴉), written roughly;
  • ꜀á pʻín꜄ ꜁nai (aa1 pin3 nai4 鴉片坭), opium.
Conway's remarks
  • 鴉片坭: Dated; opium is now simply called 鴉片.

阿 (U+963F) á꜄ (aa3)

Alternative forms
亞
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 2)
  • (see 亞 for the "second/inferior" senses)
  • a syllable prefixed to proper names, for which the second (阿) is often used;
  • it also impersonates epithets;
  • á꜄ múi꜅ (aa3 mui6-2 阿妹) a younger sister, a lass;
  • á꜄ mat꜆ (aa3 mat1 阿乜) Mr. So and So;
  • á꜄ pí꜅ (aa3 bei6) a silly fellow;
  • á꜄ ꜀chʻo (aa3 co1 阿初), a raw hand;
  • á꜄ ꜀pá (aa3 baa1 阿爸), papa;
  • á꜄ ꜃tʻai (aa3 tai5 阿娣), boy, you lad;
Conway's remarks
  • Williams does not provide a separate entry for 亞 and 阿. I have split things up accordingly.

Á꜅. (aa6 陽去)

呀 (U+5440) á꜅ (aa6)

Reading variations
(a6: shorter syllable for elided "ten" sense)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 1)
A colloquial word. (...)
  • ten, used after a higher number;
  • an answer, a word of reply like Aye!
  • á꜅ ꜂hò (aa6 hou2 呀好), yes, well;
  • sz'꜄ á꜅ ꜁tsʻín (sei3 aa6 cin4 四呀錢), forty cash.
Conway's remarks
  • Williams has this entry within that for á꜄ (aa3); see aa#呀3.
  • 四呀錢: The "ten" sense would never actually appear in writing as 呀; it would still be spelt 十, the pronunciation being a matter of preference.

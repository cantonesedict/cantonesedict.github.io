Williams (1856) variational notes
  • ГҒ. Words in a (a) or ГЎ (aa), are often heard beginning with ng (ng), as ngГЎ (ngaa), ngai (ngai), ngat (ngat).
Williams (1856) pronunciation
  • ГҒ = *[aЛҗ]
Modern pronunciation
  • aa = [aЛҗ]
  • a = [Йҗ]
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "often heard beginning with ng": Generally speaking, for literary characters, initial ng- is only expected for the light tones (йҷҪе№і, йҷҪдёҠ, йҷҪеҺ», йҷҪе…Ҙ), reflecting voiced initials in Middle Chinese; prepending initial ng- on the dark tones (йҷ°е№і, йҷ°дёҠ, йҷ°еҺ», йҷ°е…Ҙ) is hypercorrection.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (PageВ 1 кңҖеҗ–)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interrogative particle;
    • also one indicating that the affirmation is indisputable;
    • a mere final answering tone, having no meaning;
    • кңӮhГІ кңҖГЎ (hou2 aa1 еҘҪеҗ–) well, very well;
    • кңҖГЎ waiкң„ (aa1 wai3 еҗ–пјҹ) poor, necessitous;
    • кңғkК»Гј кңӮkГІm wГЎкң… кңҖГЎ (keoi5 gam2 waa6 aa1 дҪўеҷүи©ұеҗ–), he said so;
    • matкңҶ кңғyГ© кңҖГЎ (mat1 je5 aa1 д№ңеҳўеҗ–), what?
    • кңӮkГІm tsГІкң… кңҖГЎ (gam2 zou6 aa1 еҷүеҒҡеҗ–), no, this is the way to do it.
  • (PageВ 1 кңҖе‘Җ; here normalised to еҗ–)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interjection used in answers, denoting surprise or alarm;
    • alas! dreadful.

    (вҖҰ)

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗеҗ–гҖ‘ (a1, aa1)
    1. An interrogative-ish final particle suggesting the preceding.
      • д»Ҡжҷҡеҗ– (gam1 maan5-1 a1): "[How about] tonight."
      • дҪ иЎҢе…Ҳеҗ– (nei5 haang4 sin1 a1): "[Why don't] you go first."
    2. An imperative-ish final particle (when the preceding is spoken with force).
      • йЈІеҗ– (jam2 a1): "Drink [it]."
    3. An emphatic final particle.
      • еҘҪеҗ– (hou2 a1): "OK" (as an active, enthusiastic endorsement).
  • гҖҗеҗ–еҳӣгҖ‘ (a1 maa3)
    1. An indicative-ish final particle pointing out the preceding. Similar to ending on a falling intonation in English.
      • еӨ©ж°ЈеҮҚеҗ–еҳӣ (tin1 hei3 dung3 a1 maa3): "[Because] the weather's cold."
    2. An interrogative-ish final particle seeking affirmation of the preceding.
      • д»Ҡжҷҡеҗ–еҳӣ (gam1 maan5-1 a1 maa3): "[That's] tonight, [right]?"

The spellings еҗ– and еҗ–еҳӣ are arbitrary.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗе•ҠгҖ‘ (a3, aa3)
    1. A final particle (with a hint of emphasis).
      • еҲ°жңӘе•Ҡ (dou3 mei6 a3): "[Are we] there yet?"
      • жңӘеҲ°е•Ҡ (mei6 dou3 a3): "Not there yet."
      • жңүжҺ’е•Ҡ (jau5 paai4 a3): "[It'll] be ages."
    2. A vocative particle, especially used before airing a grievance.
      • йҳҝзҲ№е•Ҡ (aa3 de1 a3): "Dad!"
  • гҖҗе•ҠгҖ‘ (aa3)
    1. A particle after each item in a list of examples. Similar to the non-falling intonation for list items in English.
      • дҫӢеҰӮиҳӢжһңе•ҠгҖҒж©ҷе•ҠгҖҒйҰҷи•үе•Ҡ (lai6 jyu4 ping4 gwo2 aa3, caang4-2 aa3, hoeng1 ziu1 aa3): "Such as apples, oranges, bananas, вҖҰ."

The spelling е•Ҡ is arbitrary.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗе‘ҖгҖ‘ (a4)
    1. An interrogative-ish final particle of surprise, suspicion, disappointment, etc.
      • д»Ҡжҷҡе‘Җ (gam1 maan5-1 a4): "Tonight?"
      • е””йЈІе‘Җ (m4 jam2 a4): "[Oh, you're] not drinking [it]?"
    2. A vocative particle, especially used before asking a question.
      • зҲ№зҲ№е‘Җ (de1 de1 a4): "Daddy," (followed by a question).
      • еҘіе‘Җ (neoi5-2 a4): "Daughter," (followed by a question).

The spelling е‘Җ is arbitrary.

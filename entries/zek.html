Chek[꜆] Tsek꜆ (zek3 低陰入)

  • 拓
  • 摭
  • 隻

(Page 41*, before the last three entries for chik꜇ (zik6)) (The three next characters (隻, 蹠跖, 炙) are usually pronounced chek.) (Here, Williams has been misled by Fan Wan's placement of the three series 跖蹠拓隻炙 (chik), 尺 (chʻik), and 石碩鼫 (shik) after 劇屐 (kʻik꜇) and 溺 (nik꜇), which are 陽入; see Vol. 1 Image 78. Based on this ordering, Williams has inferred that the characters 隻, 蹠跖, and 炙 are read 陽入 chik꜇ (zik6). However, since Fan Wan has separate series for 直値殖植稙湜埴 (chik꜇) and 食蝕 (shik꜇), it appears that its authors have attempted to distinguish the vernacular rime ek from the literary rime ik, notwithstanding all series appear under 第七英影應益. I have corrected Williams by placing 隻, 蹠跖, 炙, and 摭拓 under zek3 here.)

隻 Chek[꜆] (zek3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾫ + 2
Unicode code point
U+96BB
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 78 隻) 件也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14* 隻꜇ Chek꜇ (zek6); here corrected to Chek꜆ (zek3))
    • One, single, not a pair, one of a pair;
    • a classifier of vessels, boats, animals, birds, and of things in pairs, as legs, arms eyes, cups, saucers, &c.;
    • chek[꜆] ꜀shan (zek3 san1 隻身), myself alone;
    • chek[꜆] chek[꜆] ꜃yau ꜃mí (zek3 zek3 jau5 mei5 隻隻有尾), every one has a tail;
    • tái꜅ chek[꜆] hat꜆ ꜁í (daai6 zek3 hat1 ji4-1 大隻乞兒), a sturdy beggar;
    • kiú꜄ chek[꜆] ꜃tʻeng (giu3 zek3 teng5 叫隻艇), call a boat.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams has been misled by Fan Wan into placing this under Chik꜇ (zik6) / Chek꜇ (zek6). See remarks under zek3.

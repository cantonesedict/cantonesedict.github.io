拓 [Chek꜆] (zek3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 5
Unicode code point
U+62D3
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 78 拓) 拾也又充拓又托推也斥開也拓落不偶也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14* 摭꜇拓꜇ Chik꜇ (zik6); here corrected to Chik꜆ (zik3) and normalised to Chek꜆ (zek3))

    (Alternative form of 摭. See 摭 zek3 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams has been misled by Fan Wan's placement of the series 跖蹠拓隻炙 (chik) after 劇屐 (kʻik꜇) and 溺 (nik꜇), which are 陽入 (see Vol. 1 Image 78). Based on this ordering, Williams has inferred that the character 拓 is read 陽入 chik꜇ (zik6). However, since Fan Wan has a separate series for 直値殖植稙湜埴 (chik꜇), it appears that its authors have attempted to distinguish the vernacular rime -ek from the literary rime -ik, notwithstanding all series appear under 第七英影應益. I have corrected Williams by placing 拓 under chek꜆ (cek3) here.

Radical + residual strokes

Unicode code point
U+7099
Reading variations
  • zik3 (alternative)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 78 炙) 燔炙
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14* before 隻꜇)

    (The three next characters (隻, 蹠跖, 炙) are usually pronounced chek (zek).)

  • (Page 14* 炙꜇ Chik꜇ (zik6); Chek꜇ (zek6) implied by variational note; here corrected to Chek꜆ (zek3))
    • To roast (Kangxi: 炮肉也, 炕火曰炙);
    • to broil;
    • to dry or toast before the fire;
    • to heat;
    • near (Kangxi: 親近也);
    • to approach, to approximate;
    • to fry in honey, as some physic is;
    • ꜂[kʻúi] chek[꜆] ꜁yan ꜂hau (kui2 zek3 jan4 hau2 膾炙人口), tastes well in people's mouths — said of fine poetry;
    • chek[꜆] ꜂tsʻò (zek3 cou2 炙草), dried liquorice;
    • chek[꜆] ꜂shau (zek3 sau2 炙手), to warm the hand;
    • ꜁fán chek[꜆] (faan4 zek3 燔炙) to roast or broil;
    • chek[꜆] ꜀kon (zek3 gon1 炙乾), dried thoroughly.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams has been misled by Fan Wan's placement of the series 跖蹠拓隻炙 (chik) after 劇屐 (kʻik꜇) and 溺 (nik꜇), which are 陽入 (see Vol. 1 Image 78). However, since Fan Wan has a separate series for 直値殖植稙湜埴 (chik꜇), it appears that its authors have attempted to distinguish the vernacular rime -ek from the literary rime -ik, notwithstanding all series appear under 第七英影應益. I have corrected Williams by placing 炙 under chek꜆ (cek3) here.
  • Williams' 膾炙人口: Corrected ꜂kúi (gui2) to ꜂kʻúi (kui2).
  • Williams' "near": Specifically, to come under the (positive) influence of another.

脊 Tsek꜆ (zek3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾁ + 6
Unicode code point
U+810A
Reading variations
  • zik3 (alternative)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 74 脊 Tsik꜆ (zik3)) 脊骨
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 569 before 脊꜆)

    (This (脊) and the next (席, here corrected to 蓆) are often read tsek (zek).)

  • (Page 569 脊꜆ Tsik꜆ (zik3); Tsek꜆ (zek3) implied by variational note)
    • The back-bone, the spine (Kangxi: 背呂也, 背脊);
    • the back;
    • convergent, as the ribs;
    • the ridge of a house;
    • a sierra, a ridge (Kangxi: 山脊);
    • a bone;
    • a principle (Kangxi: 理也);
    • púi꜄ tsek꜆ kwat꜆ (bui3 zek3 gwat1 背脊骨) the spine;
    • uk꜆ tsek꜆ (uk1 zek3 屋脊) the top of a roof;
    • ꜁mò tsik꜆ kwat꜆ (mou4 zik3 gwat1 無脊骨) no dependance can be put in him;
    • ꜂hí yat꜆ [꜁]tʻiú tsek꜆ (hei2 jat1 tiu4 zek3 起一條脊) raise a ridge;
    • ꜃mái ꜁ü tsek꜆ (maai5 jyu4 zek3 買魚脊) buy the fish's back.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a bone": Only as an alternative form of 㱴 (Kangxi: 與㱴通死骨也), which has a different reading.
  • Williams' 起一條脊: Corrected ꜀tʻiú (tiu1) to ꜁tʻiú (tiu4).

    • kiú꜄ chek[꜆] ꜃tʻeng (giu3 zek3 teng5 叫隻艇), call a boat.

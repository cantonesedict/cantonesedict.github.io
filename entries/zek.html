(Page 14*, before the last three entries for chik꜇ (zik6)) (The three next characters (隻, 蹠跖, 炙) are usually pronounced chek.) (Here, Williams has been misled by Fan Wan's placement of the three series 跖蹠拓隻炙 (chik), 尺 (chʻik), and 石碩鼫 (shik) after 劇屐 (kʻik꜇) and 溺 (nik꜇), which are 陽入; see Vol. 1 Image 78. Based on this ordering, Williams has inferred that the characters 隻, 蹠跖, and 炙 are read 陽入 chik꜇ (zik6). However, since Fan Wan has separate series for 直値殖植稙湜埴 (chik꜇) and 食蝕 (shik꜇), it appears that its authors have attempted to distinguish the vernacular rime ek from the literary rime ik, notwithstanding all series appear under 第七英影應益. I have corrected Williams by placing 隻, 蹠跖, 炙, and 摭拓 under zek3 here.)

(Page 569, after 踖 tsik꜆ (zik1)) (This (脊) and the next (席; here corrected to 蓆) are often read tsek.)

炙 Chek[꜆] (zek3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 4
Unicode code point
U+7099
Reading variations
  • zik3 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 78 炙) 燔炙
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14* 炙꜇ Chek꜇ (zek6); here corrected to Chek꜆ (zek3))
    • To roast;
    • to broil;
    • to dry or toast before the fire;
    • to heat;
    • near;
    • to approach, to approximate;
    • to fry in honey, as some physic is;
    • ꜂[kʻúi] chek[꜆] ꜁yan ꜂hau (kui2 zek3 jan4 hau2 膾炙人口), tastes well in people's mouths — said of fine poetry;
    • chek[꜆] ꜂tsʻò (zek3 cou2 炙草), dried liquorice;
    • chek[꜆] ꜂shau (zek3 sau2 炙手), to warm the hand;
    • ꜁fán chek[꜆] (faan4 zek3 燔炙) to roast or broil;
    • chek[꜆] ꜀kon (zek3 gon1 炙乾), dried thoroughly.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 膾炙人口: Corrected ꜂kúi (gui2) to ꜂kʻúi (kui2).

Radical + residual strokes

U+96BB
Alternative forms
  • 只
Reading variations
  • zik3 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14* 隻꜇ Chek꜇ (zek6); here corrected to Chek꜆ (zek3))
    • One, single, not a pair, one of a pair;
    • a classifier of vessels, boats, animals, birds, and of things in pairs, as legs, arms eyes, cups, saucers, &c.;
    • chek[꜆] ꜀shan (zek3 san1 隻身), myself alone;

    • tái꜅ chek[꜆] hat꜆ ꜁í (daai6 zek3 hat1 ji4-1 大隻乞兒), a sturdy beggar;
    • kiú꜄ chek[꜆] ꜃tʻeng (giu3 zek3 teng5 叫隻艇), call a boat.
  • (Page 713 隻꜆ Chik꜆ (zik3); here normalised to Chek꜆ (zek3))
    • This character is constantly contracted to 只 in Canton.

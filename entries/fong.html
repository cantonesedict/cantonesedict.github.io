荒 ꜀Fong (fong1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 6
Unicode code point
U+8352
Alternative forms
  • 𮎰
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 96 荒) 荒蕪荒廢大也遠也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 56 ꜀𮎰; here normalised to 荒)
    • Waste, wild (Kangxi 荒: 蕪也), barren, deserted, unproductive;
    • distant (Fan Wan: 遠也);
    • jungle, heath (Kangxi 荒: 一曰草掩地也);
    • empty, uncultivated, void (Kangxi 荒: 空也), unripe, blasted;
    • a famine, a dearth (Kangxi 荒: 四穀不升謂之荒, 果不熟爲荒);
    • to overshadow, to overgrow (Kangxi 荒: 掩也);
    • to magnify (Kangxi 荒: 大也);
    • ꜀fong ꜁mò (fong1 mou4 荒蕪), bushy, overgrown;
    • ꜀fong fai꜄ (fong1 fai3 荒廢) to disregard, to cease, old, desuetude;
    • ꜀fong ꜁tʻong (fong1 tong4 荒唐), incoherent, incredible, unworthy of belief;
    • ꜀fong ꜁nín (fong1 nin4 荒年), a year of scarcity;
    • ꜀fong ꜀sho (fong1 so1 荒疏), disused, neglected, obsolete;
    • ꜀fong ꜀káu (fong1 gaau1 荒郊), wilderness, wilds;
    • ꜀fong ꜁yam (fong1 jam4 荒淫), lustful.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 慌. See 慌 fong1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "unripe": Here, Williams has been misled by Kangxi's example 《爾雅釋天》果不熟爲荒. The full passage (with punctuation) is 穀不熟爲饑、蔬不熟爲饉、果不熟爲荒、仍饑爲荐, which gives the names of various types of crop failure: 饑 for grain, 饉 for vegetable, 荒 for fruit, and 荐 for repeated grain failure. It does not define 荒 as meaning "fruit not ripe".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 荒唐, 荒謬, etc. (which now mean "absurd").
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【荒】 (xwang)
    • (adjective-like) uncultivated, overgrown
    • (noun-like) dearth
    • (adjective-like) desolate
    • (adjective-like) extensive, (met.) excessive
    • (verb-like) [to] extend

舫 ꜂Fong (fong2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾈ + 4
Unicode code point
U+822B
Reading variations
  • bong3 (per 《廣韻》補曠切)
  • fong3 (per 《廣韻》甫妄切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 99 舫) 船名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 57 ꜂舫)
    • (…);
    • a pilot or steersman (Kangxi: 船師也);
    • (…);
    • wá꜅ ꜂fong (waa6 fong2 畫舫), a flower or other handsome river boat;
    • ꜂fong ꜀chung ꜁tʻám ꜂yam (fong2 zung1 taam4 jam2 舫中談飲), conversing and drinking in a boat.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a pilot or steersman": This is a faithful rendering of Kangxi 《說文》船師也, but that definition is incorrect. According to 段玉裁's annotations of 《說文》, the explanation 船師也 should simply be 船也, with the logic being that in 舫人習水者, we simply have 舫 meaning "boat" and 人 meaning "man" (i.e. it is the compound 舫人 that means "helmsman"). Kangxi gives analysis in the same vein from 《正字通》.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A boat.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【舫】 (pjangH, pangH)
    • (noun-like) boat

    • ꜂siú ꜀sam ꜀fong ꜁fong hoi꜅ (siu2 sam1 fong4 hoi6 小心妨害) be careful about receiving damage, look out for yourself;
    • ꜀fong ꜁fong ngoi꜅ (fong4 ngoi6 妨礙) an obstacle, something which is in the way, or is dangerous;
    • ꜁mò ꜀fong ꜁fong (mou4 fong4 無妨), there is nothing to fear, all clear.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 妨礙: Better rendered as a verb, "to obstruct".
  • Williams' 無妨: Better rendered "there is no harm [in doing so]".
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【妨】 (phjang)
    • (verb-like) [to] injure, [to] harm

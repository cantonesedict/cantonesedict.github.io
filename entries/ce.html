且 ꜂Chʻé (ce2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼀ + 4
Unicode code point
U+4E14
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 且) 語詞
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 ꜂且)
    • A conjunctive particle;
    • and, further, moreover, still;
    • also, and also (Kangxi: 又也);
    • thus, so (Kangxi: 此也);
    • still, yet;
    • a word of doubt, if, should (Kangxi: 借曰之辭);
    • ꜁hang ꜂chʻé (hang4 ce2 行且), on the point of being;
    • ꜂chʻé san꜄ sun꜄ ꜂chʻé ꜁í (ce2 seon3 ce2 ji4 且信且疑), half believing, half doubting;
    • fú꜄ ꜂chʻé kwai꜄ (fu3 ce2 gwai3 富且貴) rich and so honored;
    • ꜂kau ꜂chʻé ꜃liú sz'꜅ (gau2 ce2 liu5 si6 苟且了事) slovenly way of doing things;
    • fong꜄ ꜂chʻé (fong3 ce2 況且), still further;
    • ꜂chʻé mán꜅ (ce2 maan6 且慢) by and by, presently;
    • ꜀kú ꜂chʻé (gu1 ce2 姑且) or tsám꜅ ꜂chʻé (zaam6 ce2 暫且), for a time.
Conway's commentary
  • Strictly, the pre-merger reading should be ꜂tsʻé per 《廣韻》七也切. Fan Wan groups it with 扯 (same as 撦), whose reading is ꜂chʻé (ce2) per 《廣韻》昌者切. Notwithstanding, this is all academic given the initials tsʻ and chʻ are now considered merged.
  • Williams' "a word of doubt": This is an attempt at rendering Kangxi 未定之辭. A better rendering is "[a] word [indicating] uncertainness". Note that this is a separate sense to subjunctive "if", "should".
  • Williams' 且信且疑: Corrected san꜄ (san3) to sun꜄ (seon3).
  • Williams' 且慢: Not an adverbial "soon" or "shortly", but an imperative "slow down" or "hang on".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Temporarily, for now.
  2. Shall, about to (Kangxi: 將也).
  3. Still.
  4. Or.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【且】 (tshjaeX)
    • (conjunction-like) and (also), moreover
    • (determiner-like) this
    • (adverb-like) for now
    • (auxiliary-like) shall, about to
    • (adverb-like) still
    • (conjunction-like) or

扯 ꜂Chʻé (ce2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 4
Unicode code point
U+626F
Alternative forms
  • 撦
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 抯) 止也拽也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 扯) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 ꜂撦꜂抯꜂扯)
    • To tear open, to pull apart (Kangxi 撦: 裂開也);
    • to tear away;
    • to pull up or on;
    • to haul, to drag;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜃shéung (ce2 soeng5 扯上), haul up;
    • ꜀lái ꜂chʻé (laai1 ce2 拉扯), to pull and haul, to borrow and lend;
    • ꜀lái ꜀lái ꜂chʻé ꜂chʻé (laai1 laai1 ce2 ce2 拉拉扯扯), borrowing here and there; on the average, lumping the whole;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜀hoi (ce2 hoi1 扯開), to pull apart;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜁mái (ce2 maai4 扯埋), to pull up, to close, as a skylight;
    • ꜂chʻé chü꜅ (ce2 zyu6 扯住) to stop from going.

    A colloquial word.

    • To clear out, to go off, to abscond;
    • to send off;
    • to go away, to take one's self off;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜀lo (ce2 lo1 扯囉), go! let us go;
    • ꜃ngo ꜂chʻé lok꜆ (ngo5 ce2 lok3 我扯咯) I'm off.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan (and Williams following it) considers 抯 the same as 扯. Strictly, this is erroneous; the three readings of 抯 in 《廣韻》 are:
    1. ꜀chá (zaa1) per 側加切, explained 挹也 (per 說文);
    2. tsé꜅ (ze6) per 徐野切, explained 取也 (or ꜃tsʻé (ce5) if insisting on rising tone); and
    3. ꜂tsé (ze2) per 兹野切, also explained 取也.
    None of these are 陰上 ꜂chʻé (ce2).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【扯】 (ce2)
    1. To leave.
      • 你扯啦 (nei5 ce2 laa1): "Leave!"

斜 Tsʻé꜄ (ce3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽃ + 7
Unicode code point
U+659C
Reading variations
  • ce3-2 (rising second in vernacular 斜斜地)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 565 斜꜄)

    A colloquial word.

    • Not level, rising, as a road;
    • fong꜄ tsʻé꜄ (fong3 ce3 放斜) lay it one end higher;
    • tsʻé꜄ tsʻé꜄ lok꜇ (ce3 ce3 lok6 斜斜落) go down winding;
    • tsʻé꜄ ꜀po (ce3 bo1 斜坡), an ascending plain;
    • ꜁hau ꜁tʻau tsʻé꜄ (hau4 tau4 ce3 喉頭斜) greedy.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【斜路】 (ce3 lou6-2)
    1. An incline, slope, or ramp.
      • 行斜路 (haang4 ce3 lou6-2): "Walking [up/down a] ramp" (as opposed to stairs).
      • 寫字上斜路 (se2 zi6 soeng5 ce3 lou6-2): "[Your] writing [is] going up [an] incline" (i.e. not level).
  • 【長命斜】 (coeng4 meng6 ce3)
    1. A long and steep incline or slope.

Williams views departing-tone 斜 tsʻé꜄ (ce3) as a word in its own right, but personally I view it merely as a vernacular reading variation of level-tone 斜 ꜁tsʻé (ce4). (E.g. 斜坡 is read both as tsʻé꜄ ꜀po (ce3 bo1) and as ꜁tsʻé ꜀po (ce4 bo1).)

Some point to 笡 as the original character for the departing-tone reading:

《廣韻》遷謝切：笡：斜逆也

But whilst the reading tsʻé꜄ (ce3) is a match, the explanation 斜逆也 seems more a metaphorical "heretical" than a physical "inclined" or "sloped", so I am yet to be convinced of 笡.

    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁tsʻé tik꜆ (ce4 ce4 dik1 斜斜滴) it is rather awry;
    • ꜀sai ꜁tsʻé ít꜇ (sai1 ce4 jit6 西斜熱) the afternoon sun is hot;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜀fung (ce4 fung1 斜風), an unsteady wind;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜃ngán (ce4 ngaan5 斜眼), cross-eyed.

邪 ꜁Tsʻé (ce4)

Radical + residual strokes
⾢ + 4
Unicode code point
U+90AA
Alternative forms
  • 衺
Reading variations
  • ce4-2 (in 唔信邪)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 邪) 邪曲不直也又[奸]佞也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 564 ꜁邪)
    • Deflected, inclined, swerving from the right line (Kangxi: 不正也);
    • whatever leads astray;
    • depraved, bad, crooked, vicious, corrupting, obscene (Kangxi: 姦思也);
    • specious, deluding (Kangxi: 佞也);
    • heretical, illegal, not acknowledged by the state, or in the classics;
    • ꜁'m sun꜄ ꜁tsʻé (m4 seon3 ce4-2 唔信邪) don't believe heresies;
    • ꜁tsʻé tò꜅ (ce4 dou6 邪道) erroneous doctrines;
    • ꜁tsʻé peng꜅ (ce4 beng6 邪病) epileptic sickness;
    • ꜁tsʻé fát꜆ (ce4 faat3 邪法) sorceries, tricks;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁shan (ce4 san4 邪神), illegal, unacknowledged gods, those whose worship corrupts;
    • ꜀kán ꜁tsʻé (gaan1 ce4 奸邪), malicious, fornicating, illicit;
    • ꜂koi ꜁tsʻé ꜀kwai ching꜄ (goi2 ce4 gwai1 zing3 改邪歸正) forsake errors alter from wickedness and cleave return to the right;
    • ꜁tsʻé shut꜇ (ce4 seot6 邪術) charms, philtres, black art;
    • ꜀sam ꜁tsʻé (sam1 ce4 心邪), impure, lewd in mind;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁yam (ce4 jam4 邪淫), obscene, licentious.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 斜. See 斜 ce4 for the canonical entry.)

    • (adjective-like) wicked

  • 【唔信邪】 (m4 seon3 ce4-2)
    1. Not believing in negative superstitions.

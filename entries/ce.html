且 ꜂Chʻé (ce2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼀ + 4
Unicode code point
U+4E14
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 且) 語詞
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 ꜂且)
    • A conjunctive particle;
    • and, further, moreover, still;
    • also, and also;
    • thus, so;
    • still, yet;
    • a word of doubt, if, should;
    • ꜁hang ꜂chʻé (hang4 ce2 行且), on the point of being;
    • ꜂chʻé [sun]꜄ ꜂chʻé ꜁í (ce2 seon3 ce2 ji4 且信且疑), half believing, half doubting;
    • fú꜄ ꜂chʻé kwai꜄ (fu3 ce2 gwai3 富且貴) rich and so honored;
    • ꜂kau ꜂chʻé ꜃liú sz'꜅ (gau2 ce2 liu5 si6 苟且了事) slovenly way of doing things;
    • fong꜄ ꜂chʻé (fong3 ce2 況且), still further;
    • ꜂chʻé mán꜅ (ce2 maan6 且慢) by and by, presently;
    • ꜀kú ꜂chʻé (gu1 ce2 姑且) or tsám꜅ ꜂chʻé (zaam6 ce2 暫且), for a time.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 且信且疑: Corrected san꜄ (san3) to sun꜄ (seon3).
  • Williams' 且慢: More an imperative "slow down" or "hang on" than adverbial "soon" or "shortly".
  • Strictly, the reading is ꜂tsʻé (ce2) from Middle Chinese 七也切, but Fan Wan groups it with 扯 (same as 撦), whose reading is ꜂chʻé (ce2) from 昌者切. Notwithstanding, this is all academic given the initials tsʻ and chʻ are now considered merged.

扯 ꜂Chʻé (ce2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 4
Unicode code point
U+626F
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 抯) 止也拽也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 扯) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 ꜂撦꜂抯꜂扯)
    • To tear open, to pull apart;
    • to tear away;
    • to pull up or on;
    • to haul, to drag;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜃shéung (ce2 soeng5 扯上), haul up;
    • ꜀lái ꜂chʻé (laai1 ce2 拉扯), to pull and haul, to borrow and lend;
    • ꜀lái ꜀lái ꜂chʻé ꜂chʻé (laai1 laai1 ce2 ce2 拉拉扯扯), borrowing here and there; on the average, lumping the whole;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜀hoi (ce2 hoi1 扯開), to pull apart;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜁mái (ce2 maai4 扯埋), to pull up, to close, as a skylight;
    • ꜂chʻé chü꜅ (ce2 zyu6 扯住) to stop from going.

    A colloquial word.

    • To clear out, to go off, to abscond;
    • to send off;
    • to go away, to take one's self off;
    • ꜂chʻé ꜀lo (ce2 lo1 扯囉), go! let us go;
    • ꜃ngo ꜂chʻé lok꜆ (ngo5 ce2 lok3 我扯咯) I'm off.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【扯】 (ce2):
    1. To leave.
      • 你扯啦 (nei5 ce2 laa1): "Leave!"

  • (Page 31 ꜂撦꜂抯꜂扯)

    (…)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan (and Williams following it) considers 抯 the same as 扯. Strictly, this is erroneous; the three readings in Middle Chinese are:
    1. ꜀chá (zaa1) from 側加切, explained 挹也 (quoting 說文);
    2. tsé꜅ (ze6) from 徐野切, explained 取也 (or ꜃tsʻé (ce5) if insisting on rising tone); and
    3. ꜂tsé (ze2) from 兹野切, also explained 取也.
    None of these are 陰上 ꜂chʻé (ce2).

  • 斜

斜 Tsʻé꜄ (ce3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽃ + 7
Unicode code point
U+659C
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 565 斜꜄)

    A colloquial word.

    • Not level, rising, as a road;
    • fong꜄ tsʻé꜄ (fong3 ce3 放斜) lay it one end higher;
    • tsʻé꜄ tsʻé꜄ lok꜇ (ce3 ce3 lok6 斜斜落) go down winding;
    • tsʻé꜄ ꜀po (ce3 bo1 斜坡), an ascending plain;
    • ꜁hau ꜁tau tsʻé꜄ (hau4 tau4 ce3 喉頭斜) greedy.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【斜路】 (ce3 lou6-2):
    1. An incline, slope, or ramp.
      • 行斜路 (haang4 ce3 lou6-2): "Walking [up/down a] ramp" (as opposed to stairs).
      • 寫字上斜路 (se2 zi6 soeng5 ce3 lou6-2): "[Your] writing [is] going up [an] incline" (i.e. not level).
  • 【長命斜】 (coeng4 meng6 ce3):
    1. A long and steep incline or slope.

Williams views departing-tone 斜 tsʻé꜄ (ce3) as a word in its own right, but personally I view it merely as a vernacular reading variation of level-tone 斜 ꜁tsʻé (ce4). (E.g. 斜坡 is read both as tsʻé꜄ ꜀po (ce3 bo1) and as ꜁tsʻé ꜀po (ce4 bo1).)

Some point to 笡 as the original character for the departing-tone reading:

《廣韻》遷謝切：笡：斜逆也

But whilst the reading tsʻé꜄ (ce3) is a match, the explanation 斜逆也 seems more a metaphorical "heretical" than a physical "inclined" or "sloped", so I am yet to be fully convinced of 笡.

斜 ꜁Tsʻé (ce4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽃ + 7
Unicode code point
U+659C
Alternative forms
  • 邪
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 斜) 歪斜
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 565 ꜁斜)
    • Slanting, inclined, oblique, across, crisscross, diagonal, aslant, not with the rule;
    • distorted, scattered;
    • often used for the preceding (邪);
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁man pò꜄ (ce4 man4 bou3 斜紋布) drillings, twilled cloth;
    • ít꜇ ꜁tʻau ꜂pái ꜁tsʻé (jit6 tau4-2 baai2 ce4 熱頭擺斜), the sun is declining;
    • ꜁tsʻé shí꜅ (ce4 si6 斜視) to look askance;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁tsʻé tik꜆ (ce4 ce4 dik1 斜斜滴) it is rather awry;
    • ꜀sau ꜁tsʻé ít꜇ (sai1 ce4 jit6 西斜熱) the afternoon sun is hot;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜀fung (ce4 fung1 斜風), an unsteady wind;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜃ngán (ce4 ngaan5 斜眼), cross-eyed.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "used for the preceding": I have only seen this in the other direction, i.e. using 邪 for 斜.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【西斜】 (sai1 ce4):
    1. Exposure to the western sun.

邪 ꜁Tsʻé (ce4)

Radical + residual strokes
⾢ + 4
Unicode code point
U+90AA
Reading variations
  • ce4-2 (in 唔信邪)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 邪) 邪曲不直也又[奸]佞也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 564 ꜁邪)
    • Deflected, inclined, swerving from the right line;
    • whatever leads astray;
    • depraved, bad, crooked, vicious, corrupting, obscene;
    • specious, deluding;
    • heretical, illegal, not acknowledged by the state, or in the classics;
    • ꜁'m sun꜄ ꜁tsʻé (m4 seon3 ce4-2 唔信邪) don't believe heresies;
    • ꜁tsʻé tò꜅ (ce4 dou6 邪道) erroneous doctrines;
    • ꜁tsʻé peng꜅ (ce4 beng6 邪病) epileptic sickness;
    • ꜁tsʻé fát꜆ (ce4 faat3 邪法) sorceries, tricks;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁shan (ce4 san4 邪神), illegal, unacknowledged gods, those whose worship corrupts;
    • ꜀kán ꜁tsʻé (gaan1 ce4 奸邪), malicious, fornicating, illicit;
    • ꜂koi ꜁tsʻé ꜀kwai ching꜄ (goi2 ce4 gwai1 zing3 改邪歸正) forsake errors and cleave to the right;
    • ꜁tsʻé shut꜇ (ce4 seot6 邪術) charms, philtres, black art;
    • ꜀sam ꜁tsʻé (sam1 ce4 心邪), impure, lewd in mind;
    • ꜁tsʻé ꜁yam (ce4 jam4 邪淫), obscene, licentious.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 斜. See 斜 ce4 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 㚥 to 奸.
  • Williams' 改邪歸正: Better rendered "alter from wickedness [and] return unto the right."
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【唔信邪】 (m4 seon3 ce4-2):
    1. Not believing in negative superstitions.

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 592 錯꜆; here normalised to 剒)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • A hacking in the throat, a difficulty or stoppage in the throat;
    • tsʻok꜆ hí꜄ (cok3 hei3 剒氣) the heart in the throat;
    • ꜀chʻau tsʻok꜆ (cau1 cok3 抽剒) a retching cough;
    • tsʻok꜆ tsʻok꜆ ngok꜇ ngok꜇ (cok3 cok3 ngok6 ngok6 剒剒愕愕) amazed, alarmed and amused.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To work, to cut, to polish (Kangxi: 治樸之名, 亦作錯). (Alternative form of 錯 (for the "cut/work" sense). See 錯 cok3.)
  2. To chop off.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【剒】 (cok3):
    1. Choppiness in the airways, unsmoothness in breathing.
      • 剒住條氣 (cok3 zyu6 tiu4 hei3): "Choppiness [in the] airways."
    2. To shake, to jolt.
      • 剒車 (cok3 ce1): "Jerking car."
      • 剒散咗 (cok3 saan2 zo2): "Shaken to pieces."

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 588 錯꜄ Tsʻò꜄ (cou3); here corrected to Tsʻok꜆ (cok3))

    (…)

    • To cut or work stones (Kangxi: 治玉石曰錯);
    • (…).
  • (Page 592 錯꜆)
    • To wash or plate with gold (Kangxi: 金涂也);
    • the stripes, striæ, streaks, &c., in stones or fabrics (Kangxi: 錯衡文衡也);
    • to file or polish (Kangxi: 鑢也, 治玉石曰錯);
    • to tattoo (Kangxi: 錯臂亦文身謂以丹靑錯畫其臂);
    • a stone for grinding gems or glass (Kangxi: 厲石也, 錯石也可以琢玉);
    • mixed, crossed, barred, confused (Kangxi: 雜也, 亂也);
    • tsʻok꜆ pí꜄ (cok3 bei3 錯臂) to tattoo the arm.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 剒 (for the vernacular "choppiness" and "jolt" senses). See 剒 cok3 for the canonical entry.)

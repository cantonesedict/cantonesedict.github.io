之 ꜀Chí (zi1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼃ + 3
Unicode code point
U+4E4B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 35 之) 於也往也又語助詞
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 38 ꜀之)
    • A sign of the genitive;
    • when placed between two nouns or verbs, answers to who, which, that;
    • a pronoun, he, she, it, them;
    • often used in the abstract, after a noun, or as a relative particle, in apposition;
    • to progress;
    • to go to;
    • [tʻing]꜄ ꜀chí ꜀chí ꜁yan (ting3 zi1 zi1 jan4 聽之之人), the person who hears him;
    • tʻín ꜀chí ꜁ming ming꜅ (tin1 zi1 ming4 ming6 天之明命) heaven's plain decree;
    • ꜁ü ꜀chí noi꜅ ꜁ho (jyu4 zi1 noi6 ho4 如之奈何) if so, how then?
    • mí꜅ ꜀chí ꜃yau ꜃yá (mei6 zi1 jau5 jaa5 未之有也), there is no such thing;
    • ꜀tséung ꜁ho ꜀chí (zoeng1 ho4 zi1 將何之), where are you going?
    • ꜂kú ꜀chí (gu2 zi1 古之), the ancients;
    • tʻín ming꜅ ꜀chí (tin1 ming6 zi1 天命之), heaven orders it;
    • ꜁mò ꜀chí (mou4 zi1 無之), impossible;
    • ꜁hang ꜁í pat꜆ chí꜄ ꜂ché ꜃yau ꜀chí (hang4 ji4 bat1 zi3 ze2 jau5 zi1 行而不至者有之), to go and not arrive is common;
    • ꜂chü ꜀chí ꜂ché ꜀chí wai꜅ tai꜄ (zyu2 zi1 ze2 zi1 wai6 dai3 主之者之謂帝) that which rules is called supreme.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 聽之之人: Corrected tʻeng꜄ (teng3) to tʻing꜄ (ting3).

    • ꜀í chí꜅ (ji1 zi6 醫治) to cure;
    • ꜀tʻín há꜅ tái꜅ chí꜅ (tin1 haa6 daai6 zi6 天下大治) the empire well governed, firm peace;
    • ꜁mò fát꜆ ꜂ho chí꜅ (mou4 faat3 ho2 zi6 無法可治) there's no way of managing him;
    • chí꜅ ꜀ká (zi6 gaa1 治家), to order a family;
    • chí꜅ lün꜅ (zi6 lyun6 治亂) to suppress rebellion.
Conway's commentary
  • The level-tone reading ꜁chʻí (ci4) is the classical reading for verb senses of govern (e.g. 治國 "govern [the] nation"), as opposed to departing-tone chí꜅ (zi6) for adjective senses of govern (e.g. 國治 "[the] nation governed"). Notwithstanding, the only conclusion that can be drawn from 《廣韻》 is that the proper-noun sense is in level tone:
    • 《廣韻》直之切：治：水名出東萊亦理也
    • 《廣韻》直利切：治：治理也又直之切
    • 《廣韻》直吏切：治：治理也又丈之切
    Nevertheless, the distinction in reading between the verb "govern" and the adjective "governed" is not made up. In 《大學章句》, 治其國 is annotated with 治平聲, whilst 國治 is annotated with 治去聲.
  • In modern usage, level-tone ꜁chʻí (ci4) is considered obsolete.

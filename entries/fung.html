丰 ꜀Fung (fung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼁ + 3
Unicode code point
U+4E30
Han unification vigilance
May appear with first stroke ㇒ (assumed here) or ㇐, depending on the font.
Reading variations
  • fung4 (alternative per 《集韻》符風切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 丰) 丰采又面丰[滿]也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 66 ꜀丰)
    • Luxuriant (Kangxi: 丰茸草盛貌);
    • plump, good-looking (Kangxi: 容色美好貌);
    • easy, melodious;
    • a round face;
    • (…).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 風 (for the "manner/demeanour" sense). See 風 fung1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Kangxi considers the form with first stroke ㇐ (horizontal) to be standard.
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected malformed 滿.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【丰】 (phjowng)
    • (adjective-like) luxuriant, lush

封 ꜀Fung (fung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼨ + 6
Unicode code point
U+5C01
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 封) [緘]也培也又封贈
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 65 ꜀封)
    • The domain granted to princes (Kangxi: 爵諸侯之土也);
    • to heap up earth (Kangxi: 聚土曰封);
    • great (Kangxi: 大也), affluent (Kangxi: 富厚也);
    • to enrich;
    • to invest nobles with rule, to appoint to office, to confer investiture (Kangxi: 凡專利自私曰封);
    • to give a title of nobility;
    • to seal up, to close a letter (Kangxi: 緘也);
    • an envelop of a letter, classifier of letters;
    • 20 taels;
    • ꜀fung kò꜄ (fung1 gou3 封誥) to ennoble an officer's parents;
    • ꜀fung ꜁nám tséuk꜆ (fung1 naam4 zoek3 封男爵) to confer the title of viscount baron;
    • ꜀fung ꜂so (fung1 so2 封鎖), to seal up;
    • ꜀fung pʻò꜄ (fung1 pou3 封舖) to shut up a shop;
    • ꜀fung ꜁shün (fung1 syun4 封船), to taboo a boat for government use;
    • yat꜆ ꜀fung sun꜄ (jat1 fung1 seon3 一封信) a letter;
    • yat꜆ ꜀fung ꜁ngan (jat1 fung1 ngan4 一封銀), 20 taels of silver;
    • ꜀fung ꜁pʻí (fung1 pei4 封皮), an envelop for dispatches;
    • ꜀teng ꜀fung (deng1 fung1 釘封), a rescript inclosed in boards containing a death-warrant;
    • ꜀fung ꜀kéung tái꜅ ꜁shan (fung1 goeng1 daai6 san4 封疆大臣), the high provincial officers;
    • ꜀fung ꜁shan (fung1 san4 封神), to confer a title on an idol.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 絾 to 緘.
  • Williams' 封神: Better rendered "to deify".
  • In modern usage, used in 封面 (cover of a book or publication).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To earth up, to cultivate (Kangxi: 培也, 壅本曰封).
  2. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【封】 (pjowng)
    • (noun-like) raised-mound, raised-boundary
    • (verb-like) [to] raise mound, [to] earth up
    • (verb-like) [to] seal up
    • (noun-like) feoff
    • (verb-like) [to] enfeoff, [to] appoint
    • (adjective-like) great

豐 ꜀Fung (fung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾖ + 11
Unicode code point
U+8C50
Alternative forms
  • 豊
  • 丰 (Mainland simplified)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 豐) 大也[盛]也又豐熟
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 豊) 同[上]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 66 ꜀豊; here normalised to 豐)
    • A full goblet;
    • abundant, affluent, copious, exuberant (Kangxi 豐: 茂也盛也);
    • rich, fertile, prolific;
    • talented;
    • ꜀fung ꜁nín (fung1 nin4 豐年), a plenteous year;
    • ꜀fung shing꜅ (fung1 sing6 豐盛) flourishing, prosperous;
    • ꜀fung shuk꜇ (fung1 suk6 豐熟) ripe, fully grown;
    • ꜀fung hau꜅ ꜃hau (fung1 hau5 豐厚) generous (as a feast);
    • ꜀fung ꜀tang (fung1 dang1 豐登), large supplies (of corn).
  • (Page 715 ꜀豐)
    • Is the full form of 豊;
    • the latter (豊) is the least used.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Fixed missing dot in 盛, and corrected 土 to 上.
  • Williams' "A full goblet": This is a misreading of Kangxi 豆之豐滿者也. Here, 豐滿者 means "the full-sized", while 豆 is a ritual vessel much flatter than a typical goblet.
  • Williams' 豐厚: Normalised hau꜅ (hau6) to ꜃hau (hau5).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A type of ritual vessel (Kangxi: 一曰器名, 似豆而[卑]).
  2. Great (Kangxi: 大也).
  3. Ample (Kangxi: 多也, 猶厚也).
  4. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【豐】 (phjuwng)
    • (noun-like) flat-goblet
    • (adjective-like) plenteous, ample
    • (adjective-like) exuberant

鋒 ꜀Fung (fung1)

Radical + residual strokes

Alternative forms

  • 鏠
  • 锋 (Mainland simplified)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 鋒) 刀劍之芒
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 66 ꜀鋒)
    • The point of a weapon, a sharp pont (Kangxi: 兵耑也 etc.);
    • the vanguard (Kangxi: 軍之前列);
    • bristling;
    • turbulent;
    • to rise up (as spears appear);
    • ꜀káu ꜀fung (gaau1 fung1 交鋒), to attack engage in battle;
    • ꜀sín ꜀fung (sin1 fung1 先鋒), the van.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to rise up (as spears appear)": This is a reasonable rendering of Kangxi 《註》如鋒刃齊起而難犯, but that commentary applies to examples with the phrase 鋒起 rather than to 鋒 by itself.
  • Williams' 先鋒: Here, "van" is short for "vanguard".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The cutting edge of a weapon.
  2. A meteorological front.
  3. Pointed, sharp.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【鋒】 (phjowng)
    • (noun-like) [weapon]-point, (met.) vanguard, momentum
    • (adjective-like) pointed

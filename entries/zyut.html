啜 Chüt꜆ (zyut3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 8
Unicode code point
U+555C
Alternative forms
  • 啐 (for vernacular "suck" sense)
  • 嘬 (for vernacular "suck" sense)
  • 諁 (for "talkative" sense)
Reading variations
  • cyut3 (per 《廣韻》昌恱切 for "taste/sip" sense)
  • zyut3-1 (for vernacular "kiss" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 24 啜) 餔啜飮也嘗也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 42 啜꜆歠꜆)
    • To sob (Kangxi 啜: 泣貌), to talk incessantly (Kangxi 啜: 言多不止);
    • to taste (Kangxi 啜: 嘗也, 啜茹也, 啜者拾食), (…);
    • to kiss;
    • (…);
    • chüt꜆ ꜂há ꜃há (zyut3 haa5 啜吓), to sip;
    • chüt꜆ mín꜅ ꜀chü (zyut1 min6 zyu1 啜面珠), to kiss the cheeks.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 歠. See 歠 zyut3 for the canonical entry.)

  • (Page 609 啐꜆ Tsüt꜆; here normalised to 啜)
    • (…);
    • to suck, (…);
    • tsüt꜆ ꜀kon tseng꜅ (zyut3 gon1 zeng6 啜乾淨) sucked it dry.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams does not provide separate entries for 啜 (taste/sip) and 歠 (drink). Kangxi proscribes interchanging them in its entry for 歠. I have split things up accordingly.
  • Williams' 啜吓: Corrected ꜂há (haa2) to ꜃há (haa5).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To sip.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啜】 (zyut3)
    1. To suck.
      • 啜手指公 (zyut3 sau2 zi2 gung1): "Suck [the] thumb."
    2. To be stuck due to the friction caused by suction, tightness, or clinging.
      • 衫袖啜住 (saam1 zau6 zyut3 zyu6): "[The] shirt-sleeve [is] stuck."
  • 【啜】 (zyut3, zyut3-1)
    1. To kiss.
      • 啜我一啖 (zyut3-1 ngo5 jat1 daam6): "Kiss me once."

絀 Chüt꜆ (zyut3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽷ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7D40
Reading variations
  • fat1 (per 《正韻牋》曲勿切 for "bent/withdrawn" sense)
  • zeot1 (per 《廣韻》竹律切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 絀 Fat꜆ (fat1)) 嬴絀猶屈伸也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 41 絀꜆ Chut꜆ (zeot1); here normalised to Chüt꜆ (zyut3))
    • Crimson (Kangxi: 絳也) silk;
    • to sew (Kangxi: 紩也);
    • to baste or sew badly;
    • ꜁ying chut꜆ chüt꜆ (jing4 zyut3 嬴絀) to retire and come forth (Kangxi: 猶言屈伸也); unequal;
    • (…).
  • (Page 713 絀꜆ Chut꜆ (zeot1); here normalised to Chüt꜆ (zyut3))
    • This word also means obstructed, bent;
    • withdrawn, as money from circulation;
    • ꜁tsʻoi yung꜅ ꜀chí chut꜆ chüt꜆ (coi4 jung6 zi1 zyut3 財用之絀) obstructions in the way of trade insufficiency of funds.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to baste or sew badly": This is a misreading of Kangxi […]故作秫絀耳此蓋言其女工箴縷之麤拙也. Here, it is 秫 that supplies the meaning of "poor". The character 絀 simply means "sew" or "stitch".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Insufficient.

蕝 Tsüt꜆ (zyut3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 12
Unicode code point
U+855D
Han unification vigilance
May appear as either ⿱艹絕 (assumed here) or ⿱艹絶, depending on the font.
Alternative forms
  • 蕞
Reading variations
  • cyut3 (per Williams)
  • zyut6 (common)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 610 ？蕝꜆蕞꜆ Tsʻüt꜆ (cyut3); here normalised to Tsüt꜆ (zyut3))
    • A bundle or sheaf of coarse sedge used for straining or pouring spirits through (Kangxi 蕝: 蕝謂束茅而立之所以[縮]酒, 通蕞).

    (…)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' reading of tsʻüt꜆ (cyut3) takes after the Fan Wan reading for 蕞 in the unrelated sense of "small". See 蕞 cyut3.
  • Williams' "used for straining or pouring spirits through": This is a faithful rendering of Kangxi 所以[縮]酒. However, Kangxi does not give the sense from 《說文》, supplemented below.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Bundled thatch used to indicate the place of attendees in a meeting (per 《說文》朝會束茅表位曰蕝).

絕 Tsüt꜇ (zyut6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽷ + 6
Unicode code point
U+7D55
Alternative forms
  • 絶
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 25 絶) [斷]絶[滅]絶又最也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 609 絕꜇)
    • To interrupt a connection with, to cut off a thread (Kangxi: 斷絲);
    • to interrupt;
    • to sunder, to break off (Kangxi: 斷也), to terminate;
    • to exterminate, to utterly destroy (Kangxi: 滅也);
    • to renounce, to abjure;
    • to cross water (Kangxi: 絕度也, 絕過也);
    • to overpass;
    • shooting athwart, like a meteor;
    • a superlative absolutely, very, extremely, entirely, most;
    • a stanzas of four lines;
    • shát꜆ tsüt꜇ (saat3 zyut6 殺絕) to kill every one;
    • miú꜅ tsüt꜇ (miu6 zyut6 妙絕) superior to all, finest;
    • tsüt꜇ shik꜆ (zyut6 sik1 絕色) exceedingly beautiful;
    • tsüt꜇ ú꜅ (zyut6 wu6 絕戶) or tsüt꜇ hau꜅ (zyut6 hau6 絕後) leaving no heir, posterity cut off;
    • tsüt꜇ ꜁mò ꜂hò chʻü꜄ (zyut6 mou4 hou2 cyu3 絕無好處) no good luck at all; by no means a good place;
    • tün꜅ tsüt꜇ (dyun6 zyut6 斷絕) to stop, as a ration;
    • tsüt꜇ lò꜅ (zyut6 lou6 絕路) the road is stopped; resources are all cut off;
    • ꜂kon tò꜄ tsüt꜇ ꜁lung ꜃ling ꜃leng (gon2 dou3 zyut6 lung4 leng5 趕到絕龍嶺), I am driven to the edge, reduced to the last extremity;
    • ꜃'ng tsüt꜇ (ng5 zyut6 五絕) a stanzas in pentameters;
    • má꜅ pat꜆ tsüt꜇ ꜂hau (maa6 bat1 zyut6 hau2 罵不絕口), he never ceases to rail;
    • ꜀tʻín ꜁mò tsüt꜇ ꜁yan ꜀chí lò꜅ (tin1 mou4 zyut6 jan4 zi1 lou6 天無絕人之路) heaven never intends to cut off men's resources man;
    • tsüt꜇ mái꜅ (zyut6 maai6 絕賣) a final sale, as land never to be redeemed.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Fixed missing strokes in 斷 and 滅.
  • Williams' 趕到絕龍嶺: Changed ꜃ling (ling5) to vernacular reading ꜃leng (leng5).
  • Williams' 天無絕人之路: Better rendered "Heaven [lays] no path that cuts off man".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Desperate, dire, extreme.
  2. Remote, isolated.
  3. To refuse.

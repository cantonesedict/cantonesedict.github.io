Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 1 ꜀呀)

    (…)

    Read ꜁ngá (ngaa4);

    • the wrangling of children;
    • to open the mouth wide;
    • ꜁ngá ꜀lán ꜃mai (ngaa4 laan1 mai5 呀囒米), cochineal.
Conway's commentary
  • It is tempting to declare that the "wrangling" and "wide-mouth" senses ought to be read ꜀há (haa1) per 《廣韻》許加切, but it is more complicated than that. The character 呀 also appears under 《廣韻》五加切 ꜁ngá (ngaa4), explained 吧呀. In turn, the character 吧 has two readings:
    • 普巴切 ꜀pʻá (paa1), explained 吧呀大口皃 (whence big-mouth); and
    • 伯加切 ꜀pá (baa1), explained 吧呀小兒忿爭 (whence children wrangling).

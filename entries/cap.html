Tsʻap. (cap)

  • cap1
Williams (1856) headings
  • Tsʻap. (Page 557)
Williams (1856) pronunciation
  • Tsʻap = [tsʰɐp̚]
Modern pronunciation
  • cap = [tsʰɐp̚] (preferred), [tɕʰɐp̚]

戢 Tsʻap꜆ (cap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼽ + 9
Unicode code point
U+6222
Reading variations
  • zap1 (per 《廣韻》阻立切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 戢) 安集也止也歛也藏也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 557 戢꜆)
    • To collect in an arsenal, to store up weapons (Kangxi: 藏兵也);
    • to gather (Kangxi: 斂也);
    • to desist from (Kangxi: 止也), to lay aside, as arms;
    • to fold, as wings;
    • ꜁tsʻai tsʻap꜆ tsʻap꜆ (cai4 cap1 cap1 齊戢戢) to cut off evenly;
    • ꜂chʻái tsʻap꜆ tò꜄ shat꜇ (caai2 cap1 dou3 sat6 踹戢到實) tread it, press it down very solid;
    • tsoi[꜄] tsʻap꜆ ꜀kon ꜀kwo (zoi3 cap1 gon1 gwo1 載戢干戈), to lay by shields and spears.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 載戢干戈: Corrected ꜂tsoi (zoi2 上聲) to tsoi꜄ (zoi3 去聲).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【齊戢戢】 (cai4 cap1 cap1):
    1. Neat and even (not jagged).

濈 Tsʻap꜆ (cap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 13
Unicode code point
U+6FC8
Reading variations
  • zap1 (per 《廣韻》阻立切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 濈) 口舌聲
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 557 濈꜆)
    • Water rapidly flowing out (Kangxi: 濈水行出也);
    • rapid (Kangxi: 疾貌);
    • cordial, harmonious, agreeing (Kangxi: 和也);
    • to accord with.

緝 Tsʻap꜆ (cap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽷ + 9
Unicode code point
U+7DDD
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 緝) 繼續也續也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 557 緝꜆)
    • To twist a cord (Kangxi: 績也);
    • to join, to continue (Kangxi: 續也);
    • to come after, to follow, to succeed;
    • to pursue, to follow after, to be on the search for;
    • to apprehend;
    • tsʻap꜆ wok꜇ (cap1 wok6 緝獲) to pursue and seize;
    • tsʻap꜆ ꜀sz' (cap1 si1 緝私) to watch smugglers;
    • tsʻap꜆ ꜀sz' ꜁shün (cap1 si1 syun4 緝私船), revenue cutters;
    • tsʻap꜆ chéuk꜇ (cap1 zoek6 緝着) caught him;
    • ꜁chʻá tsʻap꜆ (caa4 cap1 查緝) on the search;
    • ꜁tsʻun tsʻap꜆ (ceon4 cap1 巡緝) to get on the trail.

葺 Tsʻap꜆ (cap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 9
Unicode code point
U+847A
Reading variations
  • zap1 (per 《廣韻》子入切 for "spinous-grass" sense; per Kangxi 子入切 for "piled" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 葺) 修補也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 558 葺꜆)
    • To put in order, to repair (Kangxi: 修補也);
    • to cover, to thatch (Kangxi: 覆也, 苫也);
    • a spinous grass (Kangxi: 茨也);
    • ꜀sau tsʻap꜆ (sau1 cap1 修葺) to refit, as a house;
    • tsʻap꜆ ꜂pò (cap1 bou2 葺補), to repair.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Piled, stacked (Kangxi: 累也).

輯 Tsʻap꜆ (cap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾞ + 9
Unicode code point
U+8F2F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 輯) 斂也聚也集也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 558 輯꜆)
    • To collect the materials to form a chariot, a car well arranged (Kangxi: 車和輯也 etc.);
    • union, concord, agreement (Kangxi: 和也, 睦也);
    • to make every thing agreeable, put in its proper place;
    • to compile;
    • to act gently and cordially (Kangxi: 顏色和柔亦曰輯);
    • to collect, to congregate (Kangxi: 斂也);
    • ꜁wo tsʻap꜆ (wo4 cap1 和輯) in good accord, to pacify;
    • ꜁ning tsʻap꜆ (ning4 cap1 寧輯) peaceful;
    • ꜀sau tsʻap꜆ ꜁lun ꜀pong (sau1 cap1 leon4 bong1 修輯鄰邦), to induce the neighboring states to be at peace.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Classifier for parts, volumes, etc. of published works.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 53 捹꜅; here normalised to 揈)
    • To swing;
    • to shake in the wind;
    • ꜁tʻiú ꜁tʻiú ꜁tiú ꜂tiú fing꜅ (diu3-4 diu3-2 fing6 吊吊揈) to hang and swing;
    • fing꜅ tik꜆ ꜂shui ꜀lai ꜁lai (fing6 dik1 seoi2 lai4 揈滴水嚟), sprinkle a little water;
    • fing꜅ lat꜆ (fing6 lat1 揈甩) shake it off.
  • (Page 714 Fing꜅ (fing6); here assigned to 揈)

    A colloquial word.

    • To vibrate, to swing, to dangle, as the end of a rope;
    • to sprinkle;
    • to snap away;
    • to shake off, as ink out of a pen;
    • fing꜅ kwo꜄ ꜁lai (fing6 gwo3 lai4 揈過嚟), yerk it (as the cue) forward.
  • (Page 714 after Fing꜅ (fing6))
    • The character 捹, has no authority;
    • it is read pan꜄ (ban3);
    • the hands rapidly moving.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 吊吊揈: Corrected ꜁tʻiú ꜁tʻiú (tiu4 tiu4) to ꜁tiú ꜂tiú (diu3-4 diu3-2).
  • Williams' 揈滴水嚟: Corrected ꜀lai (lai1) to ꜁lai (lai4).
  • Williams' "read pan꜄": This and the subsequent explanation are misreadings of 《集韻》蒲悶切音坌手亂貌 in Kangxi. See 捹 ban6. (TODO)
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【吊吊揈】 (diu3-4 diu3-2 fing6)
    1. Hanging and swinging about.
  • 【揈】 (fing6, fang6)
    1. To swing, to flap, to shake.
      • 揈身揈勢 (fing6 san1 fing6 sai3): "Swinging [the] body to and fro" (i.e. not staying still).
      • 揈手揈腳 (fing6 sau2 fing6 goek3): "Flapping about [the] hands [and] feet."
      • 揈甩啲塵 (fing6 lat1 di1 can4): "Flap off [the] dust."
      • 揈乾隻手 (fing6 gon1 zek3 sau2): "Shake [the] hand dry."
    2. To whirl.
      • 揈乾 (fing6 gon1): "Spin dry."
    3. To spend recklessly.
      • 揈錢 (fang6 cin4-2): "Flinging money [around]."
  • 【揈頭丸】 (fing6 tau4 jyun4-2)
    1. Ecstasy, the tablet form of MDMA. (Literally, "head-whirling pills".)

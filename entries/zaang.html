㬹 ꜀Cháng (zaang1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾁ + 8
Unicode code point
U+3B39
Alternative forms
  • 踭
Reading variations
  • zang1 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 29 踭 ꜀Chang (zang1)) 足跟筋也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜀㬹)
    • The tendon Achilles;
    • the heel;
    • the elbow;
    • yat꜆ ꜀cháng ꜂tá kwo꜄ hü꜄ (jat1 zaang1 daa2 gwo3 heoi3 一㬹打過去) elbowed him off;
    • ꜂cham ꜀cháng (zam2 zaang1 枕㬹), to lean on the elbow;
    • tʻát꜆ ꜀cháng ꜁hái (taat3 zaang1 haai4 撻㬹鞋), slipshod;
    • ꜁hái ꜀cháng (haai4 zaang1 鞋㬹), heel of the shoe;
    • ꜂shau ꜀cháng (sau2 zaang1 手㬹), the elbow.
Conway's commentary
  • The uncommon reading zang1 is that given in Fan Wan for the form 踭.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【㬹】 (zaang1):
    1. The heel.
      • 腳㬹 (goek3 zaang1): "Heel of the foot."
      • 鞋㬹 (haai4 zaang1): "Heel of [a] shoe."
    2. The elbow.
      • 手㬹 (sau2 zaang1): "The elbow."

  • 掙

掙 Cháng꜅ (zaang6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 8
Unicode code point
U+6399
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 掙꜅)

    A colloquial word.

    • To caulk;
    • to wedge in, to squeeze in, to force in;
    • tá cháng꜅ (daa2 zaang6 打掙) to caulk seams;
    • cháng꜅ ꜁ngá (zaang6 ngaa4 掙牙), to get between the teeth;
    • cháng꜅ ꜀shá ꜀kai (zaang6 saa1 gai1 掙沙雞), to stuff a cock with sand;
    • ngáng꜅ cháng[꜅] (ngaang6 zaan6 硬掙) determined, energetic, as in bearing pain.

    Read ꜀chʻáng (caang1); (see 掙 caang1).

    Read chang꜄ (zang3); (see 掙 zang3).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【掙】 (zaang6):
    1. To wedge in or stuff in, especially for the purposes of sealing off or widening.
      • 掙住 (zaang6 zyu6): "[It's] wedged in [and stuck]."
      • 掙闊啲 (zaang6 fut3 di1): "Wedge [the gap and make it] wider."
      • 掙爆咗 (zaang6 baau3 zo2): "Burst [from] stuffing."

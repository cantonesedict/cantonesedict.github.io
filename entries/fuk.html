幅 Fuk꜆ (fuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼱ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5E45
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 64 幅) 布之一片曰一幅又[邊]幅
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 63 幅꜆)
    • A wide strip The width of cloth (Kangxi: 布帛廣也 etc.);
    • a roll of paper or cloth;
    • a selvedge or hem;
    • a border, a frontier;
    • a classifier of maps, pictures, rolls, flags, walls, and cloth;
    • (…);
    • ꜀pín fuk꜆ (bin1 fuk1 邊幅) a frontier; a hem;
    • sz'꜄ fuk꜆ ꜁tʻau (sei3 fuk1 tau4-2 四幅頭), a set of four scrolls hung in halls.

    (Otherwise, a reading variation. See 幅 bik1 for the canonical entry.) (TODO)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected malformed 邊.
  • Williams' 邊幅: Metaphorically, one's appearance.
  • In modern usage, used in the sense of "extent" or "amplitude", as in 大幅, 升幅, 振幅, etc.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【幅】 (pjuwk)
    • (noun-like) cloth-width, width
    • (noun-like) hem
    • (verb-like) (met.) [to] limit

福 Fuk꜆ (fuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽰ + 9
Unicode code point
U+798F
Reading variations
  • fau3 (per 《集韻》敷救切 for "store" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 64 福) 祐也德也休也善也祥也百善之名也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 63 福꜆)
    • Happiness, good fortune, lucky, felicity (Kangxi: 祐也休也善也祥也);
    • to bless;
    • a blessing, divine prosperity;
    • good;
    • provided with all things (Kangxi: 福者備也);
    • sacrificial meats (Kangxi: 祭祀胙肉曰福);
    • to store up (Kangxi: 藏也);
    • with;
    • náp꜇ fuk꜆ (naap6 fuk1 納福) are you well?
    • ꜃'ng fuk꜆ (ng5 fuk1 五福) the five blessings (longevity, riches, wealth, virtuous honor, posterity, and a natural death);
    • fuk꜆ shín꜅ (fuk1 sin6 福善) blessed, to bless the good;
    • fuk꜆ ꜁tʻín (fuk1 tin4 福田), ancestors' fortune;
    • fuk꜆ tí꜅ (fuk1 dei6 福地) a lucky grave-spot;
    • fuk꜆ ꜁shan (fuk1 san4 福神), lares rustici, street gods;
    • mán꜅ fuk꜆ (maan6 fuk1 萬福) your Honor (used by women);
    • fuk꜆ hí꜄ (fuk1 hei3 福氣) fleshy;
    • fát꜆ fuk꜆ (faat3 fuk1 發福) to get fat;
    • ꜀tʻím fuk꜆ ꜀tʻím shau꜅ (tim1 fuk1 tim1 sau6 添福添壽) may you be blessed and long-lived;
    • yat꜆ lò꜅ fuk꜆ ꜀sing (jat1 lou6 fuk1 sing1 一路福星), let a lucky star [light] your journey.
  • (Page 715 福꜆)
    • For "wealth, virtuous" in the list of five blessings, read "honor, posterity;"
    • there is some discrepancy respecting the particular blessings included in the five.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 福地: Not specific to graves. Better rendered "blessed land", e.g. in 心田先祖種、福地後人耕.
  • Williams' 萬福: Specifically, said by women when performing the Chinese equivalent of a curtsey.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【福】 (pjuwk)
    • (noun-like) blessing, happiness

覆 Fuk꜆ (fuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾑ + 12
Unicode code point
U+8986
Alternative forms
  • 復
Reading variations
  • fau3 (per 《廣韻》敷救切 for "overspread" sense)
  • fau6 (per 《廣韻》扶富切 for "waylay" sense and Fan Wan for "overspread" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 56 覆 Fau꜅ (fau6)) 天覆地載葢也
  • (Vol. 1 Image 64 覆) 反覆
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 52 覆꜅ Fau꜅ (fau6); here normalised to Fuk꜆ (fuk1))
    • To cover, to overshadow (Kangxi: 蓋也);
    • to brood, as a hen;
    • to overspread;
    • ꜀tʻín fau꜅ fuk꜆ tí꜅ tsoi꜄ (tin1 fuk1 dei6 zoi3 天覆地載) the heaven covers and earth contains carries;
    • ꜀kai ꜂ná fau꜅ fuk꜆ ꜂tsai (gai1 naa2 fuk1 zai2 雞乸覆仔), the hen covers her chickens.
  • (Page 63 覆꜆)
    • To and fro, to repeat, back and forth (Kangxi: 反覆也);
    • unstable, unsettled;
    • to subvert, to overthrow (Kangxi: 倒也);
    • to defeat (Kangxi: 覆軍敗也);
    • to throw down, to upset, to prostrate;
    • to judge (Kangxi: 審也, 詳察曰覆);
    • ꜀kʻing fuk꜆ (king1 fuk1 傾覆) defeated, tumbled down, ruined;
    • ꜀tín fuk꜆ (din1 fuk1 顛覆) reduced to poverty, reprobate;
    • ꜃má ꜁tʻsín ꜁tsʻín fuk꜆ ꜂shui (maa5 cin4 fuk1 seoi2 馬前覆水), impossible;
    • fuk꜆ pák꜇ (fuk1 baak6 覆白) to understand clearly.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to judge": Better rendered "to examine" or "to review".
  • Williams' 顛覆: Or, to subvert, to overthrow.
  • Williams' 馬前覆水: Less common than 覆水難收.
  • Williams' 覆白: I am not convinced that this means "to understand clearly".
  • 《廣韻》 has four headline readings:
    1. 敷救切 fau꜄ (fau3) for the "overspread" sense;
    2. 扶富切 fau꜅ (fau6) for the "waylay" sense;
    3. 芳福切 fuk꜆ (fuk1) for the "overturn" sense; and
    4. 匹北切 pʻak꜆ (pak1) without explanation.
    The explanations for the first and third readings (i.e. fau꜄ (fau3) and fuk꜆ (fuk1)) refer to each other. It is unclear whether these are intended as mere cross-references or as genuine alternatives for those two senses.
  • Fan Wan fau꜅ (fau6) for the "overspread" sense is not strictly correct, and ought to be fau꜄ (fau3) if one insists on the departing tone.
  • According to 段玉裁's annotations of 《說文》, the "overspread" and "overturn" senses have the same olden reading 芳福切 fuk꜆ (fuk1), and distinguishing 敷救切 fau꜄ (fau3) for the "overspread" sense is a southern reading (南音).
  • The sense of "reply" (see below) appears to be modern. For the classical word meaning "reply", see 復 fuk6.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To waylay (Kangxi: 伏兵曰覆).
  2. To reply.
  3. Conversely.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【覆】 (bjuwH)
    • (verb-like) [to] waylay
  • 【覆】 (phjuwH)
    • (verb-like) [to] overspread
  • 【覆】 (phjuwk)
    • (verb-like) [to] overturn, [to] overthrow
    • (adverb-like) conversely
    • Used in 反覆.
  • 【反覆】 (pjonX phjuwk)
    • (adverb-like) back [and] over, (met.) over [and] over, back [and] forth

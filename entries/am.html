꜀Òm. (am1 陰平)

啽 ꜀òm (am1)

Unicode code point
(U+557D)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 724)
  • To snore;
  • ꜀òm mak꜇ (am1 mak6 啽默) to be silent; to give no answer, as one [who] is very sleepy.

꜂Am. ꜂Òm. (am2 陰上)

唵 ꜂am (am2)

Unicode code point
(U+5535)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3)
  • To feed with the hands;
  • to hold in the mouth;
  • a word used by Budhists.
Conway's commentary
  • "[A] word used by Budhists": A transliteration of the Sanskrit ॐ (Om).

揞 ꜂òm (am2)

Unicode code point
(U+63DE)
Alternative forms
掩
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3, appearing as 掩 ꜂am)
A colloquial word.
  • To cover with the hand;
  • to conceal from;
  • ꜂am ꜁mái ꜀shéung ꜃ngán, () to cover both eyes;
  • ꜂am chü꜅ () hide it;
  • ꜂am tik꜆ púi꜅ ꜂shau, () give him a little sop, cover his palm.
(Page 341)
  • To cover, to hide, to screen, by putting something on;
  • to, extinguish, to suppress;
  • ꜂òm chü꜅ ꜂hau (), to put the hand over the mouth;
  • ꜀ché ꜂òm pat꜆ chü꜅ () can not be hid;
  • ꜂òm chü꜅ ꜁tʻau hok꜆ () to lean the head on the hand;
  • ꜂pí ꜂shau ꜂òm chü꜅ () cover it with your hand;
  • ꜂òm mat꜇ ꜁lo ꜂kú (), "muffle the gong," i. e. keep it quiet;
  • ꜂òm ꜀fung ꜁kʻam (), to play a piano;
  • ꜂òm mit꜇ () to put out, as fire; to quash;
  • ꜂òm pák꜆ ꜁mai ꜁mung (), blindman's buff;
  • ꜂òm chü꜅ ꜃kʻü ꜀shéung ꜃ngán (), blindfold his eyes;
  • ꜂òm mak꜇ () to feel the pulse;
  • ꜂òm púi꜅ ꜂shau () to give hush-money, to bribe.
  • The first character (掩) is read ꜂ím (jim2), but also used with this meaning in the pʻing shing (平聲) (jim1).

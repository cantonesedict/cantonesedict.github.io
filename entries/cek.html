Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15* before 尺꜇)

    (The next character (尺) is usually pronounced chʻek (cek).)

  • (Page 15* 尺꜇ Chʻik꜇ (cik6); Chʻek꜇ (cek6) implied by variational note; here corrected to Chʻek꜆ (cek3))
    • A cubit, or 10 tsʻün꜄ (Kangxi: 十寸也);
    • the Chinese foot;
    • chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ tsʻün꜄ (cek3 cyun3 尺寸) regular, proportioned;
    • ꜂kí ꜀to chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ tsʻün꜄ (gei2 do1 cek3 cyun3 幾多尺寸) what are the dimensions?
    • ꜁'m kau꜄ chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ tsʻün꜄ (m4 gau3 cek3 cyun3 唔夠尺寸) not of full stature;
    • ꜀sám chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ fát꜆ (saam1 cek3 faat3 三尺法) laws of the land, prohibitions;
    • ꜁pʻái ꜁tsʻín chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ (paai4 cin4 cek3 排錢尺) the full length cubit;
    • kán꜄ chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ (gaan3 cek3-2 間尺) a ruler;
    • ꜁léung ꜀tʻín chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ (loeng4 tin1 cek3 量天尺) a quadrant;
    • chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ wok꜆ wok꜇ (cek3 wok6 尺蠖) a geometrical worm;
    • chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ yat꜆ ꜀chí ꜀shü (cek3 jat1 zi1 syu1 尺一之書), an epistle, a note;
    • chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ tò꜅ (cek3 dou6 尺度) to measure, measurable;
    • luk꜇ chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ ꜀chí ꜀kú (luk6 cek3 zi1 gu1 六尺之孤), a minor reigning;
    • ꜀sám chʻek꜇ chʻek꜆ pák꜇ (saam1 cek3 baak6 三尺帛) a halter, a short rope.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams has been misled by Fan Wan's placement of 尺 (chʻik) in its own series after 劇屐 (kʻik꜇) and 溺 (nik꜇), which are 陽入 (see Vol. 1 Image 78). Based on this ordering, Williams has inferred that the character 尺 is read 陽入 chʻik꜇ (cik6). However, since Fan Wan has a separate series for 勅勑敕斥叱螫𪅙赤 (chʻik꜆), it appears that its authors have attempted to distinguish the vernacular rime -ek from the literary rime -ik, notwithstanding all series appear under 第七英影應益. I have corrected Williams by placing 尺 under chʻek꜆ (cek3) here.
  • Williams' 尺蠖: Changed wok꜆ (wok3) to common reading wok꜇ (wok6).
  • Williams' 尺一之書: From the length of 1.1 chʻek for the writing-boards used for imperial decrees.
  • Williams' 尺度: Not the verb "to measure", but the noun "measure", as 度 is in departing tone.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【尺】 (tsyhek)
    • (noun-like) ruler, rule

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 571 𤷫꜆ Tsʻik꜆ (cik3); Tsʻek꜆ (cek3) exemplified)
    • A fullness in the stomach;
    • a pain from laughing;
    • rheumatic twinges, pain in the bones;
    • siú꜄ tò꜄ ꜃tʻò tsʻek꜆ (siu3 dou3 tou5 cek3 笑到肚𤷫) laughed till he ached;
    • kwat꜆ tsʻek꜆ (gwat1 cek3 骨𤷫) shooting pains;
    • ꜀sam tsʻek꜆ (sam1 cek3 心𤷫) sorry for, deeply pained for;
    • ꜁tʻau tsʻek꜆ (tau4 cek3 頭𤷫) nervous headache.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【𤷫】 (cek3)
    1. Pain or aching.

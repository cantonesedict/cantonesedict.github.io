Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜀罃 ꜀Ang (ang1); here normalised to 罌)
    • An earthen jar, with a narrow neck, to hold oil, &c.;
    • ꜀ang ong꜄ ꜂tsai (ang1 ong3 zai2 罌盎仔), a tadpole.
  • (Page 4 ꜀甖꜀罌)

    Used with the last but four (罃 ang1).

    • a vase, a jar, a gallipot, an earthen pitcher;
    • ꜁tʻong ꜂kwo ꜀áng (tong4 gwo2 aang1 糖果罌), a jar of sweetmeats;
    • ꜁yau ꜀áng (jau4 aang1 油罌), an oil can;
    • yat꜆ ꜀áng ꜃yé (jat1 aang1 je5 一罌嘢), a thing;
    • ꜂shui ꜀áng (seoi2 aang1 水罌), a water pitcher.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【罌】 (aang1):
    1. A jar or cylindrical-ish container.
      • 罌鹽 (aang1 jim4): "[The] jar of salt."
      • 買多罌 (maai5 do1 aang1): "Buy [one] more jar."

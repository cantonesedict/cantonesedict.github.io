即 Tsik꜆ (zik1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼙ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5373
Alternative forms

Reading variations
  • ze1 (lazy in 即係)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 74 卽) 就也山也則也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 568 卽꜆; here normalised to 即)
    • Eating, about to eat;
    • an adverb, now, immediately, soon, presently, when, then, forthwith;
    • now;
    • near;
    • if, perhaps;
    • this, that is, or, alias;
    • the snuff of a candle;
    • tsik꜆ tsik꜆ (zik1 zik1 即即) an abundance, full of;
    • tsik꜆ hak꜆ (zik1 hak1 即刻) instantly;
    • tsik꜆ yat꜇ (zik1 jat6 即日) the same day;
    • tsik꜆ hai꜅ (zik1 hai6 即係) just that, it is so;
    • tsik꜆ ꜀kam (zik1 gam1 即今), just now;
    • tsik꜆ [꜂sz'] ꜀kán (zik1 si2 gaan1 即使間), supposing it to be;
    • ꜁nín ꜃mán tsoi꜅ tsik꜆ (nin4 maan5 zoi6 zik1 年晚在即) the end of the year is near;
    • tsik꜆ tsik꜆ ꜀fán ꜁[lai] (zik1 zik1 faan1 loi4 即即返嚟), come back immediately;
    • tsik꜆ ꜁shí ꜂tang ꜂kan (zik1 si4 dang2 gan2 即時等緊), it is wanted very soon;
    • tsik꜆ tsik꜆ ín꜅ (zik1 zik1 jin6 即即現) money on the nail.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 即使間: Corrected ꜁shí (si4) to ꜂sz' (si2).
  • Williams' 即即返嚟: Corrected ꜁loi (loi4) to ꜁lai (lai4).

    • The noise of a crowd;

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【唧】 (zik1):
    1. A jack (for raising heavy objects, especially vehicles).
      • 攞個唧嚟 (lo2 go3 zik1 lai4): "Bring [me the] jack."
    2. To raise with a jack.
      • 唧起架車 (zik1 hei2 gaa3 ce1): "Jack up [the] car."
    3. A jack (playing card).
      • 葵扇唧 (kwai4 sin3 zik1): "Jack of spades."
      • 唧夫佬 (zik1 fu1 lou2): "Jack full [house]."

    • ꜀sai tsik꜇ (sai1 zik6 西席) a teacher;
    • ꜂í tsik꜇ (ji2 zik6 倚席) to lean on the table;

    • ꜁mái tsik꜇ (maai4 zik6 埋席) to sit at table;
    • tsik꜇ tí꜅ ꜁í tso꜅ (zik6 dei6 ji4 zo6 席地而坐) to spread a feast and sit down;

    (Alternative form of 蓆 (for the "mat" sense). See 蓆 zik6.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams has mixed up 席 and 蓆. Which is easy to do, as 席 is interchanged with 蓆 for the "mat" sense.
  • Williams' "affection for": Whilst the phrase 席寵 exists, the meaning of 席 therein is still "depend" or "rely".
  • Williams' 埋席: Strictly, to draw near and take one's seat at the table.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "used for the three next": I have not seen 直 used for 殖 or 植.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【直程】 (zik6 cing4):
    1. Directly.
      • 直程返去 (zik6 cing4 faan1 heoi3): "Directly go back."
    2. Simply, truly, clearly, especially used exaggeratively.
      • 直程冇得頂 (zik6 cing4 mou5 dak1 ding2): "Straight up can't be beat."
      • 直程係啦 (zik6 cing4 hai6 laa1): "[It] clearly is!"
    3. Used to imply decisiveness or extremeness of an action.
      • 我直程報警㗎 (ngo5 zik6 cing4 bou3 ging2 gaa3): "I [won't hesitate to] just call [the] police!"
      • 直程走咗 (zik6 cing4 zau2 zo2): "[He] simply left."
  • 【直頭】 (zik6 tau4):
    1. Synonym of 直程.

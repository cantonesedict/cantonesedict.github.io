招 ꜀Chiú (ziu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 5
Unicode code point
U+62DB
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 2 招) 手呼也以言曰召以手曰招
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 24* ꜀招)
    • To beckon, to motion to with the hand, to hail (Kangxi: 手呼也 etc.);
    • to let people know;
    • to invite, to induce (Kangxi: 來之也);
    • to raise, as troops;
    • to confess;
    • self-crimination;
    • to excite, to entangle;
    • a handbill, a sign-board;
    • a wave of the hand, a signal;
    • ꜀chiú ꜀fú (ziu1 fu1 招呼), to call and beckon;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁'m ꜀chiú (ziu1 m4 ziu1 招唔招), do you confess?
    • ꜀chiú tʻíp꜆ (ziu1 tip3 招貼) a handbill, a placard;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁pʻái (ziu1 paai4 招牌), a sign-board;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁long yap꜇ shé꜄ (ziu1 long4 jap6 se3 招郎入舍) to bring a son-in-law to one's house;
    • ꜀chiú shò꜄ (ziu1 sou3 招數) to own to the account, to become responsible for;
    • ꜀chiú ꜃yé hoi꜅ sz'꜅ (ziu1 je5 hoi6 si6 招惹害事) to involve one's business;
    • ꜀chiú ꜀ping (ziu1 bing1 招兵), to enlist soldiers;
    • ꜀chiú ying꜅ (ziu1 jing6 招認) or ꜀chiú tsúi꜅ (ziu1 zeoi6 招罪) to confess guilt;
    • ꜀chiú ká꜄ (ziu1 gaa3 招架) to ward off;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁tsʻoi ꜁tʻung ꜂tsz' (ziu1 coi4 tung4 zi2 招財童子) the god of wealth, worshiped at shop doors.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To seek (Kangxi: 求也).
  2. To recruit.
  3. To tie, to restrain (Kangxi: 𦊰也, 招羈其足也).
  4. An archery target.
  5. (martial arts) A move, a strike, a technique (or instance thereof).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【招績】 (ziu1 zik1)
    1. Conceited, arrogant.

朝 ꜀Chiú (ziu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽉ + 8
Unicode code point
U+671D
Alternative forms
  • 晁
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 2 朝) [旦]也早也又朝鮮國名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 23* ꜀朝꜀晁)
    • Dawn, morning (Kangxi: 旦也, 朝早也);
    • early;
    • ꜀chiú ꜃mán (ziu1 maan5 朝晚), morning and evening, early and late;
    • yat꜆ ꜀chiú (jat1 ziu1 一朝), in a morning, quickly, in a trice;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁tʻau ꜂tsò (ziu1 tau4 zou2 朝頭早), early in morning;
    • ꜀chiú fán꜅ (ziu1 faan6 朝飯) breakfast;
    • ꜀chiú ꜁shan (ziu1 san4 朝晨), morning time;
    • ꜀chiú ꜀chiú (ziu1 ziu1 朝朝) every morning;
    • ꜀Chiú ꜀sin ꜀sín kwok꜆ (ziu1 sin1 gwok3 朝鮮國) Corea (Kangxi: 朝鮮國名);
    • yung꜅ ꜀chiú mí꜅ (jung6 ziu1 mei6 用朝未) have you breakfasted?
    • ꜃yau ꜀chiú ꜁mò ꜃mán (jau5 ziu1 mou4 maan5 有朝無晚), there is a morning but no evening, the day is very short;
    • tái꜅ pún꜄ ꜀chiú ꜁loi (daai6 bun3 ziu1 loi4 大半朝來), come before breakfast.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 且 to 旦.
  • Williams' 朝鮮國: Opinion is divided on whether 朝 in 朝鮮 should be read ꜀chiú (ziu1) or ꜁chʻiú (ciu4).
    • ꜀chiú (ziu1) is recorded in the dictionaries:
      《廣韻》陟遥切：朝：早也[…]又朝鮮國名[…]
      《康熙字典》朝：古文晁《唐韻》《廣韻》《集韻》《類篇》《韻會》𠀤陟遙切音昭[…]又朝鮮國名[…]
    • ꜁chʻiú (ciu4) is recorded in 《史記索隱》, specifically as a note under 朝鮮列傳第五十五:
      案朝音潮直驕反鮮音仙以有汕水故名也汕一音訕
    Corrected ꜀sin to ꜀sín.
  • Williams' 朝飯, 朝晨, 用朝未: Dated.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【朝頭早】 (ziu1 tau4 zou2)
    1. The morning.
      • 朝頭早沖涼 (ziu1 tau4 zou2 cung1 loeng4): "[I] shower [in] the morning."
  • 【朝早】 (ziu1 zou2)
    1. Morning.
      • 聽朝早 (ting1 ziu1 zou2): "Tomorrow morning."
      • 琴日朝早 (kam4 jat6 ziu1 zou2): "Yesterday morning".
      • 朝早八點 (ziu1 zou2 baat3 dim2): "Eight o'clock [in the] morning."

焦 ꜀Tsiú (ziu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 8
Unicode code point
U+7126
Reading variations
  • zeu1 (vernacular for "crust" and "scab" senses)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 3 焦) 火燒黑也又勞心曰焦
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 580 ꜀焦)
    • Scorched, burned (Kangxi: 火所傷也);
    • blackened, charred (Kangxi: 火燒黑也);
    • smell of burning (Kangxi: 火之臭味也);
    • vexed, harassed, anxious (Kangxi: 凡苦焦者皆屬焉);
    • ꜀tsiú ꜀pá ꜀kon (ziu1 baa1 gon1 焦疤乾), dry, at the last extremity, starving;
    • fán꜅ ꜀tsiú (faan6 ziu1 飯焦), crust left after boiling rice;
    • ꜀tsiú ꜁tʻau lán꜅ ngák꜇ (ziu1 tau4 laan6 ngaak6 焦頭爛額) head burned and scorched, as by powder;
    • ꜀sam ꜀tsiú (sam1 ziu1 心焦), in great distress.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 膲. See 膲 ziu1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【焦】 (zeu1, ziu1)
    1. The crust of scorched rice.
      • 飯焦 (faan6 ziu1): "Rice crust."
    2. A scab on the skin.
      • 起咗焦 (hei2 zo2 ziu1): "Formed [a] scab."
      • 塊焦好痕 (faai3 zeu1 hou2 han4): "[The] scab's really itchy."

    • To sacrifice to ancestors and spirits (Kangxi: 醮祭也);
    • a sacrifice, a requiem (Kangxi: 凡僧道設壇祈禱曰醮);
    • to give a cup of wine to a son at his wedding (Kangxi: 父親醮子 etc.);
    • finished, used up (Kangxi: 盡也);
    • to complete exhaust;
    • to burn moxa;
    • ꜂tá tsiú꜄ (daa2 ziu3 打醮) or kín꜄ tsiú꜄ (gin3 ziu3 建醮) to celebrate the all-souls' festival;
    • ꜁wán ꜀tʻín tsiú꜄ (waan4 tin1 ziu3 還天醮) a thanksgiving service for heaven's mercy;
    • tsoi꜄ tsiú꜄ (zoi3 ziu3 再醮) to remarry, said mostly of widows;
    • ꜂tá ꜂fo ꜀sing tsiú꜄ (daa2 fo2 sing1 ziu3 打火星醮) the autumnal festival to the God of Fire.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "wine to a son at his wedding": Also at the capping ceremony where a twenty-year-old son is given his style-name. See below.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The giving of a cup of wine from father to son at a capping or wedding cermony (Kangxi: 冠娶禮祭), where the son does not return a cup to the father (Kangxi: 酌而無酬酢曰醮).
  2. To marry (of a woman), especially to remarry.

趙 Chiú꜅ (ziu6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾛ + 7
Unicode code point
U+8D99
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 7 趙) 趨也久也利也又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 24* 趙꜅)
    • To hasten to (Kangxi: 趨趙也);
    • to announce to a suzerain (Kangxi: 趙朝也本小邑朝事于大國也);
    • a long time;
    • sharp, acute;
    • a few;
    • a feudal state (Kangxi: 國名) in the south of Shánsí (山西) and Chihlí (直隸), its capital was the present Cháuching (趙城) in Shánsí (山西).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a long time", "a few": Per 久也 and 少也 from 《廣韻》. I am not aware of any texts that use these senses.
  • Williams' "sharp": This is perhaps a rendering of Fan Wan 利也, which is likely erroneous.
  • Williams' "acute": This is a misreading of 刺也, which belongs under the reading ꜃tʻiú (tiu5) per 《集韻》徒了切.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).

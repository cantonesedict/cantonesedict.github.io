吓 ꜃Há (haa5)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 3
Unicode code point
U+5413
Reading variations
  • haa5-2 (for Cantonese "during" suffix sense)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 67 ꜃吓)
    • Once, a time;
    • a while, momentarily;
    • ꜂tang yat꜆ ꜃há (dang2 jat1 haa5 等一吓), stop a little;
    • ꜂tá yat꜆ ꜃há (daa2 jat1 haa5 打一吓), strike it once;
    • ꜂kí ꜃há (gei2 haa5 幾吓), several times;
    • yat꜆ ꜃há ꜁tung ꜁tʻung hü꜄ (jat1 haa5 tung4 heoi3 一吓同去) I will go with you soon;
    • man꜅ hau꜅ ꜃há ꜃kʻü ꜂ché ché꜄ (man6 hau6 haa5 keoi5 ze3 問候吓佢嗻) only came to visit him.

    (Alternative form of 下. See 下 haa5.)

Conway's commentary
  • Usually spelt 下 for the above senses (i.e. the occurrence classifier and "moment/momentarily" senses). This 下 (haa5) is not exclusive to Cantonese; it is the same 下 (haa5) that is present in 一下 (jat1 haa5) and in 一下子 (jat1 haa5 zi2). However, the spelling 下 for this haa5 can be confusing in Cantonese because this 下 is not homophonous with 下 (haa6) meaning "down" or "go down". This is especially true in some of Williams' examples, where there is insufficient semantic context to disambiguate between haa5 and haa6 if 下 is used (e.g. 觀吓場 vs 觀下場 under 場 coeng4, and 詳吓 vs 詳下 under 詳 coeng4). Thus, for practical purposes, I have not normalised 吓 to 下.
  • However, there are separate haa5 that are exclusive to Cantonese; see the Cantonese entry below.
  • Williams' 一吓同去: Corrected ꜁tung (dung4) to ꜁tʻung (tung4).
  • Williams' 問候吓佢嗻: Corrected ꜂ché (ze2) to ché꜄ (ze3). In modern Cantonese the final particle would be 啫 ze1 instead of the (now) dated 嗻 ze3.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【吓】 (haa5)
    1. Suffixed after an adjective that has been degree-modified by 幾 "quite". The added suffix somewhat(?) lessens the degree.
      • 今日幾凍吓 (gam1 jat6 gei2 dung3 haa5): "[It's] quite cold today."
      • 碟餸幾辣吓 (dip6 sung3 gei2 laat6 haa5): "[The] dish [is] quite spicy."
  • 【吓】 (haa5, haa5-2)
    1. Suffixed after a reduplicated verb, before introducing an event that occurs during the action of the verb (i.e. after the action has started but before the action ought to cease). The (tone-unchanged) reading haa5 is strongly preferred when the reduplicated verb is tone 2.
      • 行行吓腳軟 (haang4 haang4 haa5-2 goek3 jyun5): "In [the] middle of walking [I felt] weak-legged."
      • 食食吓想嘔 (sik6 sik6 haa5 soeng2 au2): "In [the] middle of eating [I] wanted to vomit."
      • 講講吓唔記得咗 (gong2 gong2 haa5 m4 gei3 dak1 zo2): "In [the] middle of speaking [he] forgot [about it]."

下 Há꜅ (haa6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼀ + 2
Unicode code point
U+4E0B
Alternative forms
  • 丅
Reading variations
  • haa4 (common in 下巴)
  • haa6-2 (vernacular in 咁上下)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 76 下) 上下[低]也早也降也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 67 ꜃下 ꜃Há (haa5); here corrected to Há꜅ (haa6))
    • To descend, to come down (Kangxi: 降也自上而下也);
    • ꜃há há꜅ ꜁shün (haa6 syun4 下船) to go aboard;
    • ꜃há há꜅ ꜁lau (haa6 lau4 下樓), go down stairs;
    • ꜃há há꜅ hí꜄ (haa6 hei3 下氣) to restrain the temper;
    • ꜃há há꜅ ꜂kiú (haa6 giu6-2 下轎) to get out of a sedan;
    • ꜃há há꜅ tán꜅ (haa6 daan6-2 下蛋) to lay an egg;
    • ꜃há há꜅ tʻáp꜆ (haa6 taap3 下榻) to lodge;
    • ꜃há há꜅ pat꜆ (haa6 bat1 下筆) to write;
    • ꜀kwan ꜁nang ꜃há há꜅ há꜅ (gwan1 nang4 haa6 haa6 君能下下) a sage knows how to condescend;
    • ꜃há há꜅ ꜂shau (haa6 sau2 下手), to begin to act.
  • (Page 68 下꜅)
    • Below, down, bottom, inferior (Kangxi: 在下之下對上之稱);
    • mean, vulgar (Kangxi: 賤也);
    • next (Kangxi: 後也);
    • tsoi꜅ ꜂tai há꜅ (zoi6 dai2 haa6 在底下) it is below;
    • há꜅ ꜂tai (haa6 dai2 下底), the bottom, underneath;
    • há꜅ tʻsz'꜄ tsʻz'꜄ (haa6 ci3 下次) next time;
    • há꜅ üt꜇ (haa6 jyut6 下月) next moon;
    • há꜅ ꜁man (haa6 man4 下民), common people;
    • há꜅ ꜁lau (haa6 lau4 下流), the lowest classes, lowlive, vicious;
    • ꜁mò há꜅ lok꜇ (mou4 haa6 lok6 無下落) no fixed business or residence;
    • há꜅ ꜃má ꜀wai (haa6 maa5 wai1 下馬威), a humbug;
    • há꜅ tsok꜆ (haa6 zok3 下作) to covet, to long for, unscrupulous;
    • há꜅ ꜁pá ꜁pʻá (haa6 paa4 下巴), the chin;
    • há꜅ ꜀sheng (haa6 seng1 下聲), a low sound, or voice;
    • há꜅ pín꜅ (haa6 bin6 下便) the right side, the lower;
    • há꜅ ꜁yan (haa6 jan4 下人), official underlings.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected ⿰亻氏 to 低.
  • Williams has attempted to distinguish the now (very) obsolete rising-tone reading ꜃há (haa5) from the departing-tone reading há꜅ (haa6), but he has gotten the senses the wrong way round. Classically, it is the positional/directional sense that is rising, and the verb sense that is departing.
  • Williams' 下船: That is, to go down onto the boat. Also used to mean the opposite (to go down from the boat, i.e. to disembark).
  • Williams' 下底: Less common than 下低.
  • Williams' 下次: Corrected tʻsz'꜄ (aspirate in wrong position) to tsʻz'꜄.
  • Williams' 下馬威: Here, "humbug" means "fraud" or "sham". This is the meaning when 下馬威 is used sarcastically to mock a (vain) show of authority or might.
  • Williams' 下作: Better rendered as adjectives.
  • Williams' 下巴: Corrected ꜁pá (baa4) to ꜁pʻá (paa4).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【下】 (haeX)
    • (adverb-like) down, downward
    • (postposition-like) below, under
    • (adjective-like) below, lower, inferior
  • 【下】 (haeH)
    • (verb-like) [to] go down, [to] alight
    • (verb-like) [to] down, [to] put down

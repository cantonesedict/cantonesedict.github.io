Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 67 ꜀蝦꜀鰕)
    • A shrimp, prawn, crawfish (Kangxi 蝦: 與鰕通 etc.);
    • ꜀há ꜀chʻun (haa1 ceon1 蝦膥), crab's eggs shrimp roe;
    • ꜀há ꜀chʻun cháp꜇ (haa1 ceon1 zaap6 蝦膥鈒) a skinflint;
    • ꜁lung ꜀há (lung4 haa1 龍蝦), the sea crawfish (Palinurus);
    • ꜁ngan ꜀há (ngan4 haa1 銀蝦), sea shrimps;
    • ꜁ming ꜀há (ming4 haa1 明蝦), large yellow prawns;
    • ꜀há kòm꜄ ꜂tsán (haa1 gam3 zaan2 蝦咁趲), to throwing the arms about passionately;
    • ꜀shang ꜀sháng ꜀há kòm꜄ tʻiú꜄ (saang1 haa1 gam3 tiu3 生蝦咁跳) lively, playful.

    (…)

    (Otherwise, a reading variation. See 蝦 haa4 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 生蝦咁跳: Changed ꜀shang (sang1) to vernacular reading ꜀sháng (saang1).

    (…)

    The first character (蝦) is also read ꜁há (haa4), as ꜁há ꜁má (haa4 maa4 蝦蟆), a striped frog.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 蝦蟆: Or, a toad.
  • The common reading of 蝦蟆 (or 蛤蟆) is haa1 mou1; see Kung Fu Hustle. The prescribed reading haa4 maa4 is almost never heard.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【蝦】 (hae)
    • Used in 蝦蟆.
  • 【蝦蟆】 (hae mae)
    • (noun-like) frog, toad

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22* 折꜆; here normalised to 擳)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • To tickle;
    • to splash up, to spatter at;
    • to squirt at, as from a hose;
    • chít꜆ ꜂nan ꜀shan (zit1 nan2 san1 擳㤻身), splash me all over.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【擳】 (zit1):
    1. To tickle.
      • 怕擳 (paa3 zit1): "Ticklish."
    2. To squeeze, to pinch.
      • 擳暗瘡 (zit1 am3 cong1): "Pop [a] pimple."
      • 擳牙膏 (zit1 ngaa4 gou1): "Squeeze toothpaste" (used figuratively for slow progress in small amounts).
    3. A small amount.
      • 擳都唔笑 (zit1 dou1 m4 siu3): "Not [a] pinch of laughter."
    4. To squirt, to spray.
      • 擳到周圍都係 (zit1 dou3 zau1 wai4 dou1 hai6): "Squirted [it] all over [the place]."

折 Chít꜆ (zit3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6298
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 25 折) 損也耗也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22* 折꜆)
    • To sunder, to snap in two, to break off;
    • to fold, to bend;
    • to oppress, to repress;
    • to reprehend;
    • to decide, to discriminate;
    • to deduct;
    • to stop, to injure;
    • to lose one's heir;
    • an act of a play;
    • to exchange, to lose in trade;
    • to make amends for, to set over against;
    • to abase, to lower;
    • unmarried;
    • chít꜆ tsít꜆ há꜅ ꜀káu (zit3 zit3 haa6 gaau1 折節下交), to condescend to men of low estate;
    • chít꜆ yuk꜇ (zit3 juk6 折獄) to have a jail delivery;
    • ꜃múi pʻat꜆ chít꜆ ꜁léung í꜅ shek꜇ (mui5 pat1 zit3 loeng4 ji6 sek6 每匹折量二石) each piece was reckoned as two stone of corn;
    • ꜁'m chít꜆ tak꜆ (m4 zit3 dak1 唔折得) cannot cheapen it; can't break it;
    • ꜂kí [꜁]shing chít꜆ (gei2 sing4 zit3 幾成折) what discount will you make?
    • chít꜆ shik꜇ (zit3 sik6 折食) a composition in money for supplying viands;
    • chít꜆ kwai꜄ (zit3 gwai3 折桂) to pluck the cassia twig — to be a graduate;
    • chít꜆ hò꜄ ꜂kám tik꜆ (zit3 hou3 gaam2 dik1 折耗減滴) to discount a trifle; a little broken;
    • ꜂tá chít꜆ (daa2 zit3 打折) broken off;
    • chít꜆ ꜁mo (zit3 mo4 折磨), greatly afflicted;
    • ꜀tséung ꜀kung chít꜆ tsúi꜅ (zoeng1 gung1 zit3 zeoi6 將功折罪) to atone for error by meritorious conduct;
    • chít꜆ ꜂sün (zit3 syun2 折損), to break;
    • chít꜆ ká꜄ (zit3 gaa3 折價) to lower the price.

    A colloquial word (see 折 zit1).

    (…)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 幾成折: Corrected ꜀shing (sing1) to ꜁shing (sing4).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【折】 (zit3):
    1. A discount.
      • 冇得折 (mou5 dak1 zit3): "No discount."
      • 折咗都係貴 (zit3 zo2 dou1 hai6 gwai3): "[Even] discounted it's still expensive."
      • 九折 (gau2 zit3): "Nine-[tenths] discount" (i.e. 10% off).
      • 七五折 (cat1 ng5 zit3): "Seven-[tenths]-five discount" (i.e. 25% off).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' иІ·й—ңзҜҖ: Dated.
  • Williams' зҜҖе„ү: Corrected kГӯmкң„ (gim3) to kГӯmкң… (gim6).
  • The reading variation tsГӯtкңҮ (zit6) is a complicated matter. The rabbit hole goes like this:
    • Kangxi gives the reading гҖҠйӣҶйҹ»гҖӢжҳЁзөҗеҲҮ tsГӯtкңҮ (zit6) with the example гҖҠи©©е°Ҹйӣ…гҖӢзҜҖеҪјеҚ—еұұ, but gives no explanation.
    • The entry in гҖҠйӣҶйҹ»гҖӢ does not have this example, but gives the explanation еұұй«ҳеі»[иІҢ] "lofty", with the alternative forms рЎө’ (вҝұе·іеұұ) and рЎҫғ (вҝ°вҝұеұұйӣҖжҲҲ). Of these, the form рЎө’ (вҝұе·іеұұ) is perhaps a sloppy version of вҝұг”ҫеұұ, i.e. "knot" + "mountain".
    • In гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢ under the reading жҳЁзөҗеҲҮ tsГӯtкңҮ (zit6) there is the character вҝұвҝҙг”ҫдё¶еұұ. The top component рӯ…І (вҝҙг”ҫдё¶) is a stylised еҚӘ, so again we have "knot" + "mountain". The explanation is еұұеіҜеҸҲеӯҗзөҗеҲҮ, i.e. "mountain summit" with the alternative reading еӯҗзөҗеҲҮ tsГӯtкңҶ (zit3) (which appears to be a genuine alternative, not a cross-reference).
    • Kangxi has neither рЎө’ (вҝұе·іеұұ) nor вҝұг”ҫеұұ nor рӯ…І (вҝҙг”ҫдё¶), but instead has an entry for еІҠ (вҝұе·ҙеұұ). This seems to be sloppy formatting of the "knot" component as е·ҙ based on the similar shape of the seal character for еҚӘ "knot". Only the reading еӯҗзөҗеҲҮ tsГӯtкңҶ (zit3) is given, which makes this entry's citations of гҖҠйӣҶйҹ»гҖӢ erroneous.
    • In гҖҠиӘӘж–ҮгҖӢ the glyph is "knot" + "mountain" (д»Һеұұд»ҺеҚӘ).
    So what is the conclusion? Well, it seems like the "lofty" sense comes from the notion of being "crested", which seems to be the intent behind the glyph being "mountain knot". So, in my opinion, we read tsГӯtкңҶ (zit3) for "crested", and either tsГӯtкңҶ (zit3) or tsГӯtкңҮ (zit6) for "lofty". But it would just be easier if the "lofty" sense did not exist.

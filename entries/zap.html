執 Chap꜆ (zap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼟ + 8
Unicode code point
U+57F7
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 執) 持守也拘也捕也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 執꜆)
    • To take in the hand, to pick up, to lay hold of, to grasp;
    • to apprehend, to catch (Kangxi: 捕也);
    • to maintain, to retain (Kangxi: 守也持也);
    • to stop up (Kangxi: 塞也);
    • a handfull;
    • chap꜆ ꜃yau (zap1 jau5 執友), a father's old friend (Kangxi: 父之友曰執友);
    • ꜀shau chap꜆ (sau1 zap1 收執) to receive and keep [as evidence];
    • chap꜆ tsz'꜅ (zap1 zi6 執字) to set types;
    • chap꜆ ꜁chʻau (zap1 cau4-2 執籌), to draw lots;
    • chap꜆ ꜀chung (zap1 zung1 執中), to keep a due medium;
    • chap꜆ áu꜄ (zap1 aau3 執拗) a pertinacious dispute;
    • kú꜄ chap꜆ (gu3 zap1 固執) obstinate, set in his way;
    • kok꜆ chap꜆ yat꜆ ngai꜅ (gok3 zap1 jat1 ngai6 各執一藝) each follows his trade;
    • pò꜅ chap꜆ (bou6 zap1 捕執) to arrest;
    • chap꜆ sz'꜅ (zap1 si6 執事) to manage business; an officer's retinue;
    • chap꜆ ꜂shau (zap1 sau2 執手), take by the hand.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 蓻. See 蓻 zap1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To manage, to judge (Kangxi: 處也).
  2. To carry out, to execute.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【執】 (zap1)
    1. To pick up.
      • 執返佢 (zap1 faan1 keoi5): "Pick [it] back up."
      • 執垃圾 (zap1 laap6 saap3): "Pick up rubbish."
      • 執狗屎 (zap1 gau2 si2): "Pick up dog shit."
    2. To tidy up, or make neater.
      • 執房 (zap1 fong4-2): "Tidy up [the] room."
      • 執正啲 (zap zeng1 di1): "Make [it a] bit neater."
    3. To pack.
      • 執包袱 (zap1 baau1 fuk6): "Pack [my] bags".
      • 執行李 (zap1 hang4 lei5): "Pack luggage."
    4. Short for 執笠.
  • 【執錯字粒】 (zap1 co3 zi6 nap1)
    1. To make a typo. (Literally, "Pick up [the] wrong character block.")
  • 【執骨】 (zap1 gwat1)
    1. To take up the bones from a grave (for a reburial).
  • 【執笠】 (zap1 lap1)
    1. To shut up shop, especially after going bankrupt.
      • 執咗笠 (zap1 zo2 lap1): "[They've] shut up [shop]."
      • 就快執笠 (zau6 faai3 zap1 lap1): "About to go under."
  • 【執媽】 (zap1 maa1)
    1. A midwife (strictly female).
  • 【執碼】 (zap1 maa5)
    1. To gather ingredients for a chef to cook.
  • 【執碼】 (zap1 maa5-2)
    1. The kitchen assistant who gather ingredients for the chefs to cook.
  • 【執生】 (zap1 saang1)
    1. To improvise, or figure something out on the spot.
  • 【執輸】 (zap1 syu1)
    1. To miss out.
  • 【執仔】 (zap1 zai2)
    1. To midwive.
  • 【執仔婆】 (zap1 zai2 po4-2)
    1. A midwife (strictly female).

汁 Chap꜆ (zap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 2
Unicode code point
U+6C41
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 汁) 液也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 汁꜆)
    • Gravy, juice (Kangxi: 液也);
    • thickened liquor;
    • slosh;
    • the juices or essence of a thing got by expressing;
    • sleety rain, wet snow;
    • ꜀pan ꜁long chap꜆ (ban1 long4 zap1 檳榔汁) betel juice;
    • tsʻoi꜄ chap꜆ (coi3 zap1 菜汁) liquor left after cooking vegetables;
    • chap꜆ ꜁'m tak꜆ tám꜅ háp꜆ (zap1 m4 dak1 daam6 haap3 汁唔得啖呷) not had a mouthful of gravy to suck—I've had no benefit from it.

    (…)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "thickened liquor": From Kangxi's example 汁獻涗於醆酒, with commentary noting that 汁獻 is also spelt 汁莎.

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 縶) 羈係也[絆]馬足曰縶
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 ？) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 縶꜆)
    • To fetter a horse (Kangxi: 縶絆也) or cow;
    • fetters, shackles;
    • to bind, to secure (Kangxi: 相縶也連也);
    • a cord (Kangxi: 縷也).
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 紏 to 絆. The variant entry is 馬 with 山 replacing 灬.

蓻 Tsap꜆ (zap1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 11
Unicode code point
U+84FB
Alternative forms
  • 𠽃
  • 執
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 557 𠽃꜆; here normalised to 蓻)

    A colloquial word.

    • A heap, a handful, a pile;
    • a group, a knot;
    • chü꜅ ꜁mái yat꜆ tsap꜆ (zyu6 maai4 jat1 zap1 住埋一蓻) living in a single community;
    • tso꜅ ꜃tsʻo ꜁mái yat꜆ tsap꜆ (co5 maai4 jat1 zap1 坐埋一蓻) sitting together in a group;
    • sò꜄ ꜁mái yat꜆ tsap꜆ (sou3 maai4 jat1 zap1 掃埋一蓻) sweep it up into a pile;
    • ap꜆ tsap꜆ (ap1 zap1 洽蓻) stingy, craven, slovenly;
    • yat꜆ tsap꜆ ꜃mai (jat1 zap1 mai5 一蓻米), as much rice as the fingers can grasp;
    • ꜀tʻau tsap꜆ (tau1 zap1 偷蓻) to make a little money.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 坐埋一蓻: Corrected tso꜅ (zo6 去聲) to ꜃tsʻo (co5 上聲).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【蓻】 (zap1)
    1. A pinch or tuft.
      • 一蓻鹽 (jat1 zap1 jim4): "One pinch of salt."
      • 一蓻頭髮 (jat zap1 tau4 faat3): "One tuft of hair."

It is debatable whether this is the same word as 執 chap꜆ (zap1). In any case, Williams records the reading as tsap꜆ (zap1), and Eitel (1877) prefers the character 蓻, which matches tsap꜆ (zap1) in pronunciation:

《廣韻》子入切：蓻：草生多皃
《康熙字典》蓻：《唐韻》子入切音𥠋草生多貌　又《玉篇》茅芽也[…]

Cháp꜆ (zaap3 低陰入)

  • 劄

劄 Cháp꜆ (zaap3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 12
Unicode code point
U+5284
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 劄) 帋劄又以針[刺]也又柬疏謂之劄子
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 劄꜆)
    • To prick, to puncture;
    • to embroider;
    • a document, a paper;
    • a particular sort of paper;
    • a diploma;
    • a contract;
    • to write out, as a list of prices;
    • a reply from a higher officer;
    • a remonstrance to the emperor;
    • ꜂chí cháp꜆ (zi2 zaap3 紙劄) stationery;
    • cháp꜆ ꜂tsz' (zaap3 zi2 劄子) a remonstrance;
    • kám꜄ cháp꜆ (gaam3 zaap3 監劄), a diploma bought by a kiensang (監生);
    • cháp꜆ ká꜄ ꜁tsʻín (zaap3 gaa3 cin4 劄價錢), write the prices;
    • cháp꜆ lok꜇ pò꜅ (zaap3 lok6 bou6-2 劄落簿) put it to account;
    • cháp꜆ ꜀tán (zaap3 daan1 劄單), a contract for a purchase.

    The word chop, used in Canton is derived from this.

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 剌 to 刺.

极 Cháp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6781
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 极꜇)

    Read kʻap꜆ (kap1); (see 极 kap1).

    A colloquial word.

    • A camp chair;
    • ꜃má cháp꜇ (maa5 zaap6 馬极) a camp-stool;
    • cháp꜇ chak꜆ fan꜄ (zaap6 zak1 fan3 极側瞓) to sleep on the side.

鈒 Cháp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 4
Unicode code point
U+9212
Reading variations
  • zaap6-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 鈒꜇)

    Read sáp꜆ (saap3); (see 鈒 saap3).

    A colloquial word.

    • One bladed shears working on a pivot;
    • to slice or pare with shears;
    • cháp꜇ ꜀hoi (zaap6 hoi1 鈒開), cut it open;
    • ꜀pan ꜁long cháp꜇ (ban1 long4 zaap6-2 檳榔鈒) betel-nut shears;
    • cháp꜇ yéuk꜇ ꜁tsʻoi (zaap6 joek6 coi4 鈒藥材), to slice medicine;
    • cháp꜇ sui꜄ (zaap6 seoi3 鈒碎) slice it fine.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鈒】 (zaap6, zaap6-2):
    1. Shears.
  • 【鈒】 (zaap6):
    1. To cut or slice, especially with shears.

閘 Cháp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾨ + 5
Unicode code point
U+9598
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 閘) 閘門
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 閘꜇)
    • A guarded gate, a barrier, a barricade;
    • dam;
    • a barrier like a turnstile or a stockade;
    • a lock, a flood-gate;
    • to shut a gate;
    • cháp꜇ tsáp꜇ (zaap6 zaap6 閘？) a guard-house at a gate;
    • ꜀kwán cháp꜇ (gwaan1 zaap6 關閘) to shut the gate; the Barrier at Macao;
    • ꜂shui cháp꜇ (seoi2 zaap6 水閘) a waste-weir, a mill-race or sluice with gates;
    • cháp꜇ ꜀fú (zaap6 fu1 閘夫) a gatekeeper;
    • ꜀hon cháp꜇ (hon1 zaap6 看閘) to guard the gate;
    • cháp꜇ ꜁mún (zaap6 mun4 閘門), a street gate;
    • ꜃shéung cháp꜇ (soeng5 zaap6 上閘) put in the bars.

劄 Cháp꜆ (zaap3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 12
Unicode code point
U+5284
Alternative forms
  • 箚
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 劄) 帋劄又以針[刺]也又柬疏謂之劄子
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 劄꜆)
    • To prick, to puncture (Kangxi 箚: 以鍼刺也);
    • to embroider;
    • a document, a paper (Kangxi 箚: 牋劄用以奏事非表非狀者謂之劄子);
    • a particular sort of paper;
    • a diploma;
    • a contract;
    • to write out, as a list of prices;
    • a reply from a higher officer;
    • a remonstrance to the emperor;
    • ꜂chí cháp꜆ (zi2 zaap3 紙劄) stationery;
    • cháp꜆ ꜂tsz' (zaap3 zi2 劄子) a remonstrance;
    • kám꜄ cháp꜆ (gaam3 zaap3 監劄) a diploma bought by a kiensang (監生);
    • cháp꜆ ká꜄ ꜁tsʻín (zaap3 gaa3 cin4 劄價錢), write the prices;
    • cháp꜆ lok꜇ pò꜅ (zaap3 lok6 bou6-2 劄落簿) put it to account;
    • cháp꜆ ꜀tán (zaap3 daan1 劄單), a contract for a purchase.

    The word chop, used in Canton is derived from this.

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 剌 to 刺.
  • Kangxi has 箚 (⿱𥫗㓣) for the headword, but 劄 (⿰答刂) in the explanations.

習 Tsáp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽻ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7FD2
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 習 Tsap꜇ (zap6)) 學慣熟也狎也[練]也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 558 習꜇)
    • A continued Repeated flight (Kangxi: 數飛也);
    • to repeat the same act, to practice, to get accustomed to, to become skilled in;
    • practice, use, custom, habit, exercise, repetition (Kangxi: 習重也);
    • hok꜇ tsáp꜇ (hok6 zaap6 學習) apprenticed to; to learn about;
    • tsáp꜇ kwán꜄ ꜁shing tsz'꜅ ꜁ín (zaap6 gwaan3 sing4 zi6 jin4 習慣成自然), practice makes it natural;
    • tsáp꜇ shuk꜇ (zaap6 suk6 習熟) well versed in, habituated to;
    • tsáp꜇ lín꜅ (zaap6 lin6 習練) practice;
    • tsáp꜇ ꜂hau ꜀yam (zaap6 hau2 jam1 習口音), to practice speaking;
    • tsáp꜇ ꜁í pat꜆ chʻát꜆ (zaap6 ji4 bat1 caat3 習而不察) to take without examination, careless practice;
    • tsáp꜇ ꜁'m tò꜄ (zaap6 m4 dou3-2 習唔到) cannot learn the use of it;
    • tsáp꜇ tsuk꜇ ꜃shí ꜂sz' ꜁ín (zaap6 zuk6 si2 jin4 習俗使然), I've learned the usages.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 綀 to 練.
  • Williams' "A continued flight": Better rendered "repeated flight". In Kangxi 數飛也, note that 數 is to be read in entering tone, shok꜆ (sok3).
  • Williams' 習俗使然: Corrected ꜃shí (si5) to ꜂sz' (si2).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. An acquaintance, a familiar.
  2. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).

襲 Tsáp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾐ + 16
Unicode code point
U+8972
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 39 襲 Tsap꜇ (zap6)) 重衣也因也又潛師揜取曰襲
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 558 襲꜇)
    • Double garments (Kangxi: 重衣也), lined robes, a court dress;
    • a suit;
    • a robe fastened on the left side (Kangxi: 左袵袍也);
    • collected to match (Kangxi: 合也);
    • repeated (Kangxi: 重也);
    • to make a foray, to invade, to steal into (Kangxi: 掩其不備也);
    • to receive, to inherit (Kangxi: 受也);
    • hereditary, descending in a family;
    • shai꜄ tsáp꜇ (sai3 zaap6 世襲) hereditary;
    • tsáp꜇ yam꜄ (zaap6 jam3 襲蔭) hereditary protection and pension — for the merit of ancestors;
    • yat꜆ tsáp꜇ ꜀í (jat1 zaap6 ji1 一襲衣), a suit of clothes;
    • shai꜄ tsáp꜇ ꜂tsz' tséuk꜆ (sai3 zaap6 zi2 zoek3 世襲子爵) a hereditary baron.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "Double garments": Seen classically in the verb sense, i.e. to put on a second layer of clothing.
  • Williams' "a court dress": This is a misreading of Kangxi 襲朝服 as a definition, when it is actually an example for the verb sense 服也, to wear.
  • Williams' "collected": This is a misreading of Kangxi 合也. Here, 合 means "match".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To wear (Kangxi: 服也).

    • tsáp꜇ fo꜄ pʻò꜄ (zaap6 fo3 pou3 雜貨舖) a variety store;

    • tsáp꜇ lün꜅ (zaap6 lyun6 雜亂) confused, put in disorder;

    • ꜂tá tsáp꜇ (daa2 zaap6-2 打雜) man of all work;
    • shik꜇ tsáp꜇ (sik6 zaap6 食雜) to eat anything, not to fast;
    • láp꜇ tsáp꜇ (laap6 zaap6 垃雜) miscellaneous, a medley.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "unassorted": Meaning "unsorted" or "miscellaneous".
  • Williams' 五方雜處: Changed ꜂chʻü (cyu2) to common reading ꜃chʻü (cyu5).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【垃雜】 (laap6 zaap6):
    1. Disorganised or cluttered. Commonly spelt 立雜 (see discussion below).
  • 【雜】 (zaap6):
    1. Used to describe a place as dodgy, due to the presence of miscellaneous unsavoury characters.
      • 城好雜 (seng4-2 hou2 zaap6): "[The] city [is] very dodgy."
    2. Offal.
      • 牛雜 (ngau4 zaap6): "Beef assortment [of offal]." (In Hong Kong noodle shops, the typical parts are tripe (肚 tou5), lung (肺 fai3), intestine (腸 coeng4-2), and spleen (膀 bong2).)

There is an argument that 垃雜 láp꜇ tsáp꜇ (laap6 zaap6) should be spelt 擸𢶍. Indeed 擸𢶍 has an expected reading of lap꜇ tsap꜇ (lap6 zap6), and is explained "broken" or "cluttered":

《廣韻》盧盍切：擸：折也又擸𢶍破壞也
《廣韻》才盍切：𢶍：擸𢶍和雜
《康熙字典》𢶍：《唐韻》才盍切《集韻》疾盍切𠀤音䪞讀若慙入聲擸𢶍和雜也[…]

However, since 擸𢶍 is also considered the original spelling of 垃圾 laap6 saap3 "rubbish", it would be confusing to use it for laap6 zaap6 also. As a compromise that acknowledges the possible common ancestry, I have opted to spell laap6 zaap6 as 垃雜, even though the common spelling is 立雜.

霫 Tsáp꜇ (zaap6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾬ + 11
Unicode code point
U+972B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 40 霫 Tsap꜇ (zap6)) 大雨
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 559 霫꜇)
    • A great shower (Kangxi: 雴霫大雨也), (…);
    • an ancient tribe of northerners near Corea or Manchuria, spoken of during the Tʻáng (唐) dynasty (Kangxi: 白霫北狄國).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "near Corea or Manchuria": This is an erroneous merging of two distinct proper-noun (barbarian group) senses:
    1. 霫 sap꜆ (sap1) as in 奚霫 from the north-east (see 霫 sap1); and
    2. 霫 tsáp꜇ (zaap6) as in 白霫 from the north.
    The second of these is the one where Kangxi has an example from 《唐書回鶻傳》.

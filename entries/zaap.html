劄 Cháp꜆ (zaap3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 12
Unicode code point
U+5284
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 劄) 帋劄又以針[刺]也又柬疏謂之劄子
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22 劄꜆)
    • To prick, to puncture;
    • to embroider;
    • a document, a paper;
    • a particular sort of paper;
    • a diploma;
    • a contract;
    • to write out, as a list of prices;
    • a reply from a higher officer;
    • a remonstrance to the emperor;
    • ꜂chí cháp꜆ (zi2 zaap3 紙劄) stationery;
    • cháp꜆ ꜂tsz' (zaap3 zi2 劄子) a remonstrance;
    • kám꜄ cháp꜆ (gaam3 zaap3 監劄), a diploma bought by a kiensang (監生);
    • cháp꜆ ká꜄ ꜁tsʻín (zaap3 gaa3 cin4 劄價錢), write the prices;
    • cháp꜆ lok꜇ pò꜅ (zaap3 lok6 bou6-2 劄落簿) put it to account;
    • cháp꜆ ꜀tán (zaap3 daan1 劄單), a contract for a purchase.

    The word chop, used in Canton is derived from this.

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 剌 to 刺.

  • (Page 22 鈒꜇)

    Read sáp꜆ (saap3); (see 鈒 saap3).

Radical + residual strokes
⾫ + 4
Unicode code point
U+96C6

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 40 集 Tsap꜇ (zap6)) 聚集會集
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 558 集꜇)
    • To flock together
    • to gather, as clouds;
    • to assemble, to come together, to congregate, to collect;
    • to bring together;
    • to go directly to the mark;
    • to mix properly, to blend;
    • to make a collection, to compile;
    • a miscellany, ana;
    • a place where people collect, as a market;
    • often interchanged with the last (雜);
    • ꜁tsʻai tsáp꜇ (cai4 zaap6 齊集) came together, a full concourse;
    • ꜁man tsáp꜇ (man4 zaap6 文集) a miscellany;
    • yat꜆ tsáp꜇ (jat1 zaap6 一集) one division of a book, what is collected under one head;
    • tsáp꜇ úi꜅ hí꜄ (zaap6 wui6 hei3 集會戲) a varied bill, as at a theatre;
    • ꜀chung yat꜇ háp꜇ tsáp꜇ (zung1 jat6 haap6 zaap6 終日狎集) friends here the whole day;
    • ꜀tʻín há꜅ ꜁wan tsáp꜇ (tin1 haa6 wan4 zaap6 天下雲集) crowded together from all quarters;
    • tsü꜅ tsáp꜇ (zeoi6 zaap6 聚集) to assemble, to collect.

    • A mixture of different colors or ingredients;

    • tsáp꜇ fo꜄ (zaap6 fo3 雜貨) miscellaneous goods;
    • tsáp꜇ fo꜄ pʻò꜄ (zaap6 fo3 pou3 雜貨舖) a variety store;
    • tsáp꜇ shik꜆ (zaap6 sik1 雜色), variegated;
    • ꜀sam tsáp꜇ (sam1 zaap6 心雜) distracted, not keeping long at a purpose;
    • tsáp꜇ ꜂chung ꜂tsai (zaap6 zung2 zai2 雜種仔), a bastard;
    • tsáp꜇ ꜁tʻong ꜂kwo (zaap6 tong4 gwo2 雜糖果), chowchow sweetmeats;

    • tsáp꜇ lün꜅ (zaap6 lyun6 雜亂) confused, put in disorder;
    • ꜁hán tsáp꜇ ꜁yan ꜂tang (haan4 zaap6 jan4 dang2 閒雜人等) loafers, idlers;
    • ꜂tá tsáp꜇ (daa2 zaap6-2 打雜) man of all work;
    • shik꜇ tsáp꜇ (sik6 zaap6 食雜) to eat anything, not to fast;
    • láp꜇ tsáp꜇ (laap6 zaap6 立雜) miscellaneous, a medley.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' unassorted: An old word for unsorted or miscellaneous.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【垃雜】 (laap6 zaap6):
    1. Disorganised or cluttered. Commonly spelt 立雜.

There is an argument that 垃雜 láp꜇ tsáp꜇ (laap6 zaap6) should be spelt 擸𢶍. Indeed 擸𢶍 has an expected reading of lap꜇ tsap꜇ (lap6 zap6), and is explained "broken" or "cluttered":

  • 《廣韻》盧盍切：擸：折也又擸𢶍破壞也
  • 《廣韻》才盍切：𢶍：擸𢶍和雜
  • 《康熙字典》𢶍：《唐韻》才盍切《集韻》疾盍切𠀤音䪞讀若慙入聲擸𢶍和雜也[…]

However, since 擸𢶍 is also considered the original spelling of 垃圾 laap6 saap3 "rubbish", it would be confusing to use it for laap6 zaap6 also. As a compromise that acknowledges the (probable) common ancestry, I have opted to spell laap6 zaap6 as 垃雜, even though the common spelling is 立雜.

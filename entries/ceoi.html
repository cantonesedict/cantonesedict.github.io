Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 80 催) 促也趲也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 598 ꜀催)
    • To urge, to press, to egg on (Kangxi: 促也迫也);
    • to importune;
    • to hasten, as the payment of a debt;
    • to reiterate;
    • mok꜇ ko꜄ ꜀tsʻúi ꜃ngo (mok6 go3 ceoi1 ngo5 莫個催我), don't hurry me;
    • ꜀tsʻúi pik꜆ (ceoi3 bik1 催逼) or ꜀tsʻúi ꜂kam (ceoi1 gam2 催？), to urge on, to stimulate;
    • ꜀tsʻúi ꜃kʻü fái꜄ tik꜆ (ceoi1 keoi5 faai3 dik1 催佢快滴) hurry him on a little;
    • [fat]꜇ kái꜄ ꜂tsau ꜀tsʻúi (fat6 gaai3 zau2 ceoi1 乏价走催), I've no servant to send to hasten you — a phrase on invitation cards;
    • ꜂hí shai꜄ kòm꜄ ꜀tsʻúi (hei2 sai3 gam3 ceoi1 起勢咁催), hurrying and urging one rather causelessly.

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword (with 比-like centre-left instead of ⿲口口口) to 龡.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【吹】 (ceoi1):
    1. Short for 吹水.
    2. Short for 吹脹 in the rhetorical interrogative sense, where one taunts a victim for retributive action after inflicting an injustice upon them.
      • 吹呀 (ceoi1 a4): "[Gonna] rile [me]?" (i.e. what can you do in response?)
      • 吹咩 (ceoi1 me1): "[Gonna] rile [me]?" (i.e. what can you do in response?)
  • 【吹水】 (ceoi1 seoi2):
    1. To brag, to boast, to exaggerate.
      • 又吹水 (jau6 ceoi1 seoi2): "Talking big again [eh]?"
      • 正一吹水哥 (zing1 jat1 ceoi1 seoi2 go1): "[He's a] right big-mouth."
    2. To converse casually.
      • 得閒吹吓水 (dak1 haan4 ceoi1 haa5 seoi2): "[When we're] free [let's] have [a] chat."
  • 【吹脹】 (ceoi1 zoeng3):
    1. To vex, to irk.
      • 真係吹脹 (zan1 hai6 ceoi1 zoeng3): "[It's] really riled [me] up!"
      • 吹佢唔脹 (ceoi1 keoi5 m4 zoeng3): "Can't rile him up."

  • (Page 596 娶꜄ Tsʻü꜄ (ceoi3); here normalised to ꜂Tsʻü (ceoi2))
    • To marry a woman with the legal ceremonies (Kangxi: [取]婦也);
    • a marriage;
    • [꜂]tsʻü ꜀tsʻai (ceoi2 cai1 娶妻), or [꜂]tsʻü ꜃lò ꜁pʻo (ceoi2 lou5 po4 娶老婆), to take a wife;
    • [꜂]tsʻü ꜀san ꜃fú (ceoi2 san1 fu5 娶新婦) or ꜂[tsʻü] ꜀[san1] ꜃pʻò (ceoi2 san1 pou5 娶新婦), [somebody] marrying;
    • ꜁wing [꜂]tsʻü ꜁'m ꜁tsʻang (wing4 ceoi2 m4 cang4 榮娶唔曾), have you been married yet?
    • [꜂]tsʻü tsʻíp꜆ (ceoi2 cip3 娶妾) or [꜂]tsʻü ꜀pʻín ꜁fong (ceoi2 pin1 fong4 娶偏房), to take a concubine home to one.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 娶新婦 (second occurrence): Corrected ꜂tsʻò (cou2) to ꜂tsʻü (ceoi2) and corrected ꜀sam (sam1) to ꜀san (san1). Not somebody marrying, but to take a daughter-in-law.
  • Williams' 榮娶唔曾: Bettered rendered "have you married" rather than "have you been married". The verb 娶 is only used for a man taking a wife.

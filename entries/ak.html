Ak (ak)

Ak꜆ (ak1 高陰入)

啞 (U+555E) ak꜆ (ak1)

Williams (1856) entry
(Page 1)
  • ak꜆ ak꜆ (ak1 ak1 啞啞) the sound of giggling.
Conway's remarks
  • Williams has this entry within that for ꜂á (aa2); see aa#啞2.
See also
  • aa#啞2

握 (U+63E1) ak꜆ (ak1)

Reading variations
(aak1: longer syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3)
  • To grasp, to hold within the hand;
  • a little, as much as the palm can hold;
  • ak꜆ teng꜅ (ak1 deng6 握定) to grasp firmly;
  • ak꜆ ꜂shau (ak1 sau2 握手), to shake hands;
  • ak꜆ ꜁chʻí (ak1 ci4 握持), to hold in the hand;
  • ak꜆ iú꜄ (ak1 yiu3 握要) necessary.
Conway's remarks
  • Most modern speakers prefer the longer syllable ák꜆ (aak1); see aak#握1.
See also
  • aak#握1

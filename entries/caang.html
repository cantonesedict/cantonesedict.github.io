Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 30 撐 Chʻang1 (cang1)) 窓爭切駕船也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 30 撐) 策平聲以物撐物也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜀撐꜀撑)
    • To prop, to shore up;
    • to fasten open, as with a stretcher;
    • to pole;
    • to push off;
    • to make a show;
    • an inclined post, a fulcrum, a prop, a stay;
    • to border on, to adjoin, to stretch or run up into;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁shün (caang1 syun4 撐船), to pole a boat;
    • ꜀chʻáng tò꜅ (caang1 dou6 撐渡) to pole across the ferry; to intrigue for a friend with the officer;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁'m ꜁lai (caang1 m4 lai4 撐唔嚟), he could not show off long;
    • ꜀chʻung ꜀chʻáng (cung1 caang1 充撐), to curry favor among friends;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜃shéung hü꜄ shái꜄ (caang1 soeng5 heoi3 saai3 撐上去曬) stretch them up to dry.

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜀橕; here normalised to 撐)

    (…)

    Read chʻáng꜄ (caang3); a colloquial word[.]

    • To stretch out, to open;
    • kick at;
    • chʻáng꜄ ꜀iú ꜃kʻí chü꜅ (caang3 jiu1 kei5 zyu6 撐腰企住) to stand akimbo;
    • chʻáng꜄ ꜀hoi ꜀chéung ꜁mún (caang3 hoi1 coeng1 mun4-2 住開窗門), fasten open the window;
    • chʻáng꜄ chü꜅ (caang3 zyu6 撐住) to open by a stretcher;
    • chʻáng꜄ ꜃kʻü chʻut꜆ hü꜄ (caang3 keoi5 ceot1 heoi3 撐佢出去) kick him out; expel him;
    • chʻáng꜄ ꜁ngá káu꜄ (caang3 ngaa4 gaau3 撐牙鉸) to praise one's self.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 撐佢出去: This should probably fall under the leg-specific version of chʻáng꜄ (caang3); see 𨅝 caang3. (TODO)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Strictly, 棖 should be read ꜁chʻéung (coeng4) (per Kangxi 《唐韻古音》直良切音長) for the name 申棖.
  • The reading ꜁chʻáng (caang4) for 棖 is not present in Williams; an entry is included here for completeness.
    1. A door-post.
    2. Alternative form of 橙.

  • (Page 21 ꜁橙)
    • The coolie orange;
    • ꜁chʻáng ꜂tsau (caang4-2 zau2 橙酒), spirits in which orange peel has been soaked;
    • ꜁tʻong ꜁chʻáng (tong4 caang4-2 糖橙), orange sweetmeats;
    • ꜁chʻáng kòm꜄ ꜁mán (caang4-2 gam3 maan4 橙咁蠻) sharp as an orange;
    • tʻím ꜁chʻáng (tim4 caang4-2 甜橙), sweet oranges;
    • ꜀shán ꜁chʻáng (saan1 caang4-2 山橙), a melodinus.

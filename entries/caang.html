撐 ꜀Chʻáng (caang1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 12
Unicode code point
U+6490
Alternative forms
  • 撑
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 30 撐 ꜀Chʻang (cang1)) 窓爭切駕船也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 30 撐) 策平聲以物撐物也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜀撐꜀撑)
    • To prop, to shore up;
    • to fasten open, as with a stretcher;
    • to pole;
    • to push off (Kangxi 撐: 撥也);
    • to make a show;
    • an inclined post (Kangxi: 與樘同, 樘衺柱也), a fulcrum, a prop, a stay;
    • to border on, to adjoin, to stretch or run up into;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁shün (caang1 syun4 撐船), to pole a boat;
    • ꜀chʻáng tò꜅ (caang1 dou6 撐渡) to pole across the ferry; to intrigue for a friend with the officer;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁'m ꜁lai (caang1 m4 lai4 撐唔嚟), he could not show off long;
    • ꜀chʻung ꜀chʻáng (cung1 caang1 充撐), to curry favor among friends;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜃shéung hü꜄ shái꜄ (caang1 soeng5 heoi3 saai3 撐上去曬) stretch them up to dry.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "inclined post": According to 段玉裁's annotations of 《說文》, the qualifier 衺 (inclined) should not be present before 柱 (pillar).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鐺】 (caang1):
    1. A cooking pot with a flat bottom.
      • 飯鐺 (faan6 caang1): "Rice [cooking] pot."
      • 鐺湯凍咗 (caang1 tong1 dung3 zo2): "[The] pot of broth [has] gone cold."

Olden attestation for 鐺 ꜀chʻang (cang1) or ꜀chʻáng (caang1) meaning "pot":

《廣韻》楚庚切：鎗：鼎類
《廣韻》楚庚切：鐺：俗本音當
《康熙字典》鎗：《唐韻》楚庚切《集韻》《韻會》楚耕切𠀤音璫[…]　又《廣韻》鼎類《六書故》三足鬴也俗作鐺[…]
《康熙字典》鐺：[…]又《廣韻》楚庚切《集韻》《韻會》楚耕切𠀤音鎗釜屬《通俗文》鬴有足曰鐺[…]

The full story is that:

  • The original character is 鎗, read ꜀chʻang (cang1), meaning a pot with legs.
  • Later, 鐺 is used vulgarly for 鎗.
  • Later, the character 罉 is created for the Cantonese vernacular sense of flat-bottomed pot.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams does not consider leg-thrusting 𨅝 to be a different word to the generic 撐. I have split things up accordingly.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【𨅝】 (caang3, jaang3):
    1. To thrust outward or down with the leg or the foot.
      • 一腳𨅝開 (jat1 goek3 jaang3 hoi1): "Thrust away [in] one [action] of the foot."
      • 𨅝樓梯 (jaang3 lau4 tai1): "Leg-thrust upon stairs" (i.e. climb stairs).
    2. To brace or support by extension of the leg.
  • 【𨅝檯腳】 (caang3 toi4-2 goek3, jaang3 toi4-2 goek3):
    1. To eat or discourse at the same table. (Literally, "kick/brace with legs [the] table's legs".)

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 88 橙) 棑橙果名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜁橙)
    • The coolie orange (Kangxi: 橘屬 etc.);
    • ꜁chʻáng ꜂tsau (caang4-2 zau2 橙酒), spirits in which orange peel has been soaked;
    • ꜁tʻong ꜁chʻáng (tong4 caang4-2 糖橙), orange sweetmeats;
    • ꜁chʻáng kòm꜄ ꜁mán (caang4-2 gam3 maan4 橙咁蠻) sharp as an orange;
    • ꜁tʻím ꜁chʻáng (tim4 caang4-2 甜橙), sweet oranges;
    • ꜀shán ꜁chʻáng (saan1 caang4-2 山橙), a melodinus.
Conway's commentary
  • The original level reading caang4 is almost never heard, having been supplanted by vernacular rising caang4-2 in all senses.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The colour orange.

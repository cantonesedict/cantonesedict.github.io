Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鐺】 (caang1):
    1. A cooking pot with a flat bottom.
      • 飯鐺 (faan6 caang1): "Rice [cooking] pot."
      • 鐺湯凍咗 (caang1 tong1 dung3 zo2): "[The] pot of broth [has] gone cold."

Olden attestation for 鐺 ꜀chʻang (cang1) or ꜀chʻáng (caang1) meaning "pot":

  • 《廣韻》楚庚切：鎗：鼎類
  • 《廣韻》楚庚切：鐺：俗本音當
  • 《康熙字典》鎗：《唐韻》楚庚切《集韻》《韻會》楚耕切𠀤音璫[…]　又《廣韻》鼎類《六書故》三足鬴也俗作鐺[…]
  • 《康熙字典》鐺：[…]又《廣韻》楚庚切《集韻》《韻會》楚耕切𠀤音鎗釜屬《通俗文》鬴有足曰鐺[…]

The full story is that:

  • The original character is 鎗, read ꜀chʻang (cang1), meaning a pot with legs.
  • Later, 鐺 (otherwise read ꜀tong (dong1), an onomatopoeia for bells), is used vulgarly for 鎗.
  • Later, the character 罉 is created for the Cantonese vernacular sense of flat-bottomed pot.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【𨅝】 (caang3, jaang3):
    1. To thrust outward or down with the leg or the foot.
      • 一腳𨅝開 (jat1 goek3 jaang3 hoi1): "Thrust away [in] one [action] of the foot."
      • 𨅝樓梯 (jaang3 lau4 tai1): "Leg-thrust upon stairs" (i.e. climb stairs).
    2. To brace or support by extension of the leg.
  • 【𨅝檯腳】 (caang3 toi4-2 goek3, jaang3 toi4-2 goek3):
    1. To eat or discourse at the same table. (Literally, "kick/brace with legs [the] table's legs".)

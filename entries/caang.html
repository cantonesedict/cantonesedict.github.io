Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21 ꜀撐꜀撑)
    • To prop, to shore up;
    • to fasten open, as with a stretcher;
    • to pole;
    • to push off;
    • to make a show;
    • an inclined post, a fulcrum, a prop, a stay;
    • to border on, to adjoin, to stretch or run up into;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁shün (caang1 syun4 撐船), to pole a boat;
    • ꜀chʻáng tò꜅ (caang1 dou6 撐渡) to pole across the ferry; to intrigue for a friend with the officer;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜁'m ꜁lai (caang1 m4 lai4 撐唔嚟), he could not show off long;
    • ꜀chʻung ꜀chʻáng (cung1 caang1 充撐), to curry favor among friends;
    • ꜀chʻáng ꜃shéung hü꜄ shái꜄ (caang1 soeng5 heoi3 saai3 撐上去曬) stretch them up to dry.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啫】 (ze1):
    1. A final particle of limitation, especially used defensively.
      • 少少啫 (siu2 siu2 ze1): "[It's] just [a] little!"

  • 【啫喱】 (ze1 lei2):

嗟 ꜀Tsé (ze1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 10
Unicode code point
U+55DF
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 83 嗟) 嘆詞又痛[惜]也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 83 𭋦) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 563 ꜀嗟)
    • To sigh, to lament (Kangxi: 嗟歎也);
    • painful regrets (Kangxi: 一曰痛惜也);
    • an interjection of admiration (Kangxi: 咨也);
    • ah! alas! (Kangxi: 嗟佐也言不足以盡意故發此聲以自佐也)
    • ꜀tsé tʻán꜄ (ze1 taan3 嗟嘆) to sigh;
    • ꜀tsé ꜁loi ꜀chí shik꜇ (ze1 loi4 zi1 sik6 嗟來之食) to groan and beg one's bread.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected ⿰亻肯 to 惜.
  • Williams' 嗟來之食: Not to beg for food, but food given in contempt. Per 《禮檀弓》, the phrase 嗟來食 "alas come eat" was the utterance of the wealthy man 黔敖 as he handed out food and drink to the famished.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【遮】 (ze1):
    1. An umbrella.
      • 開遮 (hoi1 ze1): "Open [an] umbrella."
      • 擔遮 (daam1 ze1): "Hold up [an] umbrella."
      • 收遮 (sau1 ze1): "Close up [an] umbrella."
      • 縮骨遮 (suk1 gwat1 ze1): "Retractable-stem umbrella" (i.e. a pocket or foldable umbrella).

姐 ꜂Tsé (ze2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼥ + 5
Unicode code point
U+59D0
Reading variations
  • ze2-1 (vernacular)
  • ze2-4 (low endearing suffix after a name; also low first in reduplication 姐姐 ze2-4 ze2-1)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 姐) 姊也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 563 ꜂姐)
    • An elder sister (Kangxi: 今俗弟呼女兄曰姐);
    • a miss;
    • formerly applied to one's mother (Kangxi: 蜀人呼母曰姐);
    • proud, saucy (Kangxi: 一曰慢也);
    • ꜂tsé múi꜅ (ze2 mui6-2 姐妹) sisters;
    • tái꜅ ꜂tsé (daai6 ze2-1 大姐), my elder sister; also, a marriageable maidservant;
    • ꜂siú ꜂tsé (siu2 ze2 小姐), a young miss;
    • ling꜅ ꜂tsé (ling6 ze2 令姐), your sister;
    • ꜂tsé ꜀fú (ze2 fu1 姐夫), a sister's husband;
    • á꜄ ꜂tsé (aa3 ze2 阿姐), sister; also a term for a father's concubine.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 姐妹: The preferred term for "sisters" in Cantonese is 姊妹.
  • Williams' 阿姐: This is the meaning when 姐 is read in the original rising tone.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【阿姐】 (aa3 ze2-1):
    1. Generic address for an adult woman when the speaker is not a child. (Can be respectful, neutral, or impolite depending on the context.)
  • 【家姐】 (gaa1 ze2-1):
    1. Older sister (or older female paternal cousin).
  • 【細姐】 (sai3 ze2):
    1. Father's concubine (presumed to be younger than his wife).
See also

者 ꜂Ché (ze2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽼ + 4
Unicode code point
U+8005
Han unification vigilance
May appear dotless (assumed here) or dotted, depending on the font.
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 者) 語助詞指人指物之稱
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜂者)
    • A relative or demonstrative pronoun (Kangxi: 此也[…]今俗多用這字);
    • it, this, that, he, who, what;
    • following verbs, it forms a noun, as
    • ꜁hang ꜂ché (hang4 ze2 行者), a walker, he who is walking;
    • after nouns, indicates a class, as
    • ꜁ü ꜂ché (jyu4 ze2 愚者), the foolish;
    • [꜂]sz' ꜂ché (si2 ze2 死者), the dead;
    • ꜁mò ꜁tsʻing ꜂ché (mou4 cing4 ze2 無情者), people who are without affection;
    • or as put in the abstract, as
    • sing꜄ ꜂ché (sing3 ze2 性者), nature;
    • ꜁shing ꜂ché (sing4 ze2 誠者), truth;
    • placed between two nouns or clauses, it puts them in apposition, as
    • tak꜆ ꜂ché, ꜂pún ꜃yá (dak1 ze2 bun2 ja5 德者本也), virtue, that is the root;
    • ꜁yan ꜂ché ngáu꜅ ꜀shán (jan4 ze2 ngaau6 saan1 仁者樂山) the humane man delights in hills;
    • it is often a mere pause to arrest the attention;
    • ꜁ho ꜁wai ꜂ché (ho4 wai4 ze2 何為者), what are you doing?
    • ꜀chung ꜃yá ꜂ché ꜀tʻín há[꜅] ꜀chí tái꜅ ꜂pún ꜃yá (zung1 jaa5 ze2 tin1 haa6 zi1 daai6 bun2 jaa5 中也者天下之大本也), the medium is everywhere the chief point;
    • sik꜆ ꜂ché (sik1 ze2 昔者), formerly;
    • wák꜇ ꜂ché (waak6 ze2 或者), perhaps.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "after nouns": This should say "after adjectives".
  • Williams' 死者: Corrected sz'꜄ (si3) to ꜂sz' (si2).
  • Williams' 中也者天下之大本也: Changed ꜃há (haa5) to modern há꜅ (haa6).

這 ꜂Ché (ze2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾡ + 7
Unicode code point
U+9019
Reading variations
  • ze3 (per Fan Wan)
  • ze5 (common)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 這 Ché꜄ (ze3)) 是也此也這裏言此處也這事言此事也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 這꜄ Ché꜄ (ze3); here normalised to ꜂Ché (ze2))
    • A demonstrative pronoun, this, that;
    • [꜂]ché ꜃[lü] (ze2 leoi5 這裡), here;
    • [꜂]ché yéung꜅ (ze2 joeng6 這樣) thus, so;
    • [꜂]ché ꜂tang (ze2 dang2 這等), such, this sort;
    • [꜂]ché ꜁shí (ze2 si4 這時), now.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 這裡: Changed ꜃lí (lei5) to common reading ꜃lü (leoi5).
  • The character 這 is only attested in the Middle Chinese rime books under the reading 魚變切 yín꜅ (jin6), explained 迎也. Kangxi has 毛晃曰凡稱此箇爲者箇俗多改用這字這乃迎也, which proposes that 這 is a vulgar substitute for 者 when used as a demonstrative "this".

借 Tsé꜄ (ze3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 8
Unicode code point
U+501F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 借) 假借借貸又推獎也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 564 借꜄)
    • To assist (Kangxi: 助也);
    • to lend, to borrow (Kangxi: 假也貸也);
    • to ask for, to beg of;
    • to make a pretext of;
    • to pretend, to assume, to suppose;
    • if, for example, supposing, assuming (Kangxi: 設辭);
    • to use for illustration;
    • to praise (Kangxi: 推獎也);
    • tsé꜄ ꜁loi (ze3 loi4 借來), to borrow;
    • tsé꜄ hü꜄ (ze3 heoi3 借去) to lend;
    • tsé꜄ ꜁ngan (ze3 ngan4-2 借銀), to ask for money;
    • tsé꜄ ꜀tán (ze3 daan1 借單), a borrower's note;
    • tsé꜄ ꜀shám (ze3 saam1 借衫), to borrow a jacket;
    • ꜀séung tsé꜄ (soeng1 ze3 相借) to borrow of;
    • tsé꜄ ꜁ü (ze3 jyu4 借如), supposing that;
    • tsé꜄ shai꜄ ꜁wai ok꜆ (ze3 sai3 wai4 ok3 借勢為惡) to avail of power to do wickedly;
    • tsé꜄ chéuk[꜇] ꜃ngo sz'꜅ (ze3 zoek6 ngo5 si6 借着我事) he's got my business, he's stole my thunder;
    • tsé꜄ ko꜄ ꜀kwong (ze3 go3 gwong1 借個光), to use another's name, e. g. to get a letter of introduction;
    • tsé꜄ ꜂mé tik꜆ (ze3 me2 dik1 借歪滴) please step aside a little;
    • tsé꜄ lò꜅ ꜁[háng] (ze3 lou6 haang4 借路行), to beg a way through;
    • tsé꜄ í꜄ (ze3 ji3 借意) metaphorically;
    • tsé꜄ ꜁lai ꜂kong (ze3 lai4 gong2 借嚟講), said it for illustration, meant nothing by it;
    • tsé꜄ kʻít꜆ (ze3 kit3 借揭) to get a loan on interest.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 借着我事: Corrected chéuk꜆ (zoek3) to chéuk꜇ (zoek6).
  • Williams' 借路行: Changed ꜁hang (hang4) to vernacular reading ꜁háng (haang4).

嗻 Ché꜄ (ze3)

⼝ + 11

U+55FB
Alternative forms
  • 啫
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 31 啫꜄; here normalised to 嗻)

    A colloquial particle.

    • Just now;
    • a short time, for the nonce, shortly;
    • a form of the subjunctive;
    • ꜂tang ꜃há ché꜄ (dang2 haa5 ze3 等吓嗻) stop a little;
    • ꜀fán ꜁lai ché꜄ (faan1 lai4 ze3 返嚟嗻) when I come back;
    • ꜂kòm yéung꜅ ché꜄ (gam2 joeng6-2 ze3 噉樣嗻) will this do?
    • ꜂tá kwo꜄ ché꜄ (daa2 gwo3 ze3 打過嗻) let me whip you;
    • ꜂tang ꜃ngo hü꜄ tsò꜅ mat꜆ ꜃yé ché꜄ (dang2 ngo5 heoi3 zou6 mat1 je5 ze3 等我去做乜嘢嗻) let me do that first.

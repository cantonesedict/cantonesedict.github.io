啫 ꜀Ché (ze1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 8
Unicode code point
U+556B
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
(Not present)
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啫】 (ze1):
    1. A final particle of limitation, especially used defensively.
      • 少少啫 (siu2 siu2 ze1): "[It's] just [a] little!"
      • 講笑啫 (gong2 siu3 ze1): "Just kidding!"
  • 【啫喱】 (ze1 lei2):
    1. Jelly.

遮 ꜀Ché (ze1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾡ + 11
Unicode code point
U+906E
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 83 遮) 掩也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜀遮)
    • To cover, to screen, to vail, to conceal;
    • to shade;
    • to intercept;
    • a parasol, a shade;
    • ꜀ché ꜂ím (ze1 jim2 遮掩), to secrete, to screen;
    • ꜀ché kʻoi꜄ (ze1 koi3 遮蓋) to cloak, to cover;
    • ꜀ché ꜀sau (ze1 sau1 遮羞), to conceal the face (as females);
    • ꜃ü ꜀ché (jyu5 ze1 雨遮), an umbrella;
    • ꜁'m ꜀ché tak꜆ chü꜅ (m4 ze1 dak1 zyu6 唔遮得住) it [won't] cover us, it can not be concealed;
    • ꜀ché shik꜆ (ze1 sik1 遮飾) to disguise, to dupe, throw dust in his eyes;
    • ꜀ché ꜁lán (ze1 laan4 遮攔), to fend off, to ward off;
    • ꜀ché yat꜇ ꜁tʻau (ze1 jat6 tau4-2 遮日頭), keep the sun off;
    • ꜀ché ꜁mún (ze1 mun4 遮瞞), to hide from.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【遮】 (ze1):
    1. An umbrella.
      • 開遮 (hoi1 ze1): "Open [an] umbrella."
      • 擔遮 (daam1 ze1): "Hold up [an] umbrella."
      • 收遮 (sau1 ze1): "Close up [an] umbrella."
      • 縮骨遮 (suk1 gwat1 ze1): "Retractable-stem umbrella" (i.e. a pocket or foldable umbrella).

꜂Ché ꜂Tsé (ze2 陰上)

  • 者

者 ꜂Ché (ze2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽼ + 4
Unicode code point
U+8005
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 84 者) 語助詞指人指物之稱
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 30 ꜂者)
    • A relative or demonstrative pronoun;
    • it, this, that, he, who, what;
    • following verbs, it forms a noun, as
    • ꜁hang ꜂ché (hang4 ze2 行者), a walker, he who is walking;
    • after nouns, indicates a class, as
    • ꜁ü ꜂ché (jyu4 ze2 愚者), the foolish;
    • [꜂]sz' ꜂ché (si2 ze2 死者), the dead;
    • ꜁mò ꜁tsʻing ꜂ché (mou4 cing4 ze2 無情者), people who are without affection;
    • or as put in the abstract, as
    • sing꜄ ꜂ché (sing3 ze2 性者), nature;
    • ꜁shing ꜂ché (sing4 ze2 誠者), truth;
    • placed between two nouns or clauses, it puts them in apposition, as
    • tak꜆ ꜂ché, ꜂pún ꜃yá (dak1 ze2 bun2 ja5 德者本也), virtue, that is the root;
    • ꜁yan ꜂ché ngáu꜅ ꜀shán (jan4 ze2 ngaau6 saan1 仁者樂山) the humane man delights in hills;
    • it is often a mere pause to arrest the attention;
    • ꜁ho ꜁wai ꜂ché (ho4 wai4 ze2 何為者), what are you doing?
    • ꜀chung ꜃yá ꜂ché ꜀tʻín há[꜅] ꜀chí tái꜅ ꜂pún ꜃yá (zung1 jaa5 ze2 tin1 haa6 zi1 daai6 bun2 jaa5 中也者天下之大本也), the medium is everywhere the chief point;
    • sik꜆ ꜂ché (sik1 ze2 昔者), formerly;
    • wák꜇ ꜂ché (waak6 ze2 或者), perhaps.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 死者: Corrected sz'꜄ (si3) to ꜂sz' (si2).
  • Williams' 中也者天下之大本也: Changed Middle Chinese ꜃há (haa5) to common reading há꜅ (haa6).

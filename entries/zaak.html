Conway's commentary
  • This character (in the vernacular "weight/crush" senses) is commonly written 砸. In my opinion, 矺 is a better character to use than 砸 because:
    1. 矺 is attested in Middle Chinese with both reasonable sound and reasonable meaning (zaak3 per 《廣韻》陟革切, explained 磓也, to stone); and
    2. 砸 is otherwise read zaap3.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【矺】 (zaak3, zaak6):
    1. To weigh down with a heavy object.
      • 矺住張紙 (zaak6 zyu6 zoeng1 zi2): "Weigh down [the] piece of paper."
    2. To crush with a heavy object.
      • 矺扁咗 (zaak3 bin2 zo2): "Squashed flat."

Olden attestation for 矺 chák꜆ (zaak3) meaning "weight/crush":

《廣韻》陟革切：矺：磓也
《康熙字典》矺：《廣韻》《集韻》𠀤陟格切音舴同磔省詳磔字註　又《廣韻》磓也[…]

Here, 磓 is either to stone downwards or to gather stones (in a heap):

《廣韻》都回切：磓：落也亦作塠
《康熙字典》磓：《廣韻》《集韻》《韻會》《正韻》𠀤都回切音堆聚石也又以石投下也亦作塠[…]

    • Going back and forth (Kangxi: 躑躅猶踟躕也);
    • embarrassed, undecided (Kangxi: 躑躅行不進也);
    • chák꜇ chuk꜇ (zaak6 zuk6 躑躅) irresolute.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "embarrassed": In the (now) archaic sense of "impeded", "obstructed".

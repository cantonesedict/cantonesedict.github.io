矺 Chák꜆ (zaak3)

Radical + residual strokes
石 + 3
Unicode code point
U+77FA
Alternative forms
  • 砸
  • 磧
  • 責
Reading variations
  • zaak6 (alternative)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 責꜆; here normalised to 矺)
    • (…)
    • to lay a weight on;
    • to squeeze smooth, to press;
    • (…)
    • chák꜆ chü꜅ ꜃kʻü (zaak3 zyu6 keoi5 矺住佢), keep it down;
    • chák꜆ ꜂sz' (zaak3 si2 / zaak3 sei2 矺死);
    • (…)
    • chák꜆ tsoi꜅ (zaak3 zoi6 矺載) to ballast a ship;
    • chák꜆ lán꜅ (zaak3 laan6 矺爛) crushed in pieces.
Conway's commentary
  • This character (in the colloquial "weight/crush" senses) is commonly written 砸. In my opinion, 矺 is a better character to use than 砸 because:
    1. 矺 is attested in Middle Chinese with both reasonable sound and reasonable meaning (zaak3 from 《廣韻》陟革切, explained 磓也, to stone); and
    2. 砸 is otherwise read zaap3.
Conway's entry
  • 【矺】 (zaak3, zaak6):
    1. To weigh down with a heavy object.
      • 矺住張紙 (zaak6 zyu6 zoeng1 zi2): "Weigh down [the] piece of paper."
    2. To crush with a heavy object.
      • 矺扁咗 (zaak3 bin2 zo2): "Squashed flat."

宅 Chák꜇ (zaak6)

Radical + residual strokes
宀 + 3
Unicode code point
U+5B85
Reading variations
  • zaak6-2 (colloquial rising-tone noun)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 13 宅꜇)
    • A dwelling, a mansion, a residence;
    • a grave;
    • to dwell, to fix, to settle;
    • to conform to;
    • an office, a station;
    • ꜁yéung chák꜇ (joeng4 zaak6 陽宅) houses, residences;
    • tái꜅ chák꜇ í꜅ chák꜇ (daai6 zaak6-2 ji6 zaak6-2 大宅二宅) eldest son and second son's dwellings;
    • ꜀yam chák꜇ (jam1 zaak6 陰宅) a grave; ancestral halls or shrines;
    • chák꜇ [shiú]꜅ (zaak6 siu6 宅兆) to divine for a grave.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 宅兆: Changed Middle Chinese chiú꜅ (ziu6) to common reading shiú꜅ (siu6).

翟 Chák꜇ (zaak6)

Radical + residual strokes
羽 + 8
Unicode code point
U+7FDF
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 13 翟꜇)
    • A surname.

    This and the last (摘) are also read tʻik꜆ (tik1) with the same meanings.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' also read tʻik꜆ etc.: this is not quite correct.
    • 摘 is also read tʻik꜆ (tik1) (expected from 他歷切); but
    • 翟 is also read tik꜇ (dik6) (expected from 徒歷切).

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 責꜆; here normalised to 矺)
    • (…)
    • to lay a weight on;
    • to squeeze smooth, to press;
    • (…)
    • chák꜆ chü꜅ ꜃kʻü (zaak3 zyu6 keoi5 矺住佢), keep it down;
    • chák꜆ ꜂sz' (zaak3 si2 / zaak3 sei2 矺死);
    • (…)
    • chák꜆ tsoi꜅ (zaak3 zoi6 矺載) to ballast a ship;
    • chák꜆ lán꜅ (zaak3 laan6 矺爛) crushed in pieces.
Conway's commentary
  • This character (in the colloquial "weight/crush" senses) is commonly written 砸. In my opinion, 矺 is a better character to use than 砸 because:
    1. 矺 is attested in Middle Chinese with both reasonable sound and reasonable meaning (zaak3 from 《廣韻》陟革切, explained 磓也, to stone); and
    2. 砸 is otherwise read zaap3.
Conway's entry
  • 【矺】 (zaak3, zaak6):
    1. To weigh down with a heavy object.
      • 矺住張紙 (zaak6 zyu6 zoeng1 zi2): "Weigh down [the] piece of paper."
    2. To crush with a heavy object.
      • 矺扁咗 (zaak3 bin2 zo2): "Squashed flat."

責 Chák꜆ (zaak3)

Radical + residual strokes
貝 + 4
Unicode code point
U+8CAC
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 責꜆)
    • To reprove, to reprimand, to blame;
    • to punish, to fine;
    • to sustain, to be responsible for, to have charge of;
    • (see 矺 zaak3 for "weight/crush" senses)
    • to ask, to demand;
    • chák꜆ fat꜇ (zaak3 fat6 責罰) to fine;
    • (…)
    • chák꜆ ꜁[shéung] (zaak3 soeng4 責償), to compel repayment;
    • chák꜆ [fat]꜇ (zaak3 fat6 責罰) to punish;
    • chák꜆ man꜅ (zaak3 man6 責問) to subject to the question;
    • chák꜆ ꜁shing (zaak3 sing4 責成), to charge with;
    • chák꜆ yam꜅ sham꜅ chung꜅ (zaak3 jam6 sam6 zung6 責任甚重) onerous duties devolve on him;
    • (…).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 責償: Changed improper reading ꜁chʻéung (coeng4) to ꜁shéung (soeng4).
  • Williams' 責罰 (second occurrence): Changed fát꜇ (faat6) to modern fat꜇ (fat6).

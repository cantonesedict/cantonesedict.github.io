Conway's commentary
  • This character (in the vernacular "weight/crush" senses) is commonly written з ё. In my opinion, зҹә is a better character to use than з ё because:
    1. зҹә is attested in Middle Chinese with both reasonable sound and reasonable meaning (zaak3 per гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢйҷҹйқ©еҲҮ, explained зЈ“д№ҹ, to stone downward); and
    2. з ё is otherwise read zaap3.
  • This character (in the vernacular "weight/crush" senses) is sometimes written з¬®. In my opinion, з¬® is a poor character to use because it is not specific to pressure being applied vertically. Indeed for з¬®, Kangxi has еҸҲгҖҠеўһйҹ»гҖӢеЈ“д№ҹйҖҡдҪңжҺӘгҖҠеҸІиЁҳжўҒдё–е®¶гҖӢжқҺеӨӘеҗҺиҲҮзҲӯй–ҖжҺӘжҢҮгҖҠиЁ»гҖӢжҷүзҒјжӣ°жҺӘеӯ—еҖҹд»ҘзҲІз¬®её«еҸӨжӣ°зҲІй–ҖжүҮжүҖз¬®, and in this example I think it more likely for the dowager's finger to have been crushed horizontally by the door, rather than vertically.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗзҹәгҖ‘ (zaak3, zaak6)
    1. To weigh down with a heavy object.
      • зҹәдҪҸејөзҙҷ (zaak6 zyu6 zoeng1 zi2): "Weigh down [the] piece of paper."
    2. To crush (vertically) with a heavy object.

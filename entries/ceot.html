出 Chʻut꜆ (ceot1)

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 55 出) 外也退也生也發也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 42 出꜆)
    • To go out (Kangxi: 出入也), to issue (Kangxi: 吐也), to proceed forth (Kangxi: 進也), to manifest (Kangxi: 見也), according to the tenor of the following or preceding word;
    • to eject, to put out;
    • an auxiliary verb, implying completion or action;
    • chʻut꜆ yap꜆ yap꜇ (ceot1 jap6 出入) to go in and out, back and forth;
    • ꜂sé chʻut꜆ ꜁loi (se2 ceot1 loi4 寫出來), will write out;
    • ꜁chʻá chʻut꜆ ꜁loi (caa4 ceot1 loi4 查出來), about to examine;
    • chʻut꜆ chung꜄ (ceot1 zung3 出眾) preëminent, to surpass;
    • ꜃ní ꜀pín chʻü꜄ chʻut꜆ shai꜄ (nei5 bin1 cyu3 ceot1 sai3 你邊處出世) where were you born?
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀kung (ceot1 gung1 出恭), to retire, to ease nature;
    • chʻut꜆ ká꜄ (ceot1 gaa3 出嫁) to marry a husband;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀shan (ceot1 san1 出身), to hold office;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜁yau pò꜅ ꜁pʻò sát꜆ (ceot1 jau4 pou4 saat3 出遊菩薩) to carry idols in procession;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀kí ꜁kʻí (ceot1 kei4 出奇), surprising;
    • chʻut꜆ pʻiú꜄ (ceot1 piu3 出票) to issue a warrant, to advertise;
    • chʻut꜆ hí꜄ (ceot1 hei3 出氣) to fume or steam, to avenge another's quarrel;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜁tʻau (ceot1 tau4 出頭), to take the lead;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜂chʻau (ceot1 cau2 出醜), to be despised make a fool of oneself;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀tsʻai (ceot1 cai1 出妻), to divorce;
    • chʻut꜆ kai꜄ (ceot1 gai3 出繼) to be adopted by an uncle;
    • ꜁tsʻui ꜃ní chʻut꜆ ꜀sam (ceoi4 nei5 ceot1 sam1 隨你出心), as you like to do it;
    • hon꜄ ꜁'m chʻut꜆ séung꜄ (hon3 m4 ceot1 soeng3 看唔出相) to belie his looks;
    • ꜂séung ꜁'m chʻut꜆ (soeng2 m4 ceot1 想唔出) can not recall to mind;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜁ngan (ceot1 ngan4-2 出銀), to pay;
    • pat꜆ chʻut꜆ ꜂sho liú꜄ liú꜅ (bat1 ceot1 so2 liu6 不出所料) to guess aright.
Conway's commentary
  • There is also the reading variation chʻui꜄ (ceoi3) per 《廣韻》尺類切 (unexplained). Kangxi has 《正韻》凡物自出則入聲非自出而出之則去聲然亦有互用者, which prescribes entering tone chʻut꜆ (ceot1) for the intransitive verb senses and departing tone chʻui꜄ (ceoi3) for the transitive verb senses (though conceding some degree of interchangeability). In modern usage, this distinction is absolutely obsolete, and chʻut꜆ (ceot1) is the only reading in all senses.
  • Williams' 出入: Corrected yap꜆ (jap1) to yap꜇ (jap6).
  • Williams' 出恭: That is, to go to the toilet.
  • Williams' 出遊菩薩: Corrected pò꜅ (bou6) to ꜁pʻò (pou4).
  • Williams' 出奇: Corrected ꜀kí (gei1) to ꜁kʻí (kei4).
  • Williams' 不出所料: Corrected liú꜄ (liu3) to liú꜅ (liu6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To supply, to put forth.
  2. To produce.
  3. A sister's child (Kangxi: 生也, 男子謂姊妹之子爲出).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【出】 (tsyhwijH)
    • (verb-like) [to] bring out, [to] send forth
    • (verb-like) [to] supply
  • 【出】 (tsyhwit)
    • (verb-like) [to] go out
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【出】 (ceot1)
    1. To go out to a place.
      • 出街 (ceot1 gaai1): "Go out."
      • 出城 (ceot1 seng4-2): "Go out to [the] city."
    2. Out.
      • 出便 (ceot1 bin6): "Outside."
      • 褪出啲 (tan3 ceot1 di1): "Shift [it] out [a] bit."

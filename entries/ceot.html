Conway's commentary
  • гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢ does not explain the departing-tone reading е°әйЎһеҲҮ chК»uiкң„ (ceoi3). Kangxi has гҖҠжӯЈйҹ»гҖӢеҮЎзү©иҮӘеҮәеүҮе…ҘиҒІйқһиҮӘеҮәиҖҢеҮәд№ӢеүҮеҺ»иҒІз„¶дәҰжңүдә’з”ЁиҖ…, which prescribes entering tone chК»utкңҶ (ceot1) for the intransitive verb senses and departing tone chК»uiкң„ (ceoi3) for the transitive verb senses (though conceding some degree of interchangeability). In modern usage, this distinction is absolutely obsolete, and chК»utкңҶ (ceot1) is the only reading in all senses.
  • Williams' еҮәе…Ҙ: Corrected yapкңҶ (jap1) to yapкңҮ (jap6).
  • Williams' еҮәжҒӯ: That is, to go to the toilet.
  • Williams' еҮәйҒҠиҸ©и–©: Corrected pГІкң… (bou6) to кңҒpК»ГІ (pou4).
  • Williams' еҮәеҘҮ: Corrected кңҖkГӯ (gei1) to кңҒkК»Гӯ (kei4).
  • Williams' дёҚеҮәжүҖж–ҷ: Corrected liГәкң„ (liu3) to liГәкң… (liu6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To supply, to put forth.
  2. To produce.
  3. A sister's child (Kangxi: з”ҹд№ҹ, з”·еӯҗи¬Ӯе§ҠеҰ№д№ӢеӯҗзҲІеҮә).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • гҖҗеҮәгҖ‘ (ceot1)
    1. To go out to a place.
      • еҮәиЎ— (ceot1 gaai1): "Go out."
      • еҮәеҹҺ (ceot1 seng4-2): "Go out to [the] city."
    2. Out.
      • еҮәдҫҝ (ceot1 bin6): "Outside."
      • иӨӘеҮәе•І (tan3 ceot1 di1): "Shift [it] out [a] bit."

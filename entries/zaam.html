簪 ꜀Tsám (zaam1)

Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 63 簪) 首飾也又聚也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 553 ꜀簪)
    • A hairpin, clasp, or bodkin, used to confine the hair or fasten the coiffure (Kangxi: 首筓也);
    • to insert in the hair, to stick on the head;
    • quick, brisk, to collect speedily (Kangxi: 疾也);
    • ꜀tsám ꜃pʻáng (zaam1 paang5 簪棒), a hairpin;
    • ꜀tsám ꜀fá (zaam1 faa1 簪花), to stick flowers in the hair;
    • ꜃niú ꜀tsám (niu5 zaam1 裊簪), a gay headstall;
    • yuk꜇ ꜀tsám ꜀fá (juk6 zaam1 faa1 玉簪花), the tuberose;
    • ꜀kai ꜀tsám ꜃mai (gai1 zaam1 mai5 雞簪米), the fowl pecks the rice—a colloquial phrase.
  • (Page 554 ꜂篸 Tsʻám (caam2); here corrected to ꜀Tsám (zaam1) and normalised to 簪)

斬 ꜂Chám (zaam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽄ + 7
Unicode code point
U+65AC
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 64 斬) 截也斷也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂斬)
    • To sunder, to cut in two (Kangxi: 截也, 裁也);
    • to cut off (Kangxi: 斬[…]卽斷也);
    • to execute, to decapitate;
    • capital;
    • to root up;
    • to break off, as [intercourse];
    • faded, forgotten, vanished;
    • ꜂chám ꜀[tsʻúi] (zaam2 ceoi1 斬衰), mourning for parents;
    • ꜂chám ꜃tʻün (zaam2 tyun5 斬斷), to cut in two, to break off, as a business;
    • ꜂chám ꜁tʻau (zaam2 tau4-2 斬頭), to decollate;
    • ꜀iú ꜂chám (jiu1 zaam2 腰斬), to divide a criminal in the middle;
    • ꜂chám ꜀teng tsít꜇ tʻít꜆ (zaam2 deng1 zit6 tit3 斬釘截鐵) to cut an iron nail—decided, sincere;
    • ꜀kám ꜂chám (gaam1 zaam2 監斬), to superintend an execution;
    • ꜂chám ꜀hoi (zaam2 hoi1 斬開), to cut open;
    • ꜂chám [fat]꜇ (zaam2 fat6 斬伐) to subjugate; to prune.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 斬衰: Corrected ꜀shui (seoi1) to ꜀tsʻúi (ceoi1).
  • Williams' 腰斬: Now used figuratively for midway cancellation or termination.
  • Williams' 斬伐: Changed fát꜇ (faat6) to modern fat꜇ (fat6).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【䁪】 (zaam2):
    1. To blink.

暫 Tsám꜅ (zaam6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 11
Unicode code point
U+66AB
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 65 暫) 卽淡切暫時間言不久也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 553 暫꜅)
    • A part of a day;
    • for the time being, briefly, shortly, for a short time, in the interim, meanwhile (Kangxi: 不久也, 須臾也);
    • suddenly (Kangxi: 暫猶卒也);
    • tsám꜅ ꜁shí (zaam6 si4 暫時), a litte while;
    • tsám꜅ ꜂yan (zaam6 jan2 暫忍), be patient;
    • tsám꜅ ü꜅ (zaam6 jyu6 暫遇) suddenly met him;
    • tsám꜅ chü꜅ (zaam6 zyu6 暫住) a temporary lodging;
    • ꜂ho tsám꜅ pat꜆ ꜂ho ꜁shéung (ho2 zaam6 bat1 ho2 soeng4 可暫不可常), it will do for a temporary arrangement.

站 Chám꜅ (zaam6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽴ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7AD9
Reading variations
  • zaam3 (per 《廣韻》陟陷切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 71 站 Chán꜅ (zaan6)) 站立也又音賺
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 站꜅)
    • A stage, a journey;
    • yik[꜇] chám꜅ (jik6 zaam6 驛站) a stage;
    • chám꜅ ꜁tʻau (zaam6 tau4 站頭), end of a journey;
    • yat꜆ chám꜅ lò꜅ (jat1 zaam6 lou6 一站路) a stage, usually reckoned to be a league.
  • (Page 19 站꜅ Chán꜅ (zaan6); here corrected to Chám꜅ (zaam6))
    • To stand (Kangxi: 久立也);
    • to stand erect;
    • to stop;
    • a stoppage;
    • [chám]꜅ ꜂hí ꜁loi (zaam6 hei2 loi4 站起來), stand up!
    • [chám]꜅ ꜀pán (zaam6 baan1 站班), to file off in rank, and salute an officer.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Note that Fan Wan considers chán꜅ (zaan6) to be the canonical reading of 站, with 賺 to be read chám꜅ (zaam6).
  • Williams' 驛站: Stage meaning a relay station for horses.

賺 Chám꜅ (zaam6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾙ + 10
Unicode code point
U+8CFA
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 65 賺) 中淡切猶眈誤也阻之也生意[獲]利亦曰賺
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 賺꜅)
    • To impede, to hinder (Fan Wan: 阻之也);
    • to sell (Kangxi: 賣也), (…);
    • to gain unfairly, to palm off poor goods (Kangxi: 一曰市物失實);
    • ꜂cho chám꜅ (zo2 zaan6 阻賺) to deceive; to hinder.

    (Otherwise, a reading variation (per 《集韻》直陷切). See 賺 zaan6 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The supplied dissection 中淡切 is incorrect, as it results in the 陰去 reading chám꜄ (zaam3), rather than the intended 陽去 reading chám꜅ (zaam6). Normalised 𫉬 to 獲.

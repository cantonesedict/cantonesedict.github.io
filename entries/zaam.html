꜀Tsám (zaam1 陰平)

  • 簪

簪 ꜀Tsám (zaam1)

Radical + residual strokes
竹 + 12
Unicode code point
U+7C2A
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 63 簪) 首飾也又聚也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 553 ꜀簪)
    • A hairpin, clasp, or bodkin, used to confine the hair or fasten the coiffure;
    • to insert in the hair, to stick on the head;
    • quick, brisk, to collect speedily;
    • ꜀tsám ꜃pʻáng (zaam1 paang5 簪棒), a hairpin;
    • ꜀tsám ꜀fá (zaam1 faa1 簪花), to stick flowers in the hair;
    • ꜃niú ꜀tsám (niu5 zaam1 裊簪), a gay headstall;
    • yuk꜇ ꜀tsám ꜀fá (juk6 zaam1 faa1 玉簪花), the tuberose;
    • ꜀kai ꜀tsám ꜃mai (gai1 zaam1 mai5 雞簪米), the fowl pecks the rice—a colloquial phrase.

꜂Chám (zaam2 陰上)

  • 眨

斬 ꜂Chám (zaam2)

Radical + residual strokes
斤 + 7
Unicode code point
U+65AC
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 64 斬) 截也斷也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂斬)
    • To sunder, to cut in two;
    • to cut off;
    • to execute, to decapitate;
    • capital;
    • to root up;
    • to break off, as [intercourse];
    • faded, forgotten, vanished;
    • ꜂chám ꜀shui (zaam2 zeoi1 斬衰), mourning for parents;
    • ꜂chám ꜃tʻün (zaam2 tyun5 斬斷), to cut in two, to break off, as a business;
    • ꜂chám ꜁tʻau (zaam2 tau4-2 斬頭), to decollate;
    • ꜀iú ꜂chám (jiu1 zaam2 腰斬), to divide a criminal in the middle;
    • ꜂chám ꜀teng tsít꜇ tʻít꜆ (zaam2 deng1 zit6 tit3 斬釘截鐵) to cut an iron nail—decided, sincere;
    • ꜀kám ꜂chám (gaam1 zaam2 監斬), to superintend an execution;
    • ꜂chám ꜀hoi (zaam2 hoi1 斬開), to cut open;
    • ꜂chám [fat]꜇ (zaam2 fat6 斬伐) to subjugate; to prune.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 腰斬: Now used figuratively for midway cancellation or termination.
  • Williams' 斬伐: Changed fát꜇ (faat6) to modern fat꜇ (fat6).

Alternative forms

(Not present)

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【䁪】 (zaam2):
    1. To blink.
      • 䁪吓眼 (zaam2 haa5 ngaan5): "[In the] blink of [an] eye."
    2. To flash.
      • 眼燈䁪緊 (ngaan5-2 dang1 zaam2 gan2): "[The] light's flashing."

Chám꜄ (zaam3 陰去)

  • 湛
  • 蘸

湛 Chám꜄ (zaam3)

Radical + residual strokes
水 + 9
Unicode code point
U+6E5B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 65 湛) 澄湛清也淡也又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 湛꜄)
    • A northern affluent of the Yellow R. near Shánsí (山西);
    • to sink in;
    • to soak, to immerse, to imbibe, as a sponge;
    • to plunge in;
    • deep, limpid, still, as water;
    • clear, calm, as a quiet moonlight.

蘸 Chám꜄ (zaam3)

Radical + residual strokes
艸 + 19
Unicode code point
U+8638
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 65 [蘸]) 以物淬[水]中
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 蘸꜄)
    • To dip, as a pencil in ink, or sop a morsel in gravy;
    • used by some for baptize.

    Vulgarly pronounced ꜃yám (jaam5); (see 蘸 jaam5).

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword ⿱艹⿰酉隹 (missing 灬) to 蘸, and corrected 禾中 to 水中.

Chám꜅ Tsám꜅ (zaam6 陽去)

  • 站
  • 賺

站 Chám꜅ (zaam6)

Radical + residual strokes
立 + 5
Unicode code point
U+7AD9
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 71 站 Chán꜅ (zaan6)) 站立也又音賺
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 站꜅)
    • A stage, a journey;
    • yik[꜇] chám꜅ (jik6 zaam6 驛站) a stage;
    • chám꜅ ꜁tʻau (zaam6 tau4 站頭), end of a journey;
    • yat꜆ chám꜅ lò꜅ (jat1 zaam6 lou6 一站路) a stage, usually reckoned to be a league.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Note that Fan Wan considers 賺 to be read chám꜅ (zaam6).
  • Williams' 驛站: Stage meaning a relay station for horses.

賺 Chám꜅ (zaam6)

Radical + residual strokes
貝 + 10
Unicode code point
U+8CFA
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 65 賺) 中淡切猶眈誤也阻之也生意[獲]利亦曰賺
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 賺꜅)

    (The expected reflex is zaam6 from 《集韻》直陷切, but the common reading is zaan6; see 賺 zaan6 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The supplied dissection 中淡切 is incorrect, as it results in the 陰去 reading chám꜄ (zaam3), rather than the intended 陽去 reading chám꜅ (zaam6). Normalised 𫉬 (⿱艹⿰犭隻) to 獲.

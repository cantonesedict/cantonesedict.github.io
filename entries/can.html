Conway's commentary
  • Williams provides no explanation for the vernacular ꜀tsʻan (can1), notwithstanding he has supplied examples of its use.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【嚫】 (can1)
    1. Suffixed after a verb to mark negative consequence.
      • 冷嚫 (laang5 can1): "[I've] caught [a] cold."
      • 撞嚫 (zong6 can1): "[I've] bumped [something]."
      • 整嚫 (zing2 can1): "[I've] hurt [myself]."
      • 跌嚫 (dit3 can1): "[I've] had [a] fall."
    2. Suffixed after a verb to indicate "every time".
      • 上嚫都漏 (soeng5 can1 dou1 lau6): "Every time [I] chow, [I] leak [good tiles]."
      • 射嚫都中 (se6 can1 dou1 zung3): "Every time [he] shoots, [he's] on target."
      • 飲嚫都係當歸 (jam2 can1 dou1 hai6 dong1 gwai1): "Every time [she] drinks [medicine], it's dong quai."

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 554 ꜀親)
    • To love, to be attached to (Kangxi: 愛也);
    • liking, pleased with;
    • near, intimate (Kangxi: 近也);
    • near to, belonging to one's self, personal, myself (Kangxi: 躬也, 親猶自也);

    • parents (Kangxi: 六親父母[…]也);
    • a wife (Kangxi: 六親[…]妻[…]也);
    • affianced;
    • used for ꜀san (san1 新), new (Kangxi: 通作新);
    • ꜀tsʻan tsʻik꜆ (can1 cik1 親戚) one's relatives;
    • tsʻü꜄ ꜂tsʻü ꜀tsʻan (ceoi2 can1 娶親), to take a wife;
    • ꜁shing ꜀tsʻan (sing4 can1 成親), to consummate a marriage become married;
    • ꜁ím ꜀tsʻan (jim4 can1 嚴親) or fú꜅ ꜀tsʻan (fu6 can1 父親), a father;
    • ꜁tsʻz' ꜀tsʻan (ci4 can1 慈親) or ꜃mò ꜀tsʻan (mou5 can1 母親), a mother;
    • ꜁ying ꜀tsʻan (jing4 can1 迎親), to escort the bride;
    • teng꜅ ꜁tʻau ꜀tsʻan sz'꜅ (deng6 tau4 can1 si6 定頭親事) contracted the marriage;
    • ꜃yau ꜀tsʻan (jau5 can1 有親), related to him;
    • ꜀tsʻan pat꜆ ꜂sé (can1 bat1 se2 親筆寫), wrote it myself;
    • (…);
    • ꜀tsʻan ít꜇ (can1 jit6 親熱) very intimate;
    • ꜀tsʻan oi꜄ (can1 oi3 親愛) to love much;
    • ꜀tsʻan kan꜅ (can1 gan6 親近) near to one; nearly related;
    • ꜀tsʻan ꜂tsui (can1 zeoi2 親嘴), to kiss;
    • (…);
    • ꜀tsʻan ꜃ngán kín꜄ (can1 ngaan5 gin3 親眼見) I saw it myself;
    • ꜀tsʻan shéung꜅ ꜀ká ꜀tsʻan (can1 soeng6 gaa1 can1 親上加親), to be doubly connected;
    • ꜁pʻan ꜁kʻung tün꜅ luk꜇ ꜀tsʻan (pan4 kung4 dyun6 luk6 can1 貧窮斷六親), nobody knows his poor relations.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 嚫. See 嚫 can1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 娶親: Changed tsʻü꜄ (ceoi3) to common reading ꜂tsʻü (ceoi2).
  • Williams' 成親: There is no connotation of sexual consummation in 成親. Although 成 can mean "complete" or "fulfil", I think it is better rendered here as "become".
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【親】 (tshin)
    • (noun-like) close [one], parent
    • (verb-like) [to] be close, [to] touch, (met.) [to] love
    • (adjective-like) close, (met.) [one's] own

    • Dust, small particles (Kangxi: 埃也);
    • effluvia, atoms, molecules;
    • the world;
    • worldly vice and pleasures;
    • confused times;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜁tʻau tái꜅ (can4 tau4 daai6 塵頭大) a cloud of dust;
    • ꜂sai ꜁chʻan (sai2 can4 洗塵), to wash down the dust, to give a feast to friends come back;
    • pò꜅ hau꜅ ꜁chʻan (bou6 hau6 can4 步後塵), to follow another's example;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜀oi (can4 oi1 塵埃), dust;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜂kau kau꜄ (can4 gau3 塵垢), covered with dust;
    • ꜀fung ꜁chʻan (fung1 can4 風塵), dusty from travel;
    • ꜁mò yat꜆ ꜂tím ꜁chʻan (mou4 jat1 dim2 can4 無一點塵), clean;
    • ꜁chʻan tsuk꜇ (can4 zuk6 塵俗), ꜁chʻan shai꜄ (can4 sai3 塵世), or ꜁hung ꜁chʻan (hung4 can4 紅塵), the dusty world;
    • fat꜆ ꜁chʻan (fat1 can4 拂塵), to dust.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "confused times": Especially warfare.
  • Williams' 塵垢: Changed ꜂kau (gau2) to common reading kau꜄ (gau3).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【塵】 (drin)
    • (noun-like) dust
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【沙塵】 (saa1 can4)
    1. Arrogant or cocky.
      • 咁沙塵 (gam3 saa1 can4): "So arrogant [of him]."

There is also the extended version 沙塵白霍, but that leads into a rabbit hole of debate about the origins of the phrase, and whether 塵 should be written 陳, and whether 霍 should be written 攉 or 醭.

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 82 陳) 列也張也敷也告也又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18 ꜁陳)
    • To arrange, to set in order, to spread out (Kangxi: 列也布也);
    • to say, to reply;
    • to state, to express;
    • to memorialize;
    • a long time, an old thing (Kangxi: 故也, 久也);
    • many, all (Kangxi: 衆也);
    • a feudal state (Kangxi: 國名) near the present capital of Honán (河南);
    • tsoi꜅ ꜁chʻan tsʻüt꜇ tsüt꜇ ꜁léung (zoi6 can4 zyut6 loeng4 在陳絕糧), to lack food in Chʻin—to be in straits;
    • ꜀pʻò ꜁chʻan (pou1 can4 鋪陳), to seat or rank in order;
    • ꜁chʻan chʻít꜆ (can4 cit3 陳設) to arrange in order;
    • ꜃shéung ꜂piú ꜁chʻan ꜁tsʻing (soeng5 biu2 can4 cing4 上表陳情), to state one's thoughts to H. I. M.;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜁pʻí kòm꜄ kau꜅ (can4 pei4 gam3 gau6 陳皮咁舊) old as a dried orange skin, faded.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 在陳絕糧: Corrected tsʻüt꜇ (cyut6) to tsüt꜇ (zyut6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【陳】 (drin)
    • (verb-like) [to] arrange, [to] display
    • (verb-like) [to] describe
    • (adjective-like) aged

嗔 ꜀Chʻan (can1)

Radical + residual strokes
口 + 10
Unicode code point
U+55D4
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 81 嗔) 怒也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 17 ꜀嗔)
    • To get angry;
    • passionate, angry;
    • to rail;
    • ꜀shang ꜀chʻan (sang1 can1 生嗔), to get into a rage;
    • ꜀chʻan kwái꜄ (can gwaai3 嗔怪) to scold.

    Also read ꜁tʻín (tin4); (see 嗔 tin4).

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan and Williams both print this as ⿰口眞, but no separate code point to U+55D4 嗔 exists for this form (blame Han unification inconsistency).

Chʻan꜄ (can3 陰去)

  • 襯

襯 Chʻan꜄ (can3)

Radical + residual strokes
衣 + 16
Unicode code point
U+896F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 85 襯) 貼身衣也又襯貼
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18 襯꜄)
    • Inner garments;
    • to help, to assist;
    • to patronize;
    • to befriend;
    • to give;
    • to give effect to, to show off;
    • to match;
    • chʻan꜄ ꜀kan (can3 gan1 襯巾), a sash;
    • chʻan꜄ ꜀shám (can3 saam1 襯衫), inner skirts;
    • ꜀pong chʻan꜄ (bong1 can3 幫襯) to give custom to;
    • chʻan꜄ chü꜅ ꜃kʻü (can3 zyu6 keoi5 襯住佢), to fellowship;
    • chʻan꜄ ꜂chí (can3 zi2 襯紙), a flyleaf in books;
    • chʻan꜄ tʻíp꜆ (can3 tip3-2 襯貼), plaits on a petticoat.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【幫襯】 (bong1 can3):
    1. To patronise (be a customer of a business).
  • 【襯】 (can3):
    1. To match, to suit.
      • 佢哋幾襯吖 (keoi5 dei6 gei2 can3 a1): "They [do] quite match [each other]."
      • 撞色唔襯 (zong6 sik1 m4 can3): "[The] colours clash [and] don't suit."
  • 【老襯】 (lou5 can3):
    1. A gullible person.
      • 做老襯 (zou6 lou5 can3): "Be [a] mug."
      • 搵老襯 (wan2 lou5 can3): "Find [a] sucker of [someone]" (i.e. trick a gullible person).

塵 ꜁Chʻan (can4)

Radical + residual strokes
土 + 11
Unicode code point
U+5875
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 82 塵) 塵上塵埃
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18 ꜁塵)
    • Dust, small particles;
    • effluvia, atoms, molecules;
    • the world;
    • worldly vice and pleasures;
    • confused times;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜁tʻau tái꜅ (can4 tau4 daai6 塵頭大) a cloud of dust;
    • ꜂sai ꜁chʻan (sai2 can4 洗塵), to wash down the dust, to give a feast to friends come back;
    • pò꜅ hau꜅ ꜁chʻan (bou6 hau6 can4 步後塵), to follow another's example;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜀oi (can4 oi1 塵埃), dust;
    • ꜁chʻan kau[꜄] (can4 gau3 塵垢), covered with dust;
    • ꜀fung ꜁chʻan (fung1 can4 風塵), dusty from travel;
    • ꜁mò yat꜆ ꜂tím ꜁chʻan (mou4 jat1 dim2 can4 無一點塵), clean;
    • ꜁chʻan tsuk꜇ (can4 zuk6 塵俗), ꜁chʻan shai꜄ (can4 sai3 塵世), or ꜁hung ꜁chʻan (hung4 can4 紅塵), the dusty world;
    • fat꜆ ꜁chʻan (fat1 can4 拂塵), to dust.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 塵垢: Changed Middle Chinese ꜂kau (gau2) to common reading kau꜄ (gau3).

陳 ꜁Chʻan (can4)

Radical + residual strokes
阜 + 8
Unicode code point
U+9673
Reading variations
  • can4-2 (vernacular rising-tone for endearment)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 82 陳) 列也張也敷也告也又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18 ꜁陳)
    • To arrange, to set in order, to spread out;
    • to say, to reply;
    • to state, to express;
    • to memorialize;
    • a long time, an old thing;
    • many, all;
    • a feudal state near the present capital of Honán (河南);
    • tsoi꜅ ꜁chʻan [tsʻüt]꜇ ꜁léung (zoi6 can4 zyut6 loeng4 在陳絕糧), to lack food in Chʻin—to be in straits;
    • ꜀pʻò ꜁chʻan (pou1 can4 鋪陳), to seat or rank in order;
    • ꜁chʻan chʻít꜆ (can4 cit3 陳設) to arrange in order;
    • ꜁shéung ꜂piú ꜁chʻan ꜁tsʻing (soeng5 biu2 can4 cing4 上表陳情), to state one's thoughts to H. I. M.;
    • ꜁chʻan ꜁pʻí kòm꜄ kau꜅ (can4 pei4 gam3 gau6 陳皮咁舊) old as a dried orange skin, faded.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 在陳絕糧: Corrected tsʻüt꜇ (cyut6) to tsüt꜇ (zyut6).

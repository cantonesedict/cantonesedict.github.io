嗔 ꜀Chʻan (can1)

⼝ + 10
Unicode code point

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 81 嗔) 怒也

  • (Page 17 ꜀嗔)
    • To get angry;

    • to rail;

Conway's commentary
  • Williams provides no explanation for the vernacular ꜀tsʻan (can1), notwithstanding he has supplied examples of its use.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【嚫】 (can1):
    1. Suffixed after a verb to mark negative consequence.
      • 冷嚫 (laang5 can1): "[I've] caught [a] cold."
      • 撞嚫 (zong6 can1): "[I've been] bumped into."
      • 整嚫 (zing2 can1): "[I've] hurt [myself]."
    2. Suffixed after a verb to indicate "every time".
      • 上嚫都漏 (soeng5 can1 dou1 lau6): "Every time [I] chow, [I] leak [good tiles]."
      • 射嚫都中 (se6 can1 dou1 zung3): "Every time [he] shoots, [he's] on target."
      • 飲嚫都係當歸 (jam2 can1 dou1 hai6 dong1 gwai1): "Every time [she] drinks [medicine], it's dong quai."

親 ꜀Tsʻan (can1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾒ + 9
Unicode code point
U+89AA
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 81 親) 愛也近也姻也眷也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 554 ꜀親)
    • To love, to be attached to;
    • liking, pleased with;
    • near, intimate;
    • near to, belonging to one's self, personal, myself;
    • a relative, related to, having affinity, kindred, kin, kith;
    • parents;
    • a wife;
    • affianced;
    • used for ꜀san (san1 新), new;
    • ꜀tsʻan tsʻik꜆ (can1 cik1 親戚) one's relatives;
    • [꜂]tsʻü ꜀tsʻan (ceoi2 can1 娶親), to take a wife;
    • ꜁shing ꜀tsʻan (sing4 can1 成親), to consummate a matriage;
    • ꜁ím ꜀tsʻan (jim4 can1 嚴親) or fú꜅ ꜀tsʻan (fu6 can1 父親), a father;
    • tsʻz' ꜀tsʻan (ci4 can1 慈親) or ꜃mò ꜀tsʻan (mou5 can1 母親), a mother;
    • ꜁ying ꜀tsʻan (jing4 can1 迎親), to escort the bride;
    • teng꜅ ꜁tʻau ꜀tsʻan sz'꜅ (deng6 tau1 can1 si6 定頭親事) contracted the marriage;
    • ꜃yau ꜀tsʻan (jau5 can1 有親), related to him;
    • ꜀tsʻan pat꜆ ꜂sé (can1 bat1 se2 親筆寫), wrote it myself;
    • (see 嚫 can1 for vernacular senses)
    • ꜀tsʻan ít꜇ (can1 jit6 親熱) very intimate;
    • ꜀tsʻan oi꜄ (can1 oi3 親愛) to love much;
    • ꜀tsʻan kan꜅ (can1 gan6 親近) near to one; nearly related;
    • ꜀tsʻan ꜂tsui (can1 zeoi2 親嘴), to kiss;
    • (…)
    • ꜀tsʻan ꜃ngán kín꜄ (can1 ngaan5 gin3 親眼見) I saw it myself;
    • ꜀tsʻan shéung꜅ ꜀ká ꜀tsʻan (can1 soeng6 gaa1 can1 親上加親), to be doubly connected;
    • ꜁pʻan ꜁kʻung tün꜅ luk꜇ ꜀tsʻan (pan4 kung4 dyun6 luk6 can1 貧窮斷六親), nobody knows his poor relations.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams provides no explanation for the vernacular ꜀tsʻan (can1), notwithstanding he has supplied examples of its use. Here it is normalised to 嚫; see 嚫 can1 for the canonical entry.
  • Williams' 娶親: Changed Middle Chinese tsʻü꜄ (ceoi3) to common reading ꜂tsʻü (ceoi2).
  • Williams' 成親: There is no connotation of sexual consummation in 成親. Although 成 can mean "complete" or "fulfil", I think it is better rendered here as "become".

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【幫襯】 (bong1 can3):
    1. To patronise (be a customer of a business).
  • 【襯】 (can3):
    1. To match, to suit.
      • 佢哋幾襯吖 (keoi5 dei6 gei2 can3 a1): "They [do] quite match [each other]."
      • 撞色唔襯 (zong6 sik1 m4 can3): "[The] colours clash [and] don't suit."
  • 【老襯】 (lou5 can3):
    1. A gullible person.
      • 做老襯 (zou6 lou5 can3): "Be [a] mug."
      • 搵老襯 (wan2 lou5 can3): "Find [a] sucker of [someone]" (i.e. trick a gullible person).

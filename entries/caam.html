Conway's commentary
  • Whilst кңҒchК»ГЎm (caam4) is attested in Middle Chinese, it is only explained "stab" (еҲәд№ҹ). Kangxi records the canonical reading жҘҡйҠңеҲҮ кңҖchК»ГЎm (caam1), explained both "stab" (еҲәд№ҹ) and "arm-support" (дёҖжӣ°жү¶д№ҹ). It is possible that Williams has mistakenly parsed жҘҡйҠңеҲҮ as йҷҪе№і rather than йҷ°е№і.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To mix, to blend.

    • A stone-cutter's chissel;
    • a small cold chissel (Kangxi: е°Ҹй‘ҝд№ҹ);
    • to pierce, as a thorn;
    • to cut in, to cut or chissel out;
    • to carve, as flowers;
    • tsГЎm кңғtsК»ГЎm кңҖfГЎ (caam5 faa1 йҸЁиҠұ), to carve flowers;
    • tsГЎm кңғtsК»ГЎm кңҖtГІ (caam5 dou1 йҸЁеҲҖ), a stone chissel;
    • tsГЎm кңғtsК»ГЎm кңҖhoi кңҖpГӯn (caam5 hoi1 bin1 йҸЁй–ӢйӮҠ), to split open an edge;
    • кңӮpГӯ lakкңҮ tsГЎm кңғtsК»ГЎm (bei2 lak6-1 caam5 з•Җз«»йҸЁ), the thorn pierced me.
Conway's commentary
  • Whilst Fan Wan does not explicitly record the reading of йҸЁ (as is the case for most entries), the tone at the very least can be inferred based on the ordering with respect to other entries. Because йҸЁ appears in the rising tone section, and strictly after ж”¬иҰҪж“Ҙ, which are йҷҪдёҠ (toneВ 5), we conclude that йҸЁ also is йҷҪдёҠ (toneВ 5). Williams' placement of йҸЁ under кңғTsГЎm (zaam5) is problematic as an unaspirated initial cannot occur with йҷҪдёҠ (toneВ 5). I have corrected this to the aspirated кңғTsК»ГЎm (caam5), because:
    1. It is consistent with the vernacular кңғtsК»ГЎm (caam5) for "pierced"; and
    2. It is consistent with гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢжүҚж•ўеҲҮ if aspirated rising кңғtsК»ГЎm (caam5) is not recast to unaspirated departing tsГЎmкң… (zaam6).

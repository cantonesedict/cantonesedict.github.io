Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 ꜁攙 ꜁Chʻám (caam4); here normalised to ꜀Chʻám (caam1))
    • To stab;
    • to sustain, to support, to lead;
    • to supply awant, to make up;
    • to divide with another;
    • to pull out;
    • [꜀]chʻám fan꜅ ꜁lai ꜀tʻím (caam1 fan6 lai4 tim1 攙分嚟添), make another share for him;
    • [꜀]chʻám ꜁fú (caam1 fu4 攙扶), to uphold.
Conway's commentary
  • The uncommon reading caam4 is that given by Williams. Whilst caam4 is not unattested in Middle Chinese (being expected from 士咸切), the canonical reading is caam1, expected from 楚銜切 (which is the primary reading recorded in Kangxi). It is possible that Williams has mistakenly parsed 楚銜切 as 陽平 rather than 陰平.

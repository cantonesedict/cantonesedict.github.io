參 ꜀Tsʻám (caam1)

Radical + residual strokes
厶 + 9
Unicode code point
U+53C3
Alternative forms
  • 叅
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 62 參) 參差不齊又干[預]也謀度也又音深
  • (Vol. 2 Page 62 叅) 叅考叅拜上官論下官亦曰叅
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 553 ꜀參꜀[叅])
    • To be concerned with, to join with for consultation, to advise, to give counsel to;
    • to blend, to mix, to form one of three;
    • to have an audience, to salute, to see one;
    • to impeach, to report to the throne on other officers;
    • confused, mixed, as colors;
    • a deposition, an impeachment;
    • the 20th constellation, consisting of stars α, β, γ, δ, η, ξ, ε and χ in Orion;
    • ꜀tsʻám tsau꜄ (caam1 zau3 參奏) to memorialize against;
    • ꜂tá ꜀tsʻám (daa2 caam1 打參), to make an obeisance;
    • ꜀tsʻám pái꜄ (caam1 baai3 參拜) to visit one; to worship;
    • ꜀tsʻám ꜀tsʻám ꜀tsʻz' ꜀tsʻz' (caam1 caam1 ci1 ci1 參參差差) uneven, not well ranked, unassorted;
    • ꜀tsʻám kák꜆ (caam1 gaak3 參革), to degrade an officer and report it;
    • ꜀tsʻám tsáp꜇ (caam1 zaap6 參雜) to throw into confusion;
    • ꜀tsʻám tséung꜄ (caam1 zoeng3 參將) a colonel;
    • ꜀tsʻám ꜁tsʻéung (caam1 coeng4 參詳), to consult upon;
    • ꜁chʻiú ꜀tsʻám (ciu4 caam1 朝參), admitted to a levee; to worship the Supreme;
    • ꜀tsʻám ting꜄ ꜁mò ꜁ngo (caam1 ding3 mou4 ngo4 參訂無訛), I've examined it and found no mistakes.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected ⿰阝頁 to 預.

攙 ꜀Chʻám (caam1)

Radical + residual strokes
手 + 17
Unicode code point
U+6519
Reading variations
  • caam4 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 ꜁攙 ꜁Chʻám (caam4); here normalised to ꜀Chʻám (caam1))
    • To stab;
    • to sustain, to support, to lead;
    • to supply awant, to make up;
    • to divide with another;
    • to pull out;
    • [꜀]chʻám fan꜅ ꜁lai ꜀tʻím (caam1 fan6 lai4 tim1 攙分嚟添), make another share for him;
    • [꜀]chʻám ꜁fú (caam1 fu4 攙扶), to uphold.
Conway's commentary
  • The uncommon reading caam4 is that given by Williams. Whilst caam4 is not unattested in Middle Chinese (being expected from 士咸切), the canonical reading is caam1, expected from 楚銜切 (which is the primary reading recorded in Kangxi). It is possible that Williams has mistakenly parsed 楚銜切 as 陽平 rather than 陰平.

鏨 ꜃Tsʻám (caam5)

Radical + residual strokes
金 + 11
Unicode code point
U+93E8
Alternative forms
  • 劖 (for vernacular "pierce" sense)
Reading variations
  • zaam6 (expected from 藏濫切)
Fan Wan entry
    (Vol. 2 Page 64 鏨) 鉄鑿
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 553 ꜃鏨 ꜃Tsám (zaam5); here normalised to ꜃Tsʻám (caam5))
    • A stone-cutter's chissel;
    • a small cold chissel;
    • to pierce, as a thorn;
    • to cut in, to cut or chissel out;
    • to carve, as flowers;
    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀fá (caam5 faa1 鏨花), to carve flowers;
    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀tò (caam5 dou1 鏨刀), a stone chissel;
    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀hoi ꜀pín (caam5 hoi1 bin1 鏨開邊), to split open an edge;
    • ꜂pí lak꜇ ꜃[tsʻám] (bei2 lak6-1 caam5 畀竻鏨), the thorn pierced me.
Conway's commentary
  • Whilst Fan Wan does not explicitly record the reading of 鏨 (as is the case for most entries), the tone at the very least can be inferred based on the ordering with respect to other entries. Because 鏨 appears in the rising tone section, and strictly after 攬覽擥, which are 陽上 (tone 5), we conclude that 鏨 also is 陽上 (tone 5). Williams' placement of 鏨 under ꜃Tsám (zaam5) is problematic as an unaspirated initial cannot occur with 陽上 (tone 5). I have corrected this to the aspirated ꜃Tsʻám (caam5), because:
    1. It is consistent with the vernacular ꜃tsʻám (caam5) for "pierced"; and
    2. It is consistent with 《廣韻》才敢切 if aspirated rising ꜃tsʻám (caam5) is not recast to unaspirated departing tsám꜅ (zaam6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鏨】 (caam5):
    1. To pierce, especially in a wedging or chisel-like fashion.
      • 鏨刺 (caam5 ci3): "Pierced [by a] thorn."

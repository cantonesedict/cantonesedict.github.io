攙 ꜀Chʻám (caam1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 17
Unicode code point
U+6519
Reading variations
  • caam4 (per 《廣韻》士咸切 for "stab" sense)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 ꜁攙 ꜁Chʻám (caam4); here normalised to ꜀Chʻám (caam1))
    • To stab (Kangxi: 刺也);
    • to sustain, to support (Kangxi: 一曰扶也), to lead;
    • to supply a want, to make up;
    • to divide with another;
    • to pull out;
    • [꜀]chʻám fan꜅ ꜁lai ꜀tʻím (caam1 fan6 lai4 tim1 攙分嚟添), make another share for him;
    • [꜀]chʻám ꜁fú (caam1 fu4 攙扶), to uphold.
Conway's commentary
  • Whilst ꜁chʻám (caam4) is not unattested in Middle Chinese, it is only explained "stab" (刺也). Kangxi records the canonical reading 楚銜切 ꜀chʻám (caam1), explained both "stab" (刺也) and "arm-support" (一曰扶也). It is possible that Williams has mistakenly parsed 楚銜切 as 陽平 rather than 陰平.
  • Williams' "to supply a want, to make up", "to divide with another", "to pull out": Kangxi has 完補也一曰旁掣也 under the reading 仕懺切鑱去聲 chám꜅ (zaam6).

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 懺꜄)
    • Irregular, disorderly (Kangxi: 儳互不齊也), as undrilled troops (Kangxi: 師次不整);

    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀fá (caam5 faa1 鏨花), to carve flowers;
    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀tò (caam5 dou1 鏨刀), a stone chissel;
    • ꜃[tsʻám] ꜀hoi ꜀pín (caam5 hoi1 bin1 鏨開邊), to split open an edge;
    • ꜂pí lak꜇ ꜃[tsʻám] (bei2 lak6-1 caam5 畀竻鏨), the thorn pierced me.
Conway's commentary
  • Whilst Fan Wan does not explicitly record the reading of 鏨 (as is the case for most entries), the tone at the very least can be inferred based on the ordering with respect to other entries. Because 鏨 appears in the rising tone section, and strictly after 攬覽擥, which are 陽上 (tone 5), we conclude that 鏨 also is 陽上 (tone 5). Williams' placement of 鏨 under ꜃Tsám (zaam5) is problematic as an unaspirated initial cannot occur with 陽上 (tone 5). I have corrected this to the aspirated ꜃Tsʻám (caam5), because:
    1. It is consistent with the vernacular ꜃tsʻám (caam5) for "pierced"; and
    2. It is consistent with 《廣韻》才敢切 if aspirated rising ꜃tsʻám (caam5) is not recast to unaspirated departing tsám꜅ (zaam6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鏨】 (caam5):

餐 ꜀Tsʻán (caan1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾷ + 7
Unicode code point
U+9910
Alternative forms
  • 飡
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 67 餐) 熟食也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 67 飡) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 555 ꜀餐꜀飡)
    • To swallow, to eat;
    • a meal;
    • a classifier of meals;
    • to gather and to select;
    • yat꜆ ꜀tsʻán fán꜅ (jat1 caan1 faan6 一餐飯) a meal;
    • ꜂tsò ꜀tsʻán (zou2 caan1 早餐) breakfast;
    • tái꜅ ꜀tsʻán (daai6 caan1 大餐), dinner;
    • ꜃mán ꜀tsʻán (maan5 caan1 晚餐) supper;
    • yat꜇ shik꜇ ꜀sám ꜀tsʻán (jat6 sik6 saam1 caan1 日食三餐), three meals a day;
    • ꜀tsʻán ꜁tʻau fán꜅ (caan1 tau4 faan6 餐頭飯) a common meal;
    • chán꜅ ꜀tsʻán shik꜇ (zaan6 caan1 sik6 賺餐食) works for his food;
    • sau꜄ shik꜆ ꜀hòm ꜀tsʻán (sau3 sik1 ham1 caan1 秀色堪餐), handsome enongh to be eaten;
    • ꜂páu ꜀tsʻán (baau2 caan1 飽餐), a good meal;
    • ꜀tsʻán ꜁wan ꜁mín üt꜇ (caan1 wan4 min4 jyut6 餐雲眠月) dines in the clouds and sleeps on the moon — an enthusiast;
    • ꜀chʻí fán꜅ ꜀tsʻán (ci1 faan6 caan1 黐飯餐), a diner out;
    • ꜀tsʻán chuk꜆ ꜀tsʻán fán꜅ (caan1 zuk1 caan1 faan6 餐粥餐飯) a meal of congee and of rice — poor.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 大餐: Dated; 大餐 now simply means a big meal, or feast.
  • Williams' 黐飯餐: A diner out in the sense of freeloading food.

剷 ꜂Chʻán (caan2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 11
Unicode code point
U+5277
Alternative forms
  • 剗
  • 鏟
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 69 剷) 削平也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 69 剗) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20 ꜂剷꜂剗)

    Interchanged with the next (鏟).

    • To level off, to spade up;
    • to reap;
    • to trim iron;
    • ꜂chʻán tʻai꜄ ꜀tò (caan2 tai3 dou1 剷剃刀), to plane a razor;
    • hòm꜅ ꜀ká ꜂chʻán (ham6 gaa1 caan2 冚家剷), may your race all be cut off;
    • ꜂chʻán ꜀héung (caan2 hoeng1 剷香), sandalwood shavings burned for the dead.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【剷】 (caan2):
    1. To delete, especially permanently.
      • 剷咗佢 (caan2 zo2 keoi5): "Delete it."
  • 【冚家剷】 (ham6 gaa1 caan2):
    1. A curse that someone's entire family be annihilated.

產 ꜂Chʻán (caan2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽣ + 6
Unicode code point
U+7522
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 69 產) 生業也又生產
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20 ꜂產)
    • To produce, to grow;
    • to bear, to increase;
    • an estate, a patrimony;
    • an occupation;
    • the productions of a country;
    • natives;
    • a sort of flageolet;
    • a birth, act of parturition;
    • ꜂pò ꜂chʻán (bou2 caan2 保產), a strengthening pill;
    • ꜂siú ꜂chʻán (siu2 caan2 小產), an abortion;
    • ꜀fan ꜂chʻán (fan1 caan2 分產) to divide the patrimony;
    • ꜀shang ꜂chʻán (sang1 caan2 生產), an increase, to have a child;
    • ꜂chʻán íp꜇ (caan2 jip6 產業) one's property, real estate;
    • ꜀ká ꜂chʻán (gaa1 caan2 家產), family possessions;
    • ꜂tʻò ꜂chʻán (tou2 caan2 土產), productions;
    • ꜀kʻing ꜀ká pái꜅ ꜂chʻán (king1 gaa1 baai6 caan2 傾家敗產) the family lost everything.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 保產: Specifically, a strengthening pill for pregnancy.
  • Williams' 小產: Specifically, a spontaneous abortion, now called a miscarriage.
  • Williams' 土產: Specifically, native produce.

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 69 鏟) 平木之器
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20 ꜂鏟)
    • A thin iron plate;
    • a shovel, a plane;
    • a spoke-shave, an iron shave;
    • to cut and pare;
    • to level;
    • wok꜇ ꜂chʻán (wok6 caan2 鑊鏟), a rice shovel;
    • yat꜆ fú꜄ ꜂chʻán ꜁kʻím (jat1 fu3 caan2 kim4 一副鏟鉗), a set of shovel and tongs;
    • ꜂chʻán ꜂tsʻò ꜁chʻü ꜀kan (caan2 cou2 ceoi4 gan1 鏟草除根), to root uut, to extirpate;
    • ꜂chʻán ꜀shán (caan2 saan1 鏟山), to scarp hills, to dig in a level.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【煙鏟】 (jin1 caan2):
    1. A heavy smoker.
      • 正煙鏟 (zing3 jin1 caan2): "[He's a] right smokaholic."

燦 Tsʻán꜄ (caan3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 13
Unicode code point
U+71E6
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 70 燦) 光明也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 556 燦꜄)
    • Resplendent, clear;
    • attractive, bright, gay;
    • tsʻán꜄ lán꜅ (caan3 laan6 燦爛) lustrous, brilliant;
    • tsʻán꜄ ꜁tʻau (caan3 tau4 燦頭), you're a bright one!
Conway's commentary
  • The (now) derogatory sense of "immigrant from the Mainland" is ultimately traced to the 1979 TVB Series 網中人 (The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly). For an in-depth discussion on the term 阿燦, see the Chinese Wikipedia article.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【阿燦】 (aa3 caan3):
    1. (derogatory) An immigrant into Hong Kong from Mainland China.

粲 Tsʻán꜄ (caan3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽶ + 7
Unicode code point
U+7CB2
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 70 粲) 美也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 556 粲꜄)
    • Half a ꜂tau (dau2 斗) or peck;
    • a meal;
    • many, much, a multitude;
    • clear, bright;
    • to agree upon;
    • fine, excellent;
    • beautiful, as a woman;
    • to laugh, smiling;
    • fine rice;
    • pák꜇ tsʻán꜄ (ba`ak6 caan3 白粲) white rice, anciently picked over by criminals;
    • tsʻán꜄ tsʻán꜄ (caan3 caan3 粲粲) elegant, as dress;
    • tsʻán꜄ ꜁ín ꜁í siú꜄ (caan3 jin4 ji4 siu3 粲然而笑) he laughed outright;
    • tsʻán꜄ ꜁tʻau (caan3 tau4-2 粲頭), a worthless fellow — a colloquial phrase.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 556 ꜁殘)
    • To ruin, to destroy;

    • to kill, to slaughter;
    • mischievous, wicked, cruel;

    • ꜁tsʻán ꜁nín (caan4 nin4 殘年), year going ont; an old man;
    • shik꜇ ꜁tsʻán (sik6 caan4 食殘), leavings, orts;
    • ꜁tsʻán ꜁yan (caan4 jan4 殘人), to injure people; a villain;
    • ꜁tsʻán ꜀púi (caan4 bui1 殘杯), heeltaps;
    • ꜁tsʻán kʻüt꜆ (caan4 kyut3 殘缺), deficient, injured;
    • ꜁tsʻán ꜂yan (caan4 jan2 殘忍), cruel, unfeeling.

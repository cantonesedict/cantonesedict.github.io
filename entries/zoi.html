Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to burst forth, as plants in spring": This is a misreading of Kangxi иӘӘж–Үдә‘жүҚиҚүжңЁд№ӢеҲқд№ҹд»ҘиҒІиҝ‘еҖҹзҲІе“үе§Ӣд№Ӣе“ү, which says that the "begin" sense of е“ү was sometimes spelt жүҚ due to the phonetic nearness and the (now considered incorrect) explanation of жүҚ as a sprouting plant in гҖҠиӘӘж–ҮгҖӢ.
  • Williams' д»ҒйҒ д№Һе“ү: Changed кңҒГә (wu4) to common reading кңҒfГә (fu4).
  • Williams' йҮҺе“үз”ұд№ҹ: Corrected yГЎкң… (jaa6) to кңғyГЎ (jaa5).
Conway's literary rendering

Conway's commentary
  • The reading жҳЁд»ЈеҲҮ tsoiкң… (zoi6) is very poorly explained in the Middle Chinese rime books:
    • гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢ has the extremely vague explanation йҒӢд№ҹ "transport".
    • гҖҠйӣҶйҹ»гҖӢ has the better explanation иҲҹи»ҠйҒӢзү©д№ҹ "transported objects [on] boats [and] chariots".
    Kangxi uses the second of these for the example гҖҠи©©е°Ҹйӣ…гҖӢе…¶и»Ҡж—Јијү. This is not entirely satisfying, because the гҖҠйӣҶйҹ»гҖӢ explanation appears to be for "load" the noun, but the гҖҠи©©е°Ҹйӣ…гҖӢ example appears to be for "load" the verb.
  • The reading жҳЁд»ЈеҲҮ tsoiкң… (zoi6) appears to have survived as vernacular е„Һ. See е„ҺВ zoi6.
  • Williams' "incalculably high number": Based on the progression е„„е…Ҷдә¬еһ“з§ӯеЈӨжәқжҫ—жӯЈијү, one of the following:
    • 1014 (starting from е„„ = 105 and going up by factors of 10);
    • 1017 (starting from е„„ = 108 and going up by factors of 10);
    • 1044 (starting from е„„ = 108 and going up by factors of 104);
    • 104096 (starting from е„„ = 108 and going up by squares).
    Actually, it is unclear whether this sense should be read кңӮtsoi (zoi2) "year's-[worth](?)", tsoiкң„ (zoi3) "carry(?)", or tsoiкң… (zoi6) "load(?)".
  • Williams' еҶҮеҳўијүдҪҸ: Corrected chГјкң„ (zyu3) to chГјкң… (zyu6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Deeds, works (Kangxi: дәӢд№ҹ).
Conway's literary rendering

  • (PageВ 589 е„Һкң…)

    A colloquial word.

    • The cargo, the loading of a boat;
    • the tonnage or capacity of a vessel;

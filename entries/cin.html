千 ꜀Tsʻín (cin1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼗ + 1
Unicode code point
U+5343
Alternative forms
  • 仟 (anti-alteration)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 19 千) 十百曰千
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 574 ꜀千)
    • A thousand (Kangxi: 十百也);
    • many, an indefinite number;
    • ꜀tsʻín mán꜅ iú꜄ tsò꜅ (cin1 maan6 jiu3 zou6 千萬要做) it must by all means be done;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜁kʻí ꜀fán ꜁lai (cin1 kei4 faan1 lai4 千祈返嚟), I earnestly beg you to return;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kam (cin1 gam1 千金), your daughter;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜃lí ꜃má (cin1 lei5 maa5 千里馬), a racehorse; a courier;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜂tsung (cin1 zung2 千總), a lieutenant;
    • ꜃lò ꜀tsʻín (lou5 cin1 老千), an old blackleg; name of a card;
    • ꜂tá ꜀tsʻín (daa2 cin1 打千), to bend one knee;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kan (cin1 gan1 千斤), a wrench to extract nails;
    • ꜀tsʻín sui꜄ (cin1 seoi3 千歲) your Highness, said to princes;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kam kwai꜄ ꜂tʻai (cin1 gam1 gwai3 tai2 千金貴體), your honorable self, said to any one;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜂kú ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ (cin1 gu2 kei4 si6 千古奇事) exceedingly strange;
    • ꜀tsʻín yat꜆ pat꜆ ꜂koi (cin1 jat1 bat1 goi2 千一不改), a thousand to one he'll not reform.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【老千】 (lou5 cin1):
    1. A swindler, conman, or cheat.
      • 正一老千 (zing3 jat1 lou5 cin1): "[He's a] right swindler."
    2. An act of cheating, especially in gambling games.
      • 咪出老千 (mai5 ceot1 lou5 cin1): "Don't [you] do [any] cheats."

  • (Page 574 ꜀遷꜀迁)
    • To move, to remove, to place elsewhere (Kangxi: 移物曰遷);
    • to ascend (Kangxi 遷: 登也, 去下之高也);
    • to be promoted;
    • to change, to transpose (Kangxi 遷: 變易也), to improve (Kangxi 遷: 遷徙也);
    • to banish, to deport, to drive off (Kangxi 遷: 謫也放逐也);

    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kü (cin1 geoi1 遷居), to change one's lodgings;
    • [át]꜆ ꜀tsʻín (aat3 cin1 押遷), to eject, as a tenant;
    • ꜂tso ꜀tsʻín (zo2 cin1 左遷), to degrade to a lower place or rank.

  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 徒 to 徙.

韆 ꜀Tsʻín (cin1)

May appear as either ⿰革⿺辶⿳覀大㔾 (+15 assumed here) or ⿰革⿺辶⿳覀大巳 (+16), depending on the font.
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 19 韆) 鞦韆
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜀韆)
    • To swing to and fro, to play upon the whirligig;
    • ꜀tsʻau ꜀tsʻín (cau1 cin1 鞦韆), a swing (Kangxi: 鞦韆繩戲也).
Conway's commentary
  • In Cantonese, 韆鞦 (cin1 cau1) "thousand autumns" is preferred over 鞦韆 (cau1 cin1) "autumns thousand". See below.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【韆鞦】 (cin1 cau1):
    1. A hanging-seat swing.

Whilst 鞦韆 (cau1 cin1) "autumns thousand" is the form attested in Middle Chinese, Kangxi has commentary saying that 千秋 (cin1 cau1) "thousand autumns" is the original (being a well-wish), with 秋千 (cau1 cin1) "autumns thousand" being a verbal corruption that later got spelt 鞦韆:

《康熙字典》韆：[…]《廣韻》鞦韆繩戲也《集韻》鞦韆北方戲以習輕趫者《張有復古編》詞人高無際作鞦韆賦漢武帝後庭繩戲本云千秋祝壽詞也語譌轉爲秋千後人譌爲鞦韆

淺 ꜂Tsʻín (cin2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 8
Unicode code point
U+6DFA
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 22 淺) 不深也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜂淺)
    • Shallow, not deep (Kangxi: 水不深也);
    • superficial, not profound;
    • light, as coloring;
    • easy, having few strokes, simple, as characters;
    • short-haired (Kangxi: 凡獸之淺毛者皆曰淺);
    • dripping;
    • ꜀sham ꜂tsʻín (sam1 cin2 深淺), deep—shallow; hard—easy;
    • ꜂tsʻín kan꜅ ꜀chí ꜁tʻò (cin2 gan6 zi1 tou4 淺近之徒), a superficial scholar;
    • ꜂tsʻín ꜁wong (cin2 wong4 淺黃), a light yellow;
    • ꜂tsʻíns ꜂tsʻín ꜂ho ꜂kong (cin2 cin2 hou2 gong2 淺淺好講), he can understand a very little;
    • ꜂tsʻín sut꜆ (cin2 seot1 淺率) vulgar and superficial, as a scholar;
    • ꜂ho ꜂tsʻín (hou2 cin2 好淺), very shallow, very easy;
    • ꜃ngán ꜂tsʻín (ngaan5 cin2 眼淺), impatient, having respect to persons;
    • hoi꜅ ꜁yan pat꜆ ꜂tsʻín (hoi6 jan4 bat1 cin2 害人不淺), to endamage one seriously.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "dripping": This is a misinterpretation of the senses interchangeable with 濺 zin1, 濺 zin3, or 灒 zaan3.

前 ꜁Tsʻín (cin4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 7
Unicode code point
U+524D
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 21 前) 前後
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜁前)
    • Before, in front of, in presence of (Kangxi: 前後之對);
    • in advance (Kangxi: 進也);
    • previously (Kangxi: 先也, 猶故也);
    • formerly, anciently;
    • the former;
    • to advance, to lead forward (Kangxi: 前猶導也);
    • ꜁tsʻín hau꜅ (cin4 hau6 前後) before, after;
    • ꜁tsʻín yat꜇ (cin4 jat6 前日) day before yesterday;
    • tái꜅ ꜁tsʻín yat꜇ (daai6 cin4 jat6 大前日) three days ago;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁tʻau (cin4 tau4 前頭), in front;
    • ꜁tsʻín üt꜇ (cin4 jyut6 前月) last month;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁yan ꜂tá pʻá꜄ hau꜅ ꜁yan (cin4 jan4 daa2 paa3 hau6 jan4 前人打怕後人), I am afraid, I have been once deceived;
    • ꜃ngán ꜁tsʻín (ngaan5 cin4 眼前), before one's eyes;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁loi (cin4 loi4 前來), come here, advance; about coming;
    • tsoi꜅ ꜁tsʻín (zoi6 cin4 在前), before, then;
    • ꜃shéung ꜁tsʻín hü꜄ (soeng5 cin4 heoi3 上前去) go ahead, go on;
    • ꜂tsau tung꜅ pat꜆ ꜁tsʻín (zau2 dung6 bat1 cin4 走動不前), I can't well get on with it; it's a difficult job to manage;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁chʻing ꜁nán liú꜅ (cin4 cing4 naan4 liu6 前程難料) it's not easy to guess how it will turn out;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜀king (cin4 king1 前經), already, before done.
Conway's commentary
  • In the vernacular register of Cantonese there is a distinction between 以前 and 之前. See below.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【以前】 (ji5 cin4):
    1. In the relatively distant past.
      • 以前去過 (ji5 cin4 heoi3 gwo3): "[I've] been [there] in [the] past."
  • 【之前】 (zi1 cin4):
    1. In the relatively near past.
      • 之前去過 (zi1 cin4 heoi3 gwo3): "[I've] been [there] before."
    2. Before (when used as a suffix to an event).
      • 瞓覺之前 (fan3 gaau3 zi1 cin4): "Before sleeping."
      • 上機之前 (soeng5 gei1 zi1 cin4): "Before boarding [the] flight."

    • ꜂kí ꜁tsʻín [꜃chʻung] (gei2 cin4 cung5 幾錢重) how many mace does it weigh?

    • tái꜅ ꜀kong ꜁tsʻín (daai6 gong1 cin4 大？錢), large coins of Kánghí (康熙);

    • ꜂nan ꜁tsʻín (nan2 cin4-2 㤻錢), to gamble;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁léung (cin4 loeng4 錢糧), taxes, revenue;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜂léung ꜂tsz' (cin4 loeng2 zi2 錢両子) shot, bullets;

踐 ꜃Tsʻín (cin5)

Radical + residual strokes
⾜ + 8
Unicode code point
U+8E10
Reading variations
  • cin2 (common)
  • zin6 (recast to departing)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 574 踐꜅ Tsín꜅ (zin6); often read ꜃Tsʻín (cin5))
    • To tread upon (Kangxi: 履也), to trample on or over (Kangxi: 踐蹋也, 踐殘也使殘壞也);
    • to walk, to step;
    • to arrange out;
    • [꜃tsʻín] ꜁ín (cin5 jin4 踐言), to fulfill one's promise;
    • [꜃tsʻín] táp꜇ (cin5 daap6 踐踏) to tread down or in;
    • [꜃tsʻín] kü꜄ (cin5 geoi3 踐？) to tread down the heels;
    • [꜃tsʻín] wai꜅ (cin5 wai6 踐位) to ascend the throne; to walk in another's steps.

    Often read ꜃tsʻín (cin5).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to walk", "to arrange out": These are misreadings of Kangxi 行也, 列也 (which mean "column" and "row" rather than "walk" and "arrange").

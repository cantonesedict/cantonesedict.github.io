千 ꜀Tsʻín (cin1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼗ + 1
Unicode code point
U+5343
Alternative forms
  • 仟 (anti-alteration)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 19 千) 十百曰千
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 574 ꜀千)
    • A thousand;
    • many, an indefinite number;
    • ꜀tsʻín mán꜅ iú꜄ tsò꜅ (cin1 maan6 jiu3 zou6 千萬要做) it must by all means be done;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜁kʻí ꜀fán ꜁lai (cin1 kei4 faan1 lai4 千祈返嚟), I earnestly beg you to return;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kam (cin1 gam1 千金), your daughter;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜃lí ꜃má (cin1 lei5 maa5 千里馬), a racehorse; a courier;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜂tsung (cin1 zung2 千總), a lieutenant;
    • ꜃lò ꜀tsʻín (lou5 cin1 老千), an old blackleg; name of a card;
    • ꜂tá ꜀tsʻín (daa2 cin1 打千), to bend one knee;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kan (cin1 gan1 千斤), a wrench to extract nails;
    • ꜀tsʻín sui꜄ (cin1 seoi3 千歲) your Highness, said to princes;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜀kam kwai꜄ ꜂tʻai (cin1 gam1 gwai3 tai2 千金貴體), your honorable self, said to any one;
    • ꜀tsʻín ꜂kú ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ (cin1 gu2 kei4 si6 千古奇事) exceedingly strange;
    • ꜀tsʻín yat꜆ pat꜆ ꜂koi (cin1 jat1 bat1 goi2 千一不改), a thousand to one he'll not reform.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【老千】 (lou5 cin1):
    1. A swindler, conman, or cheat.
      • 正一老千 (zing3 jat1 lou5 cin1): "[He's a] right swindler."
    2. An act of cheating, especially in gambling games.
      • 咪出老千 (mai5 ceot1 lou5 cin1): "Don't [you] do [any] cheats."

    • Shallow, not deep;

    • ꜂ho ꜂tsʻín (hou2 cin2 好淺), very shallow, very easy;

前 ꜁Tsʻín (cin4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 7
Unicode code point
U+524D
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 21 前) 前後
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜁前)
    • Before, in front of, in presence of;
    • in advance;
    • previously;
    • formerly, anciently;
    • the former;
    • to advance, to lead forward;
    • ꜁tsʻín hau꜅ (cin4 hau6 前後) before, after;
    • ꜁tsʻín yat꜇ (cin4 jat6 前日) day before yesterday;
    • tái꜅ ꜁tsʻín yat꜇ (daai6 cin4 jat6 大前日) three days ago;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁tʻau (cin4 tau4 前頭), in front;
    • ꜁tsʻín üt꜇ (cin4 jyut6 前月) last month;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁yan ꜂tá pʻá꜄ hau꜅ ꜁yan (cin4 jan4 daa2 paa3 hau6 jan4 前人打怕後人), I am afraid, I have been once deceived;
    • ꜃ngán ꜁tsʻín (ngaan5 cin4 眼前), before one's eyes;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁loi (cin4 loi4 前來), come here, advance; about coming;
    • tsoi꜅ ꜁tsʻín (zoi6 cin4 在前), before, then;
    • ꜃shéung ꜁tsʻín hü꜄ (soeng5 cin4 heoi3 上前去) go ahead, go on;
    • ꜂tsau tung꜅ pat꜆ ꜁tsʻín (zau2 dung6 bat1 cin4 走動不前), I can't well get on with it; it's a difficult job to manage;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁chʻing ꜁nán liú꜅ (cin4 cing4 naan4 liu6 前程難料) it's not easy to guess how it will turn out;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜀king (cin4 king1 前經), already, before done.
Conway's commentary
  • In the vernacular register of Cantonese there is a distinction between 以前 and 之前. See below.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【以前】 (ji5 cin4):
    1. In the relatively distant past.
      • 以前去過 (ji5 cin4 heoi3 gwo3): "[I've] been [there] in [the] past."
  • 【之前】 (zi1 cin4):
    1. In the relatively near past.
      • 之前去過 (zi1 cin4 heoi3 gwo3): "[I've] been [there] before."
    2. Before (when used as a suffix to an event).
      • 瞓覺之前 (fan3 gaau3 zi1 cin4): "Before sleeping."
      • 上機之前 (soeng5 gei1 zi1 cin4): "Before boarding [the] flight."

纏 ꜁Chʻín (cin4)

  • (Page 17* ꜁纏)
    • To wind around, to bind up, to wrap, to bandage;
    • to entwine, to cling to;

    • ꜁chʻín kéuk꜆ (cin4 goek3 纏腳) to bandage the feet;
    • ꜁chʻín ꜁tʻau (cin4 tau4 纏頭), to bind the head with a turban;

    • ꜁chʻín toi꜅ (cin4 doi6-2 纏袋) a waist bag for money;

    • ꜁chʻín chü꜅ (cin4 zyu6 纏住) importunate, to bother;
    • ꜀[chʻí] ꜁chʻín (ci1 cin4 癡纏), troublesome, to annoy.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword (with 厂 and 黑-like interior) to 纏.
  • Williams' 纏綿: More to be entangled or caught up in.
  • Williams' 癡纏: Corrected ꜀chʻi to ꜀chʻí. Also to be madly in love with.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜁錢꜁⿸⿻勹丿丶)
    • Copper coin, cash, coppers;
    • coin, a piece of money;
    • cash, wealth, money, property;
    • a tenth of a tael, or mace, which the cash once weighed;
    • ꜁tʻung ꜁tsʻín (tung4 cin4 銅錢), copper cash;
    • ꜀kam ꜁tsʻín (gam1 cin4 金錢), gold coins;
    • ꜃yau ꜁tsʻín (jau5 cin4-2 有錢), rich, wealthy;
    • ꜁tsʻín kuk꜇ (cin4 guk6-2 錢局) a mint;
    • ꜂cháu ꜁tsʻín (zaau2 cin4-2 找錢), to exchange into cash;
    • ꜂kí ꜁tsʻín [꜃chʻung] (gei2 cin4 cung5 幾錢重) how many mace does it weigh?
    • fong꜄ lò꜅ ꜁tsʻín (fong3 lou6 cin4 放路錢), to scatter paper money at burials;
    • tái꜅ ꜀kong ꜁tsʻín (daai6 gong1 cin4 大？錢), large coins of Kánghí (康熙);
    • lán꜅ ꜁tsʻín (laan6 cin4-2 爛錢), bad cash;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜂pán kòm꜄ [꜃]hau (cin4 baan2 gam3 hau5 錢板咁厚) thick as a cash board;
    • lí꜅ ꜁tsʻín (lei6 cin4 利錢), interest;
    • ꜂shau ꜁tsʻín ꜁nò (sau2 cin4 nou4 守錢奴), a miser;
    • ꜂nan ꜁tsʻín (nan2 cin4-2 㤻錢), to gamble;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁léung (cin4 loeng4 錢糧), taxes, revenue;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜂léung ꜂tsz' (cin4 loeng2 zi2 錢両子) shot, bullets;
    • ꜀cháng ꜁tsʻín (zaang1 cin4-2 爭錢), disputing about money;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜁shan ꜂pái pò꜄ (cin4 san4 baai2 bou3 錢神擺布) money serves for everything;
    • ꜁tsʻín ꜂shui ꜀tai (cin4 seoi2 dai1 錢水低), the rate of exchange is low.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 幾錢重: Corrected chung꜅ (zung6 去聲) to ꜃chʻung (cung5 上聲).
  • Williams' 錢板咁厚: Changed Middle Chinese alternative hau꜅ to ꜃hau.

      • 錢銀嘢 (cin4-2 ngan4-2 je5): "Matters of money."

花 ꜀Fá (faa1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 4
Unicode code point
U+82B1
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 73 花) 草木之葩
  • (Vol. 2 Image 73 華) 華美亦作花
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 42 ꜀花꜀華)
    • A blossom, a flower (Kangxi 花: 草木之葩也);
    • pleasure, vice;
    • variegated, ornamental, carved;
    • to exaggerate;
    • yat꜆ [꜂]to ꜀fá (jat1 do2 faa1 一朵花), a flower;
    • yat꜆ [chát]꜆ ꜀fá (jat1 zaat3 faa1 一紮花), a bouquet;
    • [꜁]ming ꜀fá (ming4 faa1 名花), ornamental flowers;
    • ꜀fá ꜀shang (faa1 sang1 花生), the ground-nut;
    • ꜀fá lò꜅ ꜂shui (faa1 lou6 seoi2 花露水), [lavender] water;
    • ꜂ang ꜀fá (ang2 faa1 㾘花), to enchase on metals;
    • ꜀fá káp꜆ ꜂tsz' (faa1 gaap3 zi2 花甲子) the sexagenary cycle;
    • ꜀fá ꜂tsz' (faa1 zi2 花子) a beggar;
    • ꜀fá ꜁lam (faa1 lam4 花林), a brothel;
    • ꜀fá fai꜄ (faa1 fai3 花費) to squander in dissipation;
    • ꜀fá ꜃nü (faa1 neoi5-2 花女), a girl;
    • ꜀fá ꜂tán (faa1 daan2 花旦), an actor who personates women;
    • ꜀fá mín[꜅] (faa1 min6 花面) to paint the face for acting;
    • ꜀fá ꜀fá shai꜄ kái꜄ (faa1 faa1 sai3 gaai3 花花世界) a thoughtless age;
    • ꜀fá ꜀fá ꜀kung ꜂tsz' (faa1 faa1 gung1 zi2 花花公子) a profligate rake;
    • ꜀fá [꜁]wong (faa1 wong4 花王), the god who protects children, worshiped under the bed;
    • ꜀fá pò꜄ (faa1 bou3 花布) chintz;
    • ꜀fá ꜀kái (faa1 gaai1 花街), an illuminated street;
    • ꜀fá wá[꜅] (faa1 waa6 花話) exaggerated talk;
    • ꜀fá ꜁meng (faa1 meng4-2 花名), a nickname;
    • ꜀Fá ꜁kʻí kwok꜆ (faa1 kei4 gwok3 花旗國) the United States;
    • ꜀fá tí꜅ (faa1 dei6-2 花地) the Flower Gardens near Canton.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 一朵花: Corrected ꜀to (do1) to ꜂to (do2).
  • Williams' 一紮花: Corrected chat꜆ (zat1) to chát꜆ (zaat3).
  • Williams' 名花: Corrected ꜀ming (ming1) to ꜁ming (ming4).
  • Williams' 花費: Also simply to expend, without the negative connotation of excess.
  • Williams' 花面: Corrected mín꜄ (min3) to mín꜅ (min6).
  • Williams' 花花公子: Now used as the translation of Playboy.
  • Williams' 花王: Corrected ꜀wong (wong1) to ꜁wong (wong4).
  • Williams' 花話: Corrected wá꜄ (waa3) to wá꜅ (waa6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To flower.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【花】 (faa1):
    1. Scratched.
      • 揩花咗 (haai1 faa1 zo2): "Scratched [from being] brushed against."
    2. Messy or chaotic (like the shape of Mr Messy).
      • 畫花嗮 (waak6 faa1 saai3): "All messed-up [from being] drawn [over]."
    3. (mahjong) The flower tiles, usually the four flora (梅蘭菊竹) and the four seasons (春夏秋冬).
      • 正花 (zeng3 faa1): "[A] correct flower."
      • 爛花 (laan6 faa1): "[A] bad flower."
      • 唔打花 (m4 daa2 faa1): "Not playing [with] flower-tiles."
    4. A flush (card hand).
  • 【花碼】 (faa1 maa5):
    1. Suzhou numerals (〇〡〢〣〤〥〦〧〨〩).
  • 【花面貓】 (faa1 min6 maau1):
    1. Messy in the face.

化 Fá꜄ (faa3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼔ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5316
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 76 化) 變化王化[敎]化
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 43 化꜄)
    • To change, to influence so as to alter, to transform (Kangxi: 化敎行也 etc.);
    • to melt, to digest;
    • to convert;
    • to pass into metempsychosis;
    • to create;
    • to transmute (Kangxi: 革物曰化);
    • to barter;
    • to exchange (Kangxi: 貨賄貿易曰化);
    • to reform;
    • káu꜄ fá꜄ (gaau3 faa3 教化) to improve by instruction;
    • fá꜄ ꜀shan (faa3 san1 化身), to burn a priest's corpse;
    • [tsò꜅] fá꜄ (zou6 faa3 造化) fate, nature;
    • [꜂]hò [tsò꜅] fá꜄ (hou2 zou6 faa3 好造化) good luck;
    • ꜁wong fá꜄ (wong4 faa3 王化) the influence of law;
    • fá꜄ ꜀shang (faa3 sang1 化生), produced by metamorphosis, as insects;
    • fá꜄ yat꜇ (faa3 jat6 化日) peaceful times;
    • ꜀chʻáu fá꜄ (caau1 faa3 抄化) to subscribe for a Budhist mass;
    • ꜁'m fá꜄ (m4 faa3 唔化) did not subscribe; also, indigestible;
    • shín꜅ ꜁tsʻoi ꜁nán fá꜄ (sin6 coi4 naan4 faa3 善財難化) avaricious, niggardly.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Fixed missing strokes in 敎.
  • Williams' 化身: Also reincarnation.
  • Williams' 造化: Corrected tsʻò꜄ (cou3) to tsò꜅ (zou6).
  • Williams' 好造化: Corrected hò꜄ (hou3) to ꜂hò (hou2), and corrected tsʻò꜄ (cou3) to tsò꜅ (zou6).
  • Williams' 抄化: More specifically, for a monk to subscribe an alms-giver.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To burn (paper offerings).
    • 化元寶 (faa3 jyun4 bou2): "Transform paper-ingots [by fire]."

挨 ꜀Ái (aai1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 7
Unicode code point
U+6328
Reading variations
  • ngaai1 (hypercorrected initial)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 25 挨) 屋齋切倚也推也擊也又背負也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 2 ꜀挨)
    • To lean upon, to trust to;
    • to push away (Kangxi: 推也), to carry on the back;
    • to place alongside (Kangxi: 旁排也);
    • to strike (Kangxi: 擊也);
    • to force, to crowd (Kangxi: 强進曰挨), as with the elbows;
    • to graft;
    • next, near (Kangxi: 今俗凡物相近謂之挨);
    • passable;
    • to lie down, to recline;
    • ꜀ái ꜁mái ꜀ní pín꜅ (aai1 maai4 ni1 bin6 挨埋呢便) lean it against here; help here a little;
    • ꜃yau tak꜆ ꜀ái pang꜅ (jau5 dak1 aai1 bang6 有得挨憑) has some dependance or resource;
    • ꜃yau tái꜅ ꜀shán ꜀ái (jau5 daai6 saan1 aai1 有大山挨), he has powerful friends;
    • ꜀ái ꜃há tik꜆ (aai1 haa5 dik1 挨吓滴) lie down a little;
    • ꜀ái tak꜆ hü꜄ (aai1 dak1 heoi3 挨得去) it will answer;
    • ꜀ái ꜁mún ꜀ái ú꜅ (aai1 mun4 aai1 wu6 挨門挨戶) to gad and stare, as a virago; to go from door to door to examine;
    • ꜀ái ꜂í (aai1 ji2 挨倚), to mutually rely upon;
    • ꜀ái lái꜅ (aai1 laai6 挨賴) to trust for help;
    • ꜀ái ꜃mán (aai1 maan5-1 挨晚), towards evening.

嗌 Ái꜄ (aai3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 10

U+55CC
Reading variations
  • ngaai3 (hypercorrected initial)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 3 嗌꜄)

    A colloquial word;

    • to call after, to bawl;
    • to quarrel;
    • crowded, thronged;
    • ái꜄ ꜀káu (aai3 gaau1 嗌交), to wrangle, to dispute;
    • ái꜄ pʻo꜄ ꜁hau ꜁lung (aai3 po3 hau4 lung4 嗌破喉嚨), hallooed till he split his throat;
    • ái꜄ náu꜅ (aai3 naau6 嗌鬧) thronged; brawling;
    • ái꜄ mat꜆ ꜃yé (aai3 mat1 je5 嗌乜嘢), what are you scolding about?
    • shap꜇ ái꜄ ꜂kau ꜀cháng ꜁ngan (sap6 aai3 gau2 zaang1 ngan4 十嗌九爭銀), ten to one it is a quarrel about money.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 十嗌九爭銀: "Ten to one" is perhaps better rendered as "nine out of ten".
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【嗌】 (aai3):
    1. To shout.
      • 大聲嗌 (daai6 seng1 aai3): "Shout out loud."
    2. To quarrel.
      • 你哋嗌飽佢啦 (nei5 dei6 aai3 baau2 keoi5 la1): "[Why don't] youse quarrel over it [to your heart's] content."
      • 又嗌交喇 (jau6 aai3 gaau1 laa3): "[They're] quarrelling again."
    3. To order food.
      • 嗌餸未 (aai3 sung3 mei6): "Ordered dishes yet?"

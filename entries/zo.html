俎 ꜂Cho (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 7
Unicode code point
U+4FCE
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 60 爼) 爼豆祭器
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25* ꜂爼; here normalised to 俎)
    • A basin or bowl like a tripod, used in sacrifice;
    • ꜂cho tau꜅ (zo2 dau6 俎豆) a basin used in worship.

咗 ꜂Cho (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5497
Alternative forms
  • 唨
  • 阻
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25* ꜂阻; here normalised to 咗)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • A sign of the past tense, placed after verbs like ꜀hiú (hiu1 嘵);
    • ꜀[ling] ꜂cho ꜁lai (ling1 zo2 lai4 拎咗嚟), brought it;
    • hü꜄ ꜂cho (heoi3 zo2 去咗), gone.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 拎咗嚟: Changed ꜀ning (ning1) to ꜀ling (ling1).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【咗】 (zo2):
    1. Marker of the perfective aspect (whole completion of an action).
      • 走咗 (zau2 zo2): "[He's] gone."
      • 死咗 (sei2 zo2): "[He's] died."
      • 笨咗添 (ban6 zo2 tim1): "[Oh, I've] been foolish!"
      • 瞓着咗 (fan3 zoek6 zo2): "Fallen asleep."
      • 畢咗業 (bat1 zo2 jip6): "[I've] graduated."
      • 食咗飯未 (sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6): "[Have you] eaten dinner yet?"
    2. Used with certain imperatives.
      • 飲咗佢吖 (jam2 zo2 keoi5 a1): "Drink it [up]."
      • 剷咗條片啦 (caan2 zo2 tiu4 pin3-2 laa1): "Delete [that] video!"
      • 噉炒咗佢囉 (gam2 caau2 zo2 keoi5 lo1): "So [just] fire him!"

左 ꜂Tso (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼯ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5DE6
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 左) 左右又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 582 ꜂左)
    • The left, the left hand;
    • second;
    • an assistant;
    • deflected;
    • depraved, bad;
    • to degrade, to reduce in rank;
    • ꜂tso ím꜅ (zo2 jim6 左驗) to verify, to prove, as a coadjutor can;
    • ꜂tso pín꜅ (zo2 bin6 左便) on the left;
    • ꜂shai kwán꜄ ꜂tso ꜂shau (sai2 gwaan3 zo2 sau2 使慣左手), left-handed;
    • ꜂tso sing꜄ (zo2 sing3 左性) crotchetty, set in his way;
    • chün[꜄] ꜀wán ꜂tso pín꜅ (zyun3 waan1 zo2 bin6 轉彎左便) turn to the left hand;
    • ꜀séung ꜂tso (soeng1 zo2 相左), a mutual mistake; not to suit, disagreeing;
    • mín꜅ ꜂tso ꜂tso (min6 zo2 zo2 面左左), to dislike;
    • ꜂tso ꜀sz' yau꜅ ꜂séung (zo2 si1 jau6 soeng2 左思右想), thinking of this way and that;
    • ꜂tso ꜂shau ꜀káu yau꜅ ꜂shau (zo2 sau2 gaau1 jau6 sau2 左手交右手), to exchange from hand to hand, i. e. not to give credit;
    • lít꜇ ꜀ü ꜂tso (lit6 jyu1 zo2 列於左), written as follows.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 轉彎左便: Corrected ꜂chün (zyun2) to chün꜄ (zyun3). Now simply 轉左.
  • Williams' 列於左: Perhaps this should be 列如左.

阻 ꜂Cho (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾩ + 5
Unicode code point
U+963B
Alternative forms
  • 岨 (for "mountain-pass" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 60 阻) 阻滯阻隔險阻
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25* ꜂阻)
    • To hinder, to impede, to stop, to make delay;
    • grieved;
    • dangerous;
    • an impediment;
    • a defile;
    • to suspect, to doubt;
    • ꜂hím ꜂cho (him2 zo2 險阻), an obstruction in the way;
    • ꜂cho chai꜅ (zo2 zai6 阻滯) prevented from being ready;
    • ꜂cho kák꜆ (zo2 gaak3 阻隔) to prevent going;
    • ꜂cho ꜁nán (zo2 naan4 阻難), difficulties;
    • ꜂cho chü꜅ (zo2 zyu6 阻住) to hinder, blocked up;
    • ꜂cho ꜂tong (zo2 dong2 阻擋), to stand in the way of;
    • ꜂cho ꜁chʻí (zo2 ci4 阻遲), to delay;
    • ꜂cho 'ng꜅ sz'꜅ kon꜄ (zo2 ng6 si6 gon3 阻誤事幹) to retard or interrupt business.

    A colloquial word (here normalised to 咗; see 咗 zo2 for the canonical entry).

佐 Tso꜄ (zo3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 5
Unicode code point
U+4F50
Reading variations
  • zo2 (common in 佐敦)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 佐) 輔佐
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 582 佐꜄)
    • To assist, to second;
    • an assistant, a deputy, a second, a substitute;
    • tso꜄ ꜃ling (zo3 ling5 佐領), a major-general, a resident among the Mongols;
    • ꜁wong tso꜄ ꜀chí ꜁tsʻoi (wong4 zo3 zi1 coi4 王佐之才) talents of the prime minister;
    • tso꜄ tsáp꜇ ꜀kún (zo3 zaap6 gun1 佐雜官), low officers assisting in a yámun (衙門).

詛 Cho꜄ (zo3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾔ + 5
Unicode code point
U+8A5B
Reading variations
  • zeoi2 (common)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 詛) 竹過切咒詛
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 咀) 同[上]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26* 詛꜄)
    • To curse, to imprecate;
    • bad language, curses;
    • chau꜄ cho꜄ (zau3 zo3 咒詛) to curse.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword self-reference to 上.
  • Williams records chù (zoe) and chü (zyu) as alternative readings without specifying the tone (see remarks under zo3). The former I cannot justify. The latter is consistent with Kangxi quoting 《集韻》遵遇切娵去聲.
  • The common reading zeoi2 is perhaps from identifying 詛咒 with 咀咒, noting that Fan Wan considers 咀 an alternative form of 詛 cho꜄ (zo3).

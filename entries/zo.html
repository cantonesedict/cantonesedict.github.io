Fan Wan entry

咗 ꜂Cho (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5497
Alternative forms
  • 唨
  • 阻
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25* ꜂阻; here normalised to 咗)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • A sign of the past tense, placed after verbs like ꜀hiú (hiu1 嘵);
    • ꜀[ling] ꜂cho ꜁lai (ling1 zo2 lai4 拎咗嚟), brought it;
    • hü꜄ ꜂cho (heoi3 zo2 去咗), gone.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 拎咗嚟: Changed ꜀ning (ning1) to ꜀ling (ling1).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【咗】 (zo2):
    1. Marker of the perfective aspect (whole completion of an action).
      • 走咗 (zau2 zo2): "[He's] gone."
      • 死咗 (sei2 zo2): "[He's] died."
      • 笨咗添 (ban6 zo2 tim1): "[Oh, I've] been foolish!"
      • 瞓着咗 (fan3 zoek6 zo2): "Fallen asleep."
      • 畢咗業 (bat1 zo2 jip6): "[I've] graduated."
      • 食咗飯未 (sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6): "[Have you] eaten dinner yet?"
    2. Used with certain imperatives.
      • 飲咗佢吖 (jam2 zo2 keoi5 a1): "Drink it [up]."
      • 剷咗條片啦 (caan2 zo2 tiu4 pin3-2 laa1): "Delete [that] video!"
      • 噉炒咗佢囉 (gam2 caau2 zo2 keoi5 lo1): "So [just] fire him!"

    • mín꜅ ꜂tso ꜂tso (min6 zo2 zo2 面左左), to dislike;

坐 Tso꜅ (zo6)

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 582 坐꜅)
    • To sit;
    • to sit on the heels;
    • sitting, remaining;
    • a session;
    • a seat, a place;
    • to maintain, to hold;

    • to sit in judgement on, to judge;
    • to place, to set down;
    • to kneel;

    • ꜃ní [꜃tsʻo] (nei5 co5 你坐) or [꜃tsʻo] ché꜄ (co5 ze3 坐嗻) good bye, said to the host;
    • tso꜅ ꜃lí (zo6 lei5 坐𢃇), to let down sail;
    • [꜃tsʻo] kiú꜅ (co5 giu6-2 坐轎) to ride in a sedan;
    • tso꜅ tsúi꜅ ꜀ü ꜃ní (zo6 zeoi6 jyu1 nei5 坐罪於你), to bring the punishment upon you;
    • [꜃tsʻo] üt꜇ (co5 jyut6-2 坐月) the month after confinement;
    • tso꜅ wai꜅ (zo6 wai6-2 坐位) a seat, a station;
    • tso꜅ shat꜇ (zo6 sat6 坐實) put it in securely;
    • tso꜅ sok꜆ (zo6 sok3 坐索) to quarter on one to force him to pay;
    • [꜃tsʻo] ꜁'m lok꜇ (co5 m4 lok6 坐唔落) no place to sit in;
    • tso꜅ héung꜄ (zo6 hoeng3 坐向) the prospect, as of a grave;
    • [꜃tsʻo] ꜃há ꜀tʻím (co5 haa5 tim1 坐吓添), sit a while longer;
    • tʻít꜆ tso꜅ (tit3 zo6 鐵坐) to squat, as a tailor;
    • [꜃tsʻo] ꜀kám (co5 gaam1 坐監), in prison;
    • [꜃tsʻo] [꜃]shéung (co5 soeng5 坐上) sit up higher;
    • [꜃tsʻo] ꜁tʻong (co5 tong4-2 坐堂), in court, on the bench;
    • [꜃tsʻo] ꜃láng fán꜅ [꜂]kòm [꜃tsʻo] (co5 laang5 faan6 gam2 co5 坐冷飯噉坐) to sit till the rice is cold, i. e. to take [French] leave;
    • ꜂tá tso꜅ ꜀tsʻám ꜁[shím] (daa2 zo6 caam1 sim4 打坐參禪), to squat and contemplate, as Budhists do.
Conway's commentary
  • The rising-tone reading ꜃tsʻo (co5) is typically only heard in vernacular usage for verb-like occurrences, leading to the perception that it is non-literary. In fact, the distinction in Cantonese between aspirated rising ꜃tsʻo (co5) and unaspirated departing tso꜅ (zo6) actually preserves the two readings of 坐 in Middle Chinese (also true of 斷, 淡, 近, 重, etc.). Kangxi has an extended note,
    按坐有上去二音字韻諸書訓註皆同惟轉注古音坐註引史記高帝紀遂坐上坐正義云前坐字在果反後坐字在臥反
    the short version of which is, that the quoted line 遂坐上坐 "Then [he] sat upon [the] upper seat" should be read sui꜅ ꜃tsʻo shéung꜅ tso꜅ (seoi6 co5 soeng6 zo6), with the first 坐 ("sat") rising and the second 坐 ("seat") departing.
  • Williams' tso꜅ (zo6 去聲) has been corrected to ꜃tsʻo (co5 上聲) when appropriate.
  • Williams' 坐上: Corrected shéung꜅ (soeng6 去聲) to ꜃shéung (soeng5 上聲).
  • Williams' 坐冷飯噉坐: Corrected kòm꜄ (gam3) to ꜂kòm (gam2).
  • Williams' 打坐參禪: Changed Middle Chinese ꜁shín (sin4) to common reading ꜁shím (sim4). Not squatting on one's feet with knees bent, but cross-legged in the lotus position.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【坐】 (co5):
    1. (Chinese chess) Used to describe moves of the general.
      • 坐起 (co5 hei2): "Sit raised" (i.e. move the general up).
      • 坐上 (co5 soeng5): "Sit up" (i.e. move the general up).
      • 坐上山頂 (co5 soeng5 saan1 deng2): "Sit up [to the] mountain top" (i.e. move the general up to the top row of the palace).
      • 坐落 (co5 lok6): "Sit down" (i.e. move the general down).
      • 坐出 (co5 ceot1): "Sit out" (i.e. move the general out of cover of the centre column).
      • 坐入 (co5 jap6): "Sit in" (i.e. move the general back into cover of the centre column).
      • 坐歪 (co5 me2): "Sit off-centre" (i.e. move the general off the uncovered centre column).
      • 坐正 (co5 zeng3): "Sit right" (i.e. move the general back onto the uncovered centre column).
      • 坐咗佢 (co5 zo2 keoi5): "Sit [over] it" (i.e. capture with the general).
  • 【坐低】 (co5 dai1):
    1. To sit down.
  • 【坐穩機】 (co5 wan2 gei1):
    1. A farewell wish for a safe flight.

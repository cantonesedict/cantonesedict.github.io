咗 ꜂Cho (zo2)

Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25* ꜂阻; here normalised to 咗)

    (…)

    A colloquial word.

    • A sign of the past tense, placed after verbs like ꜀hiú (hiu1 嘵);
    • ꜀ning ꜀ling ꜂cho ꜁lai (ling1 zo2 lai4 拎咗嚟), brought it;
    • hü꜄ ꜂cho (heoi3 zo2 去咗), gone.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 拎咗嚟: Changed ꜀ning (ning1) to ꜀ling (ling1).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【咗】 (zo2):
    1. Marker of the perfective aspect (whole completion of an action).
      • 走咗 (zau2 zo2): "[He's] gone."
      • 死咗 (sei2 zo2): "[He's] died."
      • 笨咗添 (ban6 zo2 tim1): "[Oh, I've] been foolish!"
      • 瞓着咗 (fan3 zoek6 zo2): "Fallen asleep."
      • 畢咗業 (bat1 zo2 jip6): "[I've] graduated."
      • 食咗飯未 (sik6 zo2 faan6 mei6): "[Have you] eaten dinner yet?"
    2. Used with certain imperatives.
      • 飲咗佢吖 (jam2 zo2 keoi5 a1): "Drink it [up]."
      • 剷咗條片啦 (caan2 zo2 tiu4 pin3-2 laa1): "Delete [that] video!"
      • 噉炒咗佢囉 (gam2 caau2 zo2 keoi5 lo1): "So [just] fire him!"

左 ꜂Tso (zo2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼯ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5DE6
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 左) 左右又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 582 ꜂左)
    • The left (Kangxi: 左右之對), the left hand;
    • second;
    • an assistant;
    • deflected (Kangxi: 僻左不正也);
    • depraved, bad (Kangxi: 手足便右以左爲僻故凡幽猥皆曰僻左);
    • to degrade, to reduce in rank (Kangxi: 漢時依古法朝廷之列以右爲尊故謂降秩爲左遷);
    • ꜂tso ím꜅ (zo2 jim6 左驗) to verify, to prove, as a coadjutor can (Kangxi: 左證左也);
    • ꜂tso pín꜅ (zo2 bin6 左便) on the left;
    • ꜂shai kwán꜄ ꜂tso ꜂shau (sai2 gwaan3 zo2 sau2 使慣左手), left-handed;
    • ꜂tso sing꜄ (zo2 sing3 左性) crotchetty, set in his way;
    • ꜂chün chün꜄ ꜀wán ꜂tso pín꜅ (zyun3 waan1 zo2 bin6 轉彎左便) turn to the left hand;
    • ꜀séung ꜂tso (soeng1 zo2 相左), a mutual mistake; not to suit, disagreeing;
    • mín꜅ ꜂tso ꜂tso (min6 zo2 zo2 面左左), to dislike;
    • ꜂tso ꜀sz' yau꜅ ꜂séung (zo2 si1 jau6 soeng2 左思右想), thinking of this way and that;
    • ꜂tso ꜂shau ꜀káu yau꜅ ꜂shau (zo2 sau2 gaau1 jau6 sau2 左手交右手), to exchange from hand to hand, i. e. not to give credit;
    • lít꜇ ꜀ü ꜂tso (lit6 jyu1 zo2 列於左), written as follows.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 轉彎左便: Corrected ꜂chün (zyun2) to chün꜄ (zyun3). Now simply 轉左.
  • Williams' 列於左: Perhaps this should be 列如左. The idea here is that Chinese is (classically) written right to left, so text that comes afterward will be on the left.

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26* before 詛꜄)

    This character (詛) is often called chù (zoe) or chü (zyu).

  • (Page 26* 詛꜄)
    • To curse, to imprecate (Kangxi: 詶也, 呪詛);
    • bad language, curses;
    • chau꜄ cho꜄ (zau3 zo3 咒詛) to curse.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword self-reference to 上.
  • Williams records chù (zoe) and chü (zyu) as alternative readings without specifying the tone. The former I cannot justify. The latter rhymes with 《集韻》遵遇切 tsü꜄ (zyu3), explained 詶也, and also with 咀 ꜂tsü, but in both cases the pre-merger initial is inconsistent.
  • The common reading zeoi2 is perhaps from identifying 詛咒 with 咀咒, noting that Fan Wan considers 咀 an alternative form of 詛 cho꜄ (zo3).

    • ꜂tá tso꜅ ꜀tsʻám ꜁shín ꜁shím (daa2 zo6 caam1 sim4 打坐參禪), to squat sit in the lotus position and contemplate, as Budhists do.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 座. See 座 zo6 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • The rising-tone reading ꜃tsʻo (co5) is typically only heard in vernacular usage for verb-like occurrences, leading to the perception that it is non-literary. In fact, the distinction in Cantonese between aspirated rising ꜃tsʻo (co5) and unaspirated departing tso꜅ (zo6) actually preserves the two readings of 坐 in Middle Chinese (also true of 斷, 淡, 近, 重, etc.). Kangxi has an extended note,
    按坐有上去二音字韻諸書訓註皆同惟轉注古音坐註引史記高帝紀遂坐上坐正義云前坐字在果反後坐字在臥反
    the short version of which is, that the quoted line 遂坐上坐 "Then [he] sat upon [the] upper seat" should be read sui꜅ ꜃tsʻo shéung꜅ tso꜅ (seoi6 co5 soeng6 zo6), with the first 坐 ("sat") rising and the second 坐 ("seat") departing.
  • Williams' tso꜅ (zo6 去聲) has been corrected to ꜃tsʻo (co5 上聲) when appropriate.
  • Williams' 坐月: Here, "confinement" means childbirth. Confusingly, in more modern English, the act of staying home for a month afterward is also called confinement.
  • Williams' 坐上: Corrected shéung꜅ (soeng6 去聲) to ꜃shéung (soeng5 上聲).
  • Williams' 坐冷飯噉坐: Corrected kòm꜄ (gam3) to ꜂kòm (gam2).
  • Williams' 打坐參禪: Changed ꜁shín (sin4) to common reading ꜁shím (sim4).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【坐】 (co5):
    1. (Chinese chess) Used to describe moves of the general.
      • 坐起 (co5 hei2): "Sit raised" (i.e. move the general up).
      • 坐上 (co5 soeng5): "Sit up" (i.e. move the general up).
      • 坐上山頂 (co5 soeng5 saan1 deng2): "Sit up [to the] mountain top" (i.e. move the general up to the top row of the palace).
      • 坐落 (co5 lok6): "Sit down" (i.e. move the general down).
      • 坐出 (co5 ceot1): "Sit out" (i.e. move the general out of cover of the centre column).
      • 坐入 (co5 jap6): "Sit in" (i.e. move the general back into cover of the centre column).
      • 坐歪 (co5 me2): "Sit off-centre" (i.e. move the general off the uncovered centre column).
      • 坐正 (co5 zeng3): "Sit right" (i.e. move the general back onto the uncovered centre column).
      • 坐咗佢 (co5 zo2 keoi5): "Sit [over] it" (i.e. capture with the general).
  • 【坐低】 (co5 dai1):
    1. To sit down.
  • 【坐穩機】 (co5 wan2 gei1):
    1. A farewell wish for a safe flight.

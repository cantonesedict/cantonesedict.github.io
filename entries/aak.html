Williams (1856) entry

  • A yoke;

阨 (U+9628) ák꜆ (aak1)

Reading variations
(ak1: shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3)
A colloquial word; also pronounced ak꜆ (ak1) and ngak꜆ (ngak1)
  • To deceive, to delude, to take in;
  • ꜂kòm yéung꜅ ák꜆ ꜁yan ké꜄ (gam2 joeng6-2 aak1 yan4 ge3-2 噉樣阨人嘅) are you going to cheat him so!
Conway's remarks
  • Most modern speakers prefer this longer syllable over the shorter ak꜆ (ak1); see ak#阨1 for the literary senses.

Ák꜆. (aak3 低陰入)

鈪 (U+922A) ák꜆ (aak3)

Reading variations
(aak3-2: colloquial rising-tone noun)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3)
An unauthorized character.
  • A bracelet;
  • a bangle;
  • kéuk꜆ ák꜆ (goek3 aak3-2 腳鈪) a[n] anklet;
  • ꜀kam ák꜆ (gam1 aak3-2 金鈪) a gold wristlet;
  • ꜀séung ák꜆ (soeng1 aak3-2 鑲鈪) a plated or gilded ring;
  • ák꜆ ꜁tʻau (aak3-2 tau4 鈪頭), clasps of bracelets.

阨 (U+9628) ák꜆ (aak1)

Reading variations
(ak1: shorter syllable)
Williams (1856) entry
(Page 3)
A colloquial word; also pronounced ak꜆ (ak1) and ngak꜆ (ngak1)
  • To deceive, to delude, to take in;
  • ꜂kòm yéung꜅ ák꜆ ꜁yan ké꜄ (gam2 joeng6-2 aak1 yan4 ge3-2 噉樣阨人嘅) are you going to cheat him so!
Conway's remarks
  • Most modern speakers prefer this longer syllable over the shorter ak꜆ (ak1); see ak#阨1 for the literary senses.

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 3 阨꜆; here normalised to 呃)

    A colloquial word; also pronounced ak꜆ (ak1) and ngak꜆ (ngak1)

    • To deceive, to delude, to take in;
    • ꜂kòm yéung꜅ ák꜆ ꜁yan ké꜄ (gam2 joeng6-2 aak1 jan4 ge3-2 噉樣呃人嘅) are you going to cheat him so!
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【呃】 (aak1, ngaak1):
    1. To deceive, to trick, to scam.
      • 你呃人 (nei5 ngaak1 jan4): "You're deceiving others."
      • 呃錢 (aak1 cin4-2): "[That's a] scam [for my] money."

  • (Page 3 軛꜆)
    • A yoke (Kangxi: 轅端橫木駕馬領者);
    • the yoke of a carriage;
    • a yoke, a restraint, a principle of conscience.

鈪 Ák꜆ (aak3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 4
Unicode code point
U+922A
Reading variations
  • aak3-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
  • ngaak3 (alternative)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 90 鈪) [哀]百切鈪[鐲]也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 3 鈪꜆)

    An unauthorized character.

    • A bracelet;
    • a bangle;
    • kéuk꜆ ák꜆ (goek3 aak3-2 腳鈪) an anklet;
    • ꜀kam ák꜆ (gam1 aak3-2 金鈪) a gold wristlet;
    • ꜀séung ák꜆ (soeng1 aak3-2 鑲鈪) a plated or gilded ring;
    • ák꜆ ꜁tʻau (aak3-2 tau4 鈪頭), clasps of bracelets.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 表 to 哀, and 燭 to 鐲.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鈪】 (aak3-2):
    1. A bracelet or bangle.
      • 手鈪 (sau2 aak3-2): "[A] hand bracelet."

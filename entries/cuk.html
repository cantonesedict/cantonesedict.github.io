Conway's commentary
  • Much confusion exists in relation to the Middle Chinese readings of this character.
    • гҖҠе»Јйҹ»гҖӢ has two headline readings:
      1. дё‘ж•‘еҲҮ chК»auкң„ (cau3) for the "livestock" sense; and
      2. иЁұз«№еҲҮ hukкңҶ (huk1) for the "rear" sense.
      But within these two entries, it also provides alternative readings (еҸҲиЁұе®ҘиЁұе…ӯдё‘е…ӯдёүеҲҮ and еҸҲдё‘е…ӯиЁұж•‘дәҢеҲҮ respectively), and it is unclear whether these are intended as cross-references or as genuine alternatives for the senses given.
    • Kangxi has a hodgepodge:
      1. дё‘е…ӯеҲҮ chК»ukкңҶ (cuk1) for "rearing-field" (з”°з•ңд№ҹ), "gather" (з©Қд№ҹ), and "restrain" (жӯўд№ҹ), among other senses;
      2. иЁұе…ӯеҲҮ hukкңҶ (huk1) for "rear" (йӨҠд№ҹ), "accommodate" (е®№д№ҹ), "livestock" (е…ӯз•ң) (with a note that д»Ҡдәәр ҖӨи®ҖдҪңжҳҢе…ӯеҸҚ), and "obey" (й Ҷд№ҹ), among other senses;
      3. иЁұж•‘еҲҮ yauкң„ (jau3) for "non-working livestock" (йӨҠд№Ӣжӣ°з•ңз”Ёд№Ӣжӣ°зүІ); and
      4. дё‘ж•‘еҲҮ chК»auкң„ (cau3) for "domesticated animal" (еңЁйҮҺжӣ°зҚёеңЁе®¶жӣ°з•ң).
    Modern Cantonese simply merges all readings into chК»ukкңҶ (cuk1).

慉 Chʻuk꜆ (cuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼼ + 10
Unicode code point
U+6149
Reading variations
  • huk1 (per 《廣韻》許竹切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 64 慉) 亦養也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 36* 慉꜆)
    • To sustain (Kangxi: [慉]養也);
    • to encourage (Kangxi: 起也);
    • chʻuk꜆ ꜀kiú (cuk1 giu1 慉驕), to excite pride;
    • chʻuk꜆ nò꜅ (cuk1 nou6 慉怒) to stir up anger.
Conway's commentary
  • Broadly, there are two interpretations of this character:
    1. To uplift (《說文》起也, defended by 段玉裁's annotations); and
    2. To sustain (per 《朱傳》, 《箋》, 《正義》 as appears in Kangxi).
  • Williams' 慉驕: This is a misreading of Kangxi [慉]驕也, which is an explanation of meaning (to be punctuated 慉、驕也), rather than an example of a two-character phrase.

畜 Chʻuk꜆ (cuk1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽥ + 5
Unicode code point
U+755C
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 64 畜) 畜養
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 36* 畜꜆)
    • To rear, to feed, to raise (Kangxi: 養也);
    • to domesticate, to herd;
    • to cultivate;
    • to restrain (Kangxi: [畜]止也);
    • (…);
    • to bear with (Kangxi: 容也);
    • to obey (Kangxi: 順也);
    • domestic animals (Kangxi: 六[畜]);
    • chʻuk꜆ ꜀shang (cuk1 sang1 畜牲), you brute!
    • luk꜇ chʻuk꜆ (luk6 cuk1 六畜) the six domestic animals;
    • chʻuk꜆ ꜃yéung (cuk1 joeng5 畜養), to raise cattle.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 蓄. See 蓄 cuk1 for the canonical entry.)

  • (Page 36* 蓄꜆; here corrected to 畜)
    • (…);
    • to nourish, to rear;
    • (…);
    • chʻuk꜆ ꜀á ꜁tʻau (cuk1 aa1 tau4 畜丫頭), to bring up slave girls.
Conway's commentary
  • Much confusion exists in relation to the Middle Chinese readings of this character.
    • 《廣韻》 has two headline readings:
      1. 丑救切 chʻau꜄ (cau3) for the "livestock sense"; and
      2. 許竹切 huk꜆ (huk1) for the "rear" sense.
      But within these two entries, it also provides alternative readings (又許宥許六丑六三切 and 又丑六許救二切 respectively), and it is unclear whether these are intended as cross-references or as genuine alternatives for the senses given.
    • Kangxi has a hodgepodge:
      1. 丑六切 chʻuk꜆ (cuk1) for "rearing-field" (田畜也), "gather" (積也), and "restrain" (止也), among other senses;
      2. 許六切 huk꜆ (huk1) for "rear" (養也), "tolerate" (容也), "livestock" (六畜) (with a note that 今人𠀤讀作昌六反), and "obey" (順也), among other senses;
      3. 許救切 jau꜄ (jau3) for "non-working livestock" (養之曰畜用之曰牲); and
      4. 丑救切 chʻau꜄ (cau3) for "domesticated animal" (在野曰獸在家曰畜).
    Modern Cantonese simply merges all readings into chʻuk꜆ (cuk1).

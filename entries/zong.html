妝 ꜀Chong (zong1)

Radical + residual strokes

Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 95 粧) 粧飾又嫁粧
  • (Vol. 1 Image 95 妝) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 27* ꜀粧꜀妝)
    • To adorn the head;
    • ornamented, dressed;
    • to dress;
    • to rouge, to adorn;
    • to feign, to appear in false guise;
    • ꜀sho ꜀chong (so1 zong1 梳妝), to dress the hair;
    • ꜀chong pán꜅ (zong1 baan6 妝扮) the dress, the style;
    • ꜀chong pán꜅ ꜂hí ꜁lai (zong1 baan6 hei2 lai4 妝扮起嚟), her toilet is made;
    • ím꜅ ꜀chong (jim6 zong1 豔妝), elegantly dressed;
    • ꜀chong shik꜆ (zong1 sik1 妝飾) dressed out, elegant, adorned;
    • ꜀chong ꜁lím (zong1 lim4 妝奩), or ká꜄ ꜀chong (gaa3 zong1 嫁妝), a bride's trousseau, or dowry;
    • ꜀sho ꜀chong ꜁pʻún (so1 zong1 pun4 梳妝盆), a paper toilette burned to Diana in the 7th moon;
    • sò꜄ ꜀chong (sou3 zong1 素妝), plainly dressed;
    • ꜀chong ꜀hong (zong1 hong1 妝腔), proud, distant;
    • ꜁yung ꜀chong (jung4 zong1 濃妝), overdressed, bedizened.

莊 ꜀Chong (zong1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 7
Unicode code point
U+838A
Alternative forms
  • 庄
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 95 莊) 端嚴也敬也威也又齊莊
  • (Vol. 1 Image 95 庄) 田庄
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 27* ꜀莊)
    • Plants growing rank or luxuriantly;
    • sedate, serious, grave, stern;
    • correct in conduct;
    • adorned;
    • a farmhouse;
    • a road;
    • ꜀chong ꜁ím (zong1 jim4 莊嚴), severely stern;
    • ꜀chong king꜄ (zong1 ging3 莊敬) serious, solemn;
    • ꜀hong ꜀chong (hong1 zong1 康莊), a level road.
  • (Page 27* ꜀庄; here normalised to 莊)
    • Used for the above (莊);
    • a barn, a grange, a cottage, a workshed;
    • a farmhouse;
    • a dead house or public lararium;
    • a division of a township, a sort of parish;
    • ꜀shán ꜀chong (saan1 zong1 山莊), a watchman's shed;
    • ꜀chong hák꜆ (zong1 haak3 莊客) field laborers;
    • kí꜄ ꜀chong (gei3 zong1 寄莊), to store in the deadhouse;
    • ꜁chʻá ꜀chong (caa4 zong1 茶莊), a tea dépôt in the hills;
    • ꜀tsʻün ꜀chong (cyun1 zong1 村莊), a village;
    • ꜁tʻín ꜀chong (tin4 zong1 田莊), a farmstead;
    • ꜀chong ú꜅ (zong1 wu6 莊戶) a farmer;
    • ꜀chong ꜀ting (zong1 ding1 莊丁), laborers on a farm.
Conway's commentary
  • Also "dealer", in gambling games.

裝 ꜀Chong (zong1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾐ + 7
Unicode code point
U+88DD
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 95 裝) 裝[束]整飾也又嫁裝又行裝
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 27* ꜀裝)
    • Used for the preceding (妝);
    • to bind, to tie on, to dress;
    • style, dress, fashion;
    • to put into, to store, to load or stow in;
    • to pack;
    • to catch in, as rain;
    • to receive, to contain;
    • to send to, as a present;
    • ꜁hang ꜀chong (hang4 zong1 行裝), dress for travel; baggage;
    • ꜁hán ꜀chong (haan4 zong1 閒裝), dishabille, a common dress;
    • mat꜆ ꜃yé ꜀chong ꜁tʻau (mat1 je5 zong1 tau4 乜嘢裝頭), what style, what sort?
    • ꜀chong ꜃lím (zong1 lim5 裝殮), to enshroud;
    • ꜀chong ꜂piú (zong1 biu2 裝裱), to put up, as pictures;
    • ꜀chong ꜂hau (zong1 hau2 裝口), a foreign mercantile house;
    • ꜀chong [꜂]kiú (zong1 giu6-2 裝轎), tip up the sedan;
    • ꜁mò ꜁tʻín ꜀chong (mou4 tin1 zong1 無天裝), no place or heaven for you;
    • pán꜅ chéuk[꜇] ꜁tʻong ꜀chong (baan6 zoek6 tong4 zong1 扮着唐裝) to disguise one's self as a Chinese;
    • ꜀chong ꜂ching (zong1 zing2 裝整), to put in order, to adorn;
    • ꜀chong tsoi꜄ (zong1 zoi3 裝載) to pack, to contain;
    • ꜀chong tsʻiú꜄ (zong1 ciu3 裝？) to pack duties to be forwarded;
    • ꜀chong ꜁tʻau mái꜅ mín꜅ (zong1 tau4 maai6 min6 裝頭賣面) to place the best goods on top to sell by;
    • ꜁Ming ꜀chong (ming4 zong1 明裝), style of the Ming (明) dynasty;
    • ꜀chong ꜁mò tsok꜆ yéung꜅ (zong1 mou4 zok3 joeng6 裝模作樣) to take airs on one;
    • ꜀chong chʻuk꜆ ꜂hò hon꜄ (zong1 cuk1 hou2 hon3 裝束好看) well dressed.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 朿 to 束.
  • Williams' "Used for the preceding": I have not seen 裝 used for 妝.
  • Williams' 裝轎: Corrected ꜃kiú (giu6-5) to ꜂kiú (giu6-2).
  • Williams' 扮着唐裝: Corrected chéuk꜆ (zoek3) to chéuk꜇ (zoek6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【裝飯】 (zong1 faan6):
    1. To fill a bowl with rice.
  • 【裝香】 (zong1 hoeng1):
    1. To insert incense into a censer.

Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 27 ꜀䚎)

    (See 䚎 cong1 for classical senses.)

    A colloquial word.

    • To peep through a crack;
    • to hide one away;
    • ꜀chong ꜁kʻam ꜀chong shuk꜆ (zong1 kam4 zong1 suk1 䚎擒䚎縮) to peep in and out;

    • ꜀chong ꜃kʻü tsʻo꜄ chʻü꜄ (zong1 keoi5 co3 cyu3 䚎佢錯處) mark his errors.

撞 Chong꜅ (zong6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 12
Unicode code point
U+649E
Reading variations
  • zong6-2 (in 撞邪, 撞鬼)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 100 撞) 衝突也又忽至曰撞
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 28* 撞꜅)
    • To beat, to pound;
    • to run or knock against, to dash upon or against;
    • to tap on, as a hoop;
    • to strike accidentally or abruptly;
    • chong꜅ chéuk[꜇] (zong6 zoek6 撞着) to meet suddenly, to run against;
    • chong꜅ pʻín꜄ (zong6 pin3 撞騙) to swindle, to peculate;
    • chong꜅ ꜀ngám (zong6 ngaam1 撞啱), luckily met;
    • chong꜅ ngák꜇ (zong6 ngaak6 撞額) face to face;
    • chong꜅ ꜂kwai (zong6 gwai2 撞鬼), to meet a devil (a foreigner);
    • chong꜅ ꜂hau kwá꜄ (zong6 hau2 gwaa3 撞口卦) to hear an ominous word;
    • chong꜅ ꜁'m kín꜄ (zong6 m4 gin3 撞唔見) I didn't meet him;
    • chong꜅ ꜂pán (zong6 baan2 撞板), to strike castanets, i. e. discordant, a disappointment;
    • pák꜇ chong꜅ (baak6 zong6 白撞) to go into on a pretence, a thief;
    • pák꜇ chong꜅ ꜃ü (baak6 zong6 jyu5 白撞雨), a sudden shower.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 撞着: Corrected chéuk꜆ (zoek3) to chéuk꜇ (zoek6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【白撞】 (baak6 zong6):
    1. To steal by opportunistic trespass.
    2. One who steals through opportunistic trespass.
  • 【白撞雨】 (baak6 zong6 jyu5):
    1. A sun shower.
  • 【撞板】 (zong6 baan2):
    1. To meet with failure, to encounter a difficulty that halts an attempt at something.
  • 【撞彩】 (zong6 coi2):
    1. To obtain, succeed, win, etc. by luck or guessing.
  • 【撞邪】 (zong6-2 ce4):
    1. To be affected by malign supernatural forces.
  • 【撞鬼】 (zong6-2 gwai2):
    1. To be affected by a devil or ghost.
    2. To have seen a ghost.

冊 Chʻák꜆ (caak3)

Radical + residual strokes
冂 + 3
Unicode code point
U+518A
Alternative forms
  • 册
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 89 冊) 排門冊黃冊又皇后初立曰冊立
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 册꜆; here normalised to 冊)
    • A slip;
    • an inventory, a register, a list;
    • a patent or commission of nobility;
    • a census book;
    • to choose;
    • to plan, to make a plan or scheme;
    • ꜁tʻín chʻák꜆ (tin4 caak3 填冊) to enrol, to write a list;
    • ú꜅ ꜂hau chʻák꜆ (wu6 hau2 caak3 戶口冊) a register, a census of people;
    • ꜁pʻái ꜁mún chʻák꜆ (paai4 mun4 caak3 排門冊) the register of families;
    • ꜁wong chʻák꜆ (wong4 caak3 黃冊) imperial census.

拆 Chʻák꜆ (caak3)

Radical + residual strokes
手 + 5
Unicode code point
U+62C6
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 90 拆) 分開也又拆卸
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 拆[꜆])
    • To break up or open;
    • to split, to unrip;
    • to destroy, to pull down, to take away;
    • to dissect, to take to pieces;
    • chʻák꜆ shé꜄ (caak3 se3 拆舍) to pull a house down;
    • chʻák꜆ sán꜄ (caak3 saan3 拆散) to scatter, by breaking or taking to pieces;
    • chʻák꜆ ꜀fung (caak3 fung1 拆封), to open a letter;
    • chʻák꜆ ꜀í ꜁shéung (caak3 ji1 soeng4 拆衣裳), to unrip clothes;
    • chʻák꜆ sái꜄ (caak3 saai3 拆嗮) pulled to pieces;
    • ꜃yau mat꜆ chʻák꜆ fát꜆ (jau5 mat1 caak3 faat3 有乜拆法) what way shall we arrange the matter?
    • (see 坼 caak3 for "cracked" senses).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams misprints 拆꜆ as 拆꜇.
  • Williams' 拆散: Note the subtle difference between the two readings chʻák꜆ sán꜄ (caak3 saan3) "scatter" and chʻák꜆ ꜂sán (caak3 saan2) "break up".

策 Chʻák꜆ (caak3)

Radical + residual strokes
竹 + 6
Unicode code point
U+7B56
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 89 策) 簡策謀策籌策
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 13 策꜆)
    • A bamboo slip, such as books were once engraved on;
    • a book, writings;
    • a stratagem, a plan, a means, an expedient, a scheme;
    • a switch, a whip;
    • a divining slip;
    • to switch;
    • ꜂kán chʻák꜆ (gaan2 caak3 簡策) books, chapters, &c.;
    • ꜁mò chʻák꜆ (mou4 caak3 無策) without plan, schemeless;
    • ꜁chʻau chʻák꜆ (cau4 caak3 籌策) to contrive;
    • chʻák꜆ ꜃má (caak3 maa5 策馬), to whip a horse.

    Used for the next (茦).

賊 Tsʻák꜇ (caak6)

Radical + residual strokes
貝 + 6
Unicode code point
U+8CCA
Reading variations
  • caak3 (vernacular)
  • caak6-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Page 33 賊) 盗賊又賊害
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 551 賊꜇)
    • A thief, a robber, a highwayman;
    • a bandit;
    • seditious, those who rise against the government;
    • the enemy;
    • a term of contempt;
    • to rob, to murder;
    • to maltreat, to oppress;
    • a fly in grain;
    • ꜃lò tsʻák꜇ (lou5 caak6-2 老賊) an old robber;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜂kòm ꜃ngán (caak6 gam2 ngaan5 賊噉眼), eyes sharp as a thief's;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜀ping (caak6 bing1 賊兵), the enemy's force;
    • ꜂hoi tsʻák꜇ (hoi2 caak6 海賊) a pirate, corsair, freebooter;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜁tʻau (caak6 tau4-2 賊頭), a ringleader of robbers;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜂tsai (caak6-2 zai2 賊仔), a rascal, a pilferer;
    • tsò꜅ tsʻák꜇ (zou6 caak6-2 做賊) to turn robber;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜀chong (caak6 zong1 賊贓), booty;
    • tsʻák꜇ ꜁ying (caak6-2 jing4 賊型), a susplcious looking fellow.

Conway's commentary
  • An entry for the reading tsʻák꜇ (caak6) is included here for completeness.
    1. The cuttlefish, as in 鰞鰂 ꜀ú tsʻák꜇ (wu1 caak6), also written 烏賊. Note that in Cantonese, it is usually called 墨魚, "inkfish".

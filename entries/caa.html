叉 ꜀Chʻá (caa1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼜ + 1
Unicode code point
U+53C9
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 8 ꜀叉)
    • A fork in a road;
    • a prong, a crotch;
    • to cross or interlace the fingers (Kangxi: 手指相錯, 指相交也);
    • ꜁wo ꜀chʻá (wo4 caa1 禾叉), a pitch-fork;
    • ꜁ngan ꜀chʻá (ngan4 caa1 銀叉), a silver fork;
    • ꜁ü ꜀chʻá (jyu4 caa1 魚叉), a fish grains;
    • ꜀chʻá ꜀shiú (caa1 siu1 叉燒), to roast on a fork;
    • ꜂shá ꜀chʻá (saa2 caa1 耍叉), to fence with tridents.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【叉】 (caa1):
    1. Short for 叉燒 (char shiu).
      • 叉鴨米 (caa1 aap3 mai5): "Char [shiu], [roast] duck vermicelli."
    2. Euphemistic substitute for any profane intensifier.
      • 冇叉用 (mou5 caa1 jung6): "Bleeping useless."
  • 【叉燒】 (caa1 siu1):
    1. Char shiu, a type of barbecued pork.
      • 叉燒包 (caa1 siu1 baau1): "Barbecued-pork bun."
      • 叉燒腸 (caa1 siu1 coeng4-2): "Barbecued-pork [rice-noodle] rolls."

差 ꜀Chʻá (caa1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼯ + 7
Unicode code point
U+5DEE
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 74 差) 差錯舛謬也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 8 ꜀差)
    • Mistaken (Kangxi: 舛也);
    • to err, to differ (Kangxi: 貳也不相値也);
    • to miss the mark, to exceed;
    • error, fault, discrepancy, excess, differing, unlike, unassorted;
    • tái꜅ ꜀chʻá tak꜇ ꜀ch'á (daai6 caa1 dak6 caa1 大差特差), very wrong;
    • ꜀chʻá tak꜆ ꜃ün (caa1 dak1 jyun5 差得遠), very different;
    • ꜀chʻá tsʻo꜄ kéuk꜆ (caa1 co3 goek3 差錯腳) a blunder, a faux pas;
    • ꜀chʻá tít꜆ ꜁'m ꜁tʻung (caa1 dit1 m4 tung4 差啲唔同), differ very slightly;
    • ꜀chʻá pat꜆ ꜀to (caa1 bat1 do1 差不多), nearly the same;
    • ꜀chʻá tsʻo꜄ (caa1 co3 差錯) a mistake;
    • chʻing꜄ ꜀chʻá sái꜄ (cing3 caa1 saai3 秤差嗮) all weighed wrong.
  • (Page 615 ꜀差 ꜀Tsʻz' (ci1); here corrected to ꜀Chʻá (caa1))
    • (…);
    • ꜀[chʻá] ꜂ho ꜁yan í꜄ (caa1 ho2 jan4 ji3 差可人意) like what most men think.
Conway's commentary
  • The etymology of 阿差, a slur for "South Asian" (see below), is unclear, although it is almost certainly not derived from the "mistake" or "differ" senses. I have heard it said that it is from the presence of Indian policemen in colonial Hong Kong, either:
    • from 摩囉差 (mo1 lo1 caa1), an historical term for an Indian policeman, or
    • from 差人 (caai1 jan4), meaning "officer".
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【阿差】 (aa3 caa1):
    1. (derogatory) A South Asian person.

Fan Wan entry

Conway's commentary
  • I have chosen 搽 over 扠 as the canonical form of caa5 because:
    1. It seems more plausible to me that the word is derived from smudging (with a calligraphic ink brush or finger) than from crossing out (with a pencil or biro);
    2. In terms of pitch distance, it is nicer to have two readings for 搽 in 陽平 (caa4) and 陽上 (caa5), than to have two readings for 扠 in 陰平 (caa1) and 陽上 (caa5); and
    3. The form 搽 for ꜃chʻá (caa5) is attested in Eitel (1877).
  • Wiktionary as of 2023-06-23 suggests 䵦 as the original character, but I am not convinced by this. While the classical meaning (to dirty) is reasonable, the sound is less so. Starting with 《廣韻》徐野切, if we insist on initial 邪 and final 麻 in rising tone, we get ꜃tsʻá and not ꜃chʻá.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【搽】 (caa5):
    1. To smudge or scribble out.
      • 搽咗佢 (caa5 zo2 keoi5): "Scribble it out."
  • 【搽𢰸】 (caa5 wo5):
    1. To ruin or spoil.
      • 搽𢰸咗個計畫 (caa5 wo5 zo2 go3 gai3 waak6): "[It's] spoilt the plan."

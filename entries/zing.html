偵 ꜀Ching (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5075
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 偵) [伺]探也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18* ꜀偵)
    • A spy (vulgo, ye꜅ pat꜆ [꜀]shau (je6 bat1 sau1 夜不收)), a scout;
    • to reconnoitre, to inquire;
    • ꜁yau ꜀ching (jau4 zing1 遊偵) or ꜀ching tʻám꜄ (zing1 taam3 偵探) to spy.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 何 to 伺.
  • Williams' 夜不收: Corrected ꜁shau (sau4) to ꜀shau (sau1).

征 ꜀Ching (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5F81
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 征) 往也取也伐也又正也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18* ꜀征)
    • To go;
    • to take;
    • to collect taxes;
    • to go out to chastise, to reduce refractory states, to subjugate, to conquer;
    • to spy;
    • ꜀ching [fat]꜇ (zing1 fat6 征伐) to reduce to subjection;
    • ꜀ching ꜂tʻò (zing1 tou2 征討), to collect tribute by force;
    • ꜀ching ꜀fú (zing1 fu1 征夫), warriors;
    • ꜀ching ꜀shau (zing1 sau1 征收), to assess [taxes]; (these square brackets appear in the original text)
    • ꜀ching ꜀chü (zing1 zyu1 征誅), to exterminate;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀ching (ceot1 zing1 出征), to go abroad on an expedition;
    • ꜀ching shui꜄ ꜁léung (zing1 seoi3 loeng4 征稅糧), to levy taxes;
    • ꜀ching ꜂tsiú (zing1 ziu2 征勦), to exterminate rebels.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 征討: More to subjugate than to collect tribute.
  • Williams' 征伐: Changed fát꜇ (faat6) to modern fat꜇ (fat6).

  • (Page 19* ꜀徵)

烝 ꜀Ching (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽕ + 6
Unicode code point
U+70DD
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 烝) 衆也
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 蒸) 衆也進也又薰蒸又冬祭曰蒸
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18* ꜀烝)
    • (See 蒸 zing1 for "steam" senses);
    • a multitude, all;
    • to enter, to advance;
    • clouds of dust;
    • liberal, princely;
    • a winter sacrifice;
    • to commit incest with superiors in rank;
    • to lay down;
    • ꜀tʻín ꜀shang ꜀ching ꜁man (tin1 sang1 zing1 man4 天生烝民) heaven produced all men;
    • (…);
    • ꜀ching ꜁shéung (zing1 soeng4 烝嘗), sacrifice in winter; property left by a person;
    • ꜀ching tsun꜄ (zing1 zun3 烝進) to intrude one's self;
    • (…).
Conway's commentary
  • "Steam" senses have been normalised to 蒸 per modern usage. See 蒸 zing1.
  • Williams' "incest with superiors": More generally, adultery with an elder.

蒸 ꜀Ching (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 10
Unicode code point
U+84B8
Alternative forms
  • 烝
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 烝) 衆也
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 蒸) 衆也進也又薰蒸又冬祭曰蒸
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18* ꜀烝; here normalised to 蒸)
    • A vapor made by heat, steam;
    • vapor, mist;
    • to steam, to cook by steaming, to distil;
    • (…);
    • ꜀ching shuk꜇ ꜃há (zing1 suk6 haa5 蒸熟吓), steam it thoroughly;
    • ꜀ching fán꜅ (zing1 faan6 蒸飯) to steam rice;
    • ꜀ching ꜂tsau (zing1 zau2 蒸酒), to distil spirit;
    • ꜀ching ꜁lung (zing1 lung4 蒸籠), a steaming basket;
    • (…);
    • ꜀ching ꜁ngo (zing1 ngo4-2 蒸鵝) a boiled goose.
  • (Page 18* ꜀蒸)

    Used for the last (烝).

    • Twigs of hemp for burning;
    • small firewood;
    • hemp torches;
    • vapor, steam;
    • all, numerous;
    • to rise, as vapor or steam;
    • ꜀ching ꜀ching yat꜇ ꜃shéung (zing1 zing1 jat6 soeng5 蒸蒸日上), daily rising better and higher.

    Read ching꜄ (see 蒸 zing3).

整 ꜂Ching (zing2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽁ + 12
Unicode code point
U+6574
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 72 整) 修造也齊也葺也理也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 19* ꜂整)
    • To even off, to place evenly;
    • to repair, to mend, to put in order, to make;
    • to arrange;
    • to adjust;
    • to adorn;
    • to make new, to trim up;
    • ꜂ching shik꜆ ꜂shui (zing2 sik1 seoi2 整色水) or ꜂ching ꜂kú tsò꜅ kwái꜄ (zing2 gu2 zou6 gwaai3 整古做怪) to sham, as poverty; to dissemble, to simulate;
    • ꜂ching ꜃kʻü ꜂kwai ꜃má (zing2 keoi5 gwai2 maa5 整佢鬼馬), set a catch for him, as in play;
    • ꜂ching ꜁tsʻai (zing2 cai4 整齊), to put things in order, to settle;
    • ꜂ching ꜀piú (zing2 biu1 整錶), to mend a watch;
    • ꜂ching ꜁tʻín (zing2 tin4 整田), to prepare fields for planting;
    • ꜂ching ꜂hò (zing2 hou2 整好), or ꜂ching ꜀fán (zing2 faan1 整返), to repair;
    • ꜂[ching] [꜁]kʻiú ꜀sau lò꜅ (zing2 kiu4 sau1 lou6 整橋修路) to mend bridges and repair roads;
    • ꜂ching chí꜅ ꜁yan (zing2 zi6 jan4 整治人), to correct a man;
    • ꜂ching wái[꜅] (zing2 waai6 整壞), to spoil, to injure.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams records ꜂cheng (zeng2) as an alternative reading (see remarks under zing2). However, I have never heard 整 read ꜂cheng (zeng2).
  • Williams' 整色水: More commonly 整色整水.
  • Williams' 整橋修路: Corrected ꜂cheng (zeng2) to ꜂ching (zing2), and corrected ꜀kʻiú (kiu1) to ꜁kʻiú (kiu4).
  • Williams' 整壞: Corrected ꜁wái (waai4) to wái꜅ (waai6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【整】 (zing2):
    1. To make, to create.
      • 整緊蛋糕 (zing2 gan2 daan6 gou1): "Making [a] cake."
      • 整嘢食 (zing2 je5 sik6): "Make something [to] eat."
    2. To cause to become, to render.
      • 整熱啲 (zing2 jit6 di1): "Make [it] hotter."
      • 整損手 (zing2 syun2 sau2): "Abrased [my] hand."
      • 整冧咗 (zing2 lam3 zo2): "Made [it] fall over".
      • 整爛咗 (zing2 laan6 zo2): "[I] broke [it]" (physically).
      • 整壞咗 (zing2 waai6 zo2): "[I] broke [it]" (functionally).
  • 【整蠱】 (zing2 gu2):
    1. To play a trick or prank on.
  • 【整色整水】 (zing2 sik1 zing2 seoi2):
    1. To do the superficial (as cover for lack of substance), to window-dress.

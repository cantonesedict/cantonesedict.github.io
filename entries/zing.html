征 ꜀Ching (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5F81
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 征) 往也取也伐也又正也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 18* ꜀征)
    • To go;
    • to take (Kangxi: 征取也);
    • to collect taxes;
    • to go out to chastise, to reduce refractory states (Kangxi: 征伐也, 征者上伐下也), to subjugate, to conquer;
    • to spy;
    • ꜀ching [fat]꜇ (zing1 fat6 征伐) to reduce to subjection;
    • ꜀ching ꜂tʻò (zing1 tou2 征討), to collect tribute by force;
    • ꜀ching ꜀fú (zing1 fu1 征夫), warriors;
    • ꜀ching ꜀shau (zing1 sau1 征收), to assess [taxes]; (these square brackets appear in the original text)
    • ꜀ching ꜀chü (zing1 zyu1 征誅), to exterminate;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜀ching (ceot1 zing1 出征), to go abroad on an expedition;
    • ꜀ching shui꜄ ꜁léung (zing1 seoi3 loeng4 征稅糧), to levy taxes;
    • ꜀ching ꜂tsiú (zing1 ziu2 征勦), to exterminate rebels.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "To go": Better rendered "An expedition".
  • Williams' 征討: Not to collect tribute, but to punish.
  • Williams' 征伐: Changed fát꜇ (faat6) to modern fat꜇ (fat6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. An expedition, a long journey (Kangxi: 行也).

旌 ꜀Tsing (zing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽅ + 7
Unicode code point
U+65CC
Reading variations
  • sing1 (common)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 67 旌) 旌旗又旌表
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 575 ꜀旌)
    • A banner, a standard having plumes, which when waved encourage the troops (Kangxi: 析羽注旄首所以進士卒);
    • to make signals;
    • insignia, signals;
    • to discriminate, to make manifest, to prove, to show forth (Kangxi: 旌旗所以表識貴賤故傳以旌爲識, 旌章也, 旌表也, 旌明也);
    • tsing ꜁kʻí (zing1 kei4 旌旗), flags;
    • ꜁ming ꜀tsing (ming4 zing1 明旌), red banners hung by a corpse;
    • ꜀tsing ꜂piú (zing1 biu2 旌表), testimonials of merit granted by the sovereign, and often placed over the door.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to discriminate": This is a misreading of Kangxi 《書畢命》旌別淑慝《傳》言當識別[…]. Here, 旌 means "[make] known". It is 別 which means "discriminate".

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 67 精) 妙也細也純也靈也壯也熟也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 576 before ꜀精)

    (The three next (精, 井, 淨) are often pronounced tseng (zeng))

  • (Page 576 ꜀精)
    • Cleaned rice (Kangxi: 鑿也);
    • selected (Kangxi: 擇也), ripe, mature (Kangxi: 熟也);
    • fine, subtle, delicate (Kangxi: 細也);

    • the essence of things (Kangxi: 靈也眞氣也);
    • it is prefixed to other adjectives to show a high degree of;
    • the animal spirits;
    • the semen, the germinating principle of nature;
    • an apparition, form taken by spirits;
    • ꜂hò ꜀tsing ꜁shan (hou2 zing1 san4 好精神), in good spirits, smart;
    • ꜀tsing ꜀ping (zing1 bing1 精兵), flower of the troops, picked men;
    • ꜁lung ꜃má ꜀tsing ꜁shan (lung4 maa5 zing1 san4 龍馬精神), bright as a steed or dragon;
    • ꜀tseng [꜂]kong tái꜅ wá꜅ (zeng1 gong2 daai6 waa6 精講大話) very clever at lying;
    • ꜃kʻü hai꜅ ꜀tseng (keoi5 hai6 zeng1 佢係精) he's smart enough;
    • ꜀tsing yuk꜇ (zing1 juk6 精肉) lean meat;
    • ꜀tsing hí꜄ (zing1 hei3 精氣) ether, the essence of; influence, pure, the quintessence;
    • ꜀tsing chí꜄ (zing1 zi3 精緻) beautiful, handsome;
    • ꜀[tseng] ꜀kwái (zeng1 gwaai1 精乖), very clever at;
    • ꜀tsing kwái꜄ (zing1 gwaai3 精怪) an elf; spirit rappings, prodigies;
    • ꜁wai ꜀tsing (wai4 zing1 遺精), [involuntary] emissions;
    • ꜃ní ꜀tseng ꜃ngo ꜁'m ꜁ngoi (nei5 zeng1 ngo5 m4 ngoi4 你精我唔呆), if you are cute, I'm not a fool;
    • ꜀tseng [lat]꜆ ꜀pín (zeng1 lat1 bin1 精甩辮), smart as a steel-trap;
    • ꜀tseng ꜂tsai (zeng1 zai2 精仔), a shrewd fellow;
    • ꜀tseng tau꜄ ꜀tseng (zeng1 dau3 zeng1 精鬥精), met his match.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 精講大話: Corrected kong꜄ (gong3) to ꜂kong (gong2).
  • Williams' 精乖: Changed ꜀tsing (zing1) to vernacular reading ꜀tseng (zeng1).
  • Williams' 你精我唔呆: Here, "cute" means "clever".
  • Williams' 精甩辮: Corrected lut꜆ (leot1) to lat꜆ (lat1).

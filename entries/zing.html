Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【整】 (zing2):
    1. To make, to create.
      • 整緊蛋糕 (zing2 gan2 daan6 gou1): "Making [a] cake."
      • 整嘢食 (zing2 je5 sik6): "Make something [to] eat."
    2. To cause to become, to render.
      • 整熱啲 (zing2 jit6 di1): "Make [it] hotter."
      • 整損手 (zing2 syun2 sau2): "Abrased [my] hand."
      • 整冧咗 (zing2 lam3 zo2): "Made [it] fall over".
      • 整爛咗 (zing2 laan6 zo2): "[I] broke [it]" (physically).
      • 整壞咗 (zing2 waai6 zo2): "[I] broke [it]" (functionally).
  • 【整蠱】 (zing2 gu2):
    1. To play a trick or prank on.
  • 【整色整水】 (zing2 sik1 zing2 seoi2):
    1. To do the superficial (as cover for lack of substance), to window-dress.

政 Ching꜄ (zing3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽁ + 5
Unicode code point
U+653F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 72 政) 國政所以止人之不正也又典法曰政
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 19* 政꜄)
    • To rule, to govern;
    • a standard, a regulator;
    • a treatise, a guide on a subject;
    • government, politics;
    • laws, regulations;
    • ching꜄ sz'꜅ (zing3 si6 政事) governmental affairs;
    • ꜀ká ching꜄ (gaa1 zing3 家政) family regulations;
    • shín꜅ ching꜄ (sin6 zing3 善政) good rule;
    • ꜁tsʻung ching꜄ (cung4 zing3 從政) in official employ;
    • ꜁nung ching꜄ (nung4 zing3 農政) farming encyclopedia; a supervisor of agriculture;
    • tsʻat꜆ ching꜄ (cat1 zing3 七政) the sun, moon, and five planets;
    • ching꜄ ling꜅ (zing3 ling6 政令) official orders.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 家政: Now "home economics".
  • Williams' 從政: Or, "pursuing politics".

正 Ching꜄ (zing3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽌ + 1
Unicode code point
U+6B63
Reading variations
  • zeng3 (vernacular adjective)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 72 正) 公也當也不邪也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 19* 正꜄)
    • Correct, straight, right, not awry, erect, even;
    • not inclined, not deflected;
    • regular, usual[,] proper;
    • the first, principal;
    • just, at the time of;
    • constant, usual;
    • genuine, real[,] as goods;
    • to put in order;
    • to assume, to enter on, as an office;
    • to adjust, to govern, to execute the laws, to punish;
    • [cheng]꜄ ꜁'m [cheng]꜄ (zeng3 m4 zeng3 正唔正) is it proper?
    • pat꜆ ching꜄ ꜀king (bat1 zing3 ging1 不正經), licentious, immoral;
    • ching꜄ ꜁yan ꜀kwan ꜂tsz' (zing3 jan4 gwan1 zi2 正人君子) an upright man;
    • ꜀héung ching꜄ (hoeng1 zing3 鄉正) village elders;
    • ching꜄ tong[꜄] (zing3 dong3 正當), as it ought to be;
    • ching꜄ ꜀chung (zing3 zung1 正中), put in the middle;
    • [cheng]꜄ ꜀ngám (zeng3 ngaam1 正啱), placed right;
    • cheng꜄ chéuk[꜇] ꜁shí (zeng3 zoek6 si4 正着時), just in good time;
    • cheng꜄ iú꜄ (zeng3 jiu3 正要) just as I wanted it;
    • ching꜄ lò꜅ ꜀kung ꜁ming (zing3 lou6 gung1 ming4 正路功名), reputation well earned, an honorable fame;
    • ching꜄ ꜀king (zing3 ging1 正經), moral, virtuous;
    • ching꜄ tsoi꜅ ꜀ní chʻü꜄ (zing3 zoi6 ni1 cyu3 正在呢處) he was just here;
    • [꜃tsʻo] cheng꜄ (co5 zeng3 坐正) to sit properly;
    • ching꜄ ꜂kong (zing3 gong2 正講), to speak literally;
    • ching꜄ ꜁tʻong (zing3 tong4 正堂), the main hall, the chief officer;
    • ching꜄ tsung꜅ (zing3 zung6 正從) principals and seconds;
    • ching꜄ chik[꜇] (zing3 zik6 正直) honest, solid, true;
    • ching꜄ ꜁nám mín꜅ (zing3 naam4 min6 正南面) to be emperor;
    • ching꜄ fát꜆ (zing3 faat3 正法) to inflict capital punishment;
    • ching꜄ ꜀sam (zing3 sam1 正心), to reform.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 正唔正: Changed ching꜄ (zing3) to vernacular reading cheng꜄ (zeng3).
  • Williams' 正當: Corrected ꜀tong (dong1) to tong꜄ (dong3). If 當 is in level tone, then it means "just when" instead of "proper".
  • Williams' 正啱: Changed ching꜄ (zing3) to vernacular reading cheng꜄ (zeng3).
  • Williams' 正着時: Corrected chéuk꜆ (zoek3) to chéuk꜇ (zoek6).
  • Williams' 正經: Also "serious" (as opposed to "playful").
  • Williams' 坐正: Corrected tso꜅ (zo6 去聲) to ꜃tsʻo (co5 上聲).
  • Williams' 正直: Corrected chik꜆ (zik1) to chik꜇ (zik6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【快靚正】 (faai3 leng3 zeng3):
    1. Quick, attractive, and quality (said of goods and services).
  • 【平靚正】 (peng4 leng3 zeng3):
    1. Cheap, attractive, and quality (said of goods and services).
  • 【四正】 (si3 zeng3):
    1. Proper (of appearance, position, etc.).
      • 打呔四正啲 (daa2 taai1 si3 zeng3 di1): "Wearing [a] tie [would look] more proper".
    2. Neat, tidy.
      • 執得四正 (zap1 dak1 si3 zeng3): "Tidied up neatly."
  • 【正】 (zeng3):
    1. Great, excellent, fantastic.
      • 好正喎 (hou2 zeng3 wo3): "[Hey, it's] really excellent!"
      • 快靚正 (faai3 leng3 zeng3): "Quick, attractive, [and] good-[quality]."
      • 平靚正 (peng4 leng3 zeng3): "Cheap, attractive, [and] good-[quality]."
  • 【正話】 (zing3 waa6):
    1. A moment ago, just now. (Literally, "[it was] just said".)

淨 Tsing꜅ (zing6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 8
Unicode code point
U+6DE8
Alternative forms
  • 凈
Reading variations
  • zeng6 (vernacular adjective "clean")
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 73 淨) [潔]淨不汚也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 576 淨꜅)
    • Clean, pure, spotless, undefiled;
    • upright, guileless;
    • to wash clean, to cleanse;
    • only;
    • all;
    • a rapids in the R. Hán (漢);
    • ꜀kon tseng꜅ (gon1 zeng6 乾淨) clean, pure;
    • kít꜆ tseng꜅ (git3 zeng6 潔淨) clean, to purify one's heart;
    • tsing꜅ [꜃chʻung] (zing6 cung5 淨重) the nett weight;
    • tseng꜅ ꜂shui ꜁fú (zeng6 seoi2 fu4), a cleansing charm, used in houses;
    • yat꜆ ko꜄ ꜀tò hü꜄ ꜀kon tseng꜅ (jat1 go3 dou1 heoi3 gon1 zeng6 一個都去乾淨) every one has gone.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 𠎧 (⿰亻絜) to 潔.
  • Williams' 淨重: Corrected chung꜅ (zung6 去聲) to ꜃chʻung (cung5 上聲).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【淨】 (zing6):
    1. Purely, only.
      • 淨麵 (zing6 min6): "Only noodles."
      • 淨魚蛋 (zing6 jyu4 daan6-2): "Only fish-balls" (i.e. without noodles).
      • 淨食餸 (zing6 sik6 sung3): "Only eating dishes" (i.e. without rice).

Radical + residual strokes

Han unification vigilance

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 577 靖꜅)
    • To become clean and quiet;
    • small, fine;
    • to plan, to think carefully on;
    • to tranquillize, to restore peace, to clear, as the sea from pirates;

Conway's commentary
  • Arguably, ꜂tsán (zaan2) is a vernacular rising-tone reading of tsán꜅ (zaan6) (expected from 徂賛切), meaning "pretty" (美好皃); see below.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【㜺】 (zaan2):
    1. Wonderful, pretty.
  • 【㜺鬼】 (zaan2 gwai2):
    1. Intriguing, pretty, neat.

Olden attestation for 㜺 tsán꜅ (zaan6) meaning "pretty":

  • 《廣韻》徂賛切：㜺：不謹也一曰美好皃

賺 Chán꜅ (zaan6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾙ + 10
Unicode code point
U+8CFA
Alternative forms
  • 贃
Reading variations
  • zaam6 (expected from 《集韻》直陷切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 65 賺 Chám꜅ (zaam6)) 中淡切猶眈誤也阻之也生意[獲]利亦曰賺
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 16 賺꜅ Chám꜅ (zaam6); here normalised to Chán꜅ (zaan6))
    • To impede, to hinder;
    • to sell, to profit;
    • to gain unfairly, to palm off poor goods;
    • ꜂cho [chán]꜅ (zo2 zaan6 阻賺) to deceive; to hinder.
  • (Page 20 贃꜅; here normalised to 賺)

    An unauthorized word.

    • To profit, to make gain in trade;
    • chán꜅ ꜁tsʻín (zaan6 cin4-2 賺錢), to make money;
    • ꜀to chán꜅ (do1 zaan6 多賺) profitable;
    • ꜃mò tak꜆ chán꜅ (mou5 dak1 zaan6 冇得賺) no profit;
    • chán꜅ tak꜆ ꜀lò (zaan6 dak1 lou1 賺得撈), got my outlay back;
    • chán꜅ wan꜅ (zaan6 wan6 賺混) nothing but a trouble.

    Read ꜃wán (waan5);

    • to sell goods at a profit.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The supplied dissection 中淡切 is incorrect, as it results in the 陰去 reading chám꜄ (zaam3), rather than the intended 陽去 reading chám꜅ (zaam6). Normalised 𫉬 (⿱艹⿰犭隻) to 獲.
  • Williams' reading ꜃wán (waan5) for the authorised sense is incorrect, possibly based on the gloss 音綰 (which is intended in the departing tone, not the rising tone). The expected reflex is waan3 from Middle Chinese 烏患切.

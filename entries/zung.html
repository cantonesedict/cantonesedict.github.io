中 ꜀Chung (zung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼁ + 3
Unicode code point
U+4E2D
Alternative forms
  • 鍾 (in vernacular 中意 spelt 鍾意)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 57 中) 中正不偏也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 38* ꜀中)
    • The middle, the centre[;]
    • half (Kangxi: 半也), in the middle of;
    • within, in (Kangxi: 內也);
    • ꜀chung ꜀kán (zung1 gaan1 中間), between, inside;
    • ꜁'m ꜀chung yung꜅ (m4 zung1 jung6 唔中用) useless, inefficient;
    • noi꜅ ꜀chung ꜃yau (noi6 zung1 jau6 內中有), there are some (or such);
    • ꜁yan ꜀chung (jan4 zung1 人中), the suture on the lip;
    • ꜀chung ching꜄ (zung1 zing3 中正) indifferent, in the centre;
    • ꜀chung í꜄ (zung1 ji3 中意) to like, agreeable;
    • ꜀chung ꜂chí (zung1 zi2 中止), half done, incomplete;
    • ꜀chung ꜁nín (zung1 nin4 中年) middle-aged, i.e. forty;
    • ꜀chung ꜂tang ꜁yan ꜁tsʻoi (zung1 dang2 jan4 coi4 中等人才), mediocre, of common abilities;
    • ꜀Chung kwok꜆ (zung1 gwok3 中國) China;
    • ꜀sam ꜀chung (sam1 zung1 心中), in my mind;
    • ꜀chung ꜁tʻò (zung1 tou4 中途), halfway.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 唔中用: In the literary register, 中 would be departing chung꜄ (zung3).
  • Williams' 人中: That is, the philtrum.
  • Williams' 中意: In the literary register, 中 would be departing chung꜄ (zung3).
  • The Kangxi entry is rather poor, launching straight into a series of unannotated examples, starting with 《書大禹謨》允執厥中, without ever explaining the main sense, "centre".
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【中意】 (zung1 ji3):
    1. To like. Also spelt 鍾意.
      • 中意睇戲 (zung1 ji3 tai2 hei3): "[I] like watching movies."
      • 唔中意佢 (m4 zung1 ji3 keoi5): "[I] don't like him."

When I was younger, I was amongst the camp that believed 鍾意 to be the "correct" spelling of zung1 ji3, based on terms like 鍾情. However, I no longer think this is as clear cut, since 鍾 in 鍾情 derives from the sense 聚也 "gather/concentrate".

I now find it more plausible for zung1 ji3 to be a vernacular misreading of literary 中意 zung3 ji3.

  • Williams' 終不改格: Or 終不改革.

豵 ꜀Tsung (zung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾗ + 11
Unicode code point
U+8C75
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 58 豵) 一歲豕也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 607 ꜀豵)
    • A pig six months or a year old (Kangxi: 生六月豚一曰一歲豵尚叢聚也);
    • a pig, a shote;
    • the last of a litter, a litter;
    • met. a large family.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a large family": This is incorrect. Kangxi has 《爾雅釋獸》豕生三豵《註》豬生子常多故別其少者之名, which gives the sense of "sow birthing three", with the commentary saying, "Pig-birthed offspring [are] commonly many: therefore [we] distinguish [the] name of them that [have] few thereof." Based on this, having three piglets is considered few, not many.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A sow that has given birth to three (Kangxi: 豕生三豵).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "A measure equal to 4 tau": This is a misreading of Kangxi 六斛四斗曰鍾, which puts the measure at 64 tau, not 4 tau.
  • Williams' "to collect": Or, to concentrate.
  • Williams' "to bestow, to confer": More specifically, that from Heaven.
  • Williams' "to repeat, heavy": These are renderings of Kangxi 鍾重也. It is unclear whether 重 here is level-tone ꜁chʻung (cung4) "repeated" or rising-tone ꜃chʻung (cung5) "heavy", though the latter is slightly more likely due to what follows (染色欲其深重).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A vessel for storing alcohol (Kangxi: 酒器也 etc.).
  2. That bestowed or conferred from Heaven (Kangxi: 天所賦予亦曰鍾).
  3. A measure (Kangxi: 量名) of either 64 pecks (Kangxi: 六斛四斗曰鍾), 80 pecks (Kangxi: 八斛也), or 100 pecks (Kangxi: 鍾十斛).
  4. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).

重 Chung꜅ (zung6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾥ + 2
Unicode code point
U+91CD
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 63 重) 厚也又愼重輕重
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 40* 重꜅)
    • (…);
    • momentous (Kangxi: 毛氏曰[…]因其可重而重之與再重鄭重皆去聲);
    • severe, heinous;
    • decorous, grave;
    • to regard, to give importance to (Kangxi: 尊也, 尚也);
    • a sign of the comparative;
    • (…)
    • chung꜅ ꜂hò (zung6 hou2 重好), still better;
    • shan꜅ chung꜅ (san6 zung6 慎重) correct, well principled;
    • tsz'꜅ chung꜅ (zi6 zung6 自重) self-respect;
    • (…);
    • chung꜅ fú꜄ ꜀hí ꜁pʻan (zung6 fu3 hei1 pan4 重富欺貧), to court the rich and despise the poor.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams (following Fan Wan) does not provide separate entries for rising-tone ꜃chʻung (cung5) and departing-tone chung꜅ (zung6), whose distinction is still well preserved in modern Cantonese.
  • I have split things up accordingly, noting that the "severe, heinous" sense is able to fit both readings (and, in particular, is departing in 嚴重).
  • Kangxi has commentary claiming that the rising and departing readings were anciently interchangeable, and that maintaining a distinction between them is 拘泥之過 an "erroneous clinging" on the part of Sung Dynasty scholars:
    按說文柱用切厚也卽與輕重義同集韻柱勇切愼也卽與鄭重義同上去雖有二音𠀤無二義古人三聲通用必謂上去異訓不可通押此宋人拘泥之過也
    However, both Zhengzhang and Baxter–Sagart reconstruct three readings for 重 in Old Chinese, corresponding to Middle Chinese level, rising, and departing.
  • Williams' 慎重: Better rendered "prudent" or "careful".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Further (Kangxi: 更爲也, 重再也).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【重】 (zung6):
    1. Even more, still.
      • 重高過我 (zung6 gou1 gwo3 ngo5): "Even taller than me."
      • 噉重危險 (gam2 zung6 ngai4 him2): "This [is] even more dangerous."
      • 噉重污糟 (gam2 zung6 wu1 zou1): "This [is] even dirtier."
      • 比佢重平 (bei2 keoi5 zung6 peng4): "Cheaper still, compared to that."
    2. Yet, still.
      • 重嚟 (zung6 lai4): "Still coming [at me]?"
      • 重污糟 (zung6 wu1 zou1): "Still dirty."
      • 重未到 (zung6 mei6 dou3): "Still yet to arrive."
      • 重有飯 (zung6 jau5 faan6): "There's still rice."
    3. And also, as well.
      • 重有甜品送 (zung6 jau5 tim4 ban2 sung3): "And also there's dessert given for free."

All these senses of 重 are commonly spelt 仲.

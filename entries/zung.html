中 ꜀Chung (zung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼁ + 3
Unicode code point
U+4E2D
Alternative forms
  • 鍾 (in vernacular 中意 spelt 鍾意)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 57 中) 中正不偏也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 38* ꜀中)
    • The middle, the centre[;]
    • half (Kangxi: 半也), in the middle of;
    • within, in (Kangxi: 內也);
    • ꜀chung ꜀kán (zung1 gaan1 中間), between, inside;
    • ꜁'m ꜀chung yung꜅ (m4 zung1 jung6 唔中用) useless, inefficient;
    • noi꜅ ꜀chung ꜃yau (noi6 zung1 jau6 內中有), there are some (or such);
    • ꜁yan ꜀chung (jan4 zung1 人中), the suture on the lip;
    • ꜀chung ching꜄ (zung1 zing3 中正) indifferent, in the centre;
    • ꜀chung í꜄ (zung1 ji3 中意) to like, agreeable;
    • ꜀chung ꜂chí (zung1 zi2 中止), half done, incomplete;
    • ꜀chung ꜁nín (zung1 nin4 中年) middle-aged, i.e. forty;
    • ꜀chung ꜂tang ꜁yan ꜁tsʻoi (zung1 dang2 jan4 coi4 中等人才), mediocre, of common abilities;
    • ꜀Chung kwok꜆ (zung1 gwok3 中國) China;
    • ꜀sam ꜀chung (sam1 zung1 心中), in my mind;
    • ꜀chung ꜁tʻò (zung1 tou4 中途), halfway.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 唔中用: In the literary register, 中 would be departing chung꜄ (zung3).
  • Williams' 人中: That is, the philtrum.
  • Williams' 中意: In the literary register, 中 would be departing chung꜄ (zung3).
  • The Kangxi entry is rather poor, launching straight into a series of unannotated examples, starting with 《書大禹謨》允執厥中, without ever explaining the main sense, "centre".
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【中意】 (zung1 ji3):
    1. To like. Also spelt 鍾意.
      • 中意睇戲 (zung1 ji3 tai2 hei3): "[I] like watching movies."
      • 唔中意佢 (m4 zung1 ji3 keoi5): "[I] don't like him."

When I was younger, I was amongst the camp that believed 鍾意 to be the "correct" spelling of zung1 ji3, based on terms like 鍾情. However, I no longer think this is as clear cut, since 鍾 in 鍾情 derives from the sense 聚也 "gather/concentrate".

I now find it more plausible for zung1 ji3 to be a vernacular misreading of literary 中意 zung3 ji3.

  • Williams' 忠厚: Changed hau꜅ (hau6) to common reading ꜃hau (hau5).

終 ꜀Chung (zung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽷ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7D42
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 57 終) 末也盡也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 39* ꜀終)
    • The end, the termination, a finis (Kangxi: 極也窮也, 一曰盡也);
    • death;
    • to end, to terminate;
    • to the last;
    • to end one's days;
    • ꜀fún ꜂hò pat꜆ ꜀chung (fun1 hou2 bat1 zung1 歡好不終) not again to be on good terms;
    • ꜀chung ꜀sü (zung1 seoi1 終須), must have it, need it;
    • ꜀chung ꜀shan (zung1 san1 終身), all one's life;
    • ꜁lam ꜀chung (lam4 zung1 臨終), near death;
    • ꜀chung yat꜇ (zung1 jat6 終日) the whole day;
    • shín꜅ ꜀chung (sin6 zung1 善終) a happy death;
    • ꜀chung sing꜄ ꜁nán ꜂koi (zung1 sing3 naan4 goi2 終性難改), hard to change one's nature;
    • ꜀chung pat꜆ ꜂koi kák꜆ (zung1 bat1 goi2 gaak3 終不改格) never reformed;
    • ꜁tsʻung yat꜆ ꜁í ꜀chung (cung4 jat1 ji4 zung1 從一而終), firm to one [husband] (these square brackets appear in the original text).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "death": In classical usage, the death of a superior man as opposed to a little man (Kangxi: 君子曰終小人曰死).
  • Williams' "to the last": That is, the whole.
  • Williams' 終不改格: Or 終不改革.

鍾 ꜀Chung (zung1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 9
Unicode code point
U+937E
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 57 鍾) 茶鍾酒鍾又聚也又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 39* ꜀鍾)
    • A measure equal to 4 tau (dau 斗);
    • (…);
    • to collect (Kangxi: 聚也);
    • to bestow, to confer;
    • to repeat;
    • heavy;
    • gifted with, endowed;
    • ꜂sho ꜀chung oi꜄ (so2 zung1 oi3 所鍾愛) whom I love best;
    • ꜀chung ꜁ling (zung1 ling4 鍾？), the luck of a spot for a grave;
    • (…).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 鐘 (for the "bell" sense). See 鐘 zung1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 盅. See 盅 zung1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 中 (in vernacular 中意 spelt 鍾意). See 中 zung1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "A measure equal to 4 tau": This is a misreading of Kangxi 六斛四斗曰鍾, which puts the measure at 64 tau, not 4 tau.
  • Williams' "to collect": Or, to concentrate.
  • Williams' "to bestow, to confer": More specifically, that from Heaven.
  • Williams' "to repeat, heavy": These are renderings of Kangxi 鍾重也. It is unclear whether 重 here is level-tone ꜁chʻung (cung4) "repeated" or rising-tone ꜃chʻung (cung5) "heavy", though the latter is slightly more likely due to what follows (染色欲其深重).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A vessel for storing alcohol (Kangxi: 酒器也 etc.).
  2. That bestowed or conferred from Heaven (Kangxi: 天所賦予亦曰鍾).
  3. A measure (Kangxi: 量名) of either 64 pecks (Kangxi: 六斛四斗曰鍾), 80 pecks (Kangxi: 八斛也), or 100 pecks (Kangxi: 鍾十斛).
  4. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【鐘】 (zung1):
    1. An hour.
      • 半個鐘 (bun3 go3 zung1): "Half [an] hour."
      • 一個鐘 (jat1 go3 zung1): "One hour."
      • 個半鐘 (go3 bun3 zung1): "[An] hour [and a] half."
      • 三個鐘 (saam1 go3 zung1): "Three hours."
    2. Time.
      • 過咗鐘 (gwo3 zo2 zung1): "[It's] past time."
      • 夠鐘食晏 (gau3 zung1 sik6 aan3): "It's time [to] eat lunch."
      • 揼波鐘 (dam1 bo1 zung1): "Stalling for time" (originally in ball games, but now more generally).

眾 Chung꜄ (zung3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽬ + 6
Unicode code point
U+773E
Alternative forms
  • 衆
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 62 衆) 多也
  • (Vol. 1 Image 62 众) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 40* 衆꜄; here normalised to 眾)
    • A multitude (Kangxi: 多也), a concourse of at least three (Kangxi: 國語三人爲眾數成於三也);
    • a quorum, a majority;
    • a sign of the plural;
    • they;
    • much people, many, all;
    • chung꜄ ꜀to (zung3 do1 眾多), a great many;
    • chung꜄ muk꜇ ꜂sho kín꜄ (zung3 muk6 so2 gin3 眾目所見) every body saw it;
    • ꜂hò chung꜄ tí꜅ ꜀fong (hou2 zung3 dei6 fong1 好眾地方), a thickly settled place;
    • tái꜅ chung꜄ (daai6 zung3 大眾) all of you; the company;
    • chung꜄ ꜁yan (zung3 jan4 眾人), all men, mankind;
    • yat꜆ chung꜄ ꜀[tsang] (jat1 zung3 zang1 一眾僧), a company of priests;
    • yat꜆ chung꜄ (jat1 zung3 一眾) one bonze;
    • chung꜄ yéuk꜆ (zung3 joek3 眾約) all the neighborhoods;
    • chung꜄ ꜃í (zung3 ji5 眾議), public opinion;
    • chung꜄ wai꜅ (zung3 wai6-2 眾位) all the gentlemen.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 大眾: Better rendered "the masses".
  • Williams' 一眾僧: Changed ꜀sang (sang1) to common reading ꜀tsang (zang1).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The masses, the multitude (Kangxi: 寡者爲人上者也眾者爲人下者也).

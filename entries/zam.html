斟 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽃ + 9
Unicode code point
U+659F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 36 斟) 斟酌
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀斟)
    • To pour from or into (斟益也, 言斟酌而益之);
    • to lade out, to lighten;
    • to deliberate, to adjust (Kangxi: 斟取也, 計也);
    • a ladle, a spoon (Kangxi: 勺也);
    • ꜀cham ꜃tʻo lok꜇ lok꜆ (zam1 to5 lok3 斟妥咯) all well settled;
    • ꜁tʻung ꜃ní ꜀cham ꜀cham (tung4 nei5 zam1 zam1 同你斟斟), let us talk it over;
    • ꜀cham ꜁chʻá (zam1 caa4 斟茶), to pour out tea;
    • sai꜅ sai꜄ ꜀cham (sai3 zam1 細斟), to whisper;
    • ꜀cham chéuk꜆ (zam1 zoek3 斟酌) to consult about.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 斟妥咯: Corrected lok꜇ (lok6) to lok꜆ (lok3).
  • Williams' 細斟: Corrected sai꜅ (sai6) to sai꜄ (sai3).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【斟茶遞水】 (zam1 caa4 dai6 seoi2):
    1. Menial work. (Literally, "pour tea [and] pass [the] water".)

Radical + residual strokes

Fan Wan entry

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂怎 ꜂Cham)
    • An interrogative word;
    • what, how?
    • ꜂cham yéung꜅ (zam2 joeng6 怎樣) in what manner;

  • (Page 551 ꜂怎 ꜂Tsam)
    • What?
    • ꜂tsam ꜀mo (zam2 mo1 怎麼), what?
    • ꜂tsam ꜂kong (zam2 gong2 怎講), what does he say? used in the court dialect.

    This is usually read ꜂sham (sam2).

Conway's commentary
  • Kangxi has the "crossbar" and "bone" senses under departing-tone 之賃切 cham꜄ (zam3). I believe that, as nouns, they should appear here, under rising-tone 章荏切 ꜂cham (zam2).
Conway's supplementary entry

  • 【枕住】 (zam2 zyu6):

      • 枕住咳 (zam2 zyu6 kat1): "Persistently coughing."

浸 Cham꜄ Tsam꜄ (zam3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 7
Unicode code point
U+6D78
Alternative forms
  • 湛
Reading variations
  • zam6 (for vernacular "drown" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 38 浸) 漬也潤也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 浸꜄ Cham꜄)

    A colloquial word.

    • A coat, as of paint;
    • a thickness, an envelop, a skin;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜀sám cham꜄ (cat1 saam1 zam3 漆三浸) varnished it three times;
    • mok꜆ yat꜆ cham꜄ ꜁pʻí (mok1 jat1 zam3 pei4 剝一浸皮), pull off one covering.
  • (Page 16 湛꜄ Chám꜄ (zaam3); here corrected to Tsam꜄ (zam3) and normalised to 浸)
    • (…);
    • to soak, to immerse, to imbibe, as a sponge (Kangxi: 與浸同漬也);
    • to plunge in (Kangxi: 投物水中也);
    • (…).
  • (Page 551 浸꜄ Tsam꜄)
    • To soak through, to penetrate (Kangxi: 漬也);
    • to saturate, to steep in, to macerate, to wet (Kangxi: 潤也);
    • to submerge, to immerse (Kangxi: 沒也);
    • to flood, to lay under water, as fields (Kangxi: 涵也沈也澤之總名也);
    • drenched, wet, imbued with, watered;
    • gradually, gently (Kangxi: 漸也);
    • tsam꜄ ꜂sz' (zam3 sei2 浸死) drowned;
    • tsam꜄ ꜁tʻong (zam3 tong4 浸糖) soaked in syrup;
    • tsam꜄ yun꜅ (zam3 jeon6 浸潤) to imbue with, to prejudice against, to bias;
    • tsam꜄ chá꜅ (zam3 zaa6 浸？) niggardly, closefisted;
    • ꜂shui tsam꜄ (seoi2 zam3 水浸) overflowed, flooded;
    • hon꜅ tsam꜄ ꜁hung ꜁ngán (hon6 zam3 hung4 ngaan4 汗浸紅顏), the perspiration rolled down his face;
    • tsam꜄ shap꜆ (zam3 sap1 浸濕) wet it, soak it;
    • ꜂pí ꜂shui tsam꜄ chü꜅ (bei2 seoi2 zam3 zyu6 畀水浸住) cover it with water;
    • (…);
    • tsam꜄ ꜂tsau (zam3 zau2 浸酒), spirits in which fruits have been soaked;
    • tsam꜄ yap꜇ ꜀sam (zam3 jap6 sam1 浸入心), it is wet through.

    (Otherwise, a reading variation. See 浸 cam1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Some hold that the reading variation zam6 for the vernacular "drown" sense is from Middle Chinese 直禁切 cham꜅ (zam6) for 沈 (now spelt 沉). For this departing-tone reading of 沈, note that:
    • 《廣韻》 provides no explanation (it simply cross-references the level-tone reading chʻam (cam4) as 又直壬切).
    • 《集韻》 has the explanation 沒也.
    • Kangxi has 亦沒也 followed by the alternative sense 一曰投物水中也.
  • Williams' 汗浸紅顏: Although 紅顏 does not preclude men, it is generally used to describe a lady's face.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【浸】 (zam3):
    1. Classifier for layers.
      • 剷甩浸油 (caan2 lat1 zam3 jau4-2): "Strip away [the] layer of paint."
      • 有浸肥膏 (jau5 zam3 fei4 gou1): "There's [a] layer of fat."

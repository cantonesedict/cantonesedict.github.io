斟 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
斗 + 9
Unicode code point
U+659F
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀斟)
    • To pour from or into;
    • to lade out, to lighten;
    • to deliberate, to adjust;
    • a ladle, a spoon;
    • ꜀cham ꜃tʻo lok꜆ (zam1 to5 lok3 斟綏咯) all well settled;
    • ꜁tʻung ꜃ní ꜀cham ꜀cham (tung4 nei5 zam1 zam1 同你斟斟), let us talk it over;
    • ꜀cham ꜁chʻá (zam1 caa4 斟茶), to pour out tea;
    • sai[꜄] ꜀cham (sai3 zam1 細斟), to whisper;
    • ꜀cham chéuk꜆ (zam1 zoek3 斟酌) to consult about.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 斟綏咯: Corrected lok꜇ (lok6) to lok꜆ (lok3).
  • Williams' 細斟: Corrected sai꜅ (sai6) to sai꜄ (sai3).
Conway's entry
  • 【斟茶遞水】 (zam1 caa4 dai6 seoi2):
    1. Literally "pour tea [and] pass [the] water"; figuratively, menial work.

Unicode code point

砧 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
石 + 5
Unicode code point
U+7827
Alternative forms
  • 碪
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀砧꜀碪)
    • A stone to beat clothes on;
    • an anvil;
    • a block for lifting by athletes;
    • muk꜇ ꜀cham (muk6 zam1 木砧), a horse block;
    • ꜀chü ꜂pí ꜀cham (zyu1 bei2 zam1 豬髀砧), a fillet of pork;
    • ꜀cham ꜂pán (zam1 baan2 砧板), a cutting board, used by butchers.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 砧板: Now chopping boards in general (not necessarily a butcher's).

箴 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
竹 + 9
Unicode code point
U+7BB4
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀箴)

    Used for the last (針).

    • A probe, a surgeon's needle;
    • to probe;
    • a caution, a check;
    • pointed, urgent;
    • to exhort, to rebuke, to urge to reform;
    • ꜀cham ꜀kwʻai (zam1 kwai1 箴規) admonition, rebukes;
    • ꜀cham ꜁ming (zam1 ming4 箴銘), cautionary maxims.

針 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
金 + 2
Unicode code point
U+91DD
Alternative forms
  • 鍼
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀針꜀鍼)
    • A needle;
    • a cauterizing probe;
    • to prick;
    • to use needles in acupuncture;
    • leaves of the pine;
    • ꜀chʻün ꜀cham (cyun1 zam1 穿針), to thread a needle;
    • [꜂]kú ꜁chʻui ꜀cham (gu1 ceoi4 zam1 鼓錘針), a pin;
    • ꜀cham pí꜅ séuk[꜆] tʻít[꜆] (zam1 bei6 soek3 tit3 針鼻削鐵) mean, stingy;
    • ꜀cham ꜂chí (zam1 zi2 針黹), to embroider;
    • ꜁hon ꜂shü ꜀cham (hon4 syu2 zam1 寒暑針) a thermometer;
    • ꜃ngán ꜀cham (ngaan5 zam1 眼針), a needle;
    • ꜀man ꜀cham (man1 zam1 蚊針), a musquito's sting.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 鼓錘針: Corrected ꜀kú (gu1) to ꜂kú (gu2).
  • Williams' 針鼻削鐵: Corrected séuk꜇ tʻít꜇ (soek6 tit6) to séuk꜆ tʻít꜆ (soek3 tit3).

枕 ꜂Cham (zam2)

Radical + residual strokes
木 + 4
Unicode code point
U+6795
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜂枕)
    • A pillow; a stake;
    • a crossbar in a carriage;
    • (see 枕 zam3 for verb senses)
    • ꜂cham ꜀pín (zam2 bin1 枕邊), in bed, in private;
    • ꜂cham pún꜅ (zam2 bun6 枕伴) a bedfellow;
    • ꜂cham tsik꜇ ꜁nán ꜀on (zam2 zik6 naan4 on1 枕席難安), cares disturb his pillow, harassed;
    • ꜃í ꜂cham (ji5 zam2 耳枕), a support for the ear;
    • (…)
    • ꜂cham ꜁tʻau (zam2 tau4 枕頭), a pillow;
    • ꜂cham kwat꜆ (zam2 gwat1 枕骨) the occiput;
    • ꜀on ꜂cham ꜁mò ꜀yau (on1 zam2 mou4 jau1 安枕無憂), to sleep quietly.

    As a verb, also read cham꜄ (zam3) (see 枕 zam3).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' also read cham꜄: Strictly, the verb sense should only be read in the departing tone cham꜄ (zam3). The rising tone reading ꜂cham (zam2) is for the noun sense. I have split things up accordingly.
Conway's entry
  • 【後尾枕】 (hau6 mei5 zam2):
    1. The occiput (back of the head/skull).

枕 Cham꜄ (zam3)

Radical + residual strokes
木 + 4
Unicode code point
U+6795
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜂枕 ꜂Cham (zam2); here normalised to Cham꜄ (zam3))
    • (See 枕 zam2 for noun senses)
    • to pillow on, to lie near to;
    • contiguous, adjacent, lying on;
    • ꜂sz' ꜂ché ꜀séung cham[꜄] tsik꜇ (si2 ze2 soeng1 zam3 zik6 / sei2 ze2 soeng1 zam3 zik6 死者相枕藉) the dead lay pillowed on each other;
    • (…)
    • [kʻuk]꜆ ꜀kwang ꜁í cham[꜄] (kuk1 gwang1 ji4 zam3 曲肱而枕), pillowed on his arm;
    • (…).

    As a verb, also read cham꜄ (zam3).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 曲肱而枕: Changed Middle Chinese huk꜆ (huk1) to common reading kʻuk꜆ (kuk1).
  • Williams' also read cham꜄: Strictly, the verb sense should only be read in the departing tone cham꜄ (zam3). The rising tone reading ꜂cham (zam2) is for the noun sense. I have split things up accordingly.

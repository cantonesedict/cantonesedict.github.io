砧 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
石 + 5
Unicode code point
U+7827
Alternative forms
  • 碪
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀砧꜀碪)
    • A stone to beat clothes on;
    • an anvil;
    • a block for lifting by athletes;
    • muk꜇ ꜀cham (muk6 zam1 木砧), a horse block;
    • ꜀chü ꜂pí ꜀cham (zyu1 bei2 zam1 豬髀砧), a fillet of pork;
    • ꜀cham ꜂pán (zam1 baan2 砧板), a cutting board, used by butchers.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 砧板: Now chopping boards in general (not necessarily a butcher's).

箴 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
竹 + 9
Unicode code point
U+7BB4
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀箴)

    Used for the last (針).

    • A probe, a surgeon's needle;
    • to probe;
    • a caution, a check;
    • pointed, urgent;
    • to exhort, to rebuke, to urge to reform;
    • ꜀cham ꜀kwʻai (zam1 kwai1 箴規) admonition, rebukes;
    • ꜀cham ꜁ming (zam1 ming4 箴銘), cautionary maxims.

針 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
金 + 2
Unicode code point
U+91DD
Alternative forms
  • 鍼
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀針꜀鍼)
    • A needle;
    • a cauterizing probe;
    • to prick;
    • to use needles in acupuncture;
    • leaves of the pine;
    • ꜀chʻün ꜀cham (cyun1 zam1 穿針), to thread a needle;
    • [꜂]kú ꜁chʻui ꜀cham (gu1 ceoi4 zam1 鼓錘針), a pin;
    • ꜀cham pí꜅ séuk[꜆] tʻít[꜆] (zam1 bei6 soek3 tit3 針鼻削鐵) mean, stingy;
    • ꜀cham ꜂chí (zam1 zi2 針黹), to embroider;
    • ꜁hon ꜂shü ꜀cham (hon4 syu2 zam1 寒暑針) a thermometer;
    • ꜃ngán ꜀cham (ngaan5 zam1 眼針), a needle;
    • ꜀man ꜀cham (man1 zam1 蚊針), a musquito's sting.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 鼓錘針: Corrected ꜀kú (gu1) to ꜂kú (gu2).
  • Williams' 針鼻削鐵: Corrected séuk꜇ tʻít꜇ (soek6 tit6) to séuk꜆ tʻít꜆ (soek3 tit3).

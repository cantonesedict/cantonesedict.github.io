斟 ꜀Cham (zam1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽃ + 9
Unicode code point
U+659F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 36 斟) 斟酌
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜀斟)
    • To pour from or into;
    • to lade out, to lighten;
    • to deliberate, to adjust;
    • a ladle, a spoon;
    • ꜀cham ꜃tʻo lok꜆ (zam1 to5 lok3 斟綏咯) all well settled;
    • ꜁tʻung ꜃ní ꜀cham ꜀cham (tung4 nei5 zam1 zam1 同你斟斟), let us talk it over;
    • ꜀cham ꜁chʻá (zam1 caa4 斟茶), to pour out tea;
    • sai[꜄] ꜀cham (sai3 zam1 細斟), to whisper;
    • ꜀cham chéuk꜆ (zam1 zoek3 斟酌) to consult about.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 斟綏咯: Corrected lok꜇ (lok6) to lok꜆ (lok3).
  • Williams' 細斟: Corrected sai꜅ (sai6) to sai꜄ (sai3).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【斟茶遞水】 (zam1 caa4 dai6 seoi2):
    1. Menial work. (Literally, "pour tea [and] pass [the] water".)

怎 ꜂Cham ꜂Tsam (zam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼼ + 5
Unicode code point
U+600E
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 怎) 怎麽猶何也語詞也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂怎 ꜂Cham)
    • An interrogative word;
    • what, how?
    • ꜂cham yéung꜅ (zam2 joeng6 怎樣) in what manner;
    • ꜂cham tik꜆ (zam2 dik1 怎的) what?
    • ꜂cham ꜀shang ꜂tá pán꜅ (zam2 sang1 daa2 baan6 怎生打扮) how would he look?
    • ꜂cham ꜂kòm pat꜆ ꜀tai ꜁tʻau (zam2 gam2 bat1 dai1 tau4 怎敢不低頭), how could I decline assent?
  • (Page 551 ꜂怎 ꜂Tsam)
    • What?
    • ꜂tsam ꜀mo (zam2 mo1 怎麼), what?
    • ꜂tsam ꜂kong (zam2 gong2 怎講), what does he say? used in the court dialect.

    This is usually read ꜂sham (sam2).

枕 ꜂Cham (zam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6795
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 枕) 臥以薦首者
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 14 ꜂枕)
    • A pillow; a stake;
    • a crossbar in a carriage;
    • (see 枕 zam3 for verb senses)
    • ꜂cham ꜀pín (zam2 bin1 枕邊), in bed, in private;
    • ꜂cham pún꜅ (zam2 bun6 枕伴) a bedfellow;
    • ꜂cham tsik꜇ ꜁nán ꜀on (zam2 zik6 naan4 on1 枕席難安), cares disturb his pillow, harassed;
    • ꜃í ꜂cham (ji5 zam2 耳枕), a support for the ear;
    • (…);
    • ꜂cham ꜁tʻau (zam2 tau4 枕頭), a pillow;
    • ꜂cham kwat꜆ (zam2 gwat1 枕骨) the occiput;
    • ꜀on ꜂cham ꜁mò ꜀yau (on1 zam2 mou4 jau1 安枕無憂), to sleep quietly.

    As a verb, also read cham꜄ (zam3) (see 枕 zam3).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' also read cham꜄: Strictly, the literary verb sense should only be read in the departing tone cham꜄ (zam3) (notwithstanding some vernacular verb senses are in rising tone). The rising tone reading ꜂cham (zam2) is for the literary noun sense. I have split things up accordingly.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【後尾枕】 (hau6 mei5 zam2):
    1. The occiput (back of the head/skull).
  • 【枕住】 (zam2 zyu6):
    1. Persistently, continually.
      • 枕住咳 (zam2 zyu6 kat1): "Persistently coughing."

浸 Cham꜄ Tsam꜄ (zam3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 7
Unicode code point
U+6D78
Reading variations
  • cam1 (in 浸淫 etc.)
  • zam6 (for vernacular "drown" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 38 浸) 漬也潤也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 浸꜄ Cham꜄)

    A colloquial word.

    • A coat, as of paint;
    • a thickness, an envelop, a skin;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜀sám cham꜄ (cat1 saam1 zam3 漆三浸) varnished it three times;
    • mok꜆ yat꜆ cham꜄ ꜁pʻí (mok1 jat1 zam3 pei4 剝一浸皮), pull off one covering.
  • (Page 551 浸꜄ Tsam꜄)
    • To soak through, to penetrate;
    • to saturate, to steep in, to macerate, to wet;
    • to submerge, to immerse; to flood, to lay under water, as fields;
    • drenched, wet, imbued with, watered;
    • gradually, gently;
    • tsam꜄ ꜂sz' (zam3 sei2 浸死) drowned;
    • tsam꜄ ꜁tʻong (zam3 tong4 浸糖) soaked in syrup;
    • tsam꜄ yun꜅ (zam3 jeon6 浸潤) to imbue with, to prejudice against, to bias;
    • tsam꜄ chá꜅ (zam3 zaa6 浸？) niggardly, closefisted;
    • ꜂shui tsam꜄ (seoi2 zam3 水浸) overflowed;
    • hon꜅ tsam꜄ ꜁hung ꜁ngán (hon6 zam3 hung4 ngaan4 汗浸紅顏), the perspiration rolled down his face;
    • tsam꜄ shap꜆ (zam3 sap1 浸濕) wet it, soak it;
    • ꜂pí ꜂shui tsam꜄ chü꜅ (bei2 seoi2 zam3 zyu6 畀水浸住) cover it with water;
    • kʻí shüt꜆ [꜀tsʻam] ꜁yam (kei4 syut3 cam1 jam4 其說浸淫), his words are insidious and persuasive;
    • tsam꜄ ꜂tsau (zam3 zau2 浸酒), spirits in which fruits have been soaked;
    • tsam꜄ yap꜇ ꜀sam (zam3 jap6 sam1 浸入心), it is wet through.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 水浸: Not overflowed, but flooded.
  • Williams' 汗浸紅顏: Although 紅顏 does not preclude men, it is generally used to describe a lady's face.
  • Williams' 其說浸淫: Corrected tsam꜄ (zam3) to ꜀tsʻam (cam1).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【浸】 (zam3):
    1. Classifier for layers.
      • 剷甩浸油 (caan2 lat1 zam3 jau4-2): "Strip away [the] layer of paint."
      • 有浸肥膏 (jau5 zam3 fei4 gou1): "There's [a] layer of fat."

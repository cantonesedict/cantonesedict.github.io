Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25 ꜀周)
    • Universal, complete, entire;
    • to supply, to provide, to assist;
    • plenty, enough;
    • to make a circuit, to environ;
    • a famous dynasty, B. C. 1122–255;
    • secret, fine, subtle;
    • close together, fine;
    • a curve, a bend;
    • to extend everywhere;
    • honest, trustworthy;
    • to the end, entirely;
    • ꜂hò ꜀chau tò꜄ (hou2 zau1 dou3 好周到) all things prepared;
    • ꜀chau ꜂chí (zau1 zi1 周知), let all know;
    • hü꜄ kín꜄ ꜀chau ꜀kung (heoi3 gin3 zau1 gung1 去見周公), he's gone to see Duke Chau—asleep;
    • ꜀chau mat꜇ (zau1 mat6 周密) numerous, close together;
    • ꜀chau pí꜅ (zau1 bei6 周備) everything ready;
    • ꜀chau ꜁wai (zau1 wai4 周圍), everywhere, all around;
    • ꜀chau ꜁sün (zau1 syun4 周旋) to circulate, to treat friends well;
    • ꜀chau ꜁tsʻün (zau1 cyun4 周全), to bring a thing about, to remove ill feeling, to explain;
    • ꜂hò ꜀chau chí[꜄] (hou2 zau1 zi3 好周置), it is excellent.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 好周置: Corrected ꜀chí (zi1) to chí꜄ (zi3).
  • Williams records 周時 under 週 (which is interchangeable).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【周身刀冇張利】 (zau1 san1 dou1, mou5 zoeng1 lei6)
    1. Jack of all trades, master of none. (Literally, "Knives all-about the person, [but] not [one] sharp".)
  • 【周時】 (zau1 si4):
    1. All the time.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 25 ꜀週)

    Often used for the last (周).

    • To revolve, to circulate;
    • to inform people;
    • a year;
    • ꜀chau ꜁nín wong꜅ séung꜄ (zau1 nin4 wong6 soeng3 週年旺相) may the whole year be lucky;
    • túi꜄ ꜀chau (deoi3 zau1 對週), a return of the year;
    • yat꜆ ꜀chau (jat1 zau1 一週), a circuit, a year;
    • ꜀chau ꜁shí ꜀tò hai꜅ ꜂kòm (zau1 si4 dou1 hai6 gom2 週時都係噉), he is ever the same.

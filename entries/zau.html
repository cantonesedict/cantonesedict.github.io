走 ꜂Tsau (zau2)

Radical + residual strokes

Unicode code point

    • ꜂tsau ꜁'m lat꜆ (zau2 m4 lat1 走唔甩) could not get away;

    • ꜂tsau tak꜆ mán꜅ (zau2 dak1 maan6 走得慢) she sails slowly;
    • ꜂tsau hü꜄ (zau2 heoi3 走去) to run off;
    • ꜂tsau ꜂shai (zau2 sai2 走使), a servant;
    • tau꜄ ꜀kai ꜂tsau ꜂kau (dau3 gai1 zau2 gau2 鬥雞走狗), to fight cocks and race dogs;
    • ꜂tsau ꜀sz' (zau2 si1 走私) to smuggle;
    • ꜂tsau lau꜅ (zau2 lau6 走漏) to disclose, to let out, as a secret;
    • ꜂tsau ꜂shui (zau2 seoi2 走水), to voyage;
    • ꜂tsau lok꜆ (zau2 lok3 走咯) I'm going;
    • ꜂tsau ꜀lo (zau2 lo1 走囉), clear out!
    • ꜂tsau tak꜆ fái꜄ tsʻui꜄ (zau2 dak1 faai3 ceoi3 走得快脆) he runs very fast.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 走漏: In the vernacular register, 走漏 means to omit by mistake.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【走】 (zau2):
    1. To run.
      • 周圍走 (zau1 wai4 zau2): "Running all over [the place]."
      • 未學行先學走 (mei6 hok6 haang4 sin1 hok6 zau2): "[Having] not yet learnt to walk, first learning to run."
    2. To flee.
      • 走啊 (zau2 aa3): "Run [for your lives]!"
      • 咪走 (mai5 zau2): "Don't [you] run [off]."
    3. To leave.
      • 我哋走 (ngo5 dei6 zau2): "We [shall] leave."
      • 臨走 (lam4 zau2): "Just before leaving."
      • 走啦 (zau2 laa1): "Leave!"
    4. To miss (fail to utilise).
      • 走咗班車 (zau2 zo2 baan1 ce1): "Missed [the] train."
      • 走咗隻東 (zau2 zo2 zek3 dung1): "Missed [the] east [wind tile]."
    5. To leave out or omit from food, as a preference.
      • 走蔥 (zau2 cung1): "Without spring-onion."
      • 走青 (zau2 ceng1): "Without [any] green [garnishes]."
      • 走色 (zau2 sik1): "Without colour" (i.e. without adding beef stock to noodle soup).
      • 菜心全走 (coi3 sam1 cyun4 zau2): "Choy sum all without" (i.e. without adding oyster sauce or brisket juice).
  • 【走雞】 (zau2 gai1):
    1. To miss an opportunity (especially a tile in mahjong).
      • 走咗雞 (zau2 zo2 gai1): "[I] missed [it]."
  • 【走去】 (zau2 heoi3):
    1. To proceed to.
      • 走去偷嘢 (zau2 heoi3 tau1 je5): "[He's] gone and stolen stuff."
      • 走去吸毒 (zau2 heoi3 kap1 duk6): "[He's] gone and taken drugs."
  • 【走油】 (zau2 jau4-2):
    1. To wok-fry briefly in a moderate amount of oil (so as to give sheen to meat or seafood).
  • 【走甩】 (zau2 lat1):
    1. To get away, to successfully escape.
      • 走唔甩 (zau2 m4 lat1): "Couldn't get away."
      • 走甩咗 (zau2 lat1 zo2): "[He] got away."
  • 【走路】 (zau2 lou6-2):
    1. To run away, to flee.

Classically, 走 means to run, not to walk (which is 行). This classical usage is preserved by Cantonese, in contrast with Mandarin, which uses 走 for walk.

咒 Chau꜄ (zau3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5492
Alternative forms
  • 呪
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 55 咒) 符咒又咒咀
  • (Vol. 1 Image 55 [呪]) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26 咒꜄呪꜄)
    • To imprecate, to rail at, to curse;
    • to pray or recite spells;
    • an incantation, an imprecation, a charm, a spell;
    • a prayer;
    • ním꜅ chau꜄ (nim6 zau3 念咒) to recite prayers;
    • ꜂[tʻò] chau꜄ (tou2 zau3 禱咒) to curse;
    • ꜁fú chau꜄ (fu4 zau3 符咒) to prepare and bless charms;
    • chau꜄ cho꜄ (zau3 zo3 咒詛) or chau꜄ má꜅ (zau3 maa6 咒罵) to curse.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected duplicate headword 咒 to 呪.
  • Williams' 念咒: More reciting an incantation than a prayer.
  • Williams' 禱咒: Changed Middle Chinese ꜂tò (dou2) to common reading ꜂tʻò (tou2).

    • To report to the throne, to memorialize the emperor, to tell the sovereign, to cause him to hear or know;

宙 Chau꜅ (zau6)

Radical + residual strokes
⼧ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5B99
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 55 宙) 宇宙古[往]今來曰宙
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26 宙꜅)
    • To contain, as the earth does;
    • including all ages, from ancient times till now.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 行 to 往.
  • Williams' as the earth does: Not quite. Kangxi quotes 《說文》舟車所極覆也下覆爲宇上奠爲宙, so it is 宇 that is as the earth below, while 宙 is instead as the firmament above.

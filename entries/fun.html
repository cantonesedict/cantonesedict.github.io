寬 ꜀Fún (fun1)

Radical + residual strokes

Unicode code point
U+5BEC
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 77 寛) 寛大寛[裕]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 64 ꜀寛; here normalised to 寬)
    • Large, spacious (Kangxi: 屋寬大也), wide, ample (Kangxi: 裕也), broad;
    • forgiving (Kangxi: 宥也), easy (Kangxi: 舒也), benignant, clement (Kangxi: 不猛也)., kind, indulgent;
    • slow;
    • to widen, to enlarge, to relax (Kangxi: 緩也);
    • to forbear;
    • to unrobe;
    • ꜀fún fút꜆ (fun1 fut3 寬闊) wide, ample;
    • ꜀fún tái꜅ (fun1 daai6 寬大) liberal, indulgent magnanimous;
    • ꜀fún ꜀yung ꜁yung (fun1 jung4 寬容), pleasant, a jolly countenance;
    • ꜀fún ꜁ü ü꜅ (fun1 jyu6 寬裕), an abundance, overplus;
    • ꜀fún hán꜅ (fun1 haan6 寬限) to allow a longer time;
    • ꜀fún shü꜄ (fun1 syu3 寬恕) to forgive;
    • ꜀fún út꜇ (fun1 wut6 寬活) in easy circumstances;
    • sz'꜅ ꜀fún (si6 fun1 事寬), not urgent;
    • ꜀fún tsung꜄ (fun1 zung3 寬縱) over-indulgent, heedless of.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Fixed incompletely printed character 裕.
  • Williams' 寬大: Better rendered "magnanimous". This is a positive trait.
  • Williams' 寬容: Corrected ꜀yung (jung1) to ꜁yung (jung4). Better rendered "lenient" or "permissive".
  • Williams' 寬裕: Corrected ꜁ü (jyu4) to ü꜅ (jyu6).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【寬】 (khwan)
    • (adjective-like) wide, spacious
    • (adjective-like) magnanimous
    • (adjective-like) clement, lenient
    • (verb-like) [to] clemence, (met.) [to] pardon

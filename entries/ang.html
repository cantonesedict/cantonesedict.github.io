鶯 ꜀Ang (ang1)

Radical + residual strokes
鳥 + 10
Unicode code point
U+9DAF
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜀鶯)
    • The nightingale, or a gay species of thrush that nestles on the willow and sings well;
    • variegated plumes;
    • ꜁wong ꜀ang (wong4 ang1 黃鶯), the mango bird;
    • ꜀ang ꜀ko (ang1 go1 鶯哥), a parrot;
    • ꜀ang ꜀ko pí꜅ (ang1 go1 bei6 鶯哥鼻) a Roman nose.

    A colloquial word;

    • a knot in a stick of wood;
    • pʻek꜆ kau꜅ ꜁chʻái ꜀ang (pek3 gau6 caai4 ang1 劈嚿柴鶯), split this knot for me.

㾘 ꜂Ang (ang2)

Radical + residual strokes
疒 + 7
Unicode code point
U+3F98
Reading variations
  • gang2 (expected from 古杏切)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜂㾘)
    • Disease, sickness.

    A colloquial word;

    • to stick up, to press into or on;
    • to inchase or emboss;
    • ꜂ang tak꜆ kéuk꜆ tʻung꜄ (ang2 dak1 goek3 tung3 㾘得腳痛) they hurt my foot;
    • ꜂ang ꜀fá (ang2 faa1 㾘花), to emboss;
    • ꜀shang shek꜇ ꜂ang (sang1 sek6 ang2 / saang1 sek6 ang2 生石㾘), to have a stone bruise;
    • ꜂ang ꜀sam (ang2 sam1 㾘心), to disturb one, as bad news; painful.
Conway's vernacular entry
  • 【㾘】 (ang2):
    1. To exert (or experience) a pressing force over a smallish or lump-sized area.
      • 㾘牙 (ang2 ngaa4): "[The food is hard and] presses against [my] teeth."

Ang꜄ (ang3 陰去)

  • 更

更 Ang꜄ (ang3)

Radical + residual strokes
曰 + 3
Unicode code point
U+66F4
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 更꜄)

    A colloquial word, a corruption of kang꜄ (gang3).

    • More, still more;
    • try again;
    • ang꜄ ꜃yau ꜀tʻím (ang3 jau5 tim1 更有添), there are more;
    • ang꜄ noi꜅ [꜀]tí (ang3 noi6 di1 更耐啲) rather longer time.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 更耐啲: Corrected tí꜅ (dei6) to ꜀tí (di1).

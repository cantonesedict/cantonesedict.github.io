鶯 ꜀Ang (ang1)

Radical + residual strokes
⿃ + 10
Unicode code point
U+9DAF
Reading variations
  • ngang1 (hypercorrected initial)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 30 鶯) 黃鶯鳥名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜀鶯)
    • The nightingale (Kangxi: 鶯鳥也卽黃鸝), or a gay species of thrush that nestles on the willow and sings well;
    • variegated plumes (Kangxi: 鳥羽文);
    • ꜁wong ꜀ang (wong4 ang1 黃鶯), the mango bird;
    • ꜀ang ꜀ko (ang1 go1 鶯哥), a parrot;
    • ꜀ang ꜀ko pí꜅ (ang1 go1 bei6 鶯哥鼻) a Roman nose.

    A colloquial word;

    • a knot in a stick of wood;
    • pʻek꜆ kau꜅ ꜁chʻái ꜀ang (pek3 gau6 caai4 ang1 劈嚿柴鶯), split this knot for me.

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 31 哽) 鶯上聲哽咽
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜂哽)
    • Stoppage in the throat (Kangxi: 咽塞也);
    • hesitation in speech from rage or impediment (Kangxi: 語爲舌所介也, 語爲人所忿疑也);
    • ꜂ang ít꜆ (ang2 jit3 哽咽) sobbing;
    • ꜂ang sak꜆ (ang2 sak1 哽塞) unable to talk.

㾘 ꜂Ang (ang2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽧ + 7
Unicode code point
U+3F98
Reading variations
  • ngang2 (alternative)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 ꜂㾘)

    (…)

    A colloquial word;

    • to stick up, to press into or on;
    • to inchase or emboss;
    • ꜂ang tak꜆ kéuk꜆ tʻung꜄ (ang2 dak1 goek3 tung3 㾘得腳痛) they hurt my foot;
    • ꜂ang ꜀fá (ang2 faa1 㾘花), to emboss;
    • ꜀[sháng] shek꜇ ꜂ang (saang1 sek6 ang2 生石㾘), to have a stone bruise;
    • ꜂ang ꜀sam (ang2 sam1 㾘心), to disturb one, as bad news; painful.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 生石㾘: Changed ꜀shang (sang1) to vernacular reading ꜀sháng (saang1).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【㾘】 (ang2):
    1. To exert (or experience) a pressing force over a smallish or lump-sized area.
      • 㾘牙 (ang2 ngaa4): "[The food is hard and] presses against [my] teeth."
      • 㾘腳 (ang2 goek3): "[It] presses against [my] foot."

更 Ang꜄ (ang3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽈ + 3
Unicode code point
U+66F4
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 4 更꜄)

    A colloquial word, a corruption of kang꜄ (gang3).

    • More, still more;
    • try again;
    • ang꜄ ꜃yau ꜀tʻím (ang3 jau5 tim1 更有添), there are more;
    • ang꜄ noi꜅ [꜀]tí (ang3 noi6 di1 更耐啲) rather longer time.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 更耐啲: Corrected tí꜅ (dei6) to ꜀tí (di1).

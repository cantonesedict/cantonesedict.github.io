Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【薦】 (zin3, zin3-2):
    1. A pad, cushion, or mat.
      • 杯薦 (bui1 zin3): "[A] cup-coaster."
      • 鞋薦 (haai4 zin3-2): "Shoe padding".
  • 【薦】 (zin3):
    1. To pad or cushion underneath.
      • 薦底 (zin3 dai2): "Padding out [the] bottom" (i.e. coming last, especially in a higher division or more prestigious environment).
      • 薦鐺底 (zin3 caang1 dai2): "[To] cushion [under the] bottom of [the] pot."
      • 冇嘢薦住 (mou5 je5 zin3 zyu6): "Nothing cushioning [it underneath]."

Olden attestation for 薦 tsín꜄ (zin3) meaning "mat/cushion":

《說文》葬：藏也从死在茻中一其中所以薦之[…]
《說文》枕：臥所薦首者[…]
《廣韻》作甸切：薦：薦席又薦進也[…]

Strictly speaking, the original word for "mat/cushion" is 荐 tsín꜅ (zin6), with 薦 tsín꜄ (zin3) being a later spelling based on phonetic nearness.

In modern usage, the spelling 墊 is common for tsín꜄ (zin3), from appropriation of formal 墊 tín꜄ (din3). However, note that 墊 tín꜄ (din3) meaning "padding" is not attested in the Middle Chinese rime books.

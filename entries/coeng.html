  • (Page 36 ꜁腸)
    • The bowels;
    • the intestines;
    • tripe;
    • met. the feelings, the affections;
    • ꜁wáng ꜀á ꜁chʻéung (waang4 aa1 coeng4-2 橫丫腸), cunning;
    • ꜂hò ꜁chʻéung ꜃tʻò (hou2 coeng4 tou5 好腸肚), clever, learned;

    • chik꜇ ꜁chʻéung (zik6 coeng4 直腸), honest, trustworthy;
    • ꜁ngau ꜁chʻéung (ngau4 coeng4-2 牛腸), tripe;
    • tái꜅ ꜁chʻéung (daai6 coeng4-2 大腸), the colon;
    • ꜂siú ꜁chʻéung (siu2 coeng4-2 小腸), lesser intestines;
    • ꜁chʻéung tsong꜅ (coeng4 zong6 腸臟) the intestines, the inwards;
    • ꜀sam ꜁chʻéung (sam1 coeng4 心腸), the disposition, feelings;
    • ꜁mò ꜁chʻéung ꜀kung ꜂tsz' (mou4 coeng4 gung1 zi2 無腸公子) a crab.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【腸】 (coeng4-2):
    1. Short for 腸粉 (cheung fan).
      • 蝦腸 (haa1 coeng4-2): "Prawn [rice-noodle] rolls."
      • 齋腸 (zaai1 coeng4-2): "Plain [rice-noodle] rolls."
  • 【腸粉】 (coeng4-2 fan2):
    1. Rice-noodle rolls.
  • 【豬腸粉】 (zyu1 coeng4 fan2):
    1. Rice-noodle rolls, especially the tighter-rolled street food variant (smaller radius with no filling).
See also
  • Williams' 牛腸: Only tripe in the sense of entrails (specifically intestine).

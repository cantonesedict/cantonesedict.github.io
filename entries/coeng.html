昌 ꜀Chʻéung (coeng1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 4
Unicode code point
U+660C
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 90 昌) 盛也顯也善也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 35 ꜀昌)
    • Light of the sun;
    • fine words;
    • elegant, flourishing, prosperous;
    • increasing in wealth or peace;
    • powerful;
    • effulgent;
    • good;
    • rich, affluent;
    • to increase;
    • to illuminate a thing;
    • ꜀chʻéung shing꜅ (coeng1 sing6 昌盛) prosperous, well-off;
    • kat꜆ ꜀chʻéung (gat1 coeng1 吉昌), happy, successful;
    • shun꜅ ꜀tʻín ꜂ché ꜀chʻéung (seon6 tin1 ze2 coeng1 順天者昌), he will prosper who obeys heaven;
    • ꜀kwong ꜀chʻéung (gwong1 coeng1 光昌), splendid, gay.

窗 ꜀Chʻéung (coeng1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽳ + 7
Unicode code point
U+7A97
Alternative forms
  • 牕
  • 窓
  • 窻
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 90 窓) 高曰窓低曰牗
  • (Vol. 1 Image 90 牕) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 36 ꜀窓꜀窗꜀牕)
    • A window, an aperture to admit light;
    • a sash;
    • a venetian blind, a shutter;
    • a school;
    • a student;
    • ꜀chʻéung ꜁mún (coeng1 mun4-2 窗門), a window;
    • ꜀tʻín ꜀chʻéung (tin1 coeng1 天窗), a sky-light;
    • ꜀chʻéung ꜁mún ꜃[chʻám] (coeng1 mun4 caam5 窗門棖), a window-sill;
    • ꜀shü ꜀chʻéung (syu1 coeng1 書窗), or ꜀kai ꜀chʻéung (gai1 coeng1 雞窗), a schoolroom;
    • tʻong꜄ ꜀chʻéung (tong3 coeng1 摥窗), to pull the sash to;
    • ꜁hon ꜀chʻéung (hon4 coeng1 寒窗), a poor student;
    • ꜀chʻéung át꜆ (coeng1 aat3 窗壓) a window-bar;
    • ꜁tʻung ꜀chʻéung (tung4 coeng1 同窗), or ꜀chʻéung ꜃yau (coeng1 jau5 窗友), chums, fellow-students;
    • ꜀chʻéung há꜅ (coeng1 haa6 窗下) at school, while at his studies.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 窗門棖: Changed uncommon reading ꜃chʻán (caan5) to ꜃chʻám (caam5).

長 ꜁Chʻéung (coeng4)

Radical + residual strokes
⾧ + 0
Unicode code point
U+9577
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 91 長) 長久永遠也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 36 ꜁長)
    • Long in time or distance, far off;
    • constantly, regularly;
    • always, continual;
    • superiors;
    • skilled, used to, practiced;
    • direct, straight;
    • the 168th radical;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜃ün (coeng4 jyun5 長遠), durable;
    • kok꜆ ꜃yau ꜂sho ꜁chʻéung (gok3 jau5 so2 coeng4 各有所長), in what each excels;
    • ꜁chʻéung kwo꜄ ꜁tʻau (coeng4 gwo3 tau4 長過頭), too long;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜀shang (coeng4 sang1 長生) long life; a coffin;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜁shí yung꜅ (coeng4 si4 jung6 長時用) in constant use;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜂kau (coeng4 gau2 長久), old, a long time;
    • ꜁chʻéung hí꜄ (coeng4 hei3 長氣) longwinded, as a talker;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜁hung (coeng4 hung4 長紅), a hand-bill;
    • yat꜆ ꜁mò ꜂sho ꜁chʻéung (jat1 mou4 so2 coeng4 一無所長), changeful, not persevering;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜂kau ꜂fo kí꜄ (coeng4 gau2 fo2 gei3 長久伙記) an old comrade;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜀shang pat꜆ ꜃lò (coeng4 sang1 bat1 lou5 長生不老), a green old age;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜃ün shai꜄ kái꜄ (coeng4 jyun5 sai3 gaai3 長遠世界) long continued, as a family;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜂tün (coeng4 dyun2 長短), the length of, the traits of character;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜀kʻü (coeng4 keoi1 長驅), go directly there.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 長生不老: More immortal than simply vigorous in age.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【長氣】 (coeng4 hei3):
    1. Long-winded.
      • 咪咁長氣啦 (mai5 gam3 coeng4 hei3 la1): "Don't [be] so long-winded!"
  • 【長命斜】 (coeng4 meng6 ce3):
    1. A long and steep incline or slope.

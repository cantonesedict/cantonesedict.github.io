Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【黐】 (ci1)
    1. To stick, to be sticky, to cling.
      • 黐住咗 (ci1 zyu6 zo2): "[It's] stuck [onto it]."
      • 好黐牙 (hou2 ci1 ngaa4): "Really clingy to [the] teeth."
      • 黐底 (ci1 dai2): "Stuck to [the] bottom [of the pot]."
      • 黐鑊 (ci1 wok6): "Stuck to [the] wok."
      • 黐𣲷𣲷 (ci1 nap6 nap6): "Sticky [and] wet."
      • 黐手 (ci1 sau2): "Sticky hands" (a training technique in Wing Chʻun).
    2. To freeload, to impose on another (for food etc.).
      • 黐餐 (ci1 caan1): "Sponge [a] meal."
      • 黐車坐 (ci1 ce1 co5): "Freeload for [a] car ride."
    3. Short for 黐線.
      • 做到黐咗 (zou6 dou3 ci1 zo2): "Worked till [he's] crazy."
  • 【黐肺】 (ci1 fai3)
    1. To be exhausted. (Literally, "stuck-together lungs".)
      • 熱到黐肺 (jit6 dou3 ci1 fai3): "So hot [I'm] knackered."
  • 【黐脷根】 (ci1 lei6 gan1)
    1. To have a lisp. (Literally, "sticky tongue-root".)
  • 【黐孖筋】 (ci1 maa1 gan1)
    1. Crazy, insane.
  • 【黐線】 (ci1 sin3)
    1. Crazy, insane.
      • 黐咗線咩 (ci1 zo2 sin3 me1): "[Have you] gone crazy?"
      • 黐線㗎 (ci1 sin3 ga3): "[It's] crazy!"

Olden attestation for 黐 ꜀chʻí (ci1) meaning "glue", hence "sticky":

《廣韻》吕支切：黐：黏也又丑知切
《廣韻》丑知切：黐：所以粘鳥又吕支切
《康熙字典》黐：《廣韻》丑知切《集韻》《正韻》抽知切𠀤音摛《玉篇》黏也《廣韻》黐膠所以黏鳥　又《廣韻》呂支切《集韻》鄰知切𠀤音離義同

    • 杝 read ꜁lí (lei4) is used for 離.
    • 杝 read ꜁tʻo (to4) is a mistaken parsing of Kangxi 託何切音佗 ꜀tʻo (to1), which is the only reading having the same meaning of "splitting with the grain".

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 616 ꜃似)
    • Like, similar;
    • as, as if, as it were, appearing, resembling (Kangxi: 肖也);
    • to appear, to have the aspect of;
    • ꜂hò ꜃tsʻz' (hou2 ci5 好似) like;
    • ꜃tsʻz' ꜁'m ꜃tsʻz' (ci5 m4 ci5 似唔似) is it like or not?
    • ꜁'m ꜀to ꜃tsʻz' (m4 do1 ci5 唔多似) not very similar;
    • ꜃tsʻz' tsuk꜆ ꜃ní (ci5 zuk1 nei5 似足你), just like you;
    • ꜃tsʻz' shí꜅ ꜁í ꜀fí (ci5 si6 ji4 fei1 似是而非), somewhat of right, but still wrong;
    • ꜃yau shing꜄ ꜃tsʻz' ꜃ngo tik꜆ (jau5 sing3 ci5 ngo5 dik1 有勝似我的) there are those superior to me;
    • ꜁yan ꜃yau ꜀séung ꜃tsʻz' (jan4 jau5 soeng1 ci5 人有相似) they resemble each other.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To continue (Kangxi: 嗣也).
  2. To present, to give (Kangxi: 况也奉也).
Conway's Cantonese entry

Cantonese prefers classical 似 over 像.

  • 【似】 (ci5)
    1. To resemble, to be like.
      • 似佢阿媽 (ci5 keoi5 aa3 maa1): "[She] resembles her mum."
      • 好似得咗 (hou2 ci5 dak1 zo2): "Seems like [it] worked."
  • 【似樣】 (ci5 joeng6-2)
    1. To resemble in appearance.
      • 好似樣 (hou2 ci5 joeng6-2): "[They] really look alike."
    2. To appear acceptable, to look the part.
      • 噉似樣啲 (gam2 ci5 joeng6-2 di1): "[It] looks more acceptable like this."

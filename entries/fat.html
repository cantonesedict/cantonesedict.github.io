弗 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼸ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5F17
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 弗) 不也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 49 弗꜆)
    • A negative (Kangxi: 不也);
    • not, it should not be, not permissible (Kangxi: 不可也不然也);
    • contrary (Kangxi: 違也), distorted (Kangxi: 不正也);
    • fat꜆ ꜁loi (fat1 loi4 弗來), he will not come;
    • fat꜆ tsai꜄ sz'꜅ (fat1 zai3 si6 弗濟事) it will not be done.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To rid, to remove (Kangxi: 去也). (Alternative form of 祓 (for the "remove" sense). See 祓 fat1.)

拂 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes

Fan Wan entry

  • (Vol. 1 Image 88 拂 Pat꜇ (bat6)) 去也拭也除也擊也矯也逆也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 49 拂꜆)
    • To brush, to wipe, to dust (Kangxi: 去也拭也除也, 拂去塵埃也);
    • to push away, (…);

  • Fan Wan entry: The headword 拂 appears twice under the reading fat꜆ (fat1).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To strike obliquely, to glance (Kangxi: 過擊也, 擊而過之也).
  2. A duster (Kangxi: 拂塵具).

祓 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽰ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7953
Alternative forms
  • 弗
Reading variations
  • fai3 (alternative per 《廣韻》方[肺]切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 [祓]) 除也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 50 祓꜆)
    • Clean, pure To cleanse, to purify (Kangxi: 潔也);
    • to disperse;
    • to drive off;
    • to wash away;
    • a sacrifice or baptism to obtain good (Kangxi: 除[災]求福也);
    • fat꜆ ꜁chʻü (fat1 ceoi4 祓除), to ward off.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword 袚 to 祓.
  • Williams' "Clean, pure": These should be rendered as verbs instead.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To remove (除也, 通作弗, 弗之言祓也).
  2. Good fortune (Kangxi: 通作茀, 祓福也). (Alternative form of 茀. See 茀 fat1.)

紼 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽷ + 5
Unicode code point
U+7D3C
Alternative forms
  • 綍
  • 茀
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 茀) 繫印組也又引柩索
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 49 紼꜆)
    • Raveled silk (Kangxi: 亂糸也);
    • (…);
    • a trace to drag a bier (Kangxi: 從前引之曰紼紼發也發車使前);
    • chap꜆ fat꜆ (zap1 fat1 執紼) to accompany a funeral.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 紱. See 紱 fat1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 芾 (for the "knee-cover" sense). See 芾 fat1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "Raveled silk": 《說文》 actually has 亂系也, with 段玉裁's annotations saying this should instead be 亂枲也, "ravelled hemp".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A rope (Kangxi: 紼繂也, 紼縳也).
  2. A coffin-rope (for burial) (Kangxi: 引棺索也).

茀 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 5
Unicode code point
U+8300
Alternative forms
  • 祓 (for "good fortune" sense)
Reading variations
  • fuk1 (for "good fortune" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 茀) 轎簾也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 49 茀꜆)
    • Brambles, or luxuriant herbage, concealing the path (Kangxi: 道多草不可行, 茀茀茂也);
    • happiness, luck (Kangxi: 福也);
    • to screen (Kangxi: 婦人乗車不露見車之前後設障以自蔽隱謂之茀);
    • to clear away, to open (Kangxi: 治也);
    • fat꜆ ꜂tsò ꜂tsʻò (fat1 cou2 茀草), to clear away grass.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 紼. See 紼 fat1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 茀草: Corrected ꜂tsò (zou2) to ꜂tsʻò (cou2).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Strong, vigorous (Kangxi: 强盛也).
See also
  • 茀 fai3

袚 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾐ + 5
Unicode code point
U+889A
Reading variations
  • fai3 (per 《集韻》方未切 for "knee-cover" sense)
  • but3 (per 《廣韻》北末切 for "barbarian-clothing" and "knee-cover" senses)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 50 袚꜆)
    • A covering for the knees (Kangxi: 蔽膝也 etc.);
    • a short tunic worn in southern China clothing worn by south-eastern barbarians (Kangxi: 蠻[夷]服也).
Conway's commentary
  • The reading fat꜆ (fat1) is not unattested for the "barbarian-clothing" sense; 《集韻》 has the reading 敷勿切, explained 蠻夷衣.
  • Fan Wan entry: The headword 袚 is a misprint of 祓.
  • Williams' "short tunic worn in southern China": This is a poor rendering of Kangxi 蠻[夷]服也.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Clothing for small children (Kangxi: [褿]袚䙁褯也).

黻 Fat꜆ (fat1)

Radical + residual strokes
⿋ + 5
Unicode code point
U+9EFB
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 86 黻) [黼]黻
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 50 黻꜆)
    • Variegated, An embroidered symbol in black and azure mixed (Kangxi: 黑與靑相次文, 黼黻彰也, etc.);
    • to embroider in colors;
    • elegant, flowery (as writing);
    • a variegated garment (Kangxi: 黻紩也畫黻紩文綵於衣也 etc.);
    • fat꜆ ꜃mín (fat1 min5 黻冕), an embroidered coronet (Kangxi: 黻冕 etc.).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 韍. See 韍 fat1 for the canonical entry.)

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 紱. See 紱 fat1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan: Fixed missing strokes in 黼.
  • The symbol (文, 彰) referred to by Kangxi is like two 弓 back-to-back, or 亞 with the horizontal strokes disconnected.

厝 Tsʻò꜄ (cou3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼚ + 8
Unicode code point
U+539D
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 16 措 Tsʻú꜄) 處置也安着也施布也又委置也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 16 厝 Tsʻú꜄) 同上又安頓也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 587 厝꜄)
    • A large flat stone, fit for a gravestone;
    • to place, to put a body away properly;
    • interchanged with the next (措);
    • ꜀on tsʻò꜄ (on1 cou3 安厝) to put a coffin in the grave or under a lodge; quietly reposing;
    • tsʻò꜄ chü꜄ (cou3 zyu3 厝註) to cut an epitaph.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "large flat stone, fit for a gravestone" and 厝註: Williams seems to have conflated the "whetstone" sense of 厝, which should be read in entering tone tsʻok꜆ (cok3), with the "placement" sense tsʻò꜄ (cou3). Unlike the "placement" sense, the "whetstone" sense has no connotations with burial.

    • ꜁tsʻò wan꜅ (cou4 wan6 漕運) to transport grain, to take it to the army;
    • ꜁tsʻò ꜁ho (cou4 ho4 漕河), the Grand Canal;
    • ꜁tsʻò wai꜅ (cou4 wai6 漕衛) a fortified city.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 587 ꜃造)

    A colloquial word.

    • To collect, to lay up, to heap up;
    • to lay by, to pay instalments, to pay in savings;
    • yat꜆ ꜁tsʻín ꜃tsʻò yat꜆ ꜁tsʻín (jat1 cin4 cou5 jat1 cin4 一錢造一錢), for one mace lay up another;
    • ꜂siú ꜁tsʻín ꜃tsʻò tái꜅ ꜁tsʻín (siu2 cin4 cou5 daai6 cin4 小錢造大錢), a little farthing will grow to a large penny;
    • ꜃tsʻò ꜁mái (cou5 maai4 造埋), to lay up.

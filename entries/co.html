初 ꜀Chʻo (co1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 5 (per 《說文》刀部 and Kangxi radical)
⾐ + 2 (per Ministry of Education (ROC))
Unicode code point
U+521D
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 58 初) 始也先也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26* ꜀初)
    • To cut out garments (Kangxi: 裁衣之始也);
    • to begin, to commence (Kangxi: 始也);
    • the first, the beginning;
    • incipient, at first;
    • á꜄ ꜀chʻo ꜀ko (aa3 co1 go1 阿初哥), a beginner, raw;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜀kán (co1 gaan1 初間), during the first decade;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜀chʻo (co1 co1 初初), at first;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜁tʻau fát꜆ shai꜄ (co1 tau4 faat3 sai3 初頭發勢) a great show at starting;
    • [꜀]chʻo ꜁loi pò꜅ tò꜄ (co1 loi4 bou6 dou3 初來步到) unacquainted with things;
    • ꜀chʻo tsò꜅ (co1 zou6 初做) to begin;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜀shang (co1 sang1 初生), a first-born;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜁tsʻun (co1 ceon4 初旬), the first decade;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜀hoi fau꜅ (co1 hoi1 fau6 初開埠) to found a mart;
    • ꜀chʻo ꜂kí (co1 gei2 初幾), which day of the first decade is it?
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "To cut out garments": Specifically, the beginning of producing clothes.
  • Williams' 初間, 初旬, 初幾: Here, "decade" means ten days.
  • Williams' 初來步到: Corrected ꜁chʻo (co4) to ꜀chʻo (co1).
  • Williams' 初生: More newborn than firstborn.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【初初】 (co1 co1):
    1. At first, initially.
  • 【初哥】 (co1 go1):
    1. A beginner, a novice.

嵯 ꜀Tsʻo (co1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼭ + 10
Unicode code point
U+5D6F
Reading variations
  • co4 (per 《廣韻》昨何切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 58 嵯) 嵯峨
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 583 ꜀嵯)
    • Uneven hills;
    • ꜀tsʻo ꜁ngo (co1 ngo4 嵯峨), the inequalities of hills, the undulating outline of mountains.
Conway's commentary
  • The reading ꜀tsʻo (co1) is not unattested; Kangxi has 《集韻》《韻會》《正韻》𠀤倉何切音磋《說文》嵯峨山石貌.
  • Williams' "Uneven hills" etc.: These explanations only apply to the reading 楚宜切 ꜀chʻí (ci1) for use in the phrase 㠁嵯, which is the mountain version of 參差.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 嵯峨 (lofty, mountainous).

楚 ꜂Chʻo (co2)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 9
Unicode code point
U+695A
Alternative forms
  • 憷 (for "pain/suffering" senses)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 60 楚) 國名大也高起也又夏楚荊條也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26* ꜂楚)
    • A coppice, a bramble-bush, a thicket (Kangxi: 叢木也);
    • a clump of trees;
    • spinous (Kangxi: 楚楚茨棘貌);
    • distinct, clear, orderly (Kangxi: 楚楚鮮明貌);
    • well done, properly finished;
    • painful (Kangxi: 辛楚痛也), sharp;
    • a feudal state (Kangxi: 國名) in the Chau (周) dynasty now the chief part of Húnán (湖南) and Húpeh (湖北);
    • ꜀tsʻing ꜂chʻo (cing1 co2 清楚), clear, fresh, completed, well done;
    • ꜂chʻo ꜂chʻo (co2 co2 楚楚), very good, carefully;
    • ꜀king ꜂chʻo (ging1 co2 荊楚), a severe castigation;
    • ꜂fú ꜂chʻo (fu2 co2 苦楚), distressing, suffering.
  • (Page 26* ꜂憷)

    Interchanged with the last (楚), in the senses of painful, suffering (Kangxi 憷: 通作楚).

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A rod or cane used to beat offenders (Kangxi: 楚荆也扑撻犯禮者).

銼 Tsʻo꜄ (co3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 7
Unicode code point
U+92BC
Alternative forms
  • 剉
Reading variations
  • co4 (per 《廣韻》昨禾切 for "boiler" sense)
  • zuk6 (per 《廣韻》昨木切 for "boiler" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 剉) 折也
  • (Vol. 2 Image 61 銼) 與剉同
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 583 剉꜄)

    (…)

    Often used for the next (銼).

  • (Page 583 銼꜄)
    • An iron pan or boiler (Kangxi: 鍑也);
    • a file, a rasp;
    • to rasp, to file, to smooth off the sharp or rough points;
    • to rub down;
    • yat꜆ ꜂pá tsʻo꜄ (jat1 baa2 co3 一把銼) one file;
    • tsʻo꜄ wát꜇ (co3 waat6 銼滑) file it smooth;
    • ꜀kai chʻun tsʻo꜄ (gai1 ceon1 co3 雞膥銼) to file eggs, or ꜀chí ꜁má tsʻo꜄ (zi1 maa4 co3 芝麻銼) to scrape sesamum seeds, both mean a skinflint;
    • tsʻo꜄ kok꜆ (co3 gok3 銼角) to file off corners.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 雞膥銼, 芝麻銼: More accurately rendered as nouns, i.e. "chicken-egg file" and "sesame file".

錯 Tsʻo꜄ (co3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 8
Unicode code point
U+932F
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 583 錯꜄)
    • In disorder, confused;
    • to mistake, to err, to do wrong;
    • to slip;
    • erroneous, mixed, wrong;
    • ꜃mò tsʻo꜄ (mou5 co3 冇錯) no mistake, right;
    • tsʻo꜄ [꜀]ngám (co3 ngaam1 錯啱), a lucky hit, an error which is fortunate;
    • ꜁'m ying꜅ tsʻo꜄ (m4 jing6 co3 唔認錯) will not confess the wrong;
    • tsʻo꜄ kéuk꜆ ꜁nán ꜀fán (co3 goek3 naan4 faan1 錯腳難翻) a slip may be hard to retrieve;
    • tsʻo꜄ kwo꜄ pít꜆ ꜂koi (co3 gwo3 bit1 goi2 錯過必改), error must be corrected;
    • ꜂sé tsʻo꜄ (se2 co3 寫錯) written wrong.
Conway's commentary
  • The classical reading of the "erroneous" and "mixed" senses is entering-tone tsʻok꜆ (cok3).

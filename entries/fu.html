俘 ꜀Fú (fu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 7
Unicode code point
U+4FD8
Alternative forms

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 俘) [囚]也對敵被擒之人孚穀殼曰俘葢車中所[獲]者未也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 58 ꜀俘)
    • To captivate, to capture (Kangxi: 一說俘取也), to take prisoner in war;
    • a prisoner of war (Kangxi: 囚敵曰俘);
    • spoil taken in battle (Kangxi: 軍所獲也);
    • ꜀fú ꜁tsʻau (fu1 cau4 俘囚), a captive;
    • ꜀fú ꜃lò (fu1 lou5 俘虜), to take captive.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 因 to 囚, and normalised 𫉬 to 獲.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【俘】 (phju)
    • (verb-like) [to] capture
    • (noun-like) captive, (met.) spoils

呼 ꜀Fú (fu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 5
Unicode code point
U+547C
Alternative forms
  • 乎 (in 嗚呼 spelt 烏乎)
  • 嘑
  • 虖
  • 謼
Reading variations
  • fu3 (per 《廣韻》荒故切 for "call" and "shout" senses spelt 謼)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 呼) 呼喚
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 嘑) 喚也[又]咄啐之聲
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 58 ꜀呼)
    • To breathe out, an expiration (Kangxi: 外息也, 出息爲呼入息爲吸);
    • to call, to invoke (Kangxi: 喚也);
    • ꜀fú fún꜄ ún꜅ (fu1 wun6 呼喚) to bawl, to cry out;
    • ꜁chʻéung ꜀fú ꜂tün tʻán꜄ (coeng4 fu1 dyun2 taan3 長呼短嘆) groans and sighs;
    • ꜀fú kʻap꜆ (fu1 kap1 呼吸) breathing;
    • ꜀fú hám꜄ (fu1 haam3 呼喊) to cry after;
    • ꜀fú hot꜆ (fu1 hot3 呼喝) to scold, to order about.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 夂 to 又.
  • Williams' 呼喚: Changed fún꜄ (fun3) to modern ún꜅ (wun6).
  • Williams' 長呼短嘆: Likely a mistake. The actual phrase is 長吁短嘆.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To shout out.
  2. Used in 嗚呼 (Kangxi: 嗚呼歎辭).
  3. (onomatopoeia) The sound of wind.
  4. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).
  5. Used in 呼延 (a compound surname) (Kangxi: 複姓, […]姓呼延者是也).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【嗚呼】 ('u xu)
    • (onomatopoeia) boo hoo, (met.) alas
  • 【呼】 (xu, xuH)
    • (verb-like) [to] call (out), [to] shout (out)
    • Used in 嗚呼.

    • (verb-like) [to] shout (out)

夫 ꜀Fú (fu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼤ + 1
Unicode code point
U+592B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 夫) 男子之稱也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 57 ꜀夫)
    • To help, one who can assist;
    • a man (Kangxi: 男子通稱), a scholar, a distinguished man;
    • a husband (Kangxi: 男女旣配曰夫婦);
    • a porter, a workman;
    • a chair-bearer (Kangxi: 執御行役曰僕夫);
    • an exalted lady;
    • ꜀fú ꜃fú (fu1 fu5 夫婦), or ꜀fú ꜀tsʻai (fu1 cai1 夫妻), husband and wife;
    • ꜀fú ꜂tsz' (fu1 zi2 夫子) a teacher, a compellation of high scholars (Kangxi: 先生長者曰夫子);
    • ꜀fú ꜁yan (fu1 jan4 夫人), an officer's wife, your wife;
    • ꜀tám ꜀fú (daam1 fu1 擔夫), a porter;
    • ꜀fú ꜀kwan (fu1 gwan1 夫君), my husband;
    • ꜀fú ꜂chü (fu1 zyu2 夫主), my [dead] husband;
    • pʻat꜆ ꜀fú (pat1 fu1 匹夫), a useless lout, an unprofessional man;
    • tái꜅ chéung꜅ ꜀fú (daai6 zoeng6 fu1 大丈夫), a great and good man.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "To help, one who can assist": Possibly an attempt to render Kangxi 《詩註》夫有傅相之德而可倚仗謂之丈夫, which suggests that a 丈夫 is so-called because he can be relied upon (仗) and is assistive (傅). This should not be taken as a definition of 夫.
  • Williams' "a distinguished man": Especially when used in the title 大夫 (Kangxi: 官名).
  • Williams' "an exalted lady": Only in the term 夫人. More generally, any important man's wife or concubine (Kangxi: 天子有后有夫人, 妾曰如夫人).
  • Williams' 夫子: Also the master, i.e. Confucius.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【夫】 (pju)
    • (noun-like) man, -er
    • (noun-like) husband
    • Used in 夫子.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【夫佬】 (fu1 lou2)
    1. A full house (card hand).
      • 傾夫佬 (king1 fu1 lou2): "King full [house]."

The spelling 夫佬 is arbitrary.

Unicode code point
U+6874
Reading variations
  • fau4 (per Kangxi 縛謀切 for all senses except "raft", and alternative per 《廣韻》 for "ridgepole" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 桴) 編竹木爲[筏]皆曰桴
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 58 ꜀桴)
    • A raft (Kangxi: 編竹木代舟也大曰筏小曰桴);
    • a ridge pole in a roof (Kangxi: 棟名, 棟謂之桴, 謂屋脊也);
    • (…);
    • a barrow to carry tool for heaping dirt (Kangxi: 聚土器也 etc.);
    • ꜀fú (or ꜀fau ꜁fau) tán꜄ tʻán꜄ (fu1 taan3 or fau4 taan3 桴炭) light charcoal.

莩 ꜀Fú (fu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾋ + 7
Unicode code point
U+83A9
Alternative forms
  • 孚
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 莩) 葭也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 58 ꜀莩)
    • The pellicle lining the culms of reeds (Kangxi: 莩者其筩中白皮至薄者也);
    • met. near to, friendly, sincere.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 殍, and reading variation. See 殍 piu5 for the canonical entry.) (TODO)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "met. near to, friendly, sincere": This is incorrect. The metaphor 葭莩之親 is for thinness of relation (Kangxi: 至薄者也), not closeness. In other words, for the barely related, not the closely related.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 莩甲 (to sprout).

趺 ꜀Fú (fu1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾜ + 4
Unicode code point
U+8DBA
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 8 趺) [跏]趺[僧]家屈足盤坐之謂
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 57 ꜀趺)
    • To sit in audience or state, with the legs bent under one, à la Turque (Kangxi: 跏趺大坐也);
    • ꜀fú ꜀fú ꜁ín (fu1 fu1 jin4 趺趺然), a woman bending to the ground in obeisance (Kangxi: 拜于丈夫爲趺).

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 跗. See 跗 fu1 for the canonical entry.) (TODO)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 𧦤 to 跏, and corrected 曾 to 僧.
  • Williams' 趺趺然: This is a misparsing of Kangxi 《釋名》拜于丈夫爲趺趺然屈折下視地也. This should be punctuated as "拜于丈夫爲趺、趺然屈折下視地也。", with a pause separating 爲趺 from 趺然.

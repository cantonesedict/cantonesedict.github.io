徽 ꜀Fai (fai1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 14
⽷ + 11
Unicode code point
U+5FBD
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 27 [徽]) 美也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 44 ꜀徽)
    • A cord of three strands (Kangxi: 三糾繩也);
    • a string;
    • queenly garments;
    • a kind of sash;
    • good, beautiful (Kangxi: 善也, 徽者美善也);
    • a banner;
    • the stops on a guitar (Kangxi: 琴節曰徽);
    • ꜀Fai mak꜇ (fai1 mak6 徽墨) ink from Hwuichau (徽州);
    • ꜀fai ꜀yam (fai1 jam1 徽音), fine sounds;
    • ꜀fai hò꜅ (fai1 hou6 徽號) a flag.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected headword ⿲彳⿱山糸攵 to 徽.
  • Williams' "queenly garments": This is dubious. Although Kangxi has 與褘通, the character 褘 has two readings, and Kangxi appears to be referring to the second reading:
    1. 許歸切 ꜀fai (fai1), for a queen's attire for sacrificial rites (Kangxi 褘: 后祭服也); and
    2. 雨非切 ꜁wai (wai4), for a woman's bag(?) (囊也) or for a knee-cover (蔽膝也).
  • Williams' "a kind of sash": This is a misreading of Kangxi 衺幅也. Here, 幅 is to be read pik꜆ (bik1). Per 赤芾在股邪幅在下 from the Book of Poetry, this is a type of binding applied to the lower leg (i.e. from the knee downward).
  • Williams' "the stops on a guitar": Note that the Chinese zither (琴) is fretless, so these are not frets, but a set of inlaid markings away from the strings (providing a visual cue but no physical stoppage).
  • Williams' 徽號: Now "emblem" or "badge".

揮 ꜀Fai (fai1)

Radical + residual strokes

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 43 ꜀揮)
    • To move, to shake (Kangxi: 振也動也);
    • to be agitated (Kangxi: 奮也);
    • to animate;
    • to sprinkle (Kangxi: 灑也, 揮湔也);

    • ꜀fai ꜀chʻun (fai1 ceon1 揮春), to write new-year's inscriptions;
    • ꜀fai [꜁]hò (fai1 hou4 揮毫), to write;
    • ꜀fai ꜀kam ꜁ü ꜂tʻò (fai1 gam1 jyu4 tou2 揮金如土), to spend money like dirt;

    • ꜀fai ꜂shá (fai1 saa2 揮灑), to sprinkle, to spend fast;
    • ꜀fai tsau꜅ (fai1 zau6 揮袖) written.

    Also read ꜁wan (wan4), in the phrase ꜁wan ꜁lun (wan4 leon4 揮掄), entire, unbroken (Kangxi: 揮掄全而不破也).

隳 ꜀Fai (fai1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾩ + 15
Unicode code point
U+96B3
Alternative forms
  • 墮
  • 墯
  • 隓
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 27 隳) 敗也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 43 ꜀[隳])
    • To destroy, to [overthrow], to break (Kangxi 隓: 俗作隳非, 廢也毀也損也, 亦作墮);
    • to injure;
    • ꜀fai pái꜅ (fai1 baai6 隳敗) to destroy and scatter.

Conway's commentary
  • Williams misprints 隳 as ⿳隋𠆢氺; here corrected.
  • Williams' "Also read […]": The reading to꜅ (do6) is for a different sense (alternative form of 墮 "fall"), so the qualifier "Also" is incorrect. Kangxi 又通作墮 indicates that 隳 ꜀fai (fai1) can be spelt 墮. It does not indicate a reading of to꜅ (do6) for 隳 "destroy/collapse".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A collapsed wall (Kangxi 隓: 敗城阜曰隓).

疿 Fai꜄ (fai3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽧ + 5
Unicode code point
U+75BF
Alternative forms
  • 痱
Reading variations
  • fai3-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
  • fai6 (per 《廣韻》扶沸切 for 痱)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 713 疿꜄)
    • Pimples, small boils[,] sores from heat (Kangxi: 熱生小瘡 etc.);
    • ít꜇ fai꜄ (jit6 fai3-2 熱疿) or fai꜄ ꜂tsz' (fai3-2 zi2 疿子) prickly heat.
Conway's commentary
  • The original departing reading fai3 is almost never heard, having been supplanted by vernacular rising fai3-2 in all senses.
  • In modern usage, the spelling 痱 is much more common. However, this appears to be a merger of two apparently distinct words in Middle Chinese:
    • 疿, read 方味切 fai꜄ (fai3), explained 熱生小瘡; and
    • 痱, read 扶沸切 fai꜅ (fai6), explained 熱瘡.
  • The older character is 疿, as attested in 《黃帝素問》汗出見濕乃生痤疿.
  • Whilst it is 痱 that appears in 《說文》, it is for the different sense of "stroke/paralysis", to be read 符非切 ꜁fí (fei4).
  • Williams' "small boils": Corrected the following semicolon to a comma. There is only one sense to be defined here (i.e. the pimples and small boils are also from heat, not standalone).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【熱痱】 (jit6 fai3-2):
    1. Miliaria, heat rash.

費 Fai꜄ (fai3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾙ + 5
Unicode code point
U+8CBB
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 32 費) 費用又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 44 費꜄)
    • To spend, to use (Kangxi: 散財用也);
    • to exert;
    • to dissipate (Kangxi: 損也耗也);
    • expense, cost, trouble, outlay (Kangxi: 用也);
    • waste, lavishing;
    • squandering;
    • ꜂shai fai꜄ (sai2 fai3 使費) to spend, to expend;
    • fai꜄ sz'꜅ (fai3 si6 費事) troublesome, to interrupt;
    • fai꜄ ꜀sam (fai3 sam1 費心), to remember one;
    • long꜅ fai꜄ (long6 fai3 浪費) wasteful;
    • fai꜄ ꜁shí (fai3 si4 費時), waste one's time;
    • kwo꜄ fai꜄ (gwo3 fai3 過費) "great outlay," politely said when sitting at a feast;
    • pʻo꜄ fai꜄ (po3 fai3 破費) 'lost your outlay,' needless expense, politely said by one who gets a present;
    • chai꜄ fai꜄ (zai3 fai3 ？費) heedless, witless.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【費事】 (fai3 si6):
    1. Not to be bothered (to do something).
      • 費事煮 (fai3 si6 zyu2): "Can't [be] bothered cooking."
      • 費事行 (fai3 si6 haang4): "Can't [be] bothered walking."
    2. Used before a negative consequence to be prevented or avoided. Like English "lest", but informal.
      • 費事遲到 (fai3 si6 ci4 dou3): "So that [we] won't arrive late."
      • 費事冷嚫 (fai3 si6 laang5 can1): "So that [I] don't catch [a] cold."

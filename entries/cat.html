Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 559 七꜆柒꜆)
    • Seven;
    • tsʻat꜆ shap꜇ (cat1 sap6 七十) seventy;
    • shap꜇ tsʻat꜆ (sap6 cat1 十七) seventeen;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜁ling pát꜆ lok꜇ (cat1 ling4 baat3 lok6 七零八落) near destruction, devastated, almost ruined;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜁tsʻang ꜀kung ꜀fú (cat1 cang4 gung1 fu1 七層功夫), a work nearly done;
    • tsʻat꜆ tsik꜇ (cat1 zik6 七夕) 7th evening of the 7th month, a festival;
    • tsʻat꜆ tsüt꜇ (cat1 zyut6 七絕) a pentameter of four lines;
    • tsʻat꜆ lut꜆ lut꜇ (cat1 leot6 七律) one of eight lines;
    • yat꜆ yat꜆ tsʻat꜆ (jat1 jat1 cat1 一一七) I don't think much of that, so so;
    • tsò꜅ tsʻat꜆ (zou6 cat1 做七) to attend to the funereal rites on every seventh day;
    • tsok꜆ tsʻat꜆ ꜀pín (zok3 cat1 pin1 作七編), to write seven essays.

    The second (柒) is used for the compound form (…).

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 七絕: While each seven-character line can be though of as being in pentameter, the Chinese view does not have a notion of stressed vs unstressed syllables; rather, the phrasing of the line is viewed as a four-character run followed by a three-character run.
  • Williams' 七律: Corrected lut꜆ (leot1) to lut꜇ (leot6).

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 559 七꜆柒꜆)

    (…)

    The second (柒) (…) is also (…) a contracted form of the next ([漆]) (Kangxi 柒: 俗漆字).

  • (Page 559 ⿰氵⿱來氺꜆漆꜆)
    • Varnish, lacquer;
    • viscid or resinous juices which can be used in painting (Kangxi: 木汁可髤物);
    • to paint, to varnish;
    • black, as lacquer (Kangxi: 物之黑者曰漆);
    • adhesive;
    • exceedingly friendly, doating on;
    • ꜁yau tsʻat꜆ (jau4 cat1 油漆) to paint and varnish;
    • ꜁yau tsʻat꜆ ꜂lò (jau4 cat1 lou2 油漆佬), a house-painter;
    • tsʻat꜆ shik꜇ (cat1 sik6 漆蝕) poisoned with lacquer;
    • tsʻat꜆ hí꜄ (cat hei3 漆器) lacquered-ware;
    • ꜂fo tsʻat꜆ (fo2 cat1 火漆) sealing-wax;
    • ꜂fo tsʻat꜆ pʻín꜄ (fo2 cat1 pin3 火漆片) wafers;
    • ꜂hò tsʻat꜆ ꜂shui (hou2 cat1 seoi2 好漆水), lustrous varnish;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜀tsing (cat1 zing1 漆精), fine varnish;
    • ꜁tsʻing ꜁ü ꜀káu tsʻat꜆ (cing4 jyu4 gaau1 cat1 情如膠漆) his disposition is very affectionate;
    • ꜀kam tsʻat꜆ (gam1 cat1 金漆) gilt lacquer;
    • yap꜇ tsʻat꜆ (jap6 cat1 入漆) been varnished;
    • tsʻat꜆ shü꜅ (cat1 syu6 漆樹) the varnish tree.

Conway's Cantonese entry

One of the five profanities censored on Hong Kong television.

  • 【𨳍】 (cat6):
    1. (vulgar) A flaccid penis.

    A literal translation would be "droop-dick". For non-literal senses, see the Wiktionary entry for 𨳍.

Suprisingly, the alternative form 𫵱 is recorded in Eitel (1877) under tsʻat꜇ (cat6); the entry simply reads "V[ulgar]. the penis".

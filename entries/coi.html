啋 ꜀Tsʻoi (coi1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 8
Unicode code point
U+554B
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 590 ꜀啋)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interjection of contempt, tush! pish! to pish at;
    • ꜀tsʻoi, fát꜆ ngám꜅ wá꜅ ꜀mé (coi1, faat3 ngaam6 waa6-2 me1 啋、發譫話咩), pshaw, what a lie! said by women;
    • ꜀tsʻoi, ꜀wan ꜃ní (coi1 wan1 nei5 啋瘟你), pox take you!

    (…)

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啋】 (coi1):
    1. An interjection of rebuke for mentioning a bad or inauspicious outcome.
      • 啋 (coi1): "Pish!"
      • 啋過你 (coi1 gwo3 nei5): "Pish unto you [for saying that]!"

彩 ꜂Tsʻoi (coi2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼺ + 8
Unicode code point
U+5F69
Alternative forms
  • 采
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 44 彩) 光彩
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 591 ꜂彩)
    • Variegated, mixed or ornamented with different colors;
    • elegant, gay, colored;
    • beautiful, as the figures in silks or gems (Kangxi: 文章也);
    • lucky, pleased;
    • ꜀kwong ꜂tsʻoi (gwong1 coi2 光彩), brilliant; smooth, as a shaven head;
    • ꜂hò ꜂tsʻoi (hou2 coi2 好彩), fortunate, a good chance;
    • ꜃mò ꜂tsʻoi (mou5 coi2 冇彩), unlucky, unprosperous;
    • ꜂lo ꜂tsʻoi (lo2 coi2 攞彩), to get eclat, as actors do;
    • hot꜆ ꜂tsʻoi (hot3 coi2 喝彩), to clap, to encore actors;
    • ꜀kam ꜀fá ꜂tsʻoi ꜁hung (gam1 faa1 coi2 hung4 金花彩紅), tinsel and red silk, such as is used on joyful occasions;
    • ꜂hí [ko]꜄ ꜂tsʻoi (hei2 go3 coi2 起個彩), let us have a rejoicing, as after one is out of prison;
    • tüt꜇ ꜂tsʻoi (dyut6 coi2 奪彩), to get to the goal, to win the praise;
    • ꜂tsʻoi ꜁tsʻín (coi2 cin4 彩錢), the winner's douceur, the lucky man's present;
    • ꜂tsʻoi ꜁kʻí (coi2 kei4 彩旗), variegated banners, as those used at weddings;
    • ꜃'ng ꜂tsʻoi (ng5 coi2 五彩), variegated, colored.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 綵. See 綵 coi2 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 起個彩: Corrected kwo꜄ (gwo3) to ko꜄ (go3).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【彩數】 (coi2 sou3):
    1. Luck, fortune.
  • 【好彩】 (hou2 coi2):
    1. Lucky, fortunate.
      • 好彩冇落雨 (hou2 coi2 mou5 lok6 jyu5): "Fortunately [it] didn't rain."
      • 唔好彩 (m4 hou2 coi2): "Unlucky."

采 ꜂Tsʻoi (coi2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾤ + 1
Unicode code point
U+91C7
Alternative forms
  • 埰 (for "fief" sense)
Reading variations
  • coi3 (alternative for "fief" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 44 采) 文采又采取
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 590 ꜂采)

    To be distinguished from 釆 pín꜅ (bin6) to divide.

    • To pluck, to choose, to take with the hand (Kangxi: 捋取也, 擇也);
    • variegated, adorned with colors (Kangxi: 采色, 物采);
    • cities allotted to officers for revenue (Kangxi: 采地);
    • in these senses the three next (採, 寀, 彩) are used;
    • a business, an employment;
    • ꜂tsʻoi yap[꜆] (coi2 jap1 采邑), territory set apart for the support of grandees, an ancient usage;
    • náp꜇ ꜂tsʻoi (naap6 coi2 納采), to choose and send the betrothal presents.

    (Alternative form of 寀, 彩, and 採. See 寀 coi2, 彩 coi2, and 採 coi2.)

Conway's commentary
  • Note that the "fief" sense is recorded in Kangxi under both rising ꜂tsʻoi (coi2) and departing tsʻoi꜄ (coi3):
    《康熙字典》采：《唐韻》倉宰切《集韻》《韻會》此宰切𠀤音採[…]又采地《禮禮運》大夫有采以處其子孫《前漢地理志》大夫韓武子食采於韓原[…]又《韻會》《正韻》𠀤倉代切音菜臣食邑《周禮天官八則註》公卿大夫采邑音菜《孟子元士受地視子男註》所受采地之制音菜《前漢食貨志註》采官也因官食地故曰采地[…]
    Prescription of the departing-tone reading tsʻoi꜄ (coi3) is a result of zealously following 《廣韻》, which only has the departing reading 倉代切 for the alternative form 埰.
  • Williams' 采邑: Corrected yap꜇ (jap6) to yap꜆ (jap1).

Fan Wan entry

    • yat꜆ ꜂piú ꜁yan ꜁tsʻoi (jat1 biu2 jan4 coi4 一表人才), a fine looking man;
    • ꜁nò ꜁tsʻoi (nou4 coi4 奴才), a slave;
    • ꜁tsʻoi kü꜅ (coi4 geoi6 才具) parts, talents;
    • ꜁tsʻoi ꜁nang (coi4 nang4 才能), ability;
    • ꜁léung ꜁tsʻoi (loeng4 coi4 良才), goodness, rectitude;
    • ꜁tsʻoi chí꜄ (coi4 zi3 才智) talents and knowledge;
    • ꜂chʻun ꜁tsʻoi (ceon2 coi4 蠢才), you goose!

Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 590 ꜀啋)

    A colloquial word.

    • An interjection of contempt, tush! pish! to pish at;
    • ꜀tsʻoi, fát꜆ ngám꜅ wá꜅ ꜀mé (coi1, faat3 ngaam6 waa6-2 me1 啋、發譫話咩), pshaw, what a lie! said by women;
    • ꜀tsʻoi, ꜀wan ꜃ní (coi1 wan1 nei5 啋瘟你), pox take you!

    Read ꜀hiú (hiu1); (see 啋 hiu1).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啋】 (coi1):
    1. An interjection of rebuke for mentioning a bad or inauspicious outcome.
      • 啋 (coi1): "Pish!"
      • 啋過你 (coi1 gwo3 nei5): "Pish unto you [for saying that]!"

꜁Tsʻoi (coi4 陽平)

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 44 才) 能也功也畧也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 590 ꜁才)
    • Materials, stuff, or the substance of things, for which the next (材) is used;
    • talents, capacity, ability, powers;
    • endowments, genius, gifts;
    • a person, a graduate;
    • ꜁tsʻoi ꜂tsz' (coi4 zi2 才子) a genius;
    • ꜁man ꜁tsʻoi (man4 coi4 文才), literary talents;
    • ꜀sám ꜁tsʻoi (saam1 coi4 三才), the three powers—are heaven, earth, man, who rule all things;
    • yat꜆ ꜂piú ꜁yan ꜁tsʻoi (jat1 biu2 jan4 coi4 一表人才), a fine looking man;
    • ꜁nò ꜁tsʻoi (nou4 coi4 奴才), a slave;
    • ꜁tsʻoi kü꜅ (coi4 geoi6 才具) parts, talents;
    • ꜁tsʻoi ꜁nang (coi4 nang4 才能), ability;
    • ꜁léung ꜁tsʻoi (loeng4 coi4 良才), goodness, rectitude;
    • ꜁tsʻoi chí꜄ (coi4 zi3 才智) talents and knowledge;
    • ꜂chʻun ꜁tsʻoi (ceon2 coi4 蠢才), you goose!
    • ꜁wai ꜁tsʻoi (wai4 coi4 遺才), the unsuccessful siútsʻái (秀才);
    • ꜂hò ꜂hau ꜁tsʻoi (hou2 hau2 coi4 好口才), eloquent.

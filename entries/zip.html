囁 Chíp꜆ (zip3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼝ + 18
Unicode code point
U+56C1
Reading variations
  • jip6 (alternative per 《廣韻》而涉切)
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22* 囁꜆)
    • An unbridled tongue (Kangxi: 口無節也);
    • vilifying, grumbling, privately scolding (Kangxi: 私罵也), slandering;
    • chíp꜆ ꜀sü ꜁ü (zip3 jyu4 囁嚅), chattering, querulous (Kangxi: 囁嚅多言也).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 囁嚅: Corrected ꜀sü (seoi1) to ꜁ü (jyu4).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The mouth moving (Kangxi: 口動也).

接 Tsíp꜆ (zip3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 8
Unicode code point
U+63A5
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 50 接) 相續也運也受也待也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 579 接꜆)
    • To receive in the hand (Kangxi: 持也受也承也);
    • to receive, to connect, to join (Kangxi: 接續也連也), to succeed;
    • combined with, united to (Kangxi: 合也);
    • to take in, as jobs;
    • to meet, to associate with (Kangxi: 交也, 會也);
    • to take, as reflected light;
    • quick (Kangxi: 捷也);
    • ꜁ying tsíp꜆ (jing4 zip3 迎接) to greet, to go and meet, as a visitor;
    • tsíp꜆ ꜁pʻúi (zip3 pui4 接陪), to conduct a visitor to a seat;
    • tsíp꜆ tsuk꜇ (zip3 zuk6 接續) to succeed to, to continue;
    • ꜁mò tak꜆ tsíp꜆ tsuk꜇ (mou4 dak1 zip3 zuk6 無得接續) nothing comes after it;
    • tsíp꜆ yan꜄ (zip3 jan3 接印) to receive the seals;
    • tsíp꜆ ꜀fung (zip3 fung1 接風), to welcome a friend back;
    • ꜁shing tsíp꜆ ꜁nai ꜂shui (sing4 zip3 nai4 seoi2 承接泥水), orders received for masonwork;
    • tsíp꜆ ꜃lám ꜃ní (zip3 laam5 nei5 接？你), I'll divide the present with you;
    • tsíp꜆ ká꜄ ꜃má (zip3 gaa3 maa5 接駕馬), paranymphs sent for the bride;
    • kwai꜅ tsíp꜆ (gwai6 zip3 跪接) to kneel and receive, as a subaltern does;
    • ꜃ngo tak꜆ tsíp꜆ (ngo5 dak1 zip3 我得接) I have got it;
    • tsíp꜆ ꜀kún ꜁tʻeng ꜁tʻing (zip3 gun1 ting4 接官亭), a porch for receiving officers on landing.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 接官亭: Corrected ꜁tʻeng (teng4) to ꜁tʻing (ting4).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To catch.

摺 Chíp꜆ (zip3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 11
Unicode code point
U+647A
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 51 摺) 摺叠
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22* 摺꜆)
    • To fold, to double up (Kangxi: 摺疊也);
    • (…), to destroy (Kangxi: 敗也), (…);
    • to pile up;
    • to bend, as the knee;
    • a fold, a doubling over;
    • yat꜆ chíp꜆ chíp꜆ (jat1 zip3 zip3 一摺摺) folds, as of a fan;
    • chíp꜆ ꜁mái ꜀kán pʻò꜄ (zip3 maai4 gaan1 pou3 摺埋間舖) to fold (or close) a shop's business;
    • ꜂pʻáu chíp꜆ ꜂tsz' (paau2 zip3 zi2 跑摺子), a fleet courier;
    • chíp꜆ ꜂tsz' (zip3 zi2 摺子) a document or report for the emperor;
    • chíp꜆ tʻíp꜆ típ꜇ (zip3 dip6 摺疊) to heap up or on;
    • chíp꜆ ꜁mái (zip3 maai4 摺埋), to shut up, as a book;
    • chíp꜆ ꜂chí (zip3 zi2 摺紙), to fold paper;
    • chíp꜆ kok꜆ (zip3 gok3 摺角) to turn down dog's ears;
    • ꜂shau chíp꜆ (sau2 zip3 手摺) a folded paper for memoranda;
    • chíp꜆ ꜀kam ꜁ngan (zip3 gam1 ngan4 摺金銀), to fold paper to burn as clothes.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 跑摺子: Inferred unprinted characters ʻá in pʻáu, and fixed missing apostrophe at the end of ꜂tsz'.
  • Williams' 摺疊: Corrected tʻíp꜆ (tip3) to típ꜇ (dip6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A folded booklet.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【摺埋】 (zip3 maai4):
    1. To fold, to shut up (of a business).

浹 Tsíp꜆ (zip3)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 7
Unicode code point
U+6D79
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 50 浹) 浹洽融？通徹也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 578 before 浹꜆)

    (This character (浹) is also read háp (haap) and híp (hip).)

  • (Page 578 浹꜆)

    Interchanged with híp (hip) 挾 to assist.

    • Water flowing through (Kangxi: 徹也);
    • moistened, imbibed, damp (Kangxi: 謂潤澤浹洽相霑徹也);
    • to instil, to penetrate;
    • a complete turn of, a circuit (Kangxi: 周浹猶周匝也);
    • tsíp꜆ yat꜇ (zip3 jat6 浹日) a decade;
    • tsíp꜆ ꜁shan (zip3 san4 浹辰) twelve days or hours, a circuit of the 12 branches;
    • tsíp꜆ háp꜇ hap꜆ (zip3 hap1 浹洽) fully imbued with, to interpenetrate; to treat cordially, as friends.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The incomprehensible character looks like 液 minus 亻.
  • Williams records háp (haap) and híp (hip) as alternative readings without specifying the tone.
    • The former is consistent with 《集韻》轄夾切 háp꜇ (haap6) for 浹 used in 浹渫.
    • The latter is dubious. Note that the similar character 𠗉 has three readings:
      1. 胡頰切 híp꜇ (hip6), explained 𠗉𠗨冰凍.
      2. 子協切 tsíp꜆ (zip3), explained 𠗉𠗨 with cross reference 又音狎.
      3. 胡甲切 háp꜇ (haap6), explained 𠗉𠗨冰凍相著.
  • Williams' 浹日: Here, "decade" means ten days (甲 through 癸).
  • Williams' 浹洽: Changed háp꜇ (haap6) to common reading hap꜆ (hap1).

輒 Chíp꜆ (zip3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾞ + 7
Unicode code point
U+8F12
Reading variations
  • zit3 (common)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 51 輒) 急然也專也卽也就也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 22* 輒꜆)
    • The sides of a a carriage (Kangxi: 車兩輢也), where arms are stuck;
    • unceremoniously, suddenly, abruptly (Kangxi: 忽然也);
    • directly, without permission (Kangxi: 專輒也);
    • chíp꜆ ꜁ín (zip3 jin4 輒然), forthwith.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 輒然: Also "unmoving".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. The feet unable to move past (or cross over) one another (Kangxi: 足疾也, 輒者何兩足不[能]相過也 etc.).
  2. Immediately.
  3. Always, invariably.

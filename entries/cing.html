稱 ꜀Chʻing (cing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽲ + 9
Unicode code point
U+7A31
Alternative forms
  • 秤 (for "weigh" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 稱) 稱揚稱舉稱道
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 称) 同上
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 秤) 較物輕重
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20* ꜀稱)
    • To call, to name, to designate, to style (Kangxi: 名號謂之稱);
    • to say (Kangxi: 言也), to talk about;
    • to simulate;
    • to compliment, to commend (Kangxi: 揚也謂也);
    • to admire;
    • to feign, to plead as an excuse;
    • a designation, an appellation, a name;
    • tsz'꜅ ꜀chʻing ꜁kʻí fú꜅ üt꜆ üt꜇ ꜀ká fú꜅ (zi6 cing1 kei4 fu6 jyut6 gaa1 fu6 自稱其父曰家父) one styles his own father ká-fú (gaa fu);
    • ꜀chʻing ꜀fú (cing1 fu1 稱呼), termed;
    • ꜀chʻing tsán꜄ (cing1 zaan3 稱讚) to praise;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁yéung (cing1 joeng4 稱揚), to speak abroad, to laud;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁kʻí (cing1 kei4 稱奇), to regard as excellent;
    • ꜀tʻung ꜀chʻing (tung1 cing1 通稱), a general designation;
    • ꜀chʻing peng꜅ (cing1 beng6 稱病) to malinger, to feign sickness.
  • (Page 20* ꜀秤; here normalised to 稱)
    • Same as the last (稱), but usually applied only to weighing;
    • to adjust;
    • to weigh (Kangxi 稱: 知輕重也);
    • ꜀chʻing tò꜄ ꜂chun (cing1 dou3 zeon2 稱到準), it is weighed exactly;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁chʻá (cing1 caa4 稱茶), to weigh teas.
Conway's commentary
  • For the character 秤, Fan Wan records level-tone ꜀chʻing (cing1) for the verb "weigh" and departing-tone chʻing꜄ (cing3) for the noun "scales". This is consistent with the classical tone distinction for 稱 (level-tone verb and departing-tone noun), notwithstanding 《廣韻》 only records departing-tone for the character 秤.
  • In modern Cantonese usage, the verb "weigh" is nevertheless commonly read in departing tone as chʻing꜄ (cing3). Williams has also recorded departing-tone 稱 for the verb "weigh"; it is unclear whether this is a mistake on his part or a deliberate attestation of (classical-violating) usage in Cantonese.
  • Williams' 自稱其父曰家父: Corrected üt꜆ (jyut3) to üt꜇ (jyut6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To raise (Kangxi: 舉也).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【稱】 (tsyhing)
    • (verb-like) [to] weigh
    • (verb-like) [to] refer to, [to] proclaim
    • (verb-like) [to] extol
    • (verb-like) [to] raise

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【標青】 (biu1 ceng1)
    1. Outstanding, excellent.
  • 【青】 (ceng1)
    1. Green, especially light green.
      • 青吡吡 (ceng1 bi1 bi1): "Limey-green."
      • 採青 (coi2 ceng1): "Plucking [the] green" (i.e. the lettuce in a Lion Dance).
    2. Green garnishes (spring onion, coriander, parsley, etc.).
      • 走青 (zau2 ceng1): "Without [any] green [garnishes]."
    3. Faded or cut close to the scalp (of hair). Unclear if this should be spelt 清 instead.
      • 兩邊剷青 (loeng5 bin1 caan2 ceng1): "Faded [on] both sides."
      • 飛青啲 (fei1 ceng1 di1): "Cut [it] closer."
  • 【青豆】 (ceng1 dau6-2)
    1. Green peas.
  • 【青瓜】 (ceng1 gwaa1)
    1. Cucumber.
  • 【青檸】 (ceng1 ning4-2)
    1. Lime. (Literally, "green lemon".)
  • 【面青】 (min6 ceng1)
    1. Pale complexion (as a sign of unhealth).

Conway's commentary
  • For the character 秤, Fan Wan records level-tone ꜀chʻing (cing1) for the verb "weigh" and departing-tone chʻing꜄ (cing3) for the noun "scales". This is consistent with the classical tone distinction for 稱 (level-tone verb and departing-tone noun), notwithstanding 《廣韻》 only records departing-tone for the character 秤.
  • In modern Cantonese usage, the verb "weigh" is nevertheless commonly read in departing tone as chʻing꜄ (cing3). Williams has also recorded departing-tone 稱 for the verb "weigh" (here normalised to the character 秤); it is unclear whether this is a mistake on his part or a deliberate attestation of (classical-violating) usage in Cantonese.
  • Williams' 秤鉤: Changed ꜀kau (gau1) to common reading ꜀ngau (ngau1).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【稱】 (tsyhingH)
    • (noun-like) steelyard

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 69 呈) 申也示也露也

  • (Page 20* ꜁呈)

    • tsun꜄ ꜁chʻing (zeon3 cing4 進呈), to memorialize;

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to discover": This is a misreading of Kangxi 見也. Here, 見 is used for 現, "reveal".
  • Williams' 呈子: Corrected ꜂sz' (si2) to ꜂tsz' (zi2).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【猜呈沉】 (caai1 cing4 cam4)
    1. To play rock-scissors-paper.
  • 【呈沉磨鉸叉燒包】 (cing4-1 cam4-1 mo4 gaau3 caa1 siu1 baau1)
    1. Long-form chant for rock-scissors-paper.

Of the three items in rock-scissors-paper:

  • The spelling for "rock" (here 呈沉) is disputed, noting that in 猜呈沉 it is read low (cing4 cam4), and in 呈沉磨鉸叉燒包 it is read high (cing1 cam1). (Allusion to a heavy weight falling suggests 秤 for the first and 沉 for the second.)
  • The spelling for "scissors" is 磨鉸, "sharpened scissors".
  • The spelling for "paper" is 叉燒包, the bun of a barbecued-pork bun being a wrapper that wraps its filling.

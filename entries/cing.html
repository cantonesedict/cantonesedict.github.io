秤 ꜀Chʻing (cing1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽲ + 5
Unicode code point
U+79E4
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 68 秤) 較物輕重
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20* ꜀秤)
    • Same as the last (稱), but usually applied only to weighing;
    • to adjust;
    • to weigh;
    • ꜀chʻing tò꜄ ꜂chun (cing1 dou3 zeon2 秤到準), it is weighed exactly;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁chʻá (cing1 caa4 秤茶), to weigh teas.
Conway's commentary
  • For the character 秤, Fan Wan records level-tone ꜀chʻing (cing1) for the verb "weigh" and departing-tone chʻing꜄ (cing3) for the noun "scales". This is consistent with the classical tone distinction for 稱 (level-tone verb and departing-tone noun), notwithstanding 《廣韻》 only records departing-tone for the character 秤.
  • In modern Cantonese usage, the verb "weigh" is nevertheless commonly read in departing tone as chʻing꜄ (cing3). Williams has also recorded departing-tone 稱 for the verb "weigh"; it is unclear whether this is a mistake on his part or a deliberate attestation of (classical-violating) usage in Cantonese.

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20* ꜀稱)
    • To call, to name, to designate, to style;

    • to admire;
    • to feign, to plead as an excuse;
    • a designation, an appellation, a name;
    • tsz'꜅ ꜀chʻing ꜁kʻí fú꜅ üt[꜇] ꜀ká fú꜅ (zi6 cing1 kei4 fu6 jyut6 gaa1 fu6 自稱其父曰家父) one styles his own father ká-fú (gaa fu);
    • ꜀chʻing ꜀fú (cing1 fu1 稱呼), termed;
    • ꜀chʻing tsán꜄ (cing1 zaan3 稱讚) to praise;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁yéung (cing1 joeng4 稱揚), to speak abroad, to laud;
    • ꜀chʻing ꜁kʻí (cing1 kei4 稱奇), to regard as excellent;
    • ꜀tʻung ꜀chʻing (tung1 cing1 通稱), a general designation;
    • ꜀chʻing peng꜅ (cing1 beng6 稱病) to malinger, to feign sickness.

逞 ꜂Chʻing (cing2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾡ + 7
Unicode code point
U+901E
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 71 逞) 矜也誇也成也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 21* ꜂逞)
    • Froward, presuming, confident of one's power or pretensions;
    • precipitate, hasty;
    • to act on an impulse;
    • pleased with;
    • to pervade;
    • to exhaust;
    • pat꜆ ꜂chʻing (bat1 cing2 不逞), dissatisfied, careless of;
    • ꜂chʻing ꜀kung ꜁nang (cing2 gung1 nang4 逞功能), confident of one's power;
    • pat꜆ ꜂chʻing ꜀chí ꜁[tʻò] (bat1 cing2 zi1 tou4 不逞之徒), a reckless rascal;
    • ꜂chʻing ꜂pún sz'꜅ (cing2 bun2 si6 逞本事) to boast of one's abilities;
    • ꜂chʻing ok꜆ ꜁hang kíp꜆ (cing2 ok3 hang4 gip3 逞惡行劫) to rob without fear;
    • ꜀kwʻá ꜂chʻing (kwaa1 cing2 誇逞), to brag.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 不逞之徒: Corrected ꜁tò (dou4) to ꜁tʻò (tou4).

程 ꜁Chʻing (cing4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽲ + 7
Unicode code point
U+7A0B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 69 程) 限期也又程式課程路程
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 20* ꜁程)
    • The 1⁄100th part of a tsʻün꜄ (cyun3);
    • a portion or percentage;
    • a rule, a pattern, a regulation, to rule;
    • a limit, a period;
    • a job or measure;
    • an allowance;
    • a part;
    • a road, a journey;
    • to travel;
    • ꜁chʻing shò꜄ (cing4 sou3 程數) a percentage on one's accounts;
    • sung꜄ ꜁chʻing ꜁í (sung3 cing4 ji4 送程儀), to give one his traveling expenses;
    • ꜂hí ꜁chʻing (hei2 cing4 起程), to start on a journey;
    • hán꜅ ꜁chʻing (haan6 cing4 限程), a day's journey;
    • ꜀kím ꜁chʻing (gim1 cing4 兼程), a forced march, a rapid journey;
    • kok꜆ kú꜄ ꜁tsʻín ꜁chʻing (gok3 gu3 cin4 cing4 各顧前程), each regards his future preferment;
    • fái꜄ ꜁chʻing (faai3 cing4 快程), to travel fast;
    • ꜁chʻing shik꜆ (cing4 sik1 程式) a form;
    • ꜁mò ꜁chʻing shik꜆ (mou4 cing4 sik1 無程式) nothing to compare it to;
    • ꜂kau ꜁chʻing ꜂kau ꜀tò ꜃yau (gau2 cing4 gau2 dou1 jau5 九程九都有), most probably, ten to one it will;
    • yat꜆ ꜁chʻing (jat1 cing4 一程), a tenth;
    • tak[꜆] ꜀fán ꜂kau ꜁chʻing pún꜄ (dak1 faan1 gau2 cing4 bun3 得返九程半) got back 95 per cent.;
    • pat꜆ ꜁chʻing ꜁kʻí lik꜇ (bat1 cing4 kei4 lik6 不程其力) I wrongly estimated his strength.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 九程九都有: Better rendered "nine out of nine" than "ten to one".
  • Williams' 得返九程半: Corrected tak꜇ (dak6) to tak꜆ (dak1).

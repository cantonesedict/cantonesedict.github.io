Conway's commentary
  • Williams' еҲҮзЈӢ: Not to dehort, but to learn from one another, especially through an activity (e.g. sparring, a game, etc.).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in еҸҚеҲҮ (back-dissection) or alone, referring to the method of indicating (Middle) Chinese pronunciation by two characters, marking the initial (consonant) and the final (rime) respectively.
  2. To bite or gnash (the teeth).
  3. Close (in the sense of near).
Conway's literary rendering

    • (adjective-like) (met.) pressing
    • Used in еҸҚеҲҮ.

    • Pervious, perspicacious, discerning (Kangxi: йҖҡд№ҹ);

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a road, a thoroughfare": This is a misreading of Kangxi йҒ“д№ҹ (but an understandable one, since no example is supplied). According to ж®өзҺүиЈҒ's annotations of гҖҠиӘӘж–ҮгҖӢ, this explanation applies to еӨ©е‘ҪдёҚеҫ№ (in гҖҠи©©з¶“гҖӢ).     • (verb-like) [to] remove, [to] withdraw

切 Tsʻít꜆ (cit3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 2
Unicode code point
U+5207
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 26 切) 近也要也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 580 切꜆)
    • To cut, to carve, to mince, to slice (Kangxi: 割也刻也);
    • to urge, to press (Kangxi: 迫也急也);
    • a superlative, very, vehement, earnest, eager, pressing, important, serious, momentous;
    • the important parts, a resumé (Kangxi: 切要也);
    • sincere (Kangxi: 愨實也);
    • to feel, as the pulse (Kangxi: 切按也);
    • to spell, to express a sound by joining initials and finals (Kangxi: 反切);
    • tsʻít꜆ iú꜄ (cit3 jiu3 切要) important;
    • tʻau꜄ tsʻít꜆ (tau3 cit3 透切) plain, lucid;
    • tsʻít꜆ yuk꜇ (cit3 juk6 切肉) minced meat;
    • tsʻít꜆ mat꜇ hü꜄ (cit3 mat6 heoi3 切勿去) stay, don't go;
    • tsʻít꜆ mak꜇ (cit3 mak6 切脈) to feel the pulse;
    • tsʻít꜆ oi꜄ (cit3 oi3 切愛) warm love;
    • tsʻít꜆ tò꜄ sui꜄ (cit3 dou6 seoi3 切到碎) cut to pieces, cut fine;
    • tsʻít꜆ ꜀tsʻo (cit3 co1 切磋), to turn in a lathe; to work and carve; to dehort, as a friend;
    • tsʻít꜆ ꜀yam (cit3 jam1 切音), to spell, to join an initial and a final;
    • kʻok꜆ tsʻít꜆ pat꜆ ꜁í (kok3 cit3 bat1 ji4 確切不移), unchangeably fixed.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 切磋: Not to dehort, but to learn from one another, especially through an activity (e.g. sparring, a game, etc.).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 反切 (back-dissection) or alone, referring to the method of indicating (Middle) Chinese pronunciation by two characters, marking the initial (consonant) and the final (rime) respectively.
  2. To bite or gnash (the teeth).
  3. Close (in the sense of near).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【切】 (cit3):
    1. In time.
      • 嚟唔切 (lai4 m4 cit3): "Can't make [it] in time."
      • 趕得切 (gon2 dak1 cit3): "Rushed [there] in time."
    2. Short for 白切雞 (plain cut chicken).
      • 叉切飯 (caa1 cit3 faan6): "Char [shiu], [plain] cut [chicken] rice."
  • 【切雞】 (cit3 gai1):
    1. Short for 白切雞 (plain cut chicken).
      • 切雞飯 (cit3 gai1 faan6): "[Plain] cut chicken rice."

徹 Chʻít꜆ (cit3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 12
Unicode code point
U+5FB9
Han unification vigilance
May appear with 𠫓 having 3 strokes (+11) or 4 strokes (+12 assumed here), depending on the font.
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 26 徹) 通明也透也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 23* 徹꜆)
    • Pervious, perspicacious, discerning (Kangxi: 通也);
    • to penetrate, to pass through (Kangxi: 徹達也);
    • to remove, to take away (Kangxi: 去也);
    • a road, a thoroughfare;
    • to skin, to peel off (Kangxi: 剝取也), to destroy (Kangxi: 毀也);
    • to cultivate (Kangxi: 治也);
    • chʻít꜆ chán꜅ (cit3 zaan6 徹饌) to remove food;
    • chʻít꜆ ꜂tai (cit3 dai2 徹底), to understand thoroughly;
    • chʻít꜆ tʻau꜄ (cit3 tau3 徹透) fully known.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a road, a thoroughfare": This is a misreading of Kangxi 道也 (but an understandable one, since no example is supplied). According to 段玉裁's annotations of 《說文》, this explanation applies to 天命不徹 (in 《詩經》). There, 徹 is usually interpreted as "follow/comply", although James Legge interprets it as "explain".

設 Chʻít꜆ (cit3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾔ + 4
Unicode code point
U+8A2D
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 26 設) 置主也陳設也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 23* 設꜆)
    • To institute, to establish (Kangxi: 立也);
    • to set forth (Kangxi: 施陳也);
    • to spread, as a net;
    • to arrange, to place, to set up (Kangxi: 置也);
    • to suppose (Kangxi: 假借之辭);
    • a band of soldiers, or its officer (Kangxi: 突厥別部典兵者曰設);
    • large, said of a sword (Kangxi: 大也);
    • ꜁'m ꜂shai chʻít꜆ ꜀kún (m4 sai2 cit3 gun1 唔使設官), not to care anything for the magistrates, or there's no need of them;
    • chʻít꜆ lap꜇ (cit3 lap6 設立) to establish;
    • chʻít꜆ ꜁ín (cit3 jin4 設筵), to make a feast;
    • chʻít꜆ fát꜆ (cit3 faat3 設法) to devise means;
    • chʻít꜆ kai꜄ (cit3 gai3 設計) to plot against;
    • chʻít꜆ wák꜇ (cit3 waak6 設或) or chʻít꜆ ꜂sz' (cit3 si2 設使) suppose that;
    • ꜀pʻò chʻít꜆ (pou1 cit3 鋪設) to set in order, to prepare;
    • chʻít꜆ káu꜄ (cit3 gaau3 設教) or chʻít꜆ chéung꜄ (cit3 zoeng3 設帳) to open a school.

    A colloquial word.

    • A very little, a bit, not half enough;
    • ꜂tün chʻít꜆ kòm꜄ ꜂tün (dyun2 cit3 gam3 dyun2 短設咁短), not half long enough.

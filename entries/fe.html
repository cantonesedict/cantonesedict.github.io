Conway's commentary
  • I have reluctantly assigned a Williams pronunciation of fé for modern fe1, but this is an anachronism, and perhaps inappropriate. Indeed in the original Williams entry for 炒, coffee is referred to as ꜀ká ꜀fí (gaa1 fei1), not ká꜄ ꜀fé (gaa3 fe1); see 炒 caau2.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【啡】 (fe1):
    1. Short for 咖啡 (coffee).
      • 齋啡 (zaai1 fe1): "Straight coffee."
    2. Brown.
  • 【啡色】 (fe1 sik1):
    1. Short for 咖啡色.
  • 【咖啡色】 (gaa3 fe1 sik1):
    1. Brown.

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【淝】 (fe4, fe6):
    1. To spray.
      • 淝濕咗 (fe4 sap1 zo2): "Sprayed [it] wet."
      • 淝緊水 (fe4 gan2 seoi2): "[It's] spraying water" (i.e. the sprinkler is on).
    2. To shoot.
      • 淝中咗 (fe4 zung3 zo2): "Shot [and] hit."
  • 【淝淝】 (fe4 fe4-2):
    1. Diarrhoea. Commonly spelt 啡啡.
      • 屙淝淝 (o1 fe4 fe4-2): "Have diarrhoea."

仄 Chak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 2
Unicode code point
U+4EC4
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 32 仄) 平仄傾仄
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 仄꜆)
    • Inclined, slanting, leaning, oblique;
    • the name of the three deflected tones (上聲, 去聲, 入聲);
    • ꜀kʻing chak꜆ (king1 zak1 傾仄) tipped over, upset;
    • chak꜆ ꜀[shing] (zak1 sing1 仄聲) the oblique tones;
    • chak꜆ lau꜅ (zak1 lau6 仄陋) vile, erring.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 仄聲: Corrected ꜀sheng (seng1) to ꜀shing (sing1).

側 Chak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5074
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 32 側 Chʻak꜆ (cak1)) 傍邊曰側
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 側꜆)
    • Side, the side;
    • on the side;
    • to incline, to bow down, to bend;
    • lateral;
    • perverted;
    • mean, low;
    • rebellious, seditious;
    • chak꜆ shat꜆ (zak1 sat1 側室) a coucubine;
    • ꜁mò cháp꜇ chak꜆ (mou4 zaap6 zak1 毋极側) don't turn it over;
    • chak꜆ ꜀pín (zak1 bin1 側邊), the side;
    • chak꜆ chak꜆ tí꜅ (zak1 zak1 dei6-2 側側地) a little one sided;
    • ꜂fán chak꜆ (faan2 zak1 反側) the rear, the back side; a rebellious cabal;
    • chak꜆ ꜃í (zak1 ji5 側耳), apply the ear;
    • yat꜇ chak꜆ (jat6 zak1 日側) afternoon;
    • shí꜅ ꜃ngo chak꜆ (si6 ngo5 zak1 侍我側) stand at my side.

則 Tsak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼑ + 7
Unicode code point
U+5247
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 33 則) 法也準也卽也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 550 則꜆)
    • A rule, a precept, a law, a regulation;
    • a principle;
    • a pattern, an exainple;
    • a standard, a measure by which to test an act;
    • to conform to rule, to imitate, to walk by;
    • an illative particle, denoting a result, a reason for, a cause for;
    • wherefore, immediately, then;
    • a conditional particle, after that, in that case;
    • therefore, next, consequently;
    • fát꜆ tsak꜆ (faat3 zak1 法則) a rule, a pattern;
    • ꜂chun tsak꜆ (zeon2 zak1 準則) the pattern, at the time, like the rule, as a carpenter's line is;
    • ꜁ho tsak꜆ (ho4 zak1 何則) how then?
    • tsak꜆ ko꜄ (zak1 go3 則個) in the court dialect is like pa꜅ lok꜆ (baa6 lok3 罷咯) well, then; e. g. shü꜄ tsui꜅ tsak꜆ ko꜄ (syu3 zeoi6 zak1 go3 恕罪則個) well, then I'll pardon the fault;
    • ꜃yau mat꜇ ꜃yau tsak꜆ (jau5 mat6 jau5 zak1 有物有則) both the substance and the immaterial principle;
    • ꜁ín tsak꜆ (jin4 zak1 然則) if so, then;
    • yat꜆ tsak꜆ ꜃í ꜂hí, yat꜆ tsak꜆ ꜃í kü꜅ (jat1 zak1 ji5 hei2 jat1 zak1 ji5 geoi6 一則以喜一則以懼) to excite both our joy and fear;
    • tsak꜆ yat꜆ ꜃yá (zak1 jat1 jaa5 則一也), then they are alike;
    • ꜀sui tsak꜆ ꜂kòm (seoi1 zak1 gam2 雖則敢), then I shall venture.

昃 Chak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6603
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 32 昃) 日斜也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 昃꜆)
    • The sun declining to the west;
    • the afternoon;
    • to wane, as the moon;
    • yat꜇ chak꜆ (jat6 zak1 日昃) day is declining.

簀 Chak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽵ + 11
Unicode code point
U+7C00
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 32 簀) ？？也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 12 簀꜆)

    (The expected reading is zak1 from Middle Chinese 側革切, but the common reading is zaak3; see 簀 zaak3 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The first two characters of the explanation are incomprehensible. The first, ⿱𥫗章, does not exist. The second is illegible, but appears like a deformed version of 䓂.

鰂 Tsak꜆ (zak1)

Radical + residual strokes
⿂ + 9
Unicode code point
U+9C02
Alternative forms
  • 鯽
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 550 鯽꜆; here normalised to 鰂)
    • A species of bream (Cyprinus gibelioides) with a long dorsal, called tsak꜆ ꜃ná ꜁ü (zak1 naa5 jyu4-2 鰂乸魚);
    • shuk꜆ kwat꜆ tsak꜆ (suk1 gwat1 zak1 縮骨鰂) the blunt headed bream (Cyprinus abbreviatus);
    • ꜀kam tsak꜆ (gam1 zak1 金鰂) the red tailed bream (Cyprinus auratus).

作 Tsok꜆ (zok3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 5
Unicode code point
U+4F5C
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 102 作) 起也造也爲也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 592 作꜆)
    • To act, to do, to make (Kangxi: 爲也);
    • to begin (Kangxi: 始也), to discover, to invent (Kangxi: 造也);
    • to arise, to appear (Kangxi: 興起也);
    • to rouse, to animate (Kangxi: 振也), as to a reform;
    • work, workmanship;
    • tsok꜆ ꜁wai (zok3 wai4 作為) acts, conduct, doings;
    • tsok꜆ fuk꜆ (zok3 fuk1 作福) to supplicate blessings;
    • peng꜅ fát꜆ tsok꜆ (beng6 faat3 zok3 病發作) my ailment has come again;
    • há꜅ tsok꜆ (haa6 zok3 下作) mean acts;
    • tsok꜆ ꜀ká (zok3 gaa1 作家), a writer, an essayist;
    • ꜂hò tsok꜆ ꜀ká (hou2 zok3 gaa1 好作家), fine, delicate work; well set out;
    • sai꜄ tsok꜆ (sai3 zok3 細作) a spy;
    • tsok꜆ ꜁man ꜀chéung (zok3 man4 zoeng1 作文章), to compose essays;
    • tsok꜆ ꜂fán (zok3 faan2 作反), to turn rebel;
    • tsok꜆ hai꜅ ꜂kòm (zok3 hai6 gam2 作係噉), well, let it be this way;
    • tsok꜆ ꜂sz' ꜁lai lok꜆ (zok3 sei2 lai4 lok3 作死嚟咯) you wish to die, I think, said in anger to a careless man;
    • ꜀kung tsok꜆ (gung1 zok3 工作) workmanship.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 工作: Now "work".
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【老作】 (lou5 zok3)
    1. To make up, to fabricate.

Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 103 怍) 愧怍
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 592 怍꜇)
    • Ashamed, disconcerted (Kangxi: 慚也, 心作動也), out of countenance (Kangxi: 顏色變也, 色不和曰怍);
    • to blush, to redden;
    • blushing, shamefaced.

Radical + residual strokes

Reading variations
  • zaak6 (per Fan Wan)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 89 擢 Chák꜇ (zaak6)) 拔抽也取用也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 13 擢꜇ Chák꜇ (zaak6); here normalised to Chok꜇ (zok6))
    • To pull up (Kangxi: 引也);
    • to take out, to select, to raise, to promote (Kangxi: 拔也, 抽也出也);
    • to employ in office;
    • to reject, to extinguish, as good resolutions;
    • chák꜇ chok꜇ fát꜆ ꜁nán ꜂shò (zok6 faat3 naan4 sou2 擢髮難數), the hairs can not be counted by pulling them out—his crimes are unmberless numberless.

濯 Chok꜇ (zok6)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 14
Unicode code point
U+6FEF
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 104 濯) 洗濯又肥澤貌
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 26* 濯꜇)
    • To rinse, to wash (Kangxi: 𤃬也, 濯滌也, 濯漑也);
    • fat (Kangxi: 濯濯肥也), sleek, bright (Kangxi: 光明也);
    • to drink (Kangxi: 飮也);
    • chok꜇ chok꜇ (zok6 zok6 濯濯) sleek, as fat animals;
    • ꜂shá chok꜇ (saa2 zok6 灑濯) to sprinkle (i. e. cleanse) the heart (Kangxi: 洗心亦曰洒濯).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "to drink": Kangxi 將飮之而跪之曰賜濯 is erroneous. Here, 賜濯 should be 賜灌 from the Classic of Rites.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Without vegetation (of a hill) (Kangxi: 山無草木之貌).

鑿 Tsok꜇ (zok6)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 20
Unicode code point
U+947F
Reading variations
  • zok3 (per 《廣韻》則落切 for "bright/clear" sense)
  • zok6-2 (vernacular rising-tone noun)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 1 Image 103 [鑿]) [鏨鑿]又精也實也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 592 鑿꜇)
    • A chissel, a punch (Kangxi: 鏨也, 鑿所以穿木也);
    • a cold chissel;
    • to cut, to bore into, to chissel (Kangxi: 穿木也);
    • to dig, as a well;
    • to open out, as a road (Kangxi: 開也);
    • to brand, to mark (Kangxi: 黥𠛬), as a thief;
    • shek꜇ tsok꜇ (sek6 zok6-2 石鑿) a stone chissel;
    • tsok꜇ shek꜇ (zok6 sek6 鑿石) to cut stone;
    • tsün꜄ tsok꜇ (zyun3 zok6 鑽鑿) to bore a bole, to punch the face;
    • ꜀kún ꜁tsʻoi tsok꜇ (gun1 coi4 zok6-2 棺材鑿) you coffin chissel! you impertinent fellow!
    • ꜁ngo ꜁mí tsok꜇ (ngo4 mei4 zok6-2 蛾眉鑿) a half-moon gouge;
    • ꜁ín ꜀chí tsok꜇ tsok꜇ ꜂ho tʻeng꜄ tʻing꜄ (jin4 zi1 zok6 zok6 ho2 ting3 言之鑿鑿可聽) he will believe if he has repeated proofs.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword ⿱⿰凿殳金 to 鑿, and corrected ⿱⿰車⿱𠂉力金 to 鏨.
  • Williams' 言之鑿鑿可聽: Corrected tʻeng꜄ (teng3) to tʻing꜄ (ting3).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To employ far-fetched or outlandish reasoning (Kangxi: 恣意不求合義理謂之鑿).
  2. Bright, clear (Kangxi: 鮮明貌).
  3. Definite, reliable, irrefutable.

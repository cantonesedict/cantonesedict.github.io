Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 713 Fák꜆ (faak3) implied; here assigned to 𢫦)

    A colloquial word.

    • To whip;
    • to beat with a flat board;
    • to beat up, as eggs or jelly;
    • fák꜆ ꜀kon tseng꜅ (faak3 gon1 zeng6 𢫦乾淨) thrash it clean, as a carpet;
    • fák꜆ ꜂tʻung (faak3 tung2 𢫦桶), the tub into which grain is thrashed.
Conway's commentary
  • I have chosen 𢫦 as canonical over 𢪎 for the better font support.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【𢫦】 (faak3):
    1. To whip, to swing abruptly, to flap violently.
      • 𢫦甩啲塵 (faak3 lat1 di1 can4): "Flap off [the] dust."
    2. To whisk.
      • 𢫦蛋 (faak3 daan6-2): "Whisk eggs."

春 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 5
Unicode code point
U+6625
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 春) 四季之首
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀春)
    • Spring (Kangxi: 春爲靑陽 etc.), beginning of the year (Kangxi: 春者何歲之始也);
    • joyous, glad;
    • wanton;
    • met. times, period;
    • budding, starting;
    • ꜀chʻun shik꜆ (ceon1 sik1 春色) a parade of people with tables meeting the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁ngau (ceon1 ngau4 春牛), a clay ox made in the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun í[꜄] (ceon1 ji3 春意), sexual desires;
    • ꜀san ꜀chʻun (san1 ceon1 新春), the first month.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 膥. See 膥 ceon1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "wanton": Better rendered "amorous".
  • Williams' 春意: Corrected ꜁í (ji4) to í꜄ (ji3).
  • Williams' 新春: Better rendered "new spring" or "new year".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A year.
  2. Vitality, life.
  3. A type of alcohol (Kangxi: 酒名).

椿 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 9
Unicode code point
U+693F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 椿) 木名父曰椿母曰萱
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀椿)
    • A longlived tree (Kangxi: 上古有大椿者以八千歲爲春八千歲爲秋), a kind of banian;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻong (ceon1 tong4 椿堂), a father;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜀hün (ceon1 hyun1 椿萱), father and mother.

    A colloquial word.

    • A classifier of affairs;
    • an incident, an adventure;
    • yat꜆ ꜀chʻun ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ (jat1 ceon1 kei4 si6 一椿奇事) a singular affair.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 椿堂: This would be father and mother. Father alone would instead be 椿庭 ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻing (ceon1 ting4).

[膥] ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾁ + 11
Unicode code point
U+81A5
Alternative forms
  • 春
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 [膥]) 鳥之[卵]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀[膥])
    • An egg, fish roe, crab's eggs;
    • ꜁lui ꜀kung ꜀chʻun (leoi4 gung1 ceon1 雷公膥), 'thunder eggs,' truffles?
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword {𱼚=⿰未⿱成肉} to 膥, and corrected missing left dot in 卵.
  • Williams entry: Normalised headword {𱼚=⿰未⿱成肉} to 膥.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【膥】 (ceon1):
    1. Egg or roe.
      • 雞膥 (gai1 ceon1): "Chicken egg."
      • 魚膥 (jyu4 ceon1): "Fish roe."
      • 水蛇膥咁長 (seoi2 se4 ceon1 gam3 coeng4): "Long as water-snake eggs."
    2. (vulgar) The testicles.
      • 燒膥袋 (siu1 ceon1 doi6): "[Have your] ball sack burnt" (as a punishment in hell; see The Conmen in Vegas).

    For the (vulgar) intensifier senses, and also for the hypothesised relation to springtime in Old Chinese, see the Wiktionary entry for 春.

  • 【石膥】 (sek6 ceon1):
    1. A pebble.

Radical + residual strokes

蠢 ꜂Chʻun (ceon2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾍ + 15
Unicode code point
U+8822
Alternative forms
  • 惷
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 53 蠢) 愚也[鈍]也又虫[動曰蠢]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜂舂꜂蠢)
    • To crawl, to wriggle, as worms (Kangxi 蠢: 蟲動也);
    • to move, to stir (Kangxi 蠢: 蠢作也, 蠢動作也);
    • foolish, simple.

    The first also means embarrassed;

    • ꜂chʻun tun꜅ (ceon2 deon6 蠢鈍) silly, a simpleton;
    • ꜂chʻun ꜁tsʻoi (ceon2 coi4 蠢材), doltish;
    • ꜂chʻun chik꜇ (ceon2 zik6 蠢直) unaffected;
    • chai꜄ ꜂chʻun (zai3 ceon2 ？蠢), appearing foolish, silly looking[.]

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 惷. See 惷 ceon2 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected malformed 鈍, and corrected 虫曰蠢動 to 虫動曰蠢.
  • Williams erroneously has 舂 for an alternative form instead of 惷.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Insolent, disrespectful (Kangxi: 蠢不遜也).

巡 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼮ + 4
⾡ + 3
Unicode code point
U+5DE1
Alternative forms
  • 廵
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 巡) 巡視行也遍也又逡巡退却也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁廵꜁巡)
    • To cruize (Kangxi: 巡行也);
    • to go on circuit (Kangxi: 徧也, 謂巡繞徧行之), to examine what is doing (Kangxi: 巡視行貌), to go on a tour of inspection (Kangxi: 巡守也);
    • a course at a feast;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂fú (ceon4 fu2 巡撫), the governor of a province;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂kím (ceon4 gim2 巡檢), the supervisor or judge of a ꜀sz' (si1 司) or township;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁shün (ceon4 syun4 巡船), revenue cruizers;
    • ꜁tsʻun pò꜅ (ceon4 bou6 巡捕) special aids of the governor-general and governor;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀káng (ceon4 gaang1 巡更), on post, as a watchman;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂chün chʻü꜄ (ceon4 zyun2 cyu3 巡轉處) to go about and examine;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜁tsʻun (ceot1 ceon4 出巡), to start on a circuit; to reconnoitre;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀ting (ceon4 ding1 巡丁), tidewaiters, cruizing officers.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "a course at a feast": More "a round of drinks".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 逡巡 (hesitant, retreative) (Kangxi: 逡巡郤退之貌).

循 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5FAA
Alternative forms
  • 揗 (for "comply", "rub", and "console" senses)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 循) 順也又遵循撫循又循良
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁循)
    • To follow a leader;
    • to comply with (Kangxi: 自也率循也);
    • to accord (Kangxi: 行順也), to follow or go round with (Kangxi: 依也);
    • to revolve (Kangxi: 循環謂旋繞往來);
    • to inspect, to go the rounds, to perambulate, to go on the circuit (Kangxi: 巡也);
    • good (Kangxi: 善也), easy, docile;
    • leisurely;
    • to soothe, to console (Kangxi: 撫循慰安也);
    • to explain (Kangxi: 述也);
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀kwʻai tò꜅ ꜂kü (ceon4 kwai1 dou6 geoi2 循規蹈矩), to accord with and follow instructions;
    • ꜁tsʻun fát꜆ (ceon4 faat3 循法) to obey the laws;
    • ꜀yan ꜁tsʻun 'ng꜅ sz'꜅ (jan1 ceon4 ng6 si6 因循誤事) lazily hindering the prosecution of business;
    • ꜀yan ꜁tsʻun (jan1 ceon4 因循), not interfering with, indifferent to (Kangxi: 無所作爲曰因循);
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁tsʻun shín꜅ ꜃yau (ceon4 ceon4 sin6 jau5 循循善誘), gradually and gently lead him on, as to good habits;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁hang [kau꜅] sz'꜅ (ceon4 hang4 gau6 si6 循行舊事) act as has been formerly done.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 循規蹈矩: Better rendered "the rules" than "instructions".
  • Williams' 循行舊事: Corrected kò꜄ (gou3) to kau꜅ (gau6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Orderly, gradually (Kangxi: 循次序也).
  2. To rub (Kangxi: 循摩也).

揗 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 9
Unicode code point
U+63D7
Reading variations
  • seon6 (per 《廣韻》食尹切 and 食閏切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 揗) 以手相安慰曰揗
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁揗)
    • To pat, to stroke;
    • to encourage, to soothe (Kangxi: 手相安慰也);
    • to take a sympathizing interest in (Kangxi: 凡以恩相撫以心相恤皆曰揗).

    (Alternative form of 循 (for the "comply", "rub", and "console" senses). See 循 ceon4.)

Conway's supplementary entry
  1. To rub (Kangxi: 摩也).

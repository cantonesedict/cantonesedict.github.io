春 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 5
Unicode code point
U+6625
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 春) 四季之首
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀春)
    • Spring (Kangxi: 春爲靑陽 etc.), beginning of the year (Kangxi: 春者何歲之始也);
    • joyous, glad;
    • wanton;
    • met. times, period;
    • budding, starting;
    • ꜀chʻun shik꜆ (ceon1 sik1 春色) a parade of people with tables meeting the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁ngau (ceon1 ngau4 春牛), a clay ox made in the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun í[꜄] (ceon1 ji3 春意), sexual desires;
    • ꜀san ꜀chʻun (san1 ceon1 新春), the first month.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 膥. See 膥 ceon1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' "wanton": Better rendered "amorous".
  • Williams' 春意: Corrected ꜁í (ji4) to í꜄ (ji3).
  • Williams' 新春: Better rendered "new spring" or "new year".
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A year.
  2. Vitality, life.
  3. A type of alcohol (Kangxi: 酒名).

椿 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 9
Unicode code point
U+693F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 椿) 木名父曰椿母曰萱
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀椿)
    • A longlived tree (Kangxi: 上古有大椿者以八千歲爲春八千歲爲秋), a kind of banian;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻong (ceon1 tong4 椿堂), a father;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜀hün (ceon1 hyun1 椿萱), father and mother.

    A colloquial word.

    • A classifier of affairs;
    • an incident, an adventure;
    • yat꜆ ꜀chʻun ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ (jat1 ceon1 kei4 si6 一椿奇事) a singular affair.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 椿堂: This would be father and mother. Father alone would instead be 椿庭 ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻing (ceon1 ting4).

Radical + residual strokes
⾁ + 11
Unicode code point

Alternative forms
  • 春
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 [膥]) 鳥之[卵]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀[膥])
    • An egg, fish roe, crab's eggs;
    • ꜁lui ꜀kung ꜀chʻun (leoi4 gung1 ceon1 雷公膥), 'thunder eggs,' truffles?
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword {𱼚=⿰未⿱成肉} to 膥, and corrected missing left dot in 卵.
  • Williams entry: Normalised headword {𱼚=⿰未⿱成肉} to 膥.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【膥】 (ceon1):
    1. Egg or roe.
      • 雞膥 (gai1 ceon1): "Chicken egg."
      • 魚膥 (jyu4 ceon1): "Fish roe."
      • 水蛇膥咁長 (seoi2 se4 ceon1 gam3 coeng4): "Long as water-snake eggs."
    2. (vulgar) The testicles.
      • 燒膥袋 (siu1 ceon1 doi6): "Burnt ball sack" (see The Conmen in Vegas).

    For the (vulgar) intensifier senses, and also for the hypothesised relation to springtime in Old Chinese, see the Wiktionary entry for 春.

  • 【石膥】 (sek6 ceon1):
    1. A pebble.

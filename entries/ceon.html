春 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 5
Unicode code point
U+6625
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 春) 四季之首
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀春)
    • Spring (Kangxi: 春爲靑陽 etc.), beginning of the year (Kangxi: 春者何歲之始也);
    • joyous, glad;
    • wanton amorous;
    • met. times, period;
    • budding, starting;
    • ꜀chʻun shik꜆ (ceon1 sik1 春色) a parade of people with tables meeting the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁ngau (ceon1 ngau4 春牛), a clay ox made in the spring;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁í í꜄ (ceon1 ji3 春意), sexual desires;
    • ꜀san ꜀chʻun (san1 ceon1 新春), the first month new year.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 膥. See 膥 ceon1 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 春意: Corrected ꜁í (ji4) to í꜄ (ji3).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A year.
  2. Vitality, life.
  3. A type of alcohol (Kangxi: 酒名).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【春】 (tsyhwin)
    • (noun-like) spring, springtime

椿 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⽊ + 9
Unicode code point
U+693F
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 椿) 木名父曰椿母曰萱
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀椿)
    • A longlived tree (Kangxi: 上古有大椿者以八千歲爲春八千歲爲秋), a kind of banian;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻong (ceon1 tong4 椿堂), a father and mother;
    • ꜀chʻun ꜀hün (ceon1 hyun1 椿萱), father and mother.

    A colloquial word.

    • A classifier of affairs;
    • an incident, an adventure;
    • yat꜆ ꜀chʻun ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ (jat1 ceon1 kei4 si6 一椿奇事) a singular affair.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 椿堂: Father alone would instead be 椿庭 ꜀chʻun ꜁tʻing (ceon1 ting4).

𱼚膥 ꜀Chʻun (ceon1)

Radical + residual strokes
⾁ + 11
Unicode code point
U+81A5
Alternative forms
  • 春
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 [膥]) 鳥之[卵]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜀𱼚膥)
    • An egg, fish roe, crab's eggs;
    • ꜁lui ꜀kung ꜀chʻun (leoi4 gung1 ceon1 雷公膥), 'thunder eggs,' truffles?
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Normalised headword 𱼚 to 膥, and corrected missing left dot in 卵.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【膥】 (ceon1)
    1. Egg or roe.
      • 雞膥 (gai1 ceon1): "Chicken egg."
      • 魚膥 (jyu4 ceon1): "Fish roe."
      • 水蛇膥咁長 (seoi2 se4 ceon1 gam3 coeng4): "Long as water-snake eggs."
    2. (vulgar) The testicles.
      • 燒膥袋 (siu1 ceon1 doi6): "[Have your] ball sack burnt" (as a punishment in hell; see The Conmen in Vegas).

    For the (vulgar) intensifier senses, and also for the hypothesised relation to springtime in Old Chinese, see the Wiktionary entry for 春.

  • 【石膥】 (sek6 ceon1)
    1. A pebble.

蠢 ꜂Chʻun (ceon2)

Radical + residual strokes
⾍ + 15
Unicode code point
U+8822
Alternative forms
  • 惷
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 53 蠢) 愚也[鈍]也又虫[動曰蠢]
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 37* ꜂舂꜂蠢)
    • To crawl, to wriggle, as worms (Kangxi 蠢: 蟲動也);
    • to move, to stir (Kangxi 蠢: 蠢作也, 蠢動作也);
    • foolish, simple.

    The first also means embarrassed;

    • ꜂chʻun tun꜅ (ceon2 deon6 蠢鈍) silly, a simpleton;
    • ꜂chʻun ꜁tsʻoi (ceon2 coi4 蠢材), doltish;
    • ꜂chʻun chik꜇ (ceon2 zik6 蠢直) unaffected;
    • chai꜄ ꜂chʻun (zai3 ceon2 ？蠢), appearing foolish, silly looking.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 惷. See 惷 ceon2 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected malformed 鈍, and corrected 虫曰蠢動 to 虫動曰蠢.
  • Williams erroneously has 舂 for an alternative form instead of 惷.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Insolent, disrespectful (Kangxi: 蠢不遜也).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【蠢】 (tsyhwinX)
    • (verb-like) [to] wriggle, [to] stir
    • (adjective-like) insolent

巡 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼮ + 4
⾡ + 3
Unicode code point
U+5DE1
Alternative forms
  • 廵
  • 循
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 巡) 巡視行也遍也又逡巡退却也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁循)
    • (…);
    • to inspect, to go the rounds, to perambulate, to go on the circuit (Kangxi 循: 巡也);
    • (…).
  • (Page 603 ꜁廵꜁巡)
    • To cruize (Kangxi 巡: 巡行也);
    • to go on circuit (Kangxi 巡: 徧也, 謂巡繞徧行之), to examine what another is doing (Kangxi 巡: 巡視行貌), to go on a tour of inspection (Kangxi 巡: 巡守也);
    • a course at a feast round of drinks;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂fú (ceon4 fu2 巡撫), the governor of a province;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂kím (ceon4 gim2 巡檢), the supervisor or judge of a ꜀sz' (si1 司) or township;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁shün (ceon4 syun4 巡船), revenue cruizers;
    • ꜁tsʻun pò꜅ (ceon4 bou6 巡捕) special aids of the governor-general and governor;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀káng (ceon4 gaang1 巡更), on post, as a watchman;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂chün chʻü꜄ (ceon4 zyun2 cyu3 巡轉處) to go about and examine;
    • chʻut꜆ ꜁tsʻun (ceot1 ceon4 出巡), to start on a circuit; to reconnoitre;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀ting (ceon4 ding1 巡丁), tidewaiters, cruizing officers.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Used in 逡巡 (hesitant, retreative) (Kangxi: 逡巡郤退之貌).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【巡】 (zwin)
    • (verb-like) [to] patrol, [to] inspect
    • Used in 巡守.
  • 【巡守】 (zwin syuwH)
    • (verb-like) [to] make tour of inspection

循 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼻ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5FAA
Alternative forms
  • 揗 (for "comply", "rub", and "console" senses)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 循) 順也又遵循撫循又循良
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁循)
    • To follow a leader;
    • to comply with (Kangxi: 自也率循也);
    • to accord (Kangxi: 行順也), to follow or go round with (Kangxi: 依也);
    • to revolve (Kangxi: 循環謂旋繞往來);
    • (…);
    • good (Kangxi: 善也), easy, docile;
    • leisurely;
    • to soothe, to console (Kangxi: 撫循慰安也);
    • to explain (Kangxi: 述也);
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜀kwʻai tò꜅ ꜂kü (ceon4 kwai1 dou6 geoi2 循規蹈矩), to accord with and follow instructions the rules;
    • ꜁tsʻun fát꜆ (ceon4 faat3 循法) to obey the laws;
    • ꜀yan ꜁tsʻun 'ng꜅ sz'꜅ (jan1 ceon4 ng6 si6 因循誤事) lazily hindering the prosecution of business;
    • ꜀yan ꜁tsʻun (jan1 ceon4 因循), not interfering with, indifferent to (Kangxi: 無所作爲曰因循);
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁tsʻun shín꜅ ꜃yau (ceon4 ceon4 sin6 jau5 循循善誘), gradually and gently lead him on, as to good habits;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜁hang kò꜄ kau꜅ sz'꜅ (ceon4 hang4 gau6 si6 循行舊事) act as has been formerly done.

    (Otherwise, an alternative form of 巡. See 巡 ceon4 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 循行舊事: Corrected kò꜄ (gou3) to kau꜅ (gau6).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Orderly, gradually (Kangxi: 循次序也).
  2. To rub (Kangxi: 循摩也).
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【循】 (zwin)
    • (verb-like) [to] go along, [to] comply
    • (adjective-like) orderly
    • (verb-like) [to] rub

旬 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽇ + 2
Unicode code point
U+65EC
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 旬) 十日曰一旬
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 604 ꜁旬)
    • A decade of years or days (Kangxi: 徧也十日爲旬);
    • a complete number finished, completed (Kangxi: 徧也);
    • the whole of (Kangxi: 旬均也);
    • in mourning, seven days make a ꜁tsʻun (旬);
    • ꜁tsʻun sui꜄ (ceon4 seoi3 旬歲) a complete year (Kangxi: 旬滿也旬歲猶言滿歲也);
    • the shéung꜅ (soeng6 上), ꜀chung (zung1 中), and há꜅ ꜁tsʻun (haa6 ceon4 下旬), are the first, second, and third decades of a month;
    • tsʻat꜆ ꜁tsʻun (cat1 ceon4 七旬), the 49 days of mourning; also 70 years;
    • ꜂hò ꜂shui ꜁tsʻun (hou2 seoi2 ceon4 好水旬), the tide will serve you well.
Conway's literary rendering
  • 【旬】 (zwin)
    • (noun-like) ten-days

秦 ꜁Tsʻun (ceon4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽲ + 5
Unicode code point
U+79E6
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 52 秦) 國名又姓
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 603 ꜁秦)
    • A kind of rice (Kangxi: 一曰秦禾名);
    • a feudal state (Kangxi: 國名) occupying the region of the rivers Wei (渭) and King (涇);
    • the range of mountains in the south of Shensí (陝西);
    • ꜁Tsʻun ꜁chʻiú (ceon4 ciu4 秦朝), the Tsʻin (秦) dynasty, B.C. 186 221–120 207;
    • ꜁tsʻun ꜂Chí ꜂Chʻí ꜁wong tai꜄ (ceon4 ci2 wong4 dai3 秦始皇帝) the emperor First of this Tsʻin (秦) dynasty, who centralized the Chinese empire.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 秦始皇帝: Corrected ꜂Chí (zi2) to ꜂Chʻí (ci2).
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. A surname (Kangxi: 姓).

坳 ꜀Áu (aau1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼟ + 5
Unicode code point
U+5773
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 6 ꜀坳)
    • (For the literary "uneven ground" senses, the expected reflex is aau1 from 《廣韻》於交切, but the common reading is aau3 (attested in Kangxi, quoting 《集韻》於敎切音拗); see 坳 aau3 for the canonical entry.)

    A colloquial word.

    • Poor, destitute;
    • ꜀áu ai꜄ (aau1 ai3 坳屭) in want.

꜂Áu (aau2 陰上)

拗 ꜂Áu (aau2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 5
Unicode code point
U+62D7
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 42 上聲 拗) 屋狡切以手拉折物
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 ꜂拗)
    • To snap a thing in two;
    • to twist, to break;
    • to drag along;
    • ꜂áu chít꜆ (aau2 zit3 拗折) to break in two;
    • ꜂áu sui꜄ (aau2 seoi3 拗碎) to snap in shivers;
    • ꜂áu ꜂shau ꜂keng (aau2 sau2 geng2 拗手頸), to try the strength of the hands; to fall out about a matter;
    • ꜂áu ꜂chün ꜂kong (aau2 zyun2 gong2 拗轉講), to speak in irony, badinage;
    • ꜂áu ꜁ü (aau2 jyu4-2 拗魚), to catch fish in a lifting net.

    A colloquial word.

    • Warped, bent;
    • curved;
    • ꜂áu ꜂áu tik꜆ (aau2 aau2 dik1 拗拗滴) a little crooked;
    • ꜂áu ꜀lün (aau2 lyun1 拗攣), to curve.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【拗】 (aau2):
    1. To bend or curve.
      • 拗手瓜 (aau2 sau2 gwaa1): "Arm wrestle."
      • 拗柴 (aau2 caai4): "Sprained ankle."

Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 6 ꜀坳 ꜀Áu (aau1); here normalised to Áu꜄ (aau3))
    • A furrow, a hollow;
    • an unevenness in the ground;
    • undulations;
    • rolling, as land.

    • a turn, a corner;
    • ꜂chün áu꜄ (zyun2 aau3 轉坳) turn the corner.

拗 Áu꜄ (aau3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 5
Unicode code point
U+62D7
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 42 去聲 拗) 偏急不相合也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 7 拗꜄)
    • Obstinate, self-willed, perverse;
    • to rush against madly;
    • áu꜄ ꜂keng (aau3 geng2 拗頸), stiffnecked;
    • áu꜄ hí꜄ (aau3 hei3 拗氣) disputatious, opinionated;
    • áu꜄ tò꜄ tím꜅ ꜁wai ꜂chí (aau3 dou3 dim6 wai4 zi2 拗到掂為止), I must have it so or not at all;
    • chap꜆ áu꜄ (zap1 aau3 執拗) set in his way;
    • ꜂fán áu꜄ (faan2 aau3 反拗) whimsical, contrariwise.

    (Alternative form of 詏 "argue". See 詏 aau3 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • There is a subtle difference between 拗 aau3 "stubborn" and 詏 aau3 "argue". Kangxi has:
    • For 拗: 拗戾固相違也 "stubborn-perverse: obstinately going against".
    • For 詏: 言逆也 "speech defying".
    Nevertheless, being stubborn is not too far from being argumentative; together with 拗 being a more commonly seen character, its use as an alternative form of 詏 aau3 is understandable.

插 Chʻáp꜆ (caap3)

Radical + residual strokes
⼿ + 9
Unicode code point
U+63D2
Alternative forms
  • 挿
  • 揷
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 挿) 以手[刺]人
  • (Vol. 2 Image 66 扱) 同上
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 23 揷꜆; here normalised to 插)
    • To insert, to pierce, to set in, to stick into;
    • to tuck in, to thrust into or through;
    • to transplant;
    • to set in a socket;
    • to meddle, to interfere in;
    • an iron pointed pole used to carry sheaves, or pry up clods, for which 鍤 is also used;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜀yéung (caap3 joeng1 插秧), to set out rice shoots;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜂tsui (caap3 zeoi2 插嘴), to interrupt (in talking);
    • chʻáp꜆ yik꜇ ꜁nán ꜀fí (caap3 jik6 naan4 fei1 插翼難飛) he can not fly away;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜀tsong (caap3 zong1 插贓), to implicate one by leaving something in his house;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜀piú (caap3 biu1 插標), to stick on labels;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜀shiú (caap3 siu1 插燒), a stuffing put into roast pigs; a concubine's child;
    • ꜁shí chʻáp꜆ (si4 caap3 匙插) a narrow purse;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜂shau lok꜇ hü꜄ (caap3 sau2 lok6 heoi3 插手落去) to meddle in officiously;
    • pát꜆ ꜁tʻoi pát꜆ chʻáp꜆ (baat3 toi4 baat3 caap3 八擡八插) eight bearers and eight outriders;
    • chʻáp꜆ ꜃í ꜁yau ꜁ying (caap3 ji5 jau4 jing4 插耳遊營), to stick flags in the ears when whipping a thief.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: Corrected 剌 to 刺.
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【插贓嫁禍】 (caap3 zong1 gaa3 wo6):
    1. To plant evidence and shift blame (onto another). (Literally, "insert loot [and] transfer disaster".)

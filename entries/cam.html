侵 ꜀Tsʻam (cam1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 7
Unicode code point
U+4FB5
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 36 侵) 侵占侵凌侵害
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜀侵)
    • To usurp, to incroach upon, to enter gradually;
    • to appropriate, to plunder, to invade;
    • possessed, by as a spirit;
    • bad, calamitous, as a year;
    • ꜀tsʻam hoi꜅ (cam1 hoi6 侵害) to injure insensibly;
    • ꜀tsʻam chím꜄ (cam1 zim3 侵佔) to unjustly incroach on;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜂pún (cam1 bun2 侵本), to draw on the principal;
    • ꜀tsʻam tik꜆ yap꜇ hü꜄ (cam1 dik1 jap6 heoi3 侵滴入去) stick in a few bad ones;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜀pín ꜁pʻái (cam1 bin1 paai4-2 侵辮牌), to braid in false hair;
    • tái꜅ ꜀tsʻam ꜀chí ꜁nín (daai6 cam1 zi1 nin4 大侵之年), a bad year;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜁ling (cam1 ling4 侵凌), to intimidate, to oppress.

    Read ꜂tsʻam (cam2); (see 侵 cam2).

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【侵】 (cam1):
    1. To add in.
      • 侵水 (cam1 seoi2): "Add in [some] water."
    2. To join in.
      • 侵埋我玩吖 (cam1 maai4 ngo5 waan2 a1): "[Let] me join in too."

寢 ꜂Tsʻam (cam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼧ + 11
Unicode code point
U+5BE2
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 寢) 臥也息也又内室曰寢室
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜂寢)
    • To sleep;
    • rest, sleep;
    • to rest, to desist, to repose;
    • a back chamber, a bed-chamber;
    • an adytum in a temple;

    • ꜀on ꜂tsʻam (on1 cam2 安寢), to sleep quietly;

    • ꜂tsʻam shat꜆ (cam2 sat1 寢室) a dormitory; the ancestral hall in a mansion;
    • ꜁nán ꜂tsʻam (naan4 cam2 難寢), I cannot get asleep;
    • ꜂tsʻam ꜀ping (cam2 bing1 寢兵), to give rest to troops;
    • ꜁ling ꜂tsʻam (ling4 cam2 陵寢), the imperial mausolea near Mukten (瀋陽);
    • ching꜄ ꜂tsʻam (zing3 cam2 正寢), room behind the hall.

[魫] ꜂Chʻam (cam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⿂ + 4
Unicode code point
U+9B6B
Fan Wan entry
(Not present)
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂[魫])

    (Idiosyncratic reading from Williams; see 魫 cam4 for the canonical entry.)

Conway's commentary
  • Williams misprints 魫 as 𩵫 (⿰冘魚).
  • Williams' placement under ꜂Chʻam (cam2) is idiosyncratic because it does not follow any of the following:
    • zam2 from Fan Wan (which places it between 枕 and 怎)
    • cam4 from 《集韻》又持林切
    • sam2 from 《廣韻》式任切 rising.
    Alternatively, it is possible that Williams has simply misprinted ꜁魫 as ꜂魫, in which case ꜂chʻam (cam4) is intended.

讖 Chʻam꜄ (cam3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾔ + 17
Unicode code point
U+8B96
Alternative forms
  • 唚 (for vernacular "long-winded" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 讖) 符讖騐也凡讖緯皆以術數言將來之騐也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 讖꜄)
    • To verify, to prove;
    • a prognostic;
    • to fulfill;
    • chʻam꜄ ꜃ü (cam3 jyu5 讖語), a prophecy, a hidden meaning;
    • chʻam꜄ hí꜄ (cam3 hei3 讖氣) tiresome, talkative;
    • chʻam꜄ [꜃]wai (cam3 wai5 讖緯), to agree with the prophecy.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 讖緯: Corrected ꜂wai (wai2) to ꜃wai (wai5).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【讖】 (cam3):
    1. Long-winded or verbose.
      • 又長又讖 (jau6 coeng4 jau6 cam3): "Both long and verbose."
      • 咪咁讖啦 (mai5 gam3 cam3 la1): "Don't [be] so long-winded!"

尋 ꜁Tsʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼨ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5C0B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 尋) 搜求也覓也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜁尋)
    • To hunt after, to search, on the track of;
    • to seek, to investigate;
    • to use, to employ;
    • to continue, to connect with;
    • usually, ordinarily;
    • temporary, unexpectedly;
    • for a long time;
    • common;
    • a measure of eight cubits, or like the outstretched arms;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁'m chéuk꜇ (cam4 m4 zoek6 尋唔着) I can not find it;
    • ꜁tsʻam mí꜅ (cam4 mei6 尋味) to wish more of, as of a tidbit;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁tʻau lò꜅ (cam tau4 lou6 尋頭路) seeking for work;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁shéung (cam4 soeng4 尋常), usual, common;
    • ꜀kan ꜁tsʻam (gan1 cam4 跟尋), to hunt after one;
    • ꜁tsʻam mút꜇ (cam4 mut6 尋歿) died unexpectedly;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁tʻiú kai꜄ (cam4 tiu4 gai3-2 尋條計) to think out a plan;
    • ꜂cháu ꜁tsʻam (zaau2 cam4 找尋), to seek everywhere for;
    • ꜁tsʻam chʻan꜄ tò꜄ ꜃kʻü (cam4 can3 dou3-2 keoi5 尋趁到佢), I've got track of him;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜀kan man꜅ ꜂tai (cam4 gan1 man6 dai2 尋根問底), to investigate thoroughly;
    • ꜁tsʻam sz'꜅ (cam4 si6 尋事) to meddle with;
    • ꜁tsʻam mat꜇ (cam4 mat6 尋物), a colloquial phrase for yesterday.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The headword 尋 is printed like ⿳彑⿰工几寸.
  • Williams' 尋物: Probably assimilation of 尋日 (cam4 jat6) into 尋物 (cam4 mat6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【尋日】 (cam4 jat6):
    1. Yesterday.
      • 尋日病咗 (cam4 jat6 beng6 zo2): "Yesterday [I got] sick."

沉 ꜁Chʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6C89
Alternative forms
  • 沈
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 沉) 汲也溺也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜁沉꜁沈)
    • To sink;
    • to immerse;
    • to put under water, to be lost;
    • to quash, to suppress;
    • muddy, deep, dull, as colors;
    • a lake;
    • shat꜇ ꜂shau ꜁chʻam chui꜅ (sat6 sau2 cam4 zeoi6 實首沉墜) garrulous;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜁lun (cam4 leon4 沉淪), lost, ruined;
    • ꜁chʻam mút꜇ (cam4 mut6 沉沒) to lose in the water;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜀ün (cam4 jyun1 沉淵), lost in the depths;
    • ꜁chʻam on꜄ (cam4 on3 沉案) to quash a case;
    • ꜁cham nik[꜆] (cam4 nik1 沉溺) drowned, in doting on;
    • ꜁chʻam [꜃chʻung] (cam4 cung5 沉重) very sick;
    • shap꜇ ꜁chʻam ꜂kau ꜁pʻò (sap6 cam4 gau2 pou4 十沉九浮), the odds are against him;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜀héung (cam4 hoeng1 沉香), aloes.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 沉溺: Changed Middle Chinese nik꜇ (nik6) to common reading nik꜆ (nik1).
  • Williams' 沉重: Corrected chung꜅ (zung6 去聲) to ꜃chʻung (cung5 上聲).

鐔 ꜁Tsʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⾦ + 12
Unicode code point
U+9414
Reading variations
  • jam4 (expected from 餘針切)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 鐔) 劍鼻
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜁鐔)
    • The guard on the hilt of a sword;
    • a dagger, a dirk;
    • (see 鐔 taam4 for "sword's edge" sense)
Conway's commentary
  • 《廣韻》 provides three readings:
    • cam4 (from 徐林切)
    • jam4 (from 餘針切)
    • taam4 (from 徒含切).
    The first two are explained 劒鼻 "sword's nose" (meaning knob) and the third is explained 劒口 "sword's mouth" (meaning edge). Based on this, I conclude that the reading taam4 deserves its own entry; see 鐔 taam4.

魫 ꜁Chʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⿂ + 4
Unicode code point
U+9B6B
Reading variations
  • cam2 (probably errant)
  • sam2 (expected from 式任切 rising)
  • zam2 (uncommon)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 魫 ꜂Cham (zam2)) 魚名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂[魫] ꜂Chʻam (cam2); here normalised to ꜁Chʻam (cam4))
    • The roe or eggs of fish;
    • the name of a fish.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams misprints 魫 as 𩵫 (⿰冘魚).
  • The probably errant reading cam2 is that given by Williams; see 魫 cam2 for commentary.
  • The uncommon reading zam2 is that given in Fan Wan.
  • The reading cam4 is not unattested; Kangxi quotes 《集韻》又持林切音沉.

侵 ꜀Tsʻam (cam1)

Radical + residual strokes
⼈ + 7
Unicode code point
U+4FB5
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 36 侵) 侵占侵凌侵害
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜀侵)
    • To usurp, to incroach upon, to enter gradually (Kangxi: 漸進也);
    • to appropriate, to plunder, to invade (Kangxi: 朘削也);
    • possessed, by as a spirit;
    • bad, calamitous, as a year (Kangxi: 歲凶);
    • ꜀tsʻam hoi꜅ (cam1 hoi6 侵害) to injure insensibly;
    • ꜀tsʻam chím꜄ (cam1 zim3 侵佔) to unjustly incroach on;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜂pún (cam1 bun2 侵本), to draw on the principal;
    • ꜀tsʻam tik꜆ yap꜇ hü꜄ (cam1 dik1 jap6 heoi3 侵滴入去) stick in a few bad ones;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜀pín ꜁pʻái (cam1 bin1 paai4-2 侵辮牌), to braid in false hair;
    • tái꜅ ꜀tsʻam ꜀chí ꜁nín (daai6 cam1 zi1 nin4 大侵之年), a bad year;
    • ꜀tsʻam ꜁ling (cam1 ling4 侵凌), to intimidate, to oppress.

    (…)

Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【侵】 (cam1):
    1. To add in.
      • 侵水 (cam1 seoi2): "Add in [some] water."
    2. To join in.
      • 侵埋我玩吖 (cam1 maai4 ngo5 waan2 a1): "[Let] me join in too."

寢 ꜂Tsʻam (cam2)

Radical + residual strokes
⼧ + 11
Unicode code point
U+5BE2
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 寢) 臥也息也又內室曰寢室
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜂寢)
    • To sleep (Kangxi: 臥也);
    • rest, sleep;
    • to rest, to desist, to repose (Kangxi: 息也);
    • a back chamber, a bed-chamber (Kangxi: 堂室也);
    • an adytum in a temple (Kangxi: 寢廟凡廟前曰廟後曰寢);
    • a retiring-room in a palace;
    • a dwelling-house (Kangxi: 凡居室皆曰寢);
    • a mausoleum or house near a grave (Kangxi: 陵寢);
    • ꜀on ꜂tsʻam (on1 cam2 安寢), to sleep quietly;
    • ꜁kʻí sz'꜅ sui꜅ ꜂tsʻam (kei4 si6 seoi6 cam2 其事遂寢), the affair then terminated;
    • ꜂tsʻam shat꜆ (cam2 sat1 寢室) a dormitory; the ancestral hall in a mansion;
    • ꜁nán ꜂tsʻam (naan4 cam2 難寢), I cannot get asleep;
    • ꜂tsʻam ꜀ping (cam2 bing1 寢兵), to give rest to troops;
    • ꜁ling ꜂tsʻam (ling4 cam2 陵寢), the imperial mausolea near Mukten (瀋陽);
    • ching꜄ ꜂tsʻam (zing3 cam2 正寢), room behind the hall.
Conway's supplementary entry
  1. Ugly in appearance.

讖 Chʻam꜄ (cam3)

Radical + residual strokes
⾔ + 17
Unicode code point
U+8B96
Alternative forms
  • 唚 (for vernacular "long-winded" sense)
  • 譖 (for vernacular "long-winded" sense)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 讖) 符讖騐也凡讖緯皆以術數言將來之騐也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 讖꜄)
    • To verify, to prove (Kangxi: 驗也);
    • a prognostic (Kangxi: 凡讖緯皆言將來之驗也);
    • to fulfill;
    • chʻam꜄ ꜃ü (cam3 jyu5 讖語), a prophecy, a hidden meaning;
    • chʻam꜄ hí꜄ (cam3 hei3 讖氣) tiresome, talkative;
    • chʻam꜄ ꜂wai ꜃wai (cam3 wai5 讖緯), to agree with the prophecy.
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 讖緯: Corrected ꜂wai (wai2) to ꜃wai (wai5).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【讖】 (cam3):
    1. Long-winded or verbose.
      • 又長又讖 (jau6 coeng4 jau6 cam3): "Both long and verbose."
      • 咪咁讖啦 (mai5 gam3 cam3 la1): "Don't [be] so long-winded!"

尋 ꜁Tsʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⼨ + 9
Unicode code point
U+5C0B
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 尋) 搜求也覓也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 552 ꜁尋)
    • To hunt after, to search, on the track of;
    • to seek, to investigate (Kangxi: 繹理也, 求也);
    • to use, to employ (Kangxi: 用也);
    • to continue, to connect with (Kangxi: 仍也繼也);
    • usually, ordinarily;
    • temporary, unexpectedly (Kangxi: 俄也);
    • for a long time;
    • common (Kangxi: 俗謂庸常爲尋常);
    • a measure of eight cubits (度名, 八尺曰尋), or like the outstretched arms;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁'m chéuk꜇ (cam4 m4 zoek6 尋唔着) I can not find it;
    • ꜁tsʻam mí꜅ (cam4 mei6 尋味) to wish more of, as of a tidbit;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁tʻau lò꜅ (cam tau4 lou6 尋頭路) seeking for work;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁shéung (cam4 soeng4 尋常), usual, common;
    • ꜀kan ꜁tsʻam (gan1 cam4 跟尋), to hunt after one;
    • ꜁tsʻam mút꜇ (cam4 mut6 尋歿) died unexpectedly;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜁tʻiú kai꜄ (cam4 tiu4 gai3-2 尋條計) to think out a plan;
    • ꜂cháu ꜁tsʻam (zaau2 cam4 找尋), to seek everywhere for;
    • ꜁tsʻam chʻan꜄ tò꜄ ꜃kʻü (cam4 can3 dou3-2 keoi5 尋趁到佢), I've got track of him;
    • ꜁tsʻam ꜀kan man꜅ ꜂tai (cam4 gan1 man6 dai2 尋根問底), to investigate thoroughly;
    • ꜁tsʻam sz'꜅ (cam4 si6 尋事) to meddle with;
    • ꜁tsʻam mat꜇ (cam4 mat6 尋物) a colloquial phrase for yesterday.
Conway's commentary
  • Fan Wan entry: The headword 尋 is printed like ⿳彑⿰工几寸.
  • Williams' "for a long time": Kangxi's explanation 長也 is accompanied by an example ending in 凡物長謂之尋, so it appears to indicate spatial length rather than temporal.
  • Williams' 尋物: Probably assimilation of 尋日 (cam4 jat6) into 尋物 (cam4 mat6).
Conway's Cantonese entry
  • 【尋日】 (cam4 jat6):
    1. Yesterday.
      • 尋日病咗 (cam4 jat6 beng6 zo2): "Yesterday [I got] sick."

沉 ꜁Chʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⽔ + 4
Unicode code point
U+6C89
Alternative forms
  • 沈
  • 湛
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 沉) 汲也溺也
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜁沉꜁沈)
    • To sink (Kangxi 沈: 沒也);
    • to immerse;
    • to put under water, to be lost;
    • to quash, to suppress;
    • muddy, deep, dull, as colors;
    • a lake (Kangxi 沈: 齊人謂湖曰沈);
    • shat꜇ ꜂shau ꜁chʻam chui꜅ (sat6 sau2 cam4 zeoi6 實首沉墜) garrulous;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜁lun (cam4 leon4 沉淪), lost, ruined;
    • ꜁chʻam mút꜇ (cam4 mut6 沉沒) to lose in the water;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜀ün (cam4 jyun1 沉淵), lost in the depths;
    • ꜁chʻam on꜄ (cam4 on3 沉案) to quash a case;
    • ꜁chʻam nik꜇ nik꜆ (cam4 nik1 沉溺) drowned, in doting on;
    • ꜁chʻam chung꜅ ꜃chʻung (cam4 cung5 沉重) very sick;
    • shap꜇ ꜁chʻam ꜂kau ꜁pʻò (sap6 cam4 gau2 pou4 十沉九浮), the odds are against him;
    • ꜁chʻam ꜀héung (cam4 hoeng1 沉香), aloes.
  • (Page 16 湛꜄ Chám꜄ (zaam3); here corrected to ꜁Chʻam (cam4) and normalised to 沉)
    • (…);
    • to sink in (Kangxi 湛: 與沈同);
    • (…).
Conway's commentary
  • Williams' 沉溺: Changed nik꜇ (nik6) to common reading nik꜆ (nik1).
  • Williams' 沉重: Corrected chung꜅ (zung6 去聲) to ꜃chʻung (cung5 上聲).
  • For commentary on departing-tone 直禁切 cham꜅ (zam6) in relation to the vernacular zam6 for "drown", see 浸 zam3.

魫 ꜁Chʻam (cam4)

Radical + residual strokes
⿂ + 4
Unicode code point
U+9B6B
Reading variations
  • sam2 (per 《廣韻》式[荏]切)
  • zam2 (per Fan Wan)
Fan Wan entry
  • (Vol. 2 Image 37 魫 ꜂Cham (zam2)) 魚名
Williams (1856) entry
  • (Page 15 ꜂𩵫魫 ꜂Chʻam (cam2); here corrected to ꜁Chʻam (cam4))
    • The roe or eggs of fish (Kangxi: 魚子);
    • the name of a fish (Fan Wan: 魚名).
Conway's commentary
  • The reading ꜁chʻam (cam4) is not unattested; 《集韻》 has the reading 持林切, explained 魚子也.
  • Williams' placement under ꜂Chʻam (cam2) is erroneous because it does not follow any of the following:
    • ꜂cham (zam2) per Fan Wan (which places it between 枕 and 怎)
    • ꜁chʻam (cam4) per 《集韻》持林切.
    • ꜂sham (sam2) per 《廣韻》式任切 rising.
    It is possible that Williams has simply misprinted ꜁ as ꜂, in which case ꜁chʻam (cam4) is intended.
